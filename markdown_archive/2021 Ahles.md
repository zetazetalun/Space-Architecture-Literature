# Mechanical properties of meteoritic Fe–Ni alloys for in-situ extraterrestrial structures

**Authors:** Aaron A. Ahles, Jonathan D. Emery, David C. Dunand  
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 189 (2021) 465–475  
**Date:** Available online 4 September 2021

## Abstract
A critical requirement for outposts and settlements on the Moon and Mars is the use of in-situ resources to create pressurized, human-rated habitats with low mass, high reliability, and easy processability. This study assesses the capability of binary Fe–Ni alloys—with Ni content of 5–11 wt%, typical of M-type asteroids and iron meteorites—to meet these requirements. The study measures hardness and tensile properties of these alloys at 21 °C and assesses temperature-dependence relevant to the Moon and Mars (-89 to 21 °C). A pressurized habitat modeled as a Quonset hut (5 m radius, 10 m length) was analyzed via finite element analysis (FEA). Findings suggest that 10 mm thick Fe–7Ni plates are sufficient to prevent plastic deformation, corresponding to roughly 20–30 iron-nickel meteorites of 0.5 ton mass each.

## 1. Introduction
Iron-based alloys (steels) are candidates for pressurized habitats due to excellent tensile properties and fatigue resistance. Fe–Ni alloys are particularly attractive because they are available as asteroids in space or meteorites on the surfaces of the Moon and Mars, where they are already in a fully reduced metallic state, minimizing energy-intensive refinement.

## 2. Experimental Study
- **Materials:** Binary Fe-xNi alloys (x = 5, 7, 9, 11, 13 wt%) arc-melted and cast.
- **Microstructure:** Grain sizes of ~90 μm. Solidification forms a martensitic structure.
- **Mechanical Properties (at 21 °C):**
    - **Hardness:** Increases from 1410 MPa (5 wt% Ni) to 2890 MPa (13 wt% Ni).
    - **Yield Stress:** Increases from 260 to 440 MPa.
    - **UTS:** Increases from 340 to 560 MPa.
    - **Ductility:** Decreases from 20% to 3%.

## 3. Finite Element Modeling
A simulated half-cylindrical habitat (radius 5m, length 10m) with 1 bar internal pressure was modeled. 
- **Plate Thickness:** 10 mm Fe-7Ni plates prevent plastic deformation.
- **Mass:** 12–31 metric tons (MT) for a habitat suitable for ~20 people.
- **Source:** 24–60 meteorites of ~500 kg mass (as identified by NASA rovers on Mars).

## 4. Conclusion
Fe–Ni meteorites are a credible raw material for pressurized or load-bearing structures on Mars and the Moon. Simple melting, casting, and welding can produce viable habitats with infrastructure and energy costs likely lower than shipping steel sheets from Earth.
