# Mechanical properties of meteoritic Fe–Ni alloys for in-situ extraterrestrial structures

**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 189 (2021) 465–475  
**Authors:** Aaron A. Ahles, Jonathan D. Emery, David C. Dunand  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.actaastro.2021.09.001

## Abstract
A critical requirement for outposts and settlements on the Moon and Mars is the use of in-situ resources to create pressurized, human-rated habitats. This study assesses the capability of binary Fe–Ni alloys (5–11 wt% Ni), typical of M-type asteroids and iron meteorites. Hardness and tensile properties were measured at 21 °C in the as-cast state. Strength increases near-linearly with Ni content (260 to 440 MPa yield stress), while ductility decreases. Temperature-dependence was modeled using an Arrhenius-like approach. Finite element analysis of a pressurized Quonset-style habitat (5 m radius, 10 m length) indicates that 10 mm thick Fe–7Ni plates are sufficient for 1 bar pressure on the Moon or Mars, requiring approximately 12–31 metric tons of material.

## 1. Introduction
Extraterrestrial settlements require materials with high tensile strength and toughness. ISRU is favored to avoid the prohibitive cost of Earth-to-space transportation. While sintered regolith is weak in tension, metallic alloys extracted from meteorites offer high tensile properties. Fe-Ni meteorites are particularly attractive as they are already in a metallic state, reducing the energy needed for processing compared to oxide-based ores.

## 2. Experimental Study
### 2.1. Materials and methods
Binary Fe-xNi alloys (x = 5, 7, 9, 11, 13 wt%) were arc-melted. Tensile testing was performed at 21 °C. Compressive yield stress was measured between −89 and 21 °C to represent Lunar and Martian environments.

### 2.2. Results and discussion
- **Microstructure:** Alloys showed prior gamma-Fe grains transformed to martensite upon cooling, with grain sizes ~90 μm.
- **Mechanical Properties:** Hardness increased from 1410 MPa (5 wt% Ni) to 2890 MPa (13 wt% Ni). Yield stress at 21 °C for as-cast alloys ranged from 260 to 440 MPa.
- **Temperature Effects:** Yield stress increases significantly at lower temperatures (e.g., Martian night). For Fe-5Ni, yield stress predicted at 102 K (Moon night) is 1168 MPa.

## 3. Finite Element Modeling
A 400 m³ half-cylinder habitat was modeled using COMSOL. 
- **Plate thickness:** Simulated at 5, 7.5, 10, 15, 20, and 25 mm.
- **Material:** Fe-7Ni alloy (Density: 7.82 g/cm³, Young's Modulus: 201 GPa).
- **Findings:** Plates ≥ 10 mm thick prevent plastic deformation under 1 bar internal pressure, even accounting for stress concentrations at welds.

## 4. Conclusions
Fe–Ni alloys from meteorites (5–11 wt% Ni) possess mechanical properties suitable for pressurized habitats. A single habitat for 20 people could be constructed from 20–30 half-ton iron meteorites, confirming the feasibility of ISRU-based metallic construction on planetary surfaces.
