# Mechanical properties of meteoritic Fe–Ni alloys for in-situ extraterrestrial structures

**Authors:** Aaron A. Ahles, Jonathan D. Emery, David C. Dunand  
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 189 (2021) 465–475  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.actaastro.2021.09.001

## Abstract
A critical requirement for outposts and settlements on the Moon and Mars is the use of in-situ resources to create pressurized, human-rated habitats with low mass, high reliability, and easy processability. This study assesses the capability of binary Fe–Ni alloys - with Ni content of 5–11 wt%, typical of M-type asteroids and iron meteorites - to meet these requirements. Hardness and tensile properties were measured at 21 °C and cryogenic temperatures. Modeling of a 400 m³ pressurized habitat (Quonset hut) confirms the feasibility of using 10-25 mm thick plates made from melted and cast meteoritic material.

## 1. Introduction
Extraterrestrial settlements require pressurized habitats made from materials with high tensile strength and toughness. While regolith and concrete are suitable for compression, metals are needed for pressurized shells. Iron-based alloys (steels) are excellent candidates due to their availability as metallic meteorites on the Moon and Mars, which avoids the energy-intensive reduction processes required for oxide ores.

## 2. Experimental Study
### 2.1 Materials and Methods
Binary Fe-xNi alloys (x = 5, 7, 9, 11, 13 wt%) were arc-melted. Tensile testing was carried out at 21 °C, and compressive yield stress was measured between -89 and 21 °C to represent Lunar and Martian environments.

### 2.2 Results and Discussion
#### 2.2.1 Microstructure
As-cast alloys showed prior γ-Fe grains transformed to martensite upon rapid cooling. Grain sizes were approximately 90 μm. Pores (1-5 mm) were observed but can be eliminated through better casting or thermomechanical processing.

## 3. Finite Element Modeling
A half-cylindrical pressurized habitat (5 m radius, 10 m length) was modeled in COMSOL. 
- **Shell:** Fe-7Ni alloy plates.
- **Foundation:** Sintered regolith.
- **Shielding:** 2 m loose regolith pile.
- **Finding:** A 10 mm plate thickness is sufficient to prevent plastic deformation under 1 bar internal pressure, with maximum stresses concentrated at welded joints.

## 4. Conclusions
Fe–Ni alloys from meteorites are suitable for load-bearing structures in orbit or on planetary surfaces. A small habitat (~400 m³) requires 12–31 metric tons of alloy, which could be sourced from approximately 24–60 medium-sized meteorites found on the Martian surface. This provides a low-energy pathway for early settlement construction.
