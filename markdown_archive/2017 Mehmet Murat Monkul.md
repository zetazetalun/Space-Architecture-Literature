# Effect of grain size distribution on stress-strain behavior of lunar soil simulants

## Abstract
Geotechnical behavior of lunar soils is important for engineering analyses regarding future extraterrestrial settlement plans including lunar exploration and construction. The goal of this study is to investigate how grain size distribution (GSD) variations affect shear strength and volume change behaviors. Static simple shear tests were conducted on four lunar soil simulants (GRC-1, TJ-1, BP-1, QH-E) reproduced for gradation. Results indicate that mean grain size (D50), coefficient of uniformity (CU), and fines content (FC) influence shear strength and dilatancy levels at the same density or void ratio.

## 1. Introduction
Establishing permanent lunar bases involves interaction with the local regolith. Understanding geotechnical properties is significant for the sustainability of lunar bases, soft landings, site preparation, and radiation shielding.

## 3. Experimental Program

## 4. Results
### 4.1 Drained shear strength
- Shear strength increased with normal stress.
- For Y-GRC-1 and Y-TJ-1, failure surfaces were nearly parallel with peak friction angles of 27.2° and 27.1° respectively.
- Residual friction angles were 25.2° (Y-GRC-1) and 17.9° (Y-TJ-1).
- Peak secant friction angles decreased from ~58° at 10 kPa to ~38° at 40 kPa.

## 5. Conclusions
Gradational characteristics influence shear strength and volume change. Well-graded simulants with high fines content may have a looser configuration of the sand-grain matrix, leading to higher contractive behavior during shear. These effects must be evaluated when producing and utilizing future simulants for lunar construction planning.
