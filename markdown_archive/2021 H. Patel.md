# Applying Terrestrial Geo-Material Science Methodology to Lunar ISRU Construction

**Authors:** H. Patel, P. Suermann, N. Shumaker, S. Deitrick, J. W. Bullard, and R. C. Ewing  
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE

## ABSTRACT
Building on research underway for military and expeditionary construction, this research aims to establish methods for the design and testing of the materials mixtures derived from the lunar surface with minimal or no additives that need to be transported from the Earth. This paper reviews opportunities and challenges of demonstrated and experimental construction methods such as concreting and sintering as well as the need to evaluate and develop these methods using regolith simulants more representative of the lunar highlands where the NASA Artemis mission proposes to land.

## INTRODUCTION
As the world prepares for the first lunar return in nearly 50 years, there is a pressing need to develop construction methods using in-situ (locally available) materials for critical infrastructure. This includes site preparation for horizontal (landing pads, roads) and vertical structures (berms, blast and radiation shielding).

## LUNAR SURFACE ENVIRONMENT
### Terrain and Material
*   **Mare Regions:** ~15% of surface, basaltic lava flows, relatively young.
*   **Highlands:** Composed of anorthosite, much older, thicker regolith layer, higher percentage of agglutinates.

### Regolith Composition
*   **Particle Size:** 95% < 1.37 mm; 50% < 50 μm. Bulk density: 1.30 to 1.92 g/cm³.
*   **Components:** Mineral fragments, crystalline rock fragments, breccia fragments, glasses, and agglutinates (fused particles of impact glass).

## LUNAR SURFACE CONSTRUCTION METHODS

### 1. Concreting Methods
*   **Concept:** Binding grains within a chemically activated matrix.
*   **Challenges:** Scarcity of calcium-based minerals and liquid water.
*   **Alternatives:** Geopolymeric cements (aluminosilicate precursors) or sulfur binders (melting point ~115°C).

### 2. Sintering Methods
*   **Concept:** Fusing fine grains via heat (60-80% of melting temperature).
*   **Advantages:** Requires no water, works in vacuum, utilizes lunar glass/agglutinates which provide capillary attraction when viscous.
*   **Innovative Methods:** Spark Plasma Sintering (SPS) uses pulsed electric current and pressure for rapid densification.

### 3. Basalt Glass Fiber
*   Derived by melting and drawing lunar regolith. Can be used with polymer binders (from recycled waste/bioplastics) or sintered directly.

## METHODOLOGY: THE THAMES MODEL
The **Thermodynamic Hydration and Microstructure Evolution Simulator (THAMES)** is being adapted to predict mineralogical components and 3D microstructures for lunar concrete and sintered materials. This allows researchers to calculate engineering properties and assess potential deleterious product development.

## CONCLUSION
The research emphasizes the need for physically and chemically accurate simulants (specifically for highland regions) to test ISRU materials. The ultimate goal is to establish ASTM/ASCE approved standards for lunar construction to support NASA's "to stay" mission by 2024.
