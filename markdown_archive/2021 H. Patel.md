# Applying Terrestrial Geo-Material Science Methodology to Lunar ISRU Construction

**Authors:** H. Patel, P. Suermann, N. Shumaker, S. Deitrick, J. W. Bullard, and R. C. Ewing
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021 (ASCE)

## Abstract
Building on research underway for military and expeditionary construction, this research aims to establish methods for the design and testing of the materials mixtures derived from the lunar surface with minimal or no additives. This paper reviews opportunities and challenges of methods such as concreting and sintering and discusses the need to evaluate these methods using regolith simulants representative of the lunar highlands.

## Introduction
There is a pressing need to develop construction methods using in-situ materials for critical infrastructure like landing pads, roads, berms, and radiation shielding. Generating building products from locally available resources is required because the scale of operations precludes exclusive use of imported Earth resources.

## Background: Lunar Surface Environment

### Terrain and Material
- **Mare:** ~15% of the surface, basaltic lava flows, relatively young, thinner regolith.
- **Highlands:** Anorthosite, older, more cratered, thicker regolith layer.

### Regolith Properties
- **Particle Size:** ~95% < 1.37 mm; ~50% < 50 μm. Average size: 72 μm.
- **Bulk Density:** 1.30 g/cm³ to 1.92 g/cm³.
- **Composition:** Soil (< 1 mm fraction) ~70% by mass; Dust (< 20 μm) ~20% by mass.

### Agglutinates
Fused particles of impact glass, rock, and mineral fragments. Content increases with regolith maturity (5% to 65% by volume). Highlands have higher agglutinate content.

## Lunar Surface Construction—Methods and Considerations

### Concreting Methods
- **Traditional:** Scarcity of water and calcium on the Moon makes Portland cement difficult.
- **Geopolymers:** Aluminosilicate precursors (available on Moon) + chemical activator.
- **Sulfur Concrete:** Uses sulfur (from troilite) as a binder. Low melting point (~115 °C). Challenges: volatility, toxicity, low durability.

### Sintering Methods
Fusing grains via heat (60%–80% of melting temperature). 
- **Advantages:** No water required; works in vacuum.
- **Spark Plasma Sintering (SPS):** Uses pulse electric current and pressure; provides high density and short processing times.

### Additive Construction
Based on extrusion (concreting) or selective heating of a powder bed (sintering).

## Methodology: THAMES
The research utilizes the **Thermodynamic Hydration and Microstructure Evolution Simulator (THAMES)** to predict mineralogical components and 3-D virtual microstructures, allowing for the calculation of engineering properties and assessment of material stability.

## Materials and Processing Characterization
Emphasis on using accurate simulants like those from Off Planet and Outward Technologies which include agglutinates, rather than just JSC-1A (which mimics mare regions).

## Conclusion
The objective is to adapt existing terrestrial models to evaluate regolith-based materials and develop ASTM or ASCE standards for ISRU construction to support NASA's goal of a permanent lunar presence.
