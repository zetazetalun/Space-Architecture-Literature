**Abstract**— Advance of civilization has been realized through the ages by exploration of unknown parts of the Earth and by exploiting new resources thus acquired. There are currently practically no new areas on this planet to be explored and exploited. This is achieved by the completion of the period called globalization. As a natural continuation of this process, humans will now explore and exploit new areas beyond Earth, i.e. in space. Moon has already been explored partly, it awaits a complete exploration and colonization. The next step will be Mars and beyond. Habitation on these new space bodies will firstly necessitate construction of outposts on these bodies. Thus construction on the Moon, Mars, and other space bodies can be considered to be an important component of space research. In the present article, an overview of space research is presented together with the innovations awaited in space construction industry and the importance of feedback of these studies to construction industry in general.

**Keywords—Space, Earth, Moon, Mars Civil Engineering.**

## I. INTRODUCTION
First environment of humans was a few kilometers around their habitats. They were exploiting the resources in that environment. They knew the characteristics of the "nature" which was prevailing in that area.

As human groups, clans widened their environmental area, the resources became richer and they met new "natures". Richer resources enabled them to acquire a higher level of economy; meeting new natures made them more flexible and more powerful to deal with new difficulties thus increasing their technological abilities. Reaching higher levels of economy and technology gave them the power for conquering new areas thus widening their "world"... The loop shown in Fig. 1 will now lead humans to extraterrestrial places.

## II. SPATIALIZATION - WORLD 2050
The Russian scientist Konstantin E. Tsiolkovsky (1857-1935) had said "The Earth is the Cradle of Mankind, but one cannot expect to remain forever in the cradle". His epitaph, written by himself reads: "Man will not always stay on Earth; the pursuit of light and space will lead him to penetrate the bounds of the atmosphere, timidly at first, but in the end to conquer the whole of solar space". 

## III. PIONEERS OF GOING TO MOON AND MARS
Currently there are more than 70 countries and unions who have a "space program" of some sort, and only 10 of them have proved their capacity of launching satellites into space... It is expected that the number of countries with manned missions to the Moon will have increased until 2030, and there will be at least one manned mission to Mars around 2050.

## IV. HUMANS ON SPACE BODIES
A very important aspect of space bodies, at least of those that humans can see and observe currently, is their hostile environment. Indeed, on none of the space bodies mentioned in this article, men cannot live even a part of a second without taking special measures, due to differences between the atmosphere of Earth and atmosphere of these bodies.

## V. HABITATION / CONSTRUCTION ON SPACE BODIES
Construction on space bodies will be carried on by the next generation of civil engineers, of course in close collaboration with engineers and scientists from many other disciplines [2,3].

In inhabiting space bodies, the first habitations will certainly be those brought from Earth. Carrying 1 kg of material from Earth to Moon for instance, costs about 20000 US$. That price is much higher for Mars and beyond... In fact, there will be 3 ways of using in-situ materials:
- Use raw in-situ material as much as possible.
- Find effective and simple ways of treating in-situ material for being used in constructions.
- Develop techniques for advanced treatment of in-situ materials for extracting elements and minerals.

### B. A better understanding of structures
On space bodies in consideration the gravitational loads are as important as on Earth due to shielding materials. But effects coming from inside pressure is much more important. Cracks are not allowed. These changes will lead to new innovations in construction industry. Other sources of changes will be [6-9].
- Constructions in lava tubes
- Habitats embedded in regolith
- Tensegric structures
- Structures with reinforced regolith walls
- Inflatable structures
- Extensive use of prestressing
- Structures with no stress concentrations

### C. Automated construction
Due to the difficulties and dangers in using manual workmanship, automated mining, earthwork and construction techniques will be advanced and used on extraterrestrial bodies. Construction industry will be fully automated.

### D. Special construction machines
Special environmental lunar and Martian conditions will enforce development of special construction machines. They will be electrically operated, fully automatic and/or tele commanded, lighter, and with special measures against dust.

### E. Nonlinearity
On Earth, construction materials are used in linear zone for being on the safe side... On space bodies, the designers will not have that luxury because of very high material costs and difficulties in supply. All materials will be exploited up to their maximum limits, of course without concession from safety [14].

### F. Building Information Systems
All the structures will be controlled, managed by a central management system. There will be sensors measuring pressure, temperature, humidity, light, oxygen content, CO2, humidity, stresses and strains in the structures, etc... 

### G. Waste treatment
There will be a very advanced waste management system. All liquid, solid and gaseous wastes will be treated to a maximum extent and recycled for repeated use.
