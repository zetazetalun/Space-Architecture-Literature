# SPACE RESEARCH AND EXTRATERRESTRIAL CONSTRUCTION INDUSTRY ## Authors Yusuf Cengiz TOKLU (Okan University), Ali Erdem ÇERÇEVIK (Bilecik Seyh Edebali University) ## Abstract Advance of civilization is tied to resource exploration. Globalization on Earth is complete; the next step is 'spatialization' targeting the Moon and Mars. Habitation requires outposts, making construction on these bodies a vital component of space research. This article provides an overview of space construction innovations and the feedback loop to terrestrial industry. ## Introduction Mankind's history is a continuous loop of expanding territory and meeting 'new natures' to improve economy and technology. This process, which led to globalization by the 2000s, will now extend to extraterrestrial environments. ## Pioneers and Projections By 2050, manned missions to Mars are expected. Previous short trips to the Moon will evolve into long-term settlements. Currently, private organizations are joining the space race to realize these missions. ## Habitation and Construction ### Resource Utilization Carrying material from Earth costs ~$20,000/kg for the Moon and significantly more for Mars. Special lightweight, dust-protected machinery is required. ## Conclusions Civil engineering must adapt to 'new Nature' in space, which will eventually lead to technological innovations benefiting Earth construction.
