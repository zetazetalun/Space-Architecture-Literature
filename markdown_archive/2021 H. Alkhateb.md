# Optimizing Magnesium Phosphate Binders with Boric Acid for Additive Construction Applications

**Authors:** H. Alkhateb, H. Almashaqbeh, J. Edmunson, M. Fiske, Y. Najjar, and Damian Stoddard
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE

## Abstract
The authors propose a framework for using magnesia phosphate cement (MPC) as an alternative to ordinary portland cement (OPC) for extraterrestrial habitats. MPCs are promising for additive construction (AC) due to high compressive strength and the possibility of harvesting components from lunar and Martian regolith. This research investigates the use of boric acid as a retarder to improve setting times for 3D printing and utilizes artificial neural networks (ANN) to optimize mix formulations.

## Introduction
Additive Manufacturing (AM) in planetary construction faces challenges including material scarcity and extreme environmental conditions. Utilizing in-situ resources (ISRU) for habitat construction on the Moon and Mars reduces launch costs. MPC is formed by reacting magnesium oxide with a phosphate compound; since both planetary surfaces are rich in magnesium-bearing minerals, MPC is a prime candidate for ISRU binders. However, its extremely rapid setting time (seconds to minutes) currently limits its use in AC technology.

## Magnesium Phosphate Cements (MPC)
MPC belongs to the chemically bonded ceramics (CBCs) group. The reaction traditionally produces struvite [MgNH4PO4.6H2O], but to avoid ammonia odors, potassium dihydrogen phosphate (KDP) is used to produce K-struvite [MgKPO4.6H2O]. Fresh and hardened properties are heavily influenced by the Magnesia-to-Phosphate (M/P) molar ratio and the Water-to-Solid (W/S) ratio.

## Methodology
The study evaluates the effect of boric acid (H3BO3) on the performance and microstructure of MPC. It utilizes an experimental program focusing on:
1. Physical properties (Workability, Initial Setting Time)
2. Mechanical properties (Compressive Strength)
3. ANN models for real-time optimization.

## Results and Discussion
### Influence of Boric Acid
- **Setting Time:** Adding boric acid increases the initial setting time. For the B3-22 group, adding 5% boric acid increased setting time from 3.5 to 15 minutes. However, exceeding 5% can adversely affect performance due to fluidity loss.
- **Mechanism:** Boric acid likely forms a temporary layer of lunebergite [Mg3B2(PO4)2(OH)*6H2O] that coats magnesium cations and hinders dissolution.

### Compressive Strength
- **M/P Ratio:** Optimal M/P ratios depend on water content. For a W/P ratio of 3.8, the optimal M/P ratio is between 6 and 8.
- **Impact of Retarders:** Boric acid reduces compressive strength at low M/P ratios (up to 64% reduction at 10% boric acid). At high M/P ratios, the reduction is marginal.

### ANN Optimization
An ANN model (5-5-3 structure) was developed using 138 data sets. The model predicted flow, setting time, and strength with high confidence (R² = 0.93 for training and 0.84 for testing).

## Conclusions
- MPC fresh and hardened properties can be tailored by manipulating M/P, W/P, and B/M ratios.
- Using up to 5% boric acid improves setting times suitable for 3D printing.
- ANN models are effective for real-time adjustment of 3D printing processes based on material performance criteria.
