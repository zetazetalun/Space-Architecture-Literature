# Optimizing Magnesium Phosphate Binders with Boric Acid for Additive Construction Applications

**Authors:** H. Alkhateb, H. Almashaqbeh, J. Edmunson, M. Fiske, Y. Najjar, and Damian Stoddard

**Affiliations:** Univ. of Mississippi; Isra Univ., Jordan; Jacobs Technology Inc. (NASA MSFC).

## ABSTRACT
The authors propose a framework to utilize magnesia-based binders (MBBs) as an alternative to ordinary portland cement (OPC) for both extraterrestrial habitats and terrestrial 3D construction. MBBs show promise for additive construction (AC) due to high compressive strength, rapid setting, and durability. A primary objective is proving the concept of utilizing magnesium phosphate cement (MPC) composites with additives like boric acid, graphene nanoplatelets (GnP), and regolith simulants. Experimental results show that boric acid improves initial setting time and alters binding product structure. Artificial neural networks (ANN) are used to optimize formulations.

## INTRODUCTION
Additive Manufacturing (AM) in construction can reduce costs and time but faces challenges in material development for planetary environments. Utilizing in-situ resources (ISRU) for habitats on the Moon and Mars reduces launch payloads. MPC, formed by reacting magnesium oxide and a soluble phosphate, is a candidate because lunar and Martian surfaces are rich in magnesium-bearing minerals. However, MPC's rapid setting time (seconds) usually excludes it from AC unless retarders are used.

## MATERIALS AND METHODS
### Materials
- Dead-burned magnesia (MgO): 98% purity.
- Monopotassium phosphate (KDP).
- Boric acid (H3BO3): 99.9% purity.
- Distilled water.

### Specimen Preparation
39 specimens were prepared across 11 groups to test M/P (magnesia to phosphate), W/P (water to phosphate), and B/M (boric acid to magnesia) ratios. Specimens were named by ratios (e.g., B32205: M/P 3, W/P 2.2, B/M 5%).

## RESULTS AND DISCUSSION
### Influence of Boric Acid
Boric acid increases setting time up to a concentration of 5% (B/M). For the B3-22 group, 5% boric acid increased setting time from 3.5 to 15 minutes. Beyond 5%, setting time may decrease due to rapid fluidity loss. The retardation is likely caused by the formation of a temporary layer of lunebergite (Mg3B2(PO4)2(OH)*6H2O) coating MgO particles.

### Compressive Strength
- **M/P Ratio:** For W/P of 3.0, the highest strength was found at M/P 6 (~55 MPa). For higher W/P (4.6), the optimal M/P shifts toward 10.
- **B/M Ratio:** At low M/P ratios, increasing boric acid significantly reduces strength (e.g., 64% reduction for B3-18 at 10% B/M). At high M/P ratios, the effect is marginal.

### ANN Numerical Simulation
A 5-5-3 ANN structure (5 input nodes, 5 hidden, 3 output) predicted flow, setting time, and strength with high accuracy. The R-squared value for the training set was 0.93.

## CONCLUSIONS
- M/P, W/P, and B/M ratios can tailor MPC properties for 3D printing.
- Boric acid up to 5% (B/M) improves setting time but can decrease workability over time.
- Optimal M/P ratios depend heavily on water content.
- ANN models provide a reliable tool for real-time printing optimization.
