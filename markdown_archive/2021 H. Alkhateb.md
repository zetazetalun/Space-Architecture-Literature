# Optimizing Magnesium Phosphate Binders with Boric Acid for Additive Construction Applications

**Authors:** H. Alkhateb, H. Almashaqbeh, J. Edmunson, M. Fiske, Y. Najjar, and Damian Stoddard
**Conference:** Earth and Space 2021
**Publisher:** ASCE

## Abstract
The authors propose a framework to utilize magnesia-based binders (MBBs) as an alternative to ordinary portland cement (OPC) for both extraterrestrial habitats and terrestrial 3D construction. Magnesium phosphate cement (MPC) shows promise due to high compressive strength and the possibility of harvesting components from lunar and Martian regolith. The study focuses on using boric acid as an additive to retard the extremely rapid setting time of MPC, making it suitable for additive construction (AC). Artificial neural networks (ANN) are utilized to optimize formulation for real-time 3D printing.

## Introduction
Additive Manufacturing (AM) in construction faces challenges in material durability and process improvement. For planetary construction, utilizing in-situ resources (ISRU) for habitat structures is critical to reducing mission costs. MPC, formed by the reaction between magnesium oxide and a soluble phosphate, is identified as a candidate binder because Moon and Mars surfaces are rich in magnesium-bearing minerals. However, its natural setting time (seconds) is too fast for 3D printing, requiring retarders like boric acid.

## Methodology
The research evaluates three primary factors:
1. **M/P Ratio:** Magnesia to phosphate molar ratio.
2. **W/P Ratio:** Water to phosphate ratio.
3. **B/M Ratio:** Boric acid to magnesia mass ratio.

Experimental testing measured workability, initial setting time, and compressive strength. ANN models (structure 5-5-3) were trained on 138 data sets to predict material performance based on mix ingredients.

## Key Findings
- **Setting Time:** A 5% B/M ratio was found to be the optimum for retarding the setting time (increasing it from ~3.5 to 15 minutes) for a specific mix (B3-22).
- **Compressive Strength:** Higher B/M ratios generally reduce compressive strength. For low M/P ratios, adding 10% boric acid reduced strength by up to 64%.
- **Water Sensitivity:** MPC is significantly more sensitive to water content than OPC; an 18% increase in the W/P ratio can increase fluidity by over 70%.
- **ANN Prediction:** The ANN models showed high confidence ($R^2$ of 0.89 for training all data) in predicting MPC properties for real-time adjustments.

## Conclusions
MPC binders can be tailored for 3D printing by manipulating molecular ratios. While boric acid effectively increases workability and setting time, it must be balanced against loss in structural density and compressive strength. The study suggests that up to 5% B/M ratio is the ideal threshold for optimizing MPC for planetary habitation construction.
