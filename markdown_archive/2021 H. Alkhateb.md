# Optimizing Magnesium Phosphate Binders with Boric Acid for Additive Construction Applications

**Authors:** H. Alkhateb, H. Almashaqbeh, J. Edmunson, M. Fiske, Y. Najjar, and Damian Stoddard
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021 (ASCE)

## Abstract
The authors are proposing a comprehensive research approach and terminology for providing a framework to utilize an ordinary portland cement (OPC) alternative class of materials for both extraterrestrial habitats and terrestrial 3D construction applications, where sand and clay can replace regolith. Magnesia-based binders (MBBs) have shown a great promise for use in different additive construction (AC) applications due to its superior properties such as high compressive strength, rapid setting time, and excellent durability, in addition to the possibility of harvesting these binders from lunar and Martian regolith.

## Introduction
Utilizing in-situ resources for the construction of habitat structures on the Moon and Mars will increase the efficiency of long-duration space missions and will reduce the number of payloads launched from Earth. Magnesium phosphate cement (MPC) is formed by the reaction between magnesium oxide and a soluble phosphate compound. Since both the Moon and Mars’s surfaces are rich with magnesium-bearing minerals, MPCs could be a candidate construction binder.

## Methodology
The experimental program studied the effect of boric acid on the performance and microstructure of MPC. Finally, Artificial Neural Network (ANN) models are utilized to investigate and subsequently optimize both the MPC pastes and the composite mix formulations for desired real-time 3D printing performance requirements.

## Results and Discussion
- **Initial Setting Time:** Boric acid acts as a retarder. For specific mixes, adding 5% boric acid increased the initial setting time from 3.5 to 15 minutes. However, increasing the B/M ratio beyond 5% reduced the setting time.
- **Compressive Strength:** Compressive strength decreases as the B/M ratio increases in low M/P ratio mixes. For instance, in the B3-18 series, adding 5% boric acid reduced strength by 47%.
- **ANN Modeling:** The ANN1-1 model (structure 5-5-3) successfully predicted flow, setting time, and compressive strength with high confidence (R² for training = 0.93).

## Conclusions
By manipulating the M/P, W/P, and B/M molecular ratios, one can tailor MPC properties for 3D printing. Boric acid is effective for improving initial setting time up to a 5% addition, though it can negatively impact compressive strength in low M/P ratio formulations.
