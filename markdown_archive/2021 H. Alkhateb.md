# Optimizing Magnesium Phosphate Binders with Boric Acid for Additive Construction Applications

**Authors:** H. Alkhateb, H. Almashaqbeh, J. Edmunson, M. Fiske, Y. Najjar, and Damian Stoddard
**Source:** Earth and Space 2021 (ASCE)

## Abstract
The authors are proposing a comprehensive research approach and terminology for providing a framework to utilize an ordinary portland cement (OPC) alternative class of materials for both extraterrestrial habitats and terrestrial 3D construction applications. Magnesia-based binders (MBBs) have shown great promise for use in different additive construction (AC) applications due to superior properties such as high compressive strength, rapid setting time, and excellent durability, in addition to the possibility of harvesting these binders from lunar and Martian regolith. The primary objective is to prove the concept of utilizing magnesia phosphate cement (MPC) composites with different additives, including boric acid, graphene nanoplatelets (GnP), and acetic acid, for planetary habitation. The study utilizes artificial neural networks (ANN) to optimize MPC pastes for real-time 3D printing.

## Introduction
Planetary autonomous construction faces technology maturation issues, such as the scarcity of printable materials and material science challenges in extreme environments. Utilizing in-situ resources (ISRU) for habitat structures on the Moon and Mars reduces cost and payload requirements. Magnesium phosphate cement (MPC) is formed by the reaction between magnesium oxide (MgO) and a soluble phosphate compound (e.g., KDP). Since both the Moon and Mars are rich in magnesium-bearing minerals, MPC is a prime candidate for a construction binder.

## Methodology
The experimental program studied the effect of mix design parameters on physical and mechanical properties:
- **M/P Ratio:** Magnesia to phosphate molar ratio.
- **W/P Ratio:** Water to phosphate molar ratio.
- **B/M Ratio:** Boric acid to magnesia mass ratio.

Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) were employed to predict performance (flow, setting time, and compressive strength) based on mix ingredients.

### Findings on Boric Acid
- Without a retarder, MPC sets in 30 seconds to 15 minutes.
- Adding **5% boric acid** increased initial setting time from ~3.5 minutes to 15 minutes in specific mixes.
- Increasing the B/M ratio beyond 5% can actually reduce the setting time due to fluidity loss mechanisms.

## Conclusions
1. MPC properties can be tailored for 3D printing by manipulating M/P, W/P, and B/M ratios.
2. Boric acid is an effective retarder (up to 5% content), improving workability.
3. The optimal M/P molar ratio is highly dependent on the water content used.
4. ANN models proved effective (R² of 0.84 to 0.93) in predicting MPC performance for real-time printing adjustments.
