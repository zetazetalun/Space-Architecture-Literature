# Optimizing Magnesium Phosphate Binders with Boric Acid for Additive Construction Applications

**Authors:** H. Alkhateb, H. Almashaqbeh, J. Edmunson, M. Fiske, Y. Najjar, and Damian Stoddard  
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE

## Abstract
The authors are proposing a comprehensive research approach and terminology for providing a framework to utilize an ordinary portland cement (OPC) alternative class of materials for both extraterrestrial habitats and terrestrial 3D construction applications, where sand and clay can replace regolith. Magnesia-based binders (MBBs) have shown a great promise for use in different additive construction (AC) applications due to its superior properties such as high compressive strength, rapid setting time, and excellent durability, in addition to the possibility of harvesting these binders from lunar and Martian regolith. Our primary objective in this research is to prove the concept of utilizing magnesia phosphate cement (MPC) composites with different additives, including boric acid, graphene nanoplatelets (GnP), acetic acid, and potentially Martian and lunar regolith simulants as a potential construction material for the planetary habitation exploration missions. The experimental results show that boric acid can change the phase compositions of the MPC paste and affect its performance. It can improve the initial setting time of the paste and alter the structure of its binding products. Finally, our proposed research creatively utilizes artificial neural networks (ANN) to investigate and subsequently optimize both the MPC pastes and composite formulation for real-time adjusting of 3D printing processes.

## Introduction
Planetary autonomous construction faces technology maturation issues, such as the scarcity of printable materials in extreme environments. Utilizing in-situ resources (ISRU) for habitat structures on the Moon and Mars reduces payload requirements. Magnesium phosphate cement (MPC) is formed by reacting magnesium oxide with a soluble phosphate compound. As both Moon and Mars surfaces are rich in magnesium-bearing minerals, MPC is a strong candidate for a construction binder.

## Key Findings and Discussion
1. **Setting Time Retardation:** Without retarders, MPC sets in 30 seconds to 15 minutes. Adding 5% boric acid increased the initial setting time from 3.5 to 15 minutes, which is critical for the workability required in 3D construction printing (3DCP).
2. **Compressive Strength:** For low M/P (Magnesia to Phosphate) ratios, compressive strength decreased as boric acid content increased. However, for high M/P ratios, the effect was marginal, with some instances showing strength improvement (e.g., 6% increase for B6-36 series).
3. **Optimal Ratios:** The optimal M/P ratio is highly dependent on the water-to-solid (W/S) ratio. For instance, at a W/P ratio of 3, an M/P ratio of 6 yielded the highest compressive strength.
4. **ANN Optimization:** The study successfully trained a 5-5-3 Artificial Neural Network to predict flowability, setting time, and compressive strength with high confidence (R² up to 0.93 for training sets).

## Conclusions
MPC properties can be tailored for 3D printing by manipulating M/P, W/P, and B/M (Boric acid to Magnesia) ratios. Boric acid up to 5% improves initial setting time but can reduce workability if exceeded. Molar ratio representation provides a more accurate understanding of MPC behavior than traditional weight ratios.
