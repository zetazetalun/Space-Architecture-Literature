# Optimizing Magnesium Phosphate Binders with Boric Acid for Additive Construction Applications

**Authors:** H. Alkhateb, H. Almashaqbeh, J. Edmunson, M. Fiske, Y. Najjar, and Damian Stoddard  
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE

## ABSTRACT
The authors propose a framework to utilize magnesia-based binders (MBBs) as an Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC) alternative for extraterrestrial habitats. Magnesia phosphate cement (MPC) composites are evaluated for additive construction (AC) due to high compressive strength and rapid setting. This research focuses on utilizing boric acid to retard the setting time, making the material suitable for 3D printing. Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) are employed to optimize mix designs for real-time adjustments in printing processes.

## INTRODUCTION
Planetary autonomous construction faces challenges regarding material availability and environmental constraints. Utilizing in-situ resources (ISRU) for Moon and Mars habitats reduces launch mass. MPC is a candidate binder as Moon and Mars surfaces are rich in magnesium-bearing minerals. While MPC has high early strength, its prompt setting time (seconds) usually precludes it from 3D printing without retardation.

## MATERIALS AND METHODS
### Materials
*   **MgO:** Dead-burned magnesia.
*   **KDP:** Monopotassium phosphate (replacing ammonium-based phosphates to avoid odor).
*   **Retarder:** Boric Acid (H3BO3).

### Characterization
*   **Setting Time:** Vicat apparatus (ASTM C403).
*   **Compressive Strength:** 2-inch cubes tested at 7 days (ASTM C109).
*   **Microstructure:** Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM).

### Parameters
*   **M/P Ratio:** Magnesia to Phosphate molar ratio.
*   **W/P Ratio:** Water to solid mass ratio (noted as W/S or W/P in different sections, primarily molar ratios used for analysis).
*   **B/M Ratio:** Boric acid to Magnesia mass ratio.

## RESULTS AND DISCUSSION

### Influence of Boric Acid on Setting Time
*   Increasing Boric Acid up to 5% significantly increases initial setting time.
*   For the B3-22 group (M/P 3, W/P 2.2), 5% boric acid increased setting time from 3.5 to 15 minutes.
*   Exceeding 5% B/M ratio resulted in a decrease in setting time due to fluidity loss and the formation of amorphous boron-magnesium compounds.

### Compressive Strength
*   **Low M/P ratios:** Compressive strength decreases as Boric Acid increases (e.g., B3-18 series reduced by 64% with 10% boric acid).
*   **High M/P ratios:** The effect of boric acid on strength is marginal; a 5% addition to the B6-36 series slightly improved strength by 6%.
*   Optimal M/P ratios are highly dependent on water content (W/P ratio).

### ANN Modeling
An ANN structure (5-5-3) was developed to predict Flow, Initial Setting Time, and Compressive Strength. The model achieved a Correlation Coefficient (R²) of 0.89 for the total dataset.

## CONCLUSIONS
1.  M/P, W/P, and B/M ratios allow for tailoring MPC for 3D printing.
2.  Boric acid up to 5% improves setting time but reduces workability beyond that threshold.
3.  MPC is more sensitive to water content than OPC.
4.  ANN models can effectively predict material performance for real-time printing optimizations.
