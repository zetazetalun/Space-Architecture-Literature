# Optimizing Magnesium Phosphate Binders with Boric Acid for Additive Construction Applications

**Authors:** H. Alkhateb, H. Almashaqbeh, J. Edmunson, M. Fiske, Y. Najjar, and Damian Stoddard
**Published in:** Earth and Space 2021
**Publisher:** ASCE

## Abstract
The authors are proposing a framework to utilize magnesia phosphate cement (MPC) as an Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC) alternative for both extraterrestrial habitats and terrestrial 3D construction. Magnesia-based binders (MBBs) show promise for additive construction (AC) due to high compressive strength, rapid setting, and durability, plus the potential for harvesting from lunar and Martian regolith. The study focuses on using boric acid as a retarder to adjust setting times for 3D printing and utilizes Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) to optimize mix formulations.

## Introduction
Planetary autonomous construction faces challenges in material scarcity and the need for printable construction materials. Utilizing in-situ resources on the Moon and Mars reduces payload costs. MPC is formed by reacting magnesium oxide (MgO) with a soluble phosphate (e.g., KDP). While beneficial, MPC's extremely rapid setting time (seconds to minutes) limits its use in AC without retarders.

## Methodology
An experimental program was designed to study the effect of boric acid on the performance and microstructure of MPC. Parameters included:
- **M/P Ratio:** Magnesia to phosphate molar ratio.
- **W/P Ratio:** Water to phosphate molar ratio.
- **B/M Ratio:** Boric acid to magnesia mass ratio.

ANN models were developed using 138 data sets to predict flow, initial setting time, and compressive strength.

## Results and Discussion
### Physical Properties
- **Setting Time:** Increasing the B/M ratio up to 5% increases the initial setting time significantly. Beyond 5%, the setting time may decrease due to fluidity loss. For example, in the B3-22 group, 5% boric acid increased setting time from 3.5 to 15 minutes.
- **Fluidity:** MPC is highly sensitive to water content. For an M/P ratio of 3, an 18% increase in W/P ratio can increase fluidity by more than 70%.

### Mechanical Properties
- **Compressive Strength:** Strength is highly dependent on the M/P ratio. For a W/P ratio of 3, the highest strength was achieved at an M/P ratio of 6. Boric acid tends to reduce compressive strength in low M/P ratio mixes (e.g., up to 64% reduction at 10% B/M) but has marginal impact at high M/P ratios.

### ANN Simulation
The ANN1-1 model (5-5-3 structure) predicted MPC properties with high confidence (R² training = 0.93, R² testing = 0.84).

## Conclusions
- MPC fresh and hardened properties can be tailored by manipulating molecular ratios.
- Using up to 5% boric acid improves initial setting time for 3D printing applications.
- Molar ratio representation provides a better understanding of MPC behavior than weight ratios.
- ANN models are effective for real-time optimization of 3D printing formulations.
