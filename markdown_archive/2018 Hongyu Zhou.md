# Thermal and Mechanical Properties of Cementitious Composites for Additive Construction of Energy-Saving Habitats

**Authors:** Hongyu Zhou, Adam L. Brooks, Dominic Hanna, and Babak Salarieh
**Affiliation:** Dept. of Civil and Environmental Engineering, Univ. of Alabama in Huntsville
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2018, ASCE

## Abstract
Lightweight cementitious composites (LWCC) are cement-based materials combined with lightweight fillers (LWFs). Because of its advantages in reducing both structural weight and thermal conductivity, it has the potential to be used in the construction of terrestrial and extraterrestrial habitats. Furthermore, LWCC is suitable for extrusion-based 3-D printing due to its small, spherical aggregates. This research investigates the effects of different lightweight fillers (hollow glass microspheres and fly ash cenospheres) on the mechanical and thermal properties of LWCC.

## Introduction
3-D printable concrete is an alternative method to construct structures that eliminates the need for formwork, reduces material waste, and allows for highly automated construction processes. In extraterrestrial scenarios, these features are critical for reducing human error and optimizing structural/energetic performance.

## Key Findings
1. **Thermal Efficiency:** Thermal conductivity decreases significantly with the increase of LWF volume fraction, reaching as low as 0.62 W/m·K (a ~70% reduction compared to plain mortar).
2. **Mechanical Sensitivity:** While thermal properties are governed by volume fraction, mechanical strength is heavily influenced by the filler's particle size and shell thickness. Smaller, thicker-walled particles (like S60 HGMs) yield higher strength.
3. **3D Printing Suitability:** The spherical shape and smooth surface of these fillers improve the rheological behavior of fresh mortar, making them highly suitable for extrusion-based additive construction.
4. **Dynamic Loading:** LWCC is strain-rate sensitive; dynamic compressive strength increases significantly under high-impact loading scenarios.

## Conclusions
LWCC materials demonstrate a high strength-to-unit weight ratio and excellent thermal insulation properties, making them viable candidates for load-bearing structural components in energy-saving extraterrestrial habitats. Smaller LWF particles ensure better distribution and workability for automated 3D printing systems.
