# Thermal and Mechanical Properties of Cementitious Composites for Additive Construction of Energy-Saving Habitats

**Authors:** Hongyu Zhou, Adam L. Brooks, Dominic Hanna, Babak Salarieh
**Affiliation:** Dept. of Civil and Environmental Engineering, Univ. of Alabama in Huntsville
**Source:** Earth and Space 2018, pp. 600-611

## ABSTRACT
Lightweight cementitious composites (LWCC) are cement-based materials combined with lightweight fillers. Because of its advantages in reducing both structural weight and thermal conductivity, it has the potential to be used in the construction of terrestrial and extraterrestrial habitats. Furthermore, since it has relatively small and spherical shaped aggregates, LWCC is also suitable for extrusion-based 3D printing. This research investigates the effects of different lightweight fillers (hollow glass microspheres and fly ash cenospheres) on the mechanical and thermal properties of LWCC.

## INTRODUCTION
3D printable concrete has been explored as an alternative method to construct concrete structures. It eliminates the use of formwork, reduces labor costs, and allows for highly automated construction which reduces material waste and human error. LWCC brings twofold advantages: reducing structural dead loads and providing lower thermal conductivity than normal concrete, which is critical for energy-saving habitats.

### Mixture Design
The constituent materials include ASTM Type I-II Portland cement, silica sand, water, superplasticizer, and lightweight fillers (LWF). The water-to-cement ratio (w/c) was 0.43. Four LWF volume fractions (vf) were tested: 7%, 14%, 21%, and 28%.

### Key Findings
1. **Thermal Properties:** Thermal conductivity decreases notably as LWF volume fraction increases. At 28% LWF, conductivity reduces to 0.65-0.8 W/m·K (a ~70% reduction from control).
2. **Mechanical Properties:** Compressive strength is highly sensitive to the type of filler. Smaller fillers with thicker shells (like HGM S60) yield higher strength compared to larger, thinner-shelled particles. 
3. **Dynamic Performance:** LWCC exhibits strain-rate sensitivity; dynamic compressive strength increases with the strain rate.

## CONCLUSIONS
LWCC incorporating hollow glass microspheres or fly ash cenospheres is suitable for extrusion-based additive construction due to the spherical aggregates improving rheology. These materials offer a path toward high-strength, lightweight, and thermally insulating structures for extraterrestrial habitats.
