# Brazing of SiC ceramic with extraterrestrial regolith simulant in ISRU and ISRF applications

a School of Civil Engineering, Harbin Institute of Technology, Harbin 150090, China

## Abstract
In-situ resources play an important role in future extraterrestrial human activities for facility repair and habitat construction, especially in sustainable space exploration of the Moon and Mars. This paper verifies the feasibility of lunar and Martian regolith simulant for ceramic joining in which SiC ceramic was made into specific size samples and then brazed with the regolith under argon atmosphere, respectively. The microstructure and interfacial phase of the joints were characterized in detail. The results show that it is possible to braze ceramic parts together with extraterrestrial regolith simulant. The average ultimate shear strength of joints of SiC brazed with lunar regolith simulant (LRS-SiC), and SiC brazed with Martian regolith simulant (MRS-SiC) was 21.5 ± 6.5 MPa and 27.3 ± 13.4 MPa, respectively. Finally, the joining mechanism of the bonded joints was proposed. Intergranular diffusion of elements and new phases formed by reactions were identified as the main mechanism of bonding in the interface of SiC/glass. The presented method may provide insight into equipment maintenance associated with repairing and fabricating in subsequent Moon and Mars missions.

## 1. Introduction
Establishing a permanent and self-sufficient safe lunar base on Moon is foreseen to be one of the next steps in human space exploration. This process would require a diverse range of raw materials for equipment maintenance and infrastructure construction. Launching all resources from Earth is both cost and time-prohibitive. The in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) and in-situ fabrication and repair (ISFR) technologies can radically reduce the mass, cost, and risk of missions by utilizing local resources for the fabrication and repair of architectural structures and equipment components.

## 2. Experimental procedures

### 2.1. Materials preparation

#### 2.1.1. Extraterrestrial regolith simulant
HIT-L-1 lunar regolith simulant (LRS) and HIT-M-1 Martian regolith simulant (MRS) were employed. The regolith powder was ground to a particle size < 10 μm. This was pressed into sheets of approximately 1 mm thickness for use as the brazing filler.

#### 2.1.2. Ceramics
Pressureless sintering SiC ceramics were employed for the demonstration. Ceramic blocks were cut into dimensions of 4 × 4 × 4 mm³ and 4 × 4 × 10 mm³.

### 2.2. Determination of joining temperature and holding times
The wetting behavior of LRS and MRS on SiC was investigated. Full melting of simulants occurred before 1400 °C, which was selected as the joining temperature.

### 2.3. Brazing experiments
A sandwiched brazing couple was formed by placing the regolith filler between the SiC parts. The process was executed in a muffle furnace under an Argon atmosphere, heating to 1400 °C at 10 °C/min with a 60-minute hold time.

## 3. Results and discussion

### 3.1. Shear strength of brazing samples
The average ultimate shear strength for LRS-SiC was 21.5 ± 6.5 MPa and 27.3 ± 13.4 MPa for MRS-SiC. These values are suitable for emergency repairs and joining of space facilities like mirrors or radar shields.

### 3.2. Microstructures of the brazing joints
BSE images showed that the filler layer is relatively dense. The reaction layer contains Ti and Fe elements. XRD analysis determined that the interlayer consisted of TiC and Fe₃Si, confirming chemical reactions during the bonding process.

## 4. Conclusions
This study verifies the feasibility of brazing SiC ceramic with extraterrestrial regolith simulant. The joining mechanism is based on molten glass infiltrating SiC pores and forming new reaction layers (TiC and Fe₃Si). This in-situ joining method could be vital for equipment repair and infrastructure maintenance on Moon and Mars missions.
