# Brazing of SiC ceramic with extraterrestrial regolith simulant in ISRU and ISRF applications

**Authors:** Wei Zheng, Guofu Qiao
**Journal:** Advances in Space Research 70 (2022) 752–761
**Published:** May 14, 2022 (Elsevier)

## Abstract
In-situ resources play an important role in future extraterrestrial human activities for facility repair and habitat construction, especially in sustainable space exploration of the Moon and Mars. This paper verifies the feasibility of lunar and Martian regolith simulant for ceramic joining in which SiC ceramic was made into specific size samples and then brazed with the regolith under argon atmosphere, respectively. The microstructure and interfacial phase of the joints were characterized in detail. The results show that it is possible to braze ceramic parts together with extraterrestrial regolith simulant. The average ultimate shear strength of joints of SiC brazed with lunar regolith simulant (LRS-SiC), and SiC brazed with Martian regolith simulant (MRS-SiC) was 21.5 ± 6.5 MPa and 27.3 ± 13.4 MPa, respectively.

## 1. Introduction
Establishing a permanent and self-sufficient safe lunar base on Moon is foreseen to be one of the next steps in human space exploration. The in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) and in-situ fabrication and repair (ISFR) technologies can radically reduce the mass, cost, and risk of missions. High-temperature bonding of the regolith is a simple process requiring only heat and the material itself. Silicon carbide (SiC) ceramics are vital for aerospace due to high-temperature capability and resistance to oxidation.

## 2. Experimental Procedures

### 2.1. Materials Preparation
- **Regolith Simulants:** HIT-L-1 (Lunar) and HIT-M-1 (Martian). Ground to particle size < 10 µm.
- **Ceramics:** Pressureless sintering SiC ceramics (98.5% purity, 3.12 g/cm³ density).

### 2.2. Brazing Process
- **Temperature:** 1400 °C.
- **Holding Time:** 60 minutes.
- **Atmosphere:** Argon.
- **Heating/Cooling Rate:** 10 °C/min heating; 5 °C/min cooling.

## 3. Results and Discussion

### 3.1. Shear Strength
- **LRS-SiC Joint:** 21.5 ± 6.5 MPa.
- **MRS-SiC Joint:** 27.3 ± 13.4 MPa.

### 3.2. Microstructure and Mechanism
- **Infiltration:** Molten regolith glass infiltrated pores in the SiC substrate.
- **Reaction Layer:** Identified phases include TiC (Titanium Carbide) and Fe3Si (Iron Silicide).
- **Thermal Compatibility:** Differences in Coefficient of Thermal Expansion (CTE) between SiC and regolith glass did not lead to joint failure due to moderate reaction layers.

## 4. Conclusions
- The feasibility of brazing SiC ceramic with extraterrestrial regolith simulant was verified.
- The joining mechanism involves intergranular diffusion of elements and new phases formed by reactions at the SiC/glass interface.
- This method provides a potential solution for equipment repair and habitat assembly for Moon and Mars missions.
