# Brazing of SiC ceramic with extraterrestrial regolith simulant in ISRU and ISRF applications

**Authors:** Wei Zheng, Guofu Qiao  
**Publication:** Advances in Space Research 70 (2022) 752–761

## Abstract
In-situ resources play an important role in future extraterrestrial human activities for facility repair and habitat construction, especially in sustainable space exploration of the Moon and Mars. This paper verifies the feasibility of lunar and Martian regolith simulant for ceramic joining in which SiC ceramic was made into specific size samples and then brazed with the regolith under argon atmosphere, respectively. The results show that it is possible to braze ceramic parts together with extraterrestrial regolith simulant. The average ultimate shear strength of joints was 21.5 ± 6.5 MPa (Lunar) and 27.3 ± 13.4 MPa (Martian).

## 1. Introduction
Establishing a permanent lunar base requires raw materials for equipment maintenance and infrastructure construction. In-situ resource utilization (ISRU) and in-situ fabrication and repair (ISFR) technologies can radically reduce the mass, cost, and risk of missions by utilizing local resources for architectural structures.

## 3. Results and Discussion

### 3.1 Shear Strength
Brazing was conducted at 1400 °C for 60 minutes. 
- **LRS-SiC (Lunar):** 21.5 ± 6.5 MPa
- **MRS-SiC (Martian):** 27.3 ± 13.4 MPa

### 3.2 Joining Mechanism
The bonding mechanism is attributed to intergranular diffusion and the formation of new phases at the interface, specifically TiC and Fe3Si. These reaction layers help bridge the thermal expansion gap between the SiC ceramic and the regolith glass filler.

## 4. Conclusions
This study contributes to verifying the feasibility of brazing SiC ceramic with extraterrestrial regolith simulant. This in-situ joining method could be used for equipment repair and structural assembly associated with ceramic materials for future Moon and Mars missions.
