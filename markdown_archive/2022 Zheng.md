# Brazing of SiC ceramic with extraterrestrial regolith simulant in ISRU and ISRF applications

**Authors:** Wei Zheng, Guofu Qiao
**Journal:** Advances in Space Research
**Year:** 2022

## Abstract
In-situ resources play an important role in future extraterrestrial human activities for facility repair and habitat construction, especially in sustainable space exploration of the Moon and Mars. This paper verifies the feasibility of lunar and Martian regolith simulant for ceramic joining in which SiC ceramic was made into specific size samples and then brazed with the regolith under argon atmosphere, respectively. The results show that it is possible to braze ceramic parts together with extraterrestrial regolith simulant. The average ultimate shear strength of joints of SiC brazed with lunar regolith simulant (LRS-SiC), and SiC brazed with Martian regolith simulant (MRS-SiC) was 21.5 ± 6.5 MPa and 27.3 ± 13.4 MPa, respectively.

## 1. Introduction
Establishing a permanent and self-sufficient safe lunar base is a key goal. In-situ resource utilization (ISRU) and in-situ fabrication and repair (ISFR) technologies can radically reduce mass, cost, and risk by utilizing local resources for architectural structures and equipment components.

## 2. Experimental Procedures
### 2.1 Materials
- **Regolith Simulants:** HIT-L-1 (Lunar) and HIT-M-1 (Martian).
- **Ceramic:** Pressureless sintering SiC ceramics (98.5% purity).

### 2.2 Methods
- **Brazing:** Conducted at 1400 °C for 60 minutes under Argon atmosphere.
- **Preparation:** Regolith was ball-milled to < 10 μm and pressed into 13 mm diameter sheets.

## 3. Results and Discussion
- **Shear Strength:** 
  - LRS-SiC: 21.5 ± 6.5 MPa
  - MRS-SiC: 27.3 ± 13.4 MPa
- **Microstructure:** Interfacial bonding is achieved via intergranular diffusion. New phases formed include TiC and Fe3Si.
- **Thermal Compatibility:** The mismatch in Coefficient of Thermal Expansion (CTE) between the SiC and the regolith glass did not lead to failure, likely due to the reaction layers acting as buffers.

## 4. Conclusions
This study successfully demonstrated the feasibility of using extraterrestrial regolith as a brazing filler for SiC ceramics. The method provides a potential solution for the repair and maintenance of ceramic-based equipment on the Moon and Mars.
