# Brazing of SiC ceramic with extraterrestrial regolith simulant in ISRU and ISRF applications

**Authors:** Wei Zheng, Guofu Qiao
**Journal:** Advances in Space Research 70 (2022) 752–761
**Available online:** 14 May 2022

## Abstract
In-situ resources play an important role in future extraterrestrial human activities for facility repair and habitat construction, especially in sustainable space exploration of the Moon and Mars. This paper verifies the feasibility of lunar and Martian regolith simulant for ceramic joining in which SiC ceramic was made into specific size samples and then brazed with the regolith under argon atmosphere, respectively. The microstructure and interfacial phase of the joints were characterized in detail. The results show that it is possible to braze ceramic parts together with extraterrestrial regolith simulant. The average ultimate shear strength of joints of SiC brazed with lunar regolith simulant (LRS-SiC), and SiC brazed with Martian regolith simulant (MRS-SiC) was 21.5 ± 6.5 MPa and 27.3 ± 13.4 MPa, respectively.

## 1. Introduction
Establishing a permanent lunar base requires raw materials for maintenance and infrastructure. Launching all resources from Earth is cost-prohibitive. In-situ resource utilization (ISRU) and in-situ fabrication and repair (ISFR) technologies can reduce mass, cost, and risk. High-temperature bonding of regolith is a simple process requiring only heat and the material itself.

## 2. Experimental Procedures

### 2.1. Materials
*   **Regolith Simulants:** HIT-L-1 (Lunar) and HIT-M-1 (Martian).
*   **Ceramics:** Pressureless sintering SiC ceramics (98.5% purity, 3.12 g/cm³ density).

### 2.2. Procedures
1.  Regolith was ball-milled to <10 µm.
2.  Powder was pressed into sheets (approx. 1 mm thick).
3.  Sandwiched joint assembly (SiC-Regolith-SiC).
4.  Brazing in a muffle furnace under Argon atmosphere at 1400 °C for 60 min.

## 3. Results and Discussion

### 3.1. Shear Strength
*   **SiC-LRS (Lunar):** 21.5 ± 6.5 MPa
*   **SiC-MRS (Martian):** 27.3 ± 13.4 MPa

### 3.2. Microstructure and Bonding Mechanism
Intergranular diffusion of elements and new phases were identified as the main bonding mechanism. XRD results identified the formation of TiC and Fe3Si at the interface. Molten glass from the regolith infiltrated the pores of the SiC substrate.

### 3.3. Thermal Expansion
CTE (Coefficient of Thermal Expansion) of the regolith glass was measured. LRS glass showed a glass transition (Tg) at 660.2 °C, while MRS glass was at 657.5 °C. The maximum CTE was 9.84 × 10⁻⁶/°C for LRS and 9.47 × 10⁻⁶/°C for MRS.

## 4. Conclusions
This study verifies the feasibility of brazing SiC ceramic with extraterrestrial regolith simulant for ISRU and ISRF applications. The bonding mechanism involves molten glass infiltration and the formation of TiC and Fe3Si reaction layers. This method provides insight for equipment repair and infrastructure fabrication on the Moon and Mars.
