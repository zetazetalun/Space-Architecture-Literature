# Brazing of SiC ceramic with extraterrestrial regolith simulant in ISRU and ISRF applications

**Authors:** Wei Zheng, Guofu Qiao
**Journal:** Advances in Space Research 70 (2022) 752–761
**Published:** Elsevier B.V. on behalf of COSPAR

## Abstract
In-situ resources play an important role in future extraterrestrial human activities for facility repair and habitat construction. This paper verifies the feasibility of lunar and Martian regolith simulant for ceramic joining. SiC ceramic samples were brazed with regolith under an argon atmosphere. The average ultimate shear strength of joints was 21.5 ± 6.5 MPa for Lunar Regolith Simulant (LRS) and 27.3 ± 13.4 MPa for Martian Regolith Simulant (MRS). Intergranular diffusion and the formation of new phases (TiC, Fe3Si) were identified as the main bonding mechanisms.

## 1. Introduction
Establishing a permanent lunar base requires a diverse range of raw materials for equipment maintenance and infrastructure. Utilizing In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) and In-Situ Fabrication and Repair (ISFR) can reduce mass, cost, and risk. While regolith has been studied for geopolymers and sintering, this work focuses on high-temperature bonding (brazing) for ceramic components used in aerospace applications due to their thermal stability and strength.

### 2.3. Brazing Process
- Atmosphere: Argon.
- Heating Rate: 10 °C/min to 1400 °C.
- Holding Time: 60 minutes.
- Cooling Rate: 5 °C/min.

## 3. Results and Discussion

### 3.1. Shear Strength
- **SiC-LRS Joints:** 21.5 ± 6.5 MPa.
- **SiC-MRS Joints:** 27.3 ± 13.4 MPa.

### 3.2. Microstructure and Interfacial Reactions
The molten regolith successfully infiltrated the pores of the SiC substrate. XRD analysis confirmed the formation of TiC and Fe3Si in the reaction layer, contributing to the atomic bonding between the molten glass and the ceramic surface.

### 3.4. Thermal Expansion
The Coefficient of Thermal Expansion (CTE) of the filler materials (glass phases) was measured. While a mismatch exists between SiC and the regolith glass, the reaction layers containing Fe3Si (which has a relatively high CTE) helped manage the stress distributions during cooling.

## 4. Conclusions
This study demonstrates the feasibility of brazing SiC ceramics using extraterrestrial regolith. The resulting joints show sufficient strength for potential use in equipment repair and infrastructure assembly on the Moon and Mars.
