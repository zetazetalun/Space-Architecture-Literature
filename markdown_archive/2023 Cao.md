# Recent advances on ISRU technologies and study of microgravity impact on blood cells for deep space exploration

**Type:** Review  
**Published:** 19 July 2023  
**DOI:** 10.3389/frspt.2023.1146461  

## Abstract
The long-term solution to problems like resource depletion could be space colonization. This work analyzes current technology developments for deep space exploration, focusing on:  
1. Producing tangible goods from regolith using Self-propagating High-temperature Synthesis (SHS) for construction materials.  
2. Producing consumables (oxygen, water, food, fuel) from Martian atmosphere and regolith.  
3. Cultivating *Spirulina platensis* for nourishment.  
4. Using sintered lunar regolith for thermal energy storage and solar harvesting.  
5. Studying the impact of microgravity on human red blood cells.

## 2 Manufacturing Physical Assets in Extraterrestrial Environments
This research team developed an In-Situ Fabrication and Repair (ISFR) process to manufacture structural elements from lunar or Martian regolith. The core of the process is SHS, an exothermic reaction that propagates as a combustion wave through a mixture (e.g., regolith enriched with iron oxide and mixed with aluminum powder).  

### Key Technical Data (SHS):
- **Ignition Temperature:** 1,800–2,000 K.
- **Reaction Atmosphere:** Low-pressure CO2 or vacuum (30 mbar).
- **Product Shape:** Cylindrical pellets or parallelepipeds (e.g., 14 mm x 17 mm x 33 mm).
- **Mechanical Properties:** Compressive strength of 25.8–27.2 MPa, significantly higher than earlier geothermite methods (10–18 MPa).

## 6 Optical Characterization of Lunar Regolith Simulant
Sintering JSC-1A regolith using Spark Plasma Sintering (SPS) was evaluated for solar energy harvesting.  
- **Sintering conditions:** 700°C to 900°C, 15 MPa pressure, 3 minutes duration.
- **Density:** 86% (at 700°C) to 98% (at 900°C) relative density.
- **Solar Absorptance:** 0.88 (700°C) and 0.92 (900°C).
- **Thermal Emittance:** 0.87–0.96.

## 7 Red Blood Cells in Microgravity
Microgravity causes oxidative stress in erythrocytes. Studies showed remarkable changes in RBC membrane stiffness and cytoskeletal architecture under simulated microgravity using Random Positioning Machines (RPM).
