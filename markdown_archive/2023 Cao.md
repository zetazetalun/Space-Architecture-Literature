# Recent advances on ISRU technologies and study of microgravity impact on blood cells for deep space exploration

**Type:** Review  
**Published:** 19 July 2023  
**DOI:** 10.3389/frspt.2023.1146461  

## Abstract
The objective of this work is to analyze current advancements in technology development for deep space exploration and colonization. First, a method for producing tangible goods suited for industrial or civil installations on the Moon, Mars, or asteroids, using in situ available regolith as the main resource, is discussed. A new process based on self-propagating high-temperature synthesis (SHS) reactions was developed for the fabrication of composite ceramics to be used as construction materials. Next, a method that uses the atmosphere and regolith of Mars as raw feedstock to produce oxygen, water, food, fuels and fertilizers is considered, including the cultivation of *Spirulina platensis*. Finally, the potential of sintered lunar regolith for thermal energy storage and solar energy harvesting is evaluated, alongside studies on the impact of microgravity on red blood cells.

## 1. Manufacturing Physical Assets in Extraterrestrial Environments
In-situ Fabrication and Repair (ISFR) technologies aim to meet requirements for manufacturing equipment and materials at the mission location. The Italian research task force developed a process including:
1. **Regolith Extraction:** From Lunar or Martian soil.
2. **Enrichment:** Magnetic separation of specific components like iron oxide.
3. **Mixing:** Combining enriched regolith with aluminum powder (carried from Earth).
4. **SHS Reaction:** Self-propagating high-temperature synthesis produces advanced materials (ceramics, composites) via a combustion wave (1,800–2,000 K).

### Key Technical Data:
- **Compressive Strength:** 25.8–27.2 MPa for regolith-derived SHS samples.
- **Ignition:** Requires low external power; reactions are highly exothermic.
- **Shapes:** The process allows for the creation of structural elements (parallelepipeds, cylinders) for civil/industrial facilities.

## 3. Optical Characterization of Lunar Regolith Simulant
Sintered lunar regolith (JSC-1A) was processed using Spark Plasma Sintering (SPS) to improve its thermal properties for Thermal Energy Storage (TES).
- **Processing Conditions:** 3 minutes at 700°C and 900°C, 20 Pa vacuum, 15 MPa pressure.
- **Solar Absorptance:** Sintered samples (0.88–0.92) exhibited superior solar absorptance compared to original powder form (0.80), making them efficient as sunlight absorbers in architecture with cavity-like receivers.

## 4. Biological Impact of Microgravity
The paper reviews the oxidative stress and morphological changes in red blood cells (RBCs) under microgravity, highlighting the need for advanced bio-regenerative life support to mitigate health risks to astronauts.

## 5. Conclusions
- SHS technology is a viable path for producing high-strength structural elements from regolith.
- ISRU-ECLSS integration can significantly reduce the payload mass for Mars missions.
- Sintered regolith blocks are suitable for solar receivers and thermal energy storage.
