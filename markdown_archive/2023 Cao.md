# Recent advances on ISRU technologies and study of microgravity impact on blood cells for deep space exploration

**Journal:** Frontiers in Space Technologies
**Date:** July 19, 2023
**DOI:** 10.3389/frspt.2023.1146461

## Abstract
The work analyzes current advancements in technology development for deep space exploration and colonization. It discusses methods for producing tangible goods for industrial or civil installations on the Moon, Mars, or asteroids using in situ available regolith. Key technologies include self-propagating high-temperature synthesis (SHS) for composite ceramics and the cultivation of microalgae (Spirulina platensis) for nourishment. Optical characterization of sintered lunar regolith simulants for thermal energy storage is also presented.

## 1. Introduction
In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) and In Situ Fabrication and Repair (ISFR) are essential for colonization. ISRU provides life support consumables, propellants, construction materials, and energy. ISFR meets fabrication and repair requirements at the location of operation.

## 2. Manufacturing Physical Assets in Extraterrestrial Environments
Research since 2011 has focused on using SHS reactions for fabrication. The process involves:
1. Regolith extraction.
2. Enrichment (e.g., magnetic separation of iron oxide).
3. Mixing with aluminum powder (reducing agent).
4. Ignition to trigger SHS reactions (temperatures >1,500&deg;C).

## 3. Findings from SHS Testing
- Compressive strength of SHS samples: 25.8–27.2 MPa, which is significantly higher than geothermite-based products (10–18 MPa).
- Gravity affects reaction dynamics; under terrestrial conditions, bottom ignition leads to higher front velocity due to free convection.

## 5. Optical Characterization of Lunar Regolith Simulant
Sintering JSC-1A regolith via Spark Plasma Sintering (SPS) at 900&deg;C results in relative densities of 98%. Sintered regolith exhibits superior solar absorptance (0.88–0.92) compared to powder form, making it suitable for solar energy harvesting and thermal energy storage in lunar architectures.

## 6. Concluding Remarks
The paper summarizes successful SHS-based structural element production, integrated ISRU/ECLSS designs for Mars missions, and the potential of sintered regolith for lunar energy infrastructure. It also notes the biological impacts of microgravity on human health as a critical factor in long-term mission planning.
