# Recent advances on ISRU technologies and study of microgravity impact on blood cells for deep space exploration

## 1 Introduction
In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) and In Situ Fabrication and Repair (ISFR) technologies are essential components for space exploration and colonization. This paper reviews the results obtained by an Italian task force between 2012 and 2023 regarding the use of high-temperature self-propagating reactions for structural elements, ISRU processes for consumables (food, fuel, oxygen), and the impact of microgravity on biological systems.

## 2 Manufacturing physical assets in extraterrestrial environments
A new process based on Self-propagating High-temperature Synthesis (SHS) was developed for the fabrication of composite ceramics from regolith. 
- **Process Stages:** Regolith extraction, enrichment (e.g., iron oxide via magnetic separation), mixing with aluminum powder (carried from Earth), and spontaneous combustion reaction (above 1,800–2,000 K).
- **Performance:** Obtained samples show compressive strengths between 25.8–27.2 MPa, which is significantly higher than previous geothermite-based methods (10–18 MPa).
- **Testing:** The process was validated under microgravity conditions during parabolic flight campaigns.

## 3 Theoretical considerations on combustion synthesis reactions
Theoretical analysis using dimensionless numbers suggests that gravity-driven flow and convection significantly affect the stability of SHS reactions. Under terrestrial conditions, bottom ignition results in higher combustion velocities than top ignition due to free convection.

## 5 Edible microalgae on Mars
Cultivation of *Spirulina platensis* using Martian resources (regolith leachate and CO2 atmosphere) was explored. Experimental results show that *Spirulina* grows better under simulated Martian conditions (microgravity and pure CO2) than under terrestrial conditions, with a productivity of approximately 11 g L⁻¹ day⁻¹.

## 6 Optical characterization of lunar regolith simulant
Sintered lunar regolith simulant (JSC-1A) was evaluated for solar energy harvesting. Sintering using Spark Plasma Sintering (SPS) transformed pristine powders into dense samples (86%–98% density) with superior solar absorptance, making it suitable for sunlight absorbers in solar receivers.

## 7 Red blood cells behavior under microgravity conditions
Exposure to microgravity induces oxidative stress in erythrocytes and changes in membrane rigidity, contributing to "space anaemia."

## 8 Concluding remarks
The proposed SHS-based technique produces structural elements from regolith, while the integrated chemical-biological plant can sustain a crew of six on Mars by producing essential consumables.
