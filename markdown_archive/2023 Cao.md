# Recent advances on ISRU technologies and study of microgravity impact on blood cells for deep space exploration

**Type:** Review  
**Published:** 19 July 2023  
**DOI:** 10.3389/frspt.2023.1146461

## Abstract
The long-term solution to problems like overcrowding and resource depletion involves space colonization and the exploitation of extraterrestrial natural resources. This work analyzes current advancements in technology development for deep space exploration, focusing on:  
1. Production of structural goods using *in situ* regolith via Self-propagating High-temperature Synthesis (SHS).  
2. ISRU processes for consumables (oxygen, water, food, fuel) on Mars.  
3. Use of sintered regolith for solar energy harvesting and thermal storage.  
4. Impacts of microgravity on human blood cells.

## 1. Introduction
ISRU and ISFR are essential for space exploration, providing life support consumables, propellants, and construction materials. An Italian task force (University of Cagliari, INAF, CNR, CRS4) is developing technologies to manufacture physical assets on Mars, the Moon, and asteroids using local resources.

## 2. Manufacturing Physical Assets in Extraterrestrial Environments
The team developed an ISFR process involving regolith extraction, enrichment (e.g., of iron oxide), mixing with aluminum powder (brought from Earth), and ignition to trigger SHS reactions. These reactions propagate spontaneously at temperatures of 1,800–2,000 K.

### Key Findings on SHS:
- **Material:** Composite ceramics derived from Lunar (JSC) and Martian (JSC and Mojave) simulants.
- **Mechanical Properties:** Compressive strength values range from 25.8–27.2 MPa, exceeding previously reported values (10–18 MPa).
- **Gravity Impact:** Theoretical analysis shows that gravity influences heat and mass transport. Under terrestrial conditions, bottom ignition leads to higher front velocity due to free convection.

## 4. Optical Characterization of Lunar Regolith
Sintered JSC-1A lunar regolith simulant was processed using Spark Plasma Sintering (SPS) at 700°C and 900°C.
- **Density:** 86% relative density at 700°C; 98% at 900°C.
- **Optical Properties:** Sintering significantly increases solar absorptance compared to powder, making it suitable for solar receivers and thermal energy storage (TES).

## 5. Conclusions
- SHS reactions can successfully produce structural elements on the Moon and Mars.
- Integrated ISRU/ECLSS systems can provide oxygen, water, and food (*Spirulina*).
- Sintered regolith offers a viable path for solar energy harvesting.
