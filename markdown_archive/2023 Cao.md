# Recent advances on ISRU technologies and study of microgravity impact on blood cells for deep space exploration

**Type:** Review  
**Published:** 19 July 2023  
**DOI:** 10.3389/frspt.2023.1146461

## Abstract
This work analyzes advancements in technology development for deep space exploration and colonization. Key areas include methods for producing tangible goods for industrial or civil installations on the Moon, Mars, or asteroids using regolith. A process based on self-propagating high-temperature synthesis (SHS) is developed for fabricating composite ceramics. Additionally, the paper covers the production of life support consumables, the cultivation of *Spirulina platensis*, and the optical characterization of sintered lunar regolith for thermal energy storage.

## 1 Introduction
In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) and In Situ Fabrication and Repair (ISFR) are essential for space colonization, providing consumables, propellants, and construction materials. This paper summarizes results obtained from 2012 to 2023 regarding high-temperature self-propagating reactions to produce structural elements, ISRU processes for food and oxygen, and the characterization of regolith for solar absorption.

## 2 Manufacturing physical assets in extraterrestrial environments
A new process based on SHS allows for the manufacture of structural elements using regolith enriched with iron oxide and aluminum powder. The process involves:
1. Regolith extraction.
2. Enrichment (e.g., magnetic separation of iron oxide).
3. Mixing with aluminum powder.
4. Local ignition to trigger self-propagating high-temperature combustion (1,800–2,000 K).

### Mechanical Properties
Structural elements produced via SHS exhibited compressive strength values in the range of 25.8–27.2 MPa, which is significantly higher than the 10–18 MPa reported for geothermite-based processes.

## 3 Theoretical considerations on combustion synthesis reactions
Gravity affects the SHS process through free convection in the molten phase. Under terrestrial conditions, bottom ignition produces higher combustion velocities than top ignition due to preheating by convection. In microgravity, SHS reaction waves travel slower, but produce finer and more uniform microstructures.

## 4 ISRU process to sustain human missions on Mars
The proposed Martian ISRU plant integrates with Environmental Control and Life Support Systems (ECLSS). Using Martian atmosphere (CO2, N2, Ar) and regolith, the system produces oxygen, water, ammonia (fertilizer), and fuels.

## 5 Edible microalgae on Mars
*Spirulina platensis* was cultivated under simulated Martian conditions. Experiments showed that while biomass productivity is lower on Mars (due to reduced gravity and atmosphere), it can meet the protein requirements for a six-member crew using approximately 15 m³ of culture.

## 6 Optical characterization of lunar regolith simulant
Spark Plasma Sintering (SPS) was used to consolidate JSC-1A lunar regolith simulant. Sintering at 900°C resulted in:
- Relative density of 98%.
- Enhanced solar absorptance (0.88–0.92).
- High thermal emittance values (0.91–0.93).
These properties make sintered regolith suitable for solar thermal energy storage and harvesting applications.

## 8 Concluding remarks
The study concludes that SHS is a viable technique for producing structural elements on the Moon and Mars. Integrated ISRU-ECLSS systems can sustain human life by producing food and oxygen, while sintered regolith blocks provide efficient solar energy management solutions.
