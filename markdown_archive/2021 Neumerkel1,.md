# Design of an Autonomously Deployable Mars Habitat

**Authors:** Rudolf Walther Erdem Neumerkel, Miruna Vecerdi, Sandra Häuplik-Meusburger
**Conference:** 50th International Conference on Environmental Systems (ICES-2021-398)
**Date:** 12-15 July 2021

## Abstract
This paper presents a proposal for the design of an autonomously deployable habitat for a long-duration manned mission on Mars. Key drivers in the design were the payload and volume constraints of SpaceX Starship and habitability challenges of the Martian environment. The habitat is transported in compacted form and expands autonomously on the surface using integrated kinetic structures: a protective casing, a vertically sliding core, radially expanding girders, and an inflatable membrane.

## 1. Introduction
The Martian environment requires a functional enclosure addressing low pressure, extreme temperatures, MMOD impacts, and radiation. The design utilizes a deployable architecture to fit within rocket fairing dimensions while expanding to a large habitable volume upon landing.

## 2. Design Method
The habitat integrates four primary elements:
- **A. Casing:** A rigid shell divided into three interlocked parts. It protects the habitat during transport and provides docking interfaces and windows once deployed.
- **B. Telescopic Core:** The primary rigid structure with a hexagonal base. It doubles in height through a sliding frame and contains technical units (sanitary, hydroponics) and a water-tank cupola for lighting and radiation protection.
- **C. Expanding Girders:** Foldable beams that push casing elements radially outward using springs, creating a floor grid and support for internal levels.
- **D. Inflatable Membrane:** A multi-layered structure adapted from TransHab designs. It consists of a Nomex interior, Kevlar restraint layers, and an outer fillable chamber layer for regolith.

## 3. ISRU and Radiation Protection
The design uses ground-up regolith to fill the outer membrane layer to a thickness of up to 100cm. Calculations show that the internal 1 bar pressure easily supports the 0.07 bar dead weight pressure of the regolith layer under Martian gravity.

## 4. Arrival and Deployment Choreography
- **Transport:** Two units can fit within a single SpaceX Starship.
- **Mass:** Total estimated mass is approximately 28 tonnes.
- **Sequence:** Casing elements unlock -> Girders release radially -> Membrane inflates -> Internal core slides vertically.
- **Expandability:** Compacted volume of 180m³ expands to a habitable volume of 921m³ (Expandability factor of ~5).

## 6. Conclusion
The design addresses transportation constraints and habitability through a high expansion ratio and the use of local regolith for shielding. Future work should refine deployment actuators and simulate transport loads in detail.
