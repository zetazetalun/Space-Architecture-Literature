# Building Information Modeling (BIM) Workflows for Construction Sequencing & 4D-Planning of 3D-Printed ISRU Surface Habitats

**Authors:** M. Yashar, P. Michealsen, B. Hammond, J. Alvizar, C. Ciardullo, M. Morris, R. Pailes-Friedman, G. Bell, R. Tucker, T. Shen, S. Austin, L. LeBlanc

**Source:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE (Pages 1394-1408)

## Abstract
Future autonomously constructed surface habitats on the Moon and Mars will leverage additive manufacturing technologies to create airtight and durable structures. BIM workflows provide significant value in the design and engineering of space systems, particularly for habitat facilities requiring pre-integrated hardware (ECLS, airlocks, suitports). This paper defines the landscape of BIM use and simulation for the robotic deployment of space habitats, focusing on the SEArch+/Apis Cor project for NASA’s Phase 3 3D-Printed Habitat Challenge.

## Introduction
Autonomous construction on the Moon and Mars must consider site conditions, raw material quantities for ISRU collection, feedstock storage, and access to power. In the NASA Phase 3 Challenge, the team merged BIM workflows (Rhino/Grasshopper, Autodesk Revit, Navisworks) with a proprietary slicer to estimate construction time and material quantities.

### BIM in AEC
BIM enables a holistic collection of linked building information, allowing for metadata embedding (material properties, quantity takeoff) beyond traditional CAD. Within AEC, it is used for project planning, clash detection, and operational facilities management.

### Challenges for Automated Construction
BIM alone is currently insufficient for direct planning of automated construction. There is a lack of specific software to adequately represent the complexity of buildings for robot coordination. Integration with Robot Operating System (ROS) is proposed as a potential solution.

## NASA’s 3D-Printed Habitat Challenge
The challenge focused on habitat design and technology development for large-scale additive construction from in-situ materials (regolith) and recyclables. SEArch+/Apis Cor’s Mars X-House won first place in the Virtual Construction (BIM) Level 2 (100% Design) phase.

### Requirements and Scoring
- **Space:** At least 93 m² pressure-retaining living space for four astronauts.
- **Components:** Must include suit hatch, view port, equipment hatch, and comms/power penetrations.
- **LOD:** Systems (MEP/ECLS) at LOD 200; structural walls at LOD 400.

## Results and 4D Simulation
The 4D-BIM simulation demonstrated two mobile 3D-printers and mobile platforms coordinated in printing the regolith concrete shell and the high-density polyethylene interior structure. Construction sequencing included the installation of pre-integrated hardware modules (ECLS, mechanical core).

## Conclusion
Autonomous construction requires a dynamic BIM model updated in real-time to act as a 'digital twin'. This framework is critical for human-machine collaboration, ensuring site operations, field labor, and repair activities are synchronized within a unified construction sequence.
