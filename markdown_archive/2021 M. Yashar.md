# Building Information Modeling (BIM) Workflows for Construction Sequencing & 4D-Planning of 3D-Printed ISRU Surface Habitats

## Abstract
Future autonomously constructed surface habitats on the Moon and Mars will leverage additive manufacturing technologies to create airtight and durable structures. This paper defines the landscape of BIM use and 4D-simulation for mission planning of autonomous robotic construction. It describes the contribution of team SEArch+/Apis Cor to NASA’s Phase 3 3D-Printed Habitat Challenge, specifically focusing on BIM workflows for an ISRU surface habitat.

## Introduction
Autonomous deployment on the Moon and Mars requires consideration of site conditions, ISRU raw material quantities, feedstock storage, and access to power. SEArch+/Apis Cor merged BIM workflows between Rhino/Grasshopper, Autodesk Revit, Navisworks, and proprietary slicer software to estimate completion time and material quantities.

### Challenges of BIM-Integrated Frameworks for Automated Construction
*   BIM is traditionally a planning tool, not an integrated framework for robotic coordination.
*   There is a lack of specific software to represent the complexity of automated construction processes.
*   Information transfer between BIM models and numerical control (G-code) for 3D printing needs streamlining.

## 3D vs 4D BIM
4D BIM adds the dimension of time to 3D objects, allowing for simulation of site logistics, including the arrival and removal of temporary equipment. In the context of space construction, 4D BIM helps derive task orders for robotic agents.

## NASA’s Centennial Challenge: Mars X-House
The SEArch+/Apis Cor team developed the 'Mars X-House', which won first place in the Virtual Construction level. 

### Technical Requirements for Habitat Design:
*   **Living Space:** Minimum 93 m² supporting 4 astronauts for one year.
*   **Level of Development (LOD):** 
    *   MEP and ECLSS: LOD 200 (approximate quantities/location).
    *   Structural/Pressure Walls: LOD 400 (complete fabrication and detailing).
*   **Components:** Suit hatches, view ports, rover hatches, and communications-power-instrumentation penetrations.

### Software Workflow
1.  **Rhino/Grasshopper:** Parametric modeling of the regolith structural shell.
2.  **Autodesk Revit:** Native design of the habitat interior and MEP families.
3.  **Autodesk BIM 360:** Cloud-based collaboration and clash detection.
4.  **Autodesk Navisworks:** Creation of the 4D construction sequence by assigning durations to deployment tasks.

## Conclusion
Realizing autonomous construction requires dynamic BIM models acting as 'digital twins' that update in real-time based on robotic sensing and computer vision. The correlation between 3D-printing trajectories and the overall construction schedule is essential for ensuring structural integrity and mission success.
