# Building Information Modeling (BIM) Workflows for Construction Sequencing & 4D-Planning of 3D-Printed ISRU Surface Habitats

**Authors:** M. Yashar, P. Michealsen, B. Hammond, J. Alvizar, C. Ciardullo, M. Morris, R. Pailes-Friedman, G. Bell, R. Tucker, T. Shen, S. Austin, L. LeBlanc
**Conference:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE

## ABSTRACT
Future autonomously constructed surface habitats on the Moon and Mars will leverage additive manufacturing technologies to create airtight and durable structures. BIM workflows present significant value in the design and engineering of space systems, particularly for habitat facilities including pre-integrated hardware (ECLS, airlocks, suitports). This paper defines the landscape of BIM use and simulation for future robotic deployment and describes the project’s contribution to BIM workflows for ISRU surface habitats.

## INTRODUCTION
Future long-duration missions will benefit from robotic precursor missions to construct infrastructure prior to crew arrival. In SEArch+/Apis Cor’s submission to NASA’s Phase 3 Centennial Challenge, the team merged BIM workflows between Rhino/Grasshopper, Autodesk Revit, Navisworks, and a proprietary slicer software to estimate completion time and material quantities.

## KEY CONCEPTS
- **BIM in AEC:** Enables holistic collection of linked building information, material properties, and quantity takeoff.
- **3D vs 4D BIM:** 4D planning includes the simulation and control of construction site logistics and sequencing.
- **BIM for Manufacturing:** Converts 3D geometry into CNC/G-code for robotic additive deposition.
- **LOD (Level of Development):** Mars X-House utilized LOD 200 for MEP/ECLSS and LOD 400 for structural walls.

## NASA CENTENNIAL CHALLENGE: MARS X-HOUSE
The Mars X-House won first place in the Final Virtual Design level. Requirements included:
- Pressure-retaining living space of at least 93 m².
- Support for four astronauts for one year.
- Incorporation of suit hatches, view ports, and ECLS modules.
- 4D construction sequence simulating autonomous activity (machinery, 3D printing, component emplacement).

### Software Workflow
1. **Rhino/Grasshopper:** Parametric modeling of the regolith structural shell.
2. **Autodesk Revit:** Detailed interior design and material assignment.
3. **Autodesk Navisworks:** 4D construction sequencing and scheduling.
4. **BIM 360:** Cloud-based collaboration and clash detection.

## RESULTS & SIMULATION
The simulation demonstrated two mobile 3D printers and platforms coordinated to print the shell (regolith concrete) and interior structure (HDPE). Installation of windows and pre-integrated hardware modules were represented. In-progress components were 'ghosted' to show active manufacturing phases.

## CONCLUSION
Successful deployment of surface habitats requires the close correlation of 3D-printing technology with overall construction schedules. BIM provides the necessary framework for human-robot teams to coordinate site excavation, material acquisition, and assembly within a unified 4D plan.
