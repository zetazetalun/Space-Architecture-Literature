# Building Information Modeling (BIM) Workflows for Construction Sequencing & 4D-Planning of 3D-Printed ISRU Surface Habitats

**Authors:** M. Yashar, P. Michealsen, B. Hammond, J. Alvizar, C. Ciardullo, M. Morris, R. Pailes-Friedman, G. Bell, R. Tucker, T. Shen, S. Austin, L. LeBlanc  
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE  
**Pages:** 1394–1408

## Abstract
Future autonomously constructed surface habitats on the Moon and Mars will leverage additive manufacturing technologies to create airtight and durable structures. This paper defines the landscape of BIM use and simulation for future robotic deployment of space habitats, describing the project’s contribution to BIM workflows for the design of an ISRU surface habitat.

## Introduction
Future missions will benefit from robotic precursor missions that autonomously construct infrastructure prior to crew arrival. Construction sequences must consider site conditions, raw material quantities for ISRU, feedstock storage, and access to power. In SEArch+/Apis Cor’s submissions to NASA’s Phase 3 Centennial Challenge for a 3D Printed Habitat on Mars, the team merged BIM workflows between Rhino/Grasshopper, Autodesk Revit, Navisworks, and proprietary slicer software.

## Key Concepts
- **BIM in AEC:** Enables holistic collection of linked building information, material characterization, and quantity takeoff.
- **3D vs 4D BIM:** 4D BIM adds a time dimension to the 3D model, allowing for simulation of construction site logistics and task sequencing.
- **BIM for Manufacturing:** Converts 3D geometry into CNC codes (G-code) for robotic deposition mechanisms.

## NASA’s Centennial Challenge: Mars X-House
SEArch+ / Apis Cor won first place in the Final Virtual Design (Level 2) of Phase 3. The design, known as Mars X-House, prioritized radiation shielding and natural light.

### Requirements
- Pressure-retaining living space: $\ge 93 \text{ m}^2$.
- Support for 4 astronauts for 1 year.
- 4D construction sequence simulating all autonomous activities.
- Integration of pre-integrated components: ECLS, airlocks, suitports, and MEP.

## Software Workflow
- **Autodesk BIM 360:** Cloud-based platform for collaboration.
- **Rhino/Grasshopper:** Parametric modeling of the regolith structural shell.
- **Autodesk Revit:** Interior design and family assignment for materials.
- **Autodesk Navisworks:** Creation of the 4D construction sequence, calculating durations based on printer trajectory speed and material volume.

## Conclusion
Autonomous construction requires precise synchronization of 3D-printing technologies with the robotic placement of pre-integrated modules (windows, airlocks, ECLSS). Dynamic BIM models, updated in real-time, will be essential for human-machine collaboration and error correction during Mars precursor missions.
