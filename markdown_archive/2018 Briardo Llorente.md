# The Multiplanetary Future of Plant Synthetic Biology

**Authors:** Briardo Llorente, Thomas C. Williams, and Hugh D. Goold  
**Journal:** Genes (MDPI)  
**Published:** 10 July 2018  

## Abstract
Plants could play a critical role in producing oxygen, fixing carbon, and recycling waste/water in long-term interplanetary outposts. They also provide food and biomass feedstock for microbial manufacture of materials and medicines. This article discusses bioengineering endeavors to adapt plants to Martian greenhouses and proposes a roadmap for synthetic biology research for Mars exploration.

## 1. Taking Full Advantage of Plants on Extraterrestrial Human Outposts
Developing robust technologies to enable sustainable long-duration human operations in space is paramount. Plants contribute to sustaining bioregenerative life support systems (BLSS), providing food and precursors for manufacturing materials on the Moon or Mars. 

## 2. Refactoring Plants for Enhanced Performance on Mars
### 2.1 Enhancing Photosynthesis and Photoprotection
*   **Light Intensity:** Mars sunlight is ~43% of Earth's. 
*   **Spectral Coverage:** Targets for engineering include expanding light-harvesting to UV and infrared regions.
*   **UV Flux:** Higher surface flux of UV on Mars requires superior protection mechanisms.
*   **Carbon Fixation:** The high CO2/O2 ratio on Mars may make Rubisco-based carbon fixation highly effective.

### 2.2 Improving Drought and Cold Tolerance
*   **Water Management:** Developing plants that require less water per unit mass.
*   **Cryoprotection:** Engineering cold-hardy plants and synthetic circadian regulation to anticipate Martian night temperature drops.

### 2.3 Engineering High Yield and Functional Food
*   Manipulation of shade avoidance and root system architecture for optimal nutrient acquisition in Martian regolith.

## 3. Tailoring Microorganisms to Complement and Facilitate Plant Life on Mars
### 3.1 Conditioning Martian Soil
*   **Perchlorate Detoxification:** Martian soil contains toxic perchlorates. Biological removal using engineered CO2-utilizing bacteria is proposed.
*   **Soil Moisture:** Bacteria could be engineered to produce extracellular polysaccharides to bind soil particles and mitigate desiccation.

### 3.2 Microbes for Metabolite and Protein Production
*   Plant biomass can serve as a renewable feedstock for heterotrophic microbial fermentation to produce medicines, chemicals, and materials (e.g., spider silk).

## 4. A Roadmap for Research: The Mars Biofoundry
A 'Mars Biofoundry' is proposed: an automated platform for expedited engineering and high-throughput phenotyping of biological systems adapted to Martian conditions. It uses a design-build-test cycle and remote monitoring.

## 5. From Earth to Mars and Back
Advancements in microbial-mediated soil conditioning and crop yield for Mars have direct applications for sustainable agriculture and industry on Earth.
