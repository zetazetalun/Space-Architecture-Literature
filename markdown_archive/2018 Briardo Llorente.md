# The Multiplanetary Future of Plant Synthetic Biology

**Authors:** Briardo Llorente, Thomas C. Williams, and Hugh D. Goold  
**Publication:** *Genes* 2018, 9, 348; doi:10.3390/genes9070348  
**Published:** 10 July 2018

## Abstract
The interest in human space journeys to distant planets and moons has been re-ignited. Plants could play a critical role in producing food, oxygen, and biomass feedstock for the microbial manufacture of materials, chemicals, and medicines in long-term outposts. However, plants evolved for Earth and will not perform optimally in planetary habitats. This article discusses bioengineering endeavors to enable plants to thrive in Martian greenhouses and proposes a roadmap for adapting life to Mars via synthetic biology.

## 1. Taking Full Advantage of Plants on Extraterrestrial Human Outposts
Developing robust technologies to enable sustainable long-duration human operations in space is paramount. Plants contribute to bioregenerative life support systems (BLSS) by:
- Supplying oxygen (O2)
- Fixing carbon dioxide (CO2)
- Recycling waste and water (H2O)
- Providing food and precursors for manufacturing medicines and materials.

## 2. Refactoring Plants for Enhanced Performance on Mars
Mars presents challenges such as reduced sunlight (43% of Earth's), UV radiation, and low temperatures. Potential bioengineering solutions include:
- **Enhancing Photosynthesis:** Expanding light-harvesting spectra into UV or infrared regions.
- **Drought and Cold Tolerance:** Engineering stomatal behavior and cryoprotective responses (e.g., ice-binding proteins).
- **High Yield & Functional Food:** Modifying plant architecture for high-density cultivation and biofortification.
- **Nitrogen Fixation:** Endowing plants with the capacity to directly assimilate atmospheric nitrogen.

## 3. Tailoring Microorganisms to Facilitate Plant Life on Mars
Microbes are essential for conditioning Martian soil (regolith):
- **Soil Detoxification:** Biological removal of perchlorates, which are toxic to humans.
- **Moisture & Nutrients:** Engineering microbes to produce extracellular polysaccharides to bind soil particles and prevent desiccation.
- **Biomanufacturing:** Using plant-derived sugars and biomass as feedstock for microbial production of fuels, materials, and pharmaceuticals.

## 4. A Roadmap: The Mars Biofoundry
Establishing a 'Mars Biofoundry'—an automated platform for expedited engineering and high-throughput phenotyping—is proposed. This platform would use the design-build-test cycle to optimize biological systems for Martian conditions on Earth before deployment.

## 5. Conclusion
Efforts to develop plants for Mars will ultimately benefit Earth by advancing agricultural productivity and sustainability under environmental stress.
