# Mechanical behavior of the metal parts welded with extraterrestrial regolith simulant by the solar concentrator in ISRU & ISRF application

**Authors:** Wei Zheng, Guofu Qiao  
**Journal:** Advances in Space Research 65 (2020) 2303–2314  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.asr.2020.02.008

## Abstract
The use of in-situ resources plays an important role on future extraterrestrial human activities for the facility repair and habitat construction, especially in sustainable space exploration of Moon and Mars. A method of the metal welded with extraterrestrial regolith simulant using solar processing under ambient conditions is presented. Metal parts are made of Q235B ferroalloy and TA2 titanium alloy into standard tensile members... The results show that it is possible to weld metal parts together with extraterrestrial regolith simulant by the solar concentrator. 

## 1. Introduction
In-situ resource utilization (ISRU) and in-situ fabrication and repair (ISFR) technologies can significantly reduce the required launch mass and cost. The surface of the planets is covered with basaltic formed material (lunar and Martian regolith), which is abundant and available. This study proposes using directly solar energy as the heat resource to weld metal parts.

### 2.2. Welding Process
A Fresnel lens (450mm diameter) was used to concentrate solar radiance onto the interface between two metal parts sandwiched with a layer of regolith simulant (~50mg). The heat flux density of the focal spot reached approximately 56.5 MW/m².

## 3. Results and Discussion
The study successfully produced 20 welded members. The tensile tests revealed brittle failure modes for all specimens.

## 4. Conclusions
- It is possible to weld metal parts using extraterrestrial regolith and solar concentrators.
- The highest average strength was achieved by Titanium welded with lunar simulant (4.95 MPa).
- Regolith successfully fused into the base metal, although joints were brittle due to oxygenated compounds formed during ambient processing.
- This method provides a potential solution for repairing and fabricating structures on the Moon and Mars.
