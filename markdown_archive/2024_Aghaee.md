# Additive manufacturing evolution in construction: From individual terrestrial to collective, aerial, and extraterrestrial applications\n\n**Authors:** Kamran Aghaee, Linfei Li, Alireza Roshan, Parsa Namakiaraghi\n\n**Journal:** Journal of Building Engineering 96 (2024) 110389\n\n## Abstract\nThis study reviews the advancement of Additive Manufacturing (AM) in the construction industry, tracing its evolution from individual terrestrial to collective, aerial, and extraterrestrial construction. It elaborates on bio-inspired technology, topology optimization, and materials for terrestrial and space environments. Challenges and limitations associated with AM construction on terrestrial, aerial, and lunar surfaces are elucidated.\n\n## 1. Introduction\nAdopting innovative construction technologies such as AM can expedite construction, enhance safety, and contribute to economic efficiency and environmental sustainability.\n\n## 4.3. Lunar AM\nExploration and potential colonization of celestial bodies like Mars and the Moon have garnered significant interest. The goal is to establish habitats on the Moon or Mars before 2040. In 2022, NASA awarded ICON a $57.2 million contract to develop technology for AM roads, launchpads, and homes on the Moon's surface. AI SpaceFactory designed 'Marsha', a double-layered 3D-printed habitat for Mars. \n\n### Materials and ISRU\nExtraterrestrial AM hinges on leveraging local resources (ISRU), such as lunar soil and Martian dust. Geopolymerization processes create concrete with mechanical performance comparable to conventional concrete (e.g., calcium aluminate concrete). 'Marscrete', composed of sulfur and regolith, is a primary candidate for Martian construction.\n\n### Table 1: Environmental conditions on the Moon & Mars\n| Planet | Temperature (°C) | Oxygen (%) | Relative humidity (%) | Gravity (m/s²) |\n|---|---|---|---|---|\n| Moon | -171-127 | 45 | ~0 | 1.625 |\n| Mars | -143-17 | 0.13 | 80-100 (night); ~0 (day) | 3.721 |\n| Earth | -89.3-56.7 | 21 | 0-100 | 9.807 |\n\n## 9. Summary and outlook\nAM is one of the most promising approaches for construction on other planets due to its automation, adaptability, and resource utilization. Future directions include identifying compatible Magnetorheological Cementitious Binder (MCB) for extended print times and integrating bio-inspired concepts with topology optimization.
