# Additive manufacturing evolution in construction: From individual terrestrial to collective, aerial, and extraterrestrial applications

**Journal:** Journal of Building Engineering 96 (2024) 110389  
**Authors:** Kamran Aghaee, Linfei Li, Alireza Roshan, Parsa Namakiaraghi  
**Available Online:** 10 August 2024

## Abstract
The third industrial revolution is marked by the fast-growing evolution of Additive Manufacturing (AM) in construction. This transformative shift is driving the automation of construction processes to bestow economic, sustainable, and functional habitats on earth and space. AM has enabled innovative manipulation and design of materials, architectures, and structures. This study reviews the advancement of AM in the construction industry, tracing its evolution from individual terrestrial to collective, aerial, and extraterrestrial construction. Challenges related to lunar surfaces and insights for the future of AM are elucidated.

## 4.3. Lunar AM
Recently, the exploration and potential colonization of celestial bodies, such as Mars and the Moon, have garnered significant interest. The construction of structures on these extraterrestrial surfaces presents unique challenges. In 2019, NASA and ESA introduced the concept of 'Advanced 3D Printing Construction Systems for the Moon and Mars'. The goal is to establish habitats on the Moon or Mars before 2040. In 2022, NASA awarded a $57.2 million contract to ICON to develop technology for AM roads, launchpads, and homes on the Moon’s surface.

### Material Considerations for Space
- **Marscrete:** Primarily composed of sulfur and regolith, it can be layered and deposited by 3D printers with minimum human supervision.
- **Simulated Regolith:** Researchers utilize simulants to create geopolymer concrete with mechanical performance comparable to conventional concrete.
- **Phase Change Materials (PCMs):** The 'Mars Ice House' concept utilizes H2O as a resource in AM, creating ice structures due to low surface temperatures.

## Key Space Architecture Concepts Mentioned
- **Marsha:** A cylindrical 3D-printed habitat designed by AI SpaceFactory for Mars (NASA 2018 competition winner), featuring a double-layered shell for insulation and radiation protection.
- **ICON Olympus:** A project aimed at building lunar infrastructure using local regolith.
- **Cable-driven printers:** Proposed for large-scale lunar architectural construction.

## Challenges and Future Outlook
Extraterrestrial AM faces challenges such as high radiation, vacuum conditions (on the Moon), extreme temperature fluctuations, and low gravity. The implementation of autonomous collective robotic construction (CRC) and laser sintering of regolith are seen as the most promising pathways to establishing permanent space infrastructure.
