# Robotic 3D printed lunar bionic architecture based on lunar regolith selective laser sintering technology

## Abstract
The lunar base is not only an experimental station for extraterrestrial space exploration but also a dwelling for humans performing this exploration. Building a lunar base presents numerous obstacles and requires environmental perception, feedback design, and construction methods. An integrated fabrication process that incorporates design, 3D printing workflow, and construction details to build a bionic, reconfigurable and high-performance lunar base prototype is presented in this paper.

## Key Findings
1. **Material Strategy:** Proposed a hybrid powder of lunar regolith simulant and Polyaryletherketone (PAEK) at a 20% resin ratio to achieve optimal sintering and structural properties.
2. **Technological Innovation:** Developed a dual-light field laser printing system (large spot for preheating, small focused spot for sintering) to improve thermal efficiency and bond quality.
3. **Bionic Design:** Utilized mollusk shell (nacre) structures as a design prototype for lightweight, high-strength lunar architecture to resist extreme environmental loads.
4. **Experimental Validation:** Determined optimal sintering temperatures (360–450°C) and successfully produced decimeter-scale complex structural samples.
