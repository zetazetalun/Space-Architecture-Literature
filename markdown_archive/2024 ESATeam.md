# Building a lunar base with 3D printing

**Date:** January 31, 2013  
**Source:** Phys.org / European Space Agency (ESA)

## Overview
Setting up a lunar base could be made much simpler by using a 3D printer to build it from local materials. Industrial partners, including renowned architects Foster + Partners, have joined with ESA to test the feasibility of 3D printing using lunar soil.

## Design Concept
Foster + Partners devised a weight-bearing 'catenary' dome design featuring:
- **Cellular Structured Wall:** Designed to shield against micrometeoroids and space radiation.
- **Internal Inflatable:** A pressurized inflatable structure to shelter astronauts.
- **Bio-inspired Structure:** A hollow closed-cell structure reminiscent of bird bones, providing a high strength-to-weight ratio.

## Technology: D-Shape Printer
The study utilized the D-Shape printer supplied by the UK’s Monolite. 
- **Mechanism:** A mobile printing array of nozzles on a 6m frame sprays a binding solution onto a sand-like building material.
- **Process:** The simulated lunar material is mixed with magnesium oxide to turn it into 'paper.' A structural 'ink' (binding salt) is then applied, converting the material into a stone-like solid.
- **Performance:** Current printing rates are approximately 2m per hour, with next-generation designs targeting 3.5m per hour.

## Testing and Results
- **Demonstration:** A 1.5-tonne building block was produced as a proof-of-concept.
- **Vacuum Operations:** Testing conducted by Alta SpA and Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna confirmed that printing can work in a vacuum by inserting the nozzle 2mm beneath the regolith layer, allowing capillary forces to trap the liquid droplets before they boil.
- **Simulant:** Basaltic rock from a volcano in central Italy was identified as a 99.8% match to lunar soil.

## Future Challenges
- **Dust Control:** Managing hazardous lunar dust.
- **Thermal Factors:** 3D printing currently works best at room temperature, while Moon temperatures fluctuate extremely. The lunar poles are suggested as potential locations due to more moderate temperature ranges.
