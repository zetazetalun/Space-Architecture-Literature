# Building a lunar base with 3D printing

**Date:** January 31, 2013
**Source:** Phys.org / European Space Agency

![Lunar base made with 3D printing](https://phys.org/news/2013-01-lunar-base-3d.html)

Setting up a lunar base could be made much simpler by using a 3D printer to build it from local materials. Industrial partners including renowned architects Foster + Partners have joined with ESA to test the feasibility of 3D printing using lunar soil.

### Project Overview

"Terrestrial 3D printing technology has produced entire structures," said Laurent Pambaguian, heading the project for ESA. "Our industrial team investigated if it could similarly be employed to build a lunar habitat."

Foster + Partners devised a weight-bearing 'catenary' dome design with a cellular structured wall to shield against micrometeoroids and space radiation, incorporating a pressurised inflatable to shelter astronauts. A hollow closed-cell structure — reminiscent of bird bones — provides a good combination of strength and weight.

### Technical Demonstration

The base's design was guided by the properties of 3D-printed lunar soil, with a 1.5 tonne building block produced as a demonstration. The UK’s Monolite supplied the D-Shape printer for the study, featuring a mobile printing array of nozzles on a 6 m frame to spray a binding solution onto sand-like building material.

#### Printing Process
1. **Material Prep:** Simulated lunar material is mixed with magnesium oxide to create 'paper' for printing.
2. **Structural Bonding:** A binding salt is applied as 'ink', converting the material into a stone-like solid.
3. **Vacuum Adaptation:** To prevent liquids from boiling in a vacuum, the printer nozzle is inserted beneath the regolith layer, utilizing capillary forces to trap 2 mm-scale droplets.

### Performance and Materials
- **Printing Speed:** Current rate is 2 m per hour; next-generation designs aim for 3.5 m per hour.
- **Simulant:** Basaltic rock from a volcano in central Italy was found to have a 99.8% resemblance to lunar soil.
- **Environmental Factors:** Printing works best at room temperature; lunar poles offer the most moderate temperature ranges for settlement.
