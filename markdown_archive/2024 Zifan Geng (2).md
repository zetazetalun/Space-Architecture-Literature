# Silicate-lunar regolith composite: A vacuum self-hardened and comprehensively durable extraterrestrial construction material

**Authors:** Zifan Geng, Lizhi Zhang, Zhiwen Wu, Jiale Huang, Xiangyu Wang, Ming Jen Tan, Wei She, Hao Zhou, Guoqing Geng
**Journal:** Construction and Building Materials 449 (2024) 138517
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.conbuildmat.2024.138517

## Abstract
Extraterrestrial exploration, represented by Lunar base construction, has been promoted worldwide. Herein we proposed a novel extraterrestrial construction material composed of Lunar/Martian regolith simulants and only 4 wt% of silicate adhesive. This silicate-regolith composite can be directly self-hardened in vacuum within 4.5 h without any curing to achieve 29 MPa of hardening strength. Meanwhile, it shows comprehensive durability with stable microstructures and >70 % of residual strength against long-term vacuum exposure, huge temperature difference and intense γ-ray and proton radiations.

## 1. Introduction
Lunar construction faces rigorous environments: temperature differences (-200 °C to 120 °C), high vacuum (10⁻¹⁰-10⁻⁹ Pa), and intense radiation. Existing methods like high-temperature sintering (>1000 °C) have low efficiency and high energy demands. Binders like polymers or sulfur face durability risks. This study proposes Silicate-Lunar Regolith Composite (SLRC) using vacuum dehydration as the primary hardening mechanism.

## 2. Materials and Methods
- **Regolith Simulants:** LRS-1 (Apollo-17 target), LRS-2, LRS-3, and MRS (Martian regolith simulant).
- **Binder:** Sodium silicate (SS) solution (30 wt% solution), utilizing only 4 wt% of silicate solids relative to the regolith.
- **Preparation:** Mixing in a hermetic environment, followed by cold-pressing (9.5 MPa) and immediate exposure to vacuum (<10 Pa).
- **Environmental Simulation:** Mimicked lunar diurnal cycles (27 days per cycle) with temperatures from -196 °C to 78 °C.

## 4. Results and Discussion
- **Self-Hardening:** Within 4.5 h of vacuum exposure, SLRC reaches ~29 MPa strength (specifically for SS2.5HT modified groups). 
- **Durability:** After 108 days (4 lunar days) of cycling, samples reserved 71%-81% of flexural strength and 88%-98% of compressive strength.
- **Radiation Resistance:** Maintained 7.38 MPa flexural and 23.30 MPa compressive strength after intense γ-ray and proton attacks.
- **Cryogenic Preparation:** Successfully hardened at temperatures as low as -40 °C, generating 19.54 MPa compressive strength.

## 5. Conclusion
The SLRC system offers a low-energy, low-carried-mass solution for lunar and Martian construction. The vacuum-driven hardening mechanism removes the need for high-temperature curing, making it suitable for polar regions and sunless environments.
