# Structural synthesis of the reconfigurable legged mobile lander for strong geometry-physical interaction with extraterrestrial environment

**Authors:** Youcheng Han, Weizhong Guo, Changjie Zhao, Ziyue Li, Ze Fu, Yinghui Li
**Journal:** Mechanism and Machine Theory 189 (2023) 105446
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mechmachtheory.2023.105446

## Abstract
To design the engineering prototype of reconfigurable legged mobile lander (ReLML) for strong geometry-physical interaction with extraterrestrial environment, this paper proposes a tailored structural synthesis methodology unifying kinematic geometry and statics. The ReLML integrates three operation modes: adjusting (3R) for terrain adaptation, landing (2R1T) for impact survival, and roving (2R1T) for locomotion. The ReLML can serve alien base construction and adjustable launch brackets.

## 1. Introduction
As extraterrestrial exploration develops on the moon and Mars, innovative detection equipment is required. The paper identifies a need for systems that integrate heavy-load landing buffers with mobile capabilities to serve lunar base construction. Previous designs lacked the integration of orientation adjustment for complex terrains.

## 2. Procedure of the structural synthesis
The methodology follows these steps:
1. Define mission-oriented design requirements and functional modes.
2. Extract interaction characteristics (energy, force, motion).
3. Derive output finite motion characteristics for the leg end-effector.
4. Derive instantaneous force characteristics.
5. Implement motion and force synergistic synthesis of metamorphic limbs and joints.
6. Assemble the reconfigurable topology system.

## 3. Operation Modes and Interaction Characteristics

### Table 1: Operation mode and interaction characteristics
| Operation mode | Interaction behavior | Environment | Interaction motion characteristics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Adjusting | Foothold matching | Uneven terrain | aSf,Leg = (1, 1, 1; 0, 0, 0) |
| Landing | Vertical/Horizontal landing | Flat/Uneven terrain | lSf,Leg (various translation/rotation) |
| Roving | Translation/Rotation gait | Flat/Uneven terrain | rSf,Leg (various translation/rotation) |

## 4. Key Components
- **Metamorphic active joint (mRv):** Changes between switchable rotations for adjusting/roving and a rigid connection for landing.
- **Metamorphic passive joint (mPb):** Functions as a translation damper for landing and a rigid connection in other modes.
- **Leg End-effector:** Typically a footpad arranged with a spherical joint at the ankle.

## 5. Conclusions
The paper proposes a universal methodology for designing parallel and reconfigurable mechanisms for strong physical interaction. The resulting ReLML facilitates multiple functional integrations including fold/deployment, landing buffering, roving, and terrain adaptation for lunar base infrastructure development.
