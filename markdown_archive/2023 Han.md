# Structural synthesis of the reconfigurable legged mobile lander for strong geometry-physical interaction with extraterrestrial environment

## Metadata
- **Journal:** Mechanism and Machine Theory 189 (2023) 105446
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mechmachtheory.2023.105446
- **Authors:** Youcheng Han, Weizhong Guo, Changjie Zhao, Ziyue Li, Ze Fu, Yinghui Li
- **Keywords:** Reconfigurable legged mobile lander, Structural synthesis, Extraterrestrial exploration, Kinematic geometry and statics, Finite and instantaneous screws, Robot-physical interaction

## Abstract
To design the engineering prototype of reconfigurable legged mobile lander (ReLML) for strong geometry-physical interaction with extraterrestrial environment, this paper proposes a tailored structural synthesis methodology unifying kinematic geometry and statics. Compared with preceding works, three inbuilt operation modes are first integrated: adjusting mode (3R), landing mode (2R1T), and roving mode (2R1T). The ReLML can serve the alien base construction and adjustable launch bracket. The unified structural synthesis methodology is universal and effective for designing parallel and reconfigurable mechanisms.

## 1. Introduction
The background on developing innovative intelligent detection equipment includes: (1) propulsive braking+wheeled mobile lander concepts (Lockheed Martin, Boeing); (2) legged passive buffering+wheeled mobile lander (China's first manned lunar base idea); (3) legged active buffering+repeated takeoff and landing+legged mobile probes (Gao). The fifth development route integrates both legged passive buffering and legged locomotion to build a manned/robotized lunar base.

## 3. Conclusions
(1) Mission-oriented design demands are guaranteed by considering strong geometry-physical interaction between ReLML and environment.
(2) Legged mobile lander is defined with three modes: 3R adjusting for terrain adaptation, 2R1T landing (inherits Chang'E), and 2R1T roving for motion/force interactivity.
(3) A unified structural synthesis methodology for ReLML integrating kinematic geometry and statics is proposed.
(4) Metamorphosis principle reveals reconfiguration essence of topology and force systems.
(5) ReLML serves multiple functions: alien base construction, launch bracket support, and terrain adaptation.
