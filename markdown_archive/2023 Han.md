# Structural synthesis of the reconfigurable legged mobile lander for strong geometry-physical interaction with extraterrestrial environment

**Journal:** Mechanism and Machine Theory 189 (2023) 105446  
**Authors:** Youcheng Han, Weizhong Guo, Changjie Zhao, Ziyue Li, Ze Fu, Yinghui Li  
**Affiliation:** State Key Laboratory of Mechanical System and Vibration, School of Mechanical Engineering, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, China  
**Published:** 31 July 2023

## Abstract
To design the engineering prototype of reconfigurable legged mobile lander (ReLML) for strong geometry-physical interaction with extraterrestrial environment, this paper proposes a tailored structural synthesis methodology unifying kinematic geometry and statics. Compared with our preceding works, three inbuilt operation modes are first integrated: adjusting mode (3R) for terrain adaptation; landing mode (2R1T) for impact survival; and roving mode (2R1T) for motion/force interactivity. The ReLML can serve the alien base construction and adjustable launch bracket.

## 1. Introduction
Extraterrestrial exploration involves complex terrain and heavy-load landing requirements. Current designs include propulsive braking+wheeled landers (e.g., Boeing/Lockheed Martin concepts) and legged passive buffering+wheeled systems. However, integrated systems that combine landing, walking, and orientation adjustment for base construction are still needed. This work proposes the fifth development route: integrating legged passive buffering and legged locomotion to build robotic or manned lunar bases.

## 2. Methodology
The tailored mission-oriented structural synthesis methodology involves:
1. Defining design requirements and operation modes.
2. Extracting interaction characteristics (energy, force, motion).
3. Deriving output finite motion characteristics for the leg end-effector.
4. Deriving instantaneous force characteristics.
5. Unified structural synthesis of metamorphic execution, transmission, and joint mechanisms.

### Operation Modes
- **Adjusting Mode (3R):** Three rotations to match uneven terrain topography before touchdown.
- **Landing Mode (2R1T):** Two rotations and one translation to absorb high-impact energy using dampers.
- **Roving Mode (2R1T):** Optimized for motion/force interactivity during locomotion.

### Mechanical Generators
The paper identifies four families of metamorphic limb structures based on the required Degrees of Freedom (DoF) and constraint wrenches. These families allow the lander to transition between being a stable landing platform and a mobile exploration vehicle. 

## 4. Conclusions
The proposed ReLML integrates fold/deployment, foothold adjustment, landing buffering, roving walking, and orientation adjustment. This multifunctionality makes it a viable candidate for supporting alien base construction and sample return missions on hostile planetary surfaces.
