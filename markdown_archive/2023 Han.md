# Structural synthesis of the reconfigurable legged mobile lander for strong geometry-physical interaction with extraterrestrial environment

**Authors:** Youcheng Han, Weizhong Guo, Changjie Zhao, Ziyue Li, Ze Fu, Yinghui Li  
**Publication:** Mechanism and Machine Theory 189 (2023) 105446  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mechmachtheory.2023.105446

## Abstract
To design the engineering prototype of reconfigurable legged mobile lander (ReLML) for strong geometry-physical interaction with extraterrestrial environment, this paper proposes a tailored structural synthesis methodology unifying kinematic geometry and statics. Three inbuilt operation modes are integrated: adjusting mode (3R), landing mode (2R1T), and roving mode (2R1T). The ReLML can serve alien base construction and adjustable launch brackets. The methodology is universal for designing parallel and reconfigurable mechanisms in strong physical interaction scenarios.

## 1. Introduction
Extraterrestrial exploration requires innovative intelligent detection equipment. Current designs include propulsive braking+wheeled landers, legged passive buffering+wheeled mobile landers, and legged active buffering probes. However, these systems often lack the orientation adjustment needed for base construction. This paper focuses on integrating legged passive buffering and legged locomotion to build a landing and walking integrated robot.

## 2. Related Works
Previous structural synthesis methods (group and manifold, differential geometry, finite screw method) often focus on motion but lack emphasis on strong geometry-physical interaction. This work utilizes metamorphic theory to handle the transition between adjusting, landing, and roving modes, ensuring realistic mission-oriented design demands.

## 3. Procedure of the structural synthesis methodology
The tailored methodology involves:
- **Step 1:** Defining mission-oriented design requirements.
- **Step 2:** Extracting interaction characteristics (energy, force, motion).
- **Step 3:** Deriving output finite motion characteristics of leg end-effectors.
- **Step 4:** Deriving instantaneous force characteristics.
- **Step 5-7:** Unified topology synthesis of metamorphic execution, joint, and transmission mechanisms.
- **Step 8-9:** Assembly and optimal selection of the reconfigurable system.

## 10. Conclusions
1. Mission-oriented design demands are guaranteed by considering strong geometry-physical interaction.
2. Three exact modes are defined: adjusting (3R), landing (2R1T), and roving (2R1T).
3. A unified structural synthesis methodology for ReLML is proposed based on finite and instantaneous screw theory.
4. The metamorphosis principle is demonstrated to reveal reconfiguration essences of topology and force systems.
5. The ReLML can serve functions including fold/deployment, foothold adjustment, landing buffering, roving walking, and orientation adjustment for alien base construction.
