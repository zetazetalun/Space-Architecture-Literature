# Structural synthesis of the reconfigurable legged mobile lander for strong geometry-physical interaction with extraterrestrial environment

**Journal:** Mechanism and Machine Theory 189 (2023) 105446  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mechmachtheory.2023.105446

## Abstract
To design the engineering prototype of reconfigurable legged mobile lander (ReLML) for strong geometry-physical interaction with extraterrestrial environment, this paper proposes a tailored structural synthesis methodology unifying kinematic geometry and statics. Three inbuilt operation modes are first integrated: **adjusting mode** (3R) for geometrical adaptation to hostile terrain; **landing mode** (2R1T) for strong impact survival; and **roving mode** (2R1T) for motion/force interactivity. The ReLML can serve the alien base construction and adjustable launch bracket. The unified structural synthesis methodology of kinematic geometry and statics is universal and effective for designing parallel and reconfigurable mechanisms.

## 1. Introduction
Extraterrestrial exploration has progressed with landers on the Moon and Mars. However, existing landers have limitations in adaptability to complex terrain and heavy-load transport for human/cargo. Innovative routes include: (1) wheeled mobile landers, (2) passive buffering+wheeled mobile landers, and (3) legged mobile landers. This work focuses on a reconfigurable legged mobile lander (ReLML) to integrate landing, adjusting, and walking for robotic and/or manned lunar bases.

## 10. Conclusions
1. The ReLML is designed for strong geometry-physical interaction with extraterrestrial environments with fewer limbs and joints for reliable reconfigurability.
2. Three modes are integrated: Adjusting (3R), Landing (2R1T), and Roving (2R1T).
3. A unified structural synthesis methodology using finite and instantaneous screw theory was developed to derive motion, transmission, and constraint wrenches.
4. The ReLML is shown to be capable of supporting **alien base construction**, sample launch/return, and terrain adaptation.
