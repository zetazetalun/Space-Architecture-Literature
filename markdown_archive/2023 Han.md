# Structural synthesis of the reconfigurable legged mobile lander for strong geometry-physical interaction with extraterrestrial environment

## Abstract
To design the engineering prototype of reconfigurable legged mobile lander (ReLML) for strong geometry-physical interaction with extraterrestrial environment, this paper proposes a tailored structural synthesis methodology unifying kinematic geometry and statics. The ReLML can serve the alien base construction and adjustable launch bracket.

## 1. Introduction
As many countries successfully launched legged stationary landers to the moon and Mars, the backgrounds on developing innovative intelligent detection equipments are being demonstrated. This work focuses on a legged active buffering lander that integrates both legged passive buffering and legged locomotion to build a robotized lunar base.

## 2. Operation Modes
- **Adjusting Mode:** Three rotations (3R) with a strong geometrical adaptation to hostile terrain.
- **Landing Mode:** Two rotations one translation (2R1T) designed to survive strong impact interactions, inheriting the Chang’E lander topology.
- **Roving Mode:** 2R1T motion for motion/force interactivity and locomotion on the surface.

## 3. Findings and Conclusion
The unified structural synthesis methodology of kinematic geometry and statics is universal and effective for designing parallel mechanisms and reconfigurable mechanisms, especially for strong physical interaction scenarios like planetary base construction.
