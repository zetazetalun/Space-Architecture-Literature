# Unity of terrestrial and extraterrestrial soils in granular configuration

**Authors:** Jun Zhang, Yong Li, Yifei Cui, Zi Wu, Yuan Xue, Jianyi Cheng, Hu Jiang, Yao Li, Jian Guo, Jiayan Nie, Guodong Wang, Ao Luo

**Journal:** Earth and Planetary Science Letters, 654 (2025) 119239
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.epsl.2025.119239

## Abstract
Grain size distribution (GSD) is crucial for understanding soil properties and surface processes. We find that both terrestrial soils and lunar soils are subjected to a unified GSD function, $P(D)= g(\mu)D^{-\mu} \exp(-D/D_c)$, reducing the textural fractions and grade modes to a parameter pair $(\mu, D_c)$, which unifies terrestrial and extraterrestrial soils in granular configuration, beyond the environments and mechanisms of soil genesis. Random simulation reproduces the multiple grade modes observed in soils. The GSD unity also provides a tool for generating “numerical simulants” of lunar soils to fill the gap in material simulants. The study leads to a GSD-paradigm in soil study, which is expected to facilitate the data harmonization on earth and promote the generation of lunar regolith data in favor of the in-situ resource utilization and base construction on moon.

## 1. Introduction
Studies on lunar soil (LS) and other extraterrestrial soils (ES) take place under extreme conditions specific to the moon, e.g., hypervelocity impacts, cosmic rays, and solar winds. Properties of LS are investigated based on GSD, as mechanical properties are more dependent on GSD than on material composition. This is critical for ISRU and base construction.

## 2. Materials and Methods
### 2.1 Variety in textures and grade modes
This study is based on thousands of samples: TS (terrestrial), LS (Apollo, Luna, Chang'E missions), and MS (Mars Curiosity rover). Most LS and MS samples fall into bimodal grade modes due to coarse fractions from fresh ejecta and fine fractions from meteoritic impacts.

### 2.2 Unity in GSD
All soils satisfy the unified GSD function:
$$P(D) = g(\mu)D^{-\mu}\exp(-D/D_c)$$
Where:
- $P(D)$ is exceedance percentage
- $\mu$ and $D_c$ are fitting parameters
- $g(\mu)$ is a coefficient function

## 3. Unified Model of Soil Genesis
Soil genesis is conceptualized as a stochastic process involving:
1. Comminution/fragmentation (cascading fragmentation)
2. Aggregation/agglutination
3. Mixing

## 4. Discussions: Application and Implication
### 4.2 GSD-based numerical simulants for LS
Existing simulants often only achieve similarity in coarse content. The GSD function allows for "numerical simulants" that replicate the full granular configuration, allowing LS properties to be expressed as functions of $(\mu, D_c)$. This facilitates establishing simulant standards.

### 4.4 Prospect of GSD in ISRU
GSD-fields enable the development of 3D maps of regolith, revealing segregation of fine and coarse grains. This is essential for identifying sources for oxygen production and preparing feedstock for extraction processes.

## 5. Conclusion
Soils on Earth and Moon (and Mars) universally satisfy a unified GSD function. This GSD-paradigm allows soils to be classified by a $(\mu, D_c)$ pair. Numerical simulants created through these parameters can establish properties required for base construction.
