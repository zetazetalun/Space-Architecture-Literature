# Experimental Investigations of Water Extraction Process within Permanently Shadowed Regions of the Moon

**T. Gordon Wasilewski**
1Space Research Centre PAS, Space Mechatronics and Robotics Laboratory, Warsaw, PL; Colorado School of Mines, Center for Space Resources, Golden, CO. Email: gwasilewski@cbk.waw.pl

## ABSTRACT
Heat and mass transfers occurring on the surface and within the subsurface of extraterrestrial bodies during natural and future industrial heating processes require knowledge of thermal properties of porous dry and icy materials. A series of experiments have been employed to simulate borehole heating of icy regolith samples under low vacuum (0.1 to 1.5 Torr) and very low temperatures (>93 K), using JSC-1A analog prepared as “mud-pies” with water contents from 0 to 20% wt. Real-time measurements of temperatures in an array of points and vacuum chamber pressures were recorded. To decipher thermal properties data in this inverse heat equation problem, a 3D finite element method modelling has been used to simulate the behaviour of both the samples and the vacuum chamber. The determined thermal conductivities vary with temperature, pressure, ice content, and porosity, while heat capacities vary with temperature, ice content, and porosity. These results should be applicable to research on properties and composition of other rocky and icy extraterrestrial objects or design of water ice prospecting instruments.

## INTRODUCTION
The current paradigm in research of rocky planets, moons and small objects within the Solar System is to “follow the water”. Of all material resources in Space, water has the most promising and possibly fastest commercial utilization potential (Sowers, 2016), (Kornuta, et al., 2019).

Thermal properties of water ice deposits in planetary environments are not well-constraint and known. This paper investigates both thermal conductivities and heat capacities of icy JSC-1A materials of ice contents from 0 to 20% wt based on experimental measurements.

## EXPERIMENTAL METHOD AND SETUP
Experiments used a medium vacuum chamber (0.3375 m³). Regolith analog: JSC-1A. 
- **Mud-pie preparation**: Regolith-water proportions.
- **Sample size**: ~500 g, 2.5 in height, 3.28 in diameter.
- **Cooling**: Freezing to -30 °C, then cryo-cooling in liquid nitrogen.
- **Heating**: Using OMEGA CSH-1011001 cartridge heater (approx. 7 W).

## DATA PROCESSING

## CONCLUSIONS
- The study validated thermal properties of icy deposits based on experimental data.
- Thermal properties of dry materials and their volumetric mixtures with ice are proper only if dynamism of ice phase change and pressure domain are included.
- Low ice content surfaces are easier to heat to specific temperatures; high ice content surfaces are significantly harder due to energy consumption of phase change.
- Successful water extraction is highly non-linear and counter-intuitive due to co-dependent thermal, pressure, and mass evolution.
