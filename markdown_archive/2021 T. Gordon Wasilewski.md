# Experimental Investigations of Water Extraction Process within Permanently Shadowed Regions of the Moon\n\n**Author:** T. Gordon Wasilewski\n**Affiliation:** Space Research Centre PAS; Colorado School of Mines\n**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE\n\n## Abstract\nHeat and mass transfers occurring on the surface and within the subsurface of extraterrestrial bodies require knowledge of thermal properties of porous materials. This study uses experiments simulating borehole heating of icy regolith (JSC-1A) under vacuum (0.1 to 1.5 Torr) and low temperatures (>93 K). A 3D finite element method (FEM) model was used to determine thermal conductivities and heat capacities, which vary with temperature, pressure, ice content, and porosity.\n\n## Experimental Method and Setup\n- **Analog:** JSC-1A regolith.\n- **Water Content:** 0% to 20% wt.\n- **Equipment:** Medium vacuum chamber (0.3375 m³), IEP instrument, cartridge heater (7 W).\n- **Process:** 1. Mud-pie preparation. 2. Freezing to -30°C and cryo-cooling in liquid nitrogen. 3. Heating in simulated PSR conditions.\n\n## Data Processing (Modeling)\nGoverned by the 3D heat equation accounting for phase change. The model distinguishes between icy regolith (Phase 1) and desiccated regolith (Phase 2).\n\n### Thermal Conductivity\nDefined as the sum of radiation, conduction, and gas conduction ($k_{eff} = k_{rad} + k_{cond} + k_{gas}$).\n\n**Table 1: Effective thermal conductivity parameters for dry Lunar regolith in vacuum.**\n| Lunar regolith type | Parameter A [Wm⁻¹K⁻⁴] | Parameter Bc [-] | Reference |\n|---|---|---|---|\n| Apollo soil | 1.3 · 10⁻¹¹ | (9.9 · 10⁻⁴ + 9.2 · 10⁻¹⁰ · φ²)(1 - φ) | Woods-Robinson et al., 2019 |\n| JSC 1-A (1690 kgm⁻³) | 3.31 · 10⁻¹¹ | 6.35 · 10⁻⁴ | Sakatani et al., 2018 |\n| JSC 1-A (1870 kgm⁻³) | 2.39 · 10⁻¹¹ | 9.15 · 10⁻⁴ | Sakatani et al., 2018 |\n\n## Key Findings\n1. **Heating Rates:** Low ice content surfaces are easier to heat to specific temperatures; high ice content surfaces are significantly harder to heat up.\n2. **Permeability:** Highly influences the heating process. Unsuccessful pressure decrease in pores can switch phase change from sublimation to fusion.\n3. **Property Interdependence:** Thermal conductivities are strongly pressure-dependent, especially as gas is added to the system (shifting modes to gas conductance).\n\n## Conclusions\nWater extraction in planetary environments is counter-intuitive and non-linear due to co-dependent thermal, pressure, and mass evolution. Successful modeling requires accounting for the dynamism of ice phase change and the pressure domain.
