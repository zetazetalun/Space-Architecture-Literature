# Preparation and characterization of elevated and cryogenic temperature-resistant regolith-based epoxy resin composites

**Journal:** Construction and Building Materials 387 (2023) 131560  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.conbuildmat.2023.131560  
**Published:** 5 May 2023

## Abstract
The construction of lunar bases requires the integration of multiple manufacturing techniques. Extreme environments on lunar surfaces pose challenges for construction. In-situ utilization of lunar regolith for fabrication has been proven feasible, among which polymer forming possesses promising prospects. Specimens with varying proportions were prepared based on the Taguchi method. The results revealed that maximum 24 h compressive and flexural strength of regolith-based polymer composites was 156.22 MPa and 103.40 MPa, respectively. This study demonstrates the feasibility of preparing polymer utilizing LRS that accommodates immense temperature fluctuation on the Moon.

## 1. Introduction
Lunar bases provide shielding against solar and cosmic radiation, meteorite impacts, and large temperature fluctuations. In-situ resource utilization (ISRU) technology provides a promising solution for lunar base construction due to the high cost of transporting materials from Earth. Polymerization refers to preparing mortar-type, three-dimensional network structures with silicate-based material and appropriate additives. 

## 2. Materials and Methods

## 3. Results and Discussion
- **Compressive Strength:** Maximum reached 156.22 MPa (No. 8).
- **Flexural Strength:** Maximum reached 103.40 MPa (No. 8).
- **Thermal Stability:** The starting decomposition temperature was 348.65°C. Samples remained stable over 160 MPa after ten freeze-thaw cycles (ranging from 180°C to -196°C).

## 4. Conclusion
1. LRS can be used as feedstock for polymer concrete preparation.
2. Optimal ratio for curing agent:resin matrix:LRS was determined to be 0.4:1:4.
3. The material is suitable for rapid repair and reinforcement of lunar launch pads and runways.
