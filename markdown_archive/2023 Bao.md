# Preparation and characterization of elevated and cryogenic temperature-resistant regolith-based epoxy resin composites

**Journal:** Construction and Building Materials 387 (2023) 131560
**Authors:** Chengwei Bao, Yanen Wang, Ray Tahir Mushtaq, Xiaohu Chen, Zhisheng Liu, Xinpei Li, Minyan Liu

## Abstract
The construction of lunar bases requires in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) to reduce costs and risks. This study investigates the feasibility of regolith-based polymer composites using epoxy resins and lunar regolith simulants (LRS). Using the Taguchi method, the researchers optimized the formulation for extreme temperature resistance (-196°C to 180°C). Results showed high mechanical strength and stability through freeze-thaw cycles, suggesting potential for rapid repair and construction of lunar infrastructure like launch pads and runways.

## 1. Introduction
Lunar construction is limited by the prohibitive cost of transporting materials from Earth. ISRU strategies, particularly those using polymers for regolith consolidation, offer low energy consumption and excellent mechanical properties. This research focuses on creating a three-dimensional network polymer using LRS and epoxy binders cured under controlled conditions.

## 2. Materials and Methods
- **Lunar Regolith Simulants (LRS):** LR150 and SC80.
- **Binders:** Hybrid epoxy system (AG-80 and E42) with amine curing agents (D400 and MXDA).
- **Optimization:** Taguchi method (L16) used to evaluate ratios of curing agent (mca), epoxy matrix (mep), LRS (mlrs), and temperature.

## 3. Results and Discussion
- **Mechanical Performance:** Maximum compressive strength reached 156.22 MPa and flexural strength 103.40 MPa.
- **Thermal Stability:** The starting decomposition temperature was approximately 348.65°C.
- **Freeze-Thaw Cycling:** After 10 cycles between -196°C and 180°C, the compressive strength remained stable above 160 MPa.
- **Optimization Factors:** Curing temperature was found to be the most influential factor on mechanical strength.

## 4. Conclusion
1. LRS (LR150 and SC80) are viable feedstocks for polymer concrete.
2. Optimal formulation: mca:mep:mlrs ratio of 0.4:1:4 with a 60°C curing temperature.
3. The composite provides high strength and thermal resistance suitable for lunar infrastructure repair.
