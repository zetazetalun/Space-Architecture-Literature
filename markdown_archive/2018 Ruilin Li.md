# Arching Effect of Planetary Regolith Simulant under Extraterrestrial Gravities

**Authors:** Ruilin Li, Guoqing Zhou, Ph.D., and Matthew R. Hall, Ph.D.
**Publication:** Journal of Aerospace Engineering, 2018, 31(6): 04018097
**DOI:** 10.1061/(ASCE)AS.1943-5525.0000927

## Abstract
Attention has been devoted to the construction of off-earth base construction and mineral mining in recent years. This paper studies the influence of extraterrestrial gravities on the arching effect surrounding deeply buried structures in planetary regolith using trapdoor experiments. Off-earth regoliths are physically simulated using a magnetically sensitive planetary regolith simulant, whereas the gravities are simulated by applying a uniform magnetic body force. The results show that the gravity has a significant influence on both the unloading rate for soil pressure on the trapdoor and the reduction in soil pressure. A theoretical solution for the soil pressure on the trapdoor is proposed which includes the effect of gravity-induced stress level and stress gradient on interparticle friction resistance.

## Methodology

### Planetary Regolith Simulant
The authors developed a magnetically sensitive simulant by bonding Fe₃O₄ magnetic nanoparticles around quartz particles. This simulant mimics the (1) granular morphology, (2) fine-grained particles, and (3) porosity/density-dependent weak interparticle cohesion typical of lunar and Martian regolith.

### Extraterrestrial Gravity Simulation
Extraterrestrial gravities (ranging from 0.3g to 2.9g) were simulated using a magnetic device consisting of solenoids that applied a uniform magnetic body force and a constant axial gradient to the magnetically sensitive simulant.

### Trapdoor Experiments
A cylindrical model box was used with a trapdoor of 85 x 8 mm. Soil pressure was measured via a fiber Bragg grating (FBG) sensing element. Tests were performed by moving the trapdoor down in sequence (0.5, 1.0, 1.5, and 2.0 mm) while monitoring soil pressure changes.

## Key Conclusions
1. **Gravity Impact:** Gravity significantly influences the unloading rate for soil pressure on buried structures. Soil pressure unloads more rapidly at lower gravities during initial movement.
2. **Arching Effect:** Increased gravity weakens the soil arching effect in the simulant, but arching tends to stabilize as gravity increases further. 
3. **Practical Application:** The proposed theoretical model provides a method for estimating downward loading on trapdoor-like underground structures on the Moon, Mars, and asteroids, where gravity is significantly less than 1.0g.
