# Arching Effect of Planetary Regolith Simulant under Extraterrestrial Gravities

**Authors:** Ruilin Li; Guoqing Zhou, Ph.D.; and Matthew R. Hall, Ph.D.

**Journal:** Journal of Aerospace Engineering, ASCE
**DOI:** 10.1061/(ASCE)AS.1943-5525.0000927
**Published:** August 2, 2018

## Abstract
Attention has been devoted to the construction of off-earth base construction and mineral mining in recent years. This paper studies the influence of extraterrestrial gravities on the arching effect surrounding deeply buried structures in planetary regolith using trapdoor experiments. Off-earth regoliths are physically simulated using a magnetically sensitive planetary regolith simulant, whereas the gravities are simulated by applying a uniform magnetic body force. The results show that the gravity has a significant influence on both the unloading rate for soil pressure on the trapdoor and the reduction in soil pressure. Both soil pressure and normalized soil pressure unload rapidly when moving the trapdoor down at a lower gravity. The unloading rate for soil pressure increases when gravity increases. However, that of the normalized soil pressure decreases. With further increases in gravity, the normalized soil pressure appears to reach a stable state. A theoretical solution for the soil pressure on the trapdoor is proposed which includes the effect of gravity-induced stress level and stress gradient on interparticle friction resistance. The predicted soil pressure demonstrates good agreement with the experimental results.

## Introduction
The limited resource reserves, increased environmental pollution, and potential nuclear threat on Earth have all promoted planetary exploration in recent decades. Off-earth base construction and mineral mining will be the primary mission when humans first land on the Moon, Mars, or asteroids. However, significantly different environments and geomaterial properties on these bodies bring challenges to geotechnical engineering. The gravity-related mechanical behavior of off-earth regoliths needs to be studied before landing, constructing, or mining.

Soil pressure plays an important role in both terrestrial and extraterrestrial geotechnical engineering. The arching effect is a universal phenomenon that results in a redistribution of soil pressure on underground structures.

## Trapdoor Experiments

### Planetary Regolith Simulant
The authors developed a magnetically sensitive planetary regolith simulant to physically model off-earth regoliths. Typical characteristics simulated included: (1) granular morphology; (2) fine-grained particles; and (3) porosity/density-dependent weak interparticle cohesion.

### Extraterrestrial Gravities Simulation
Extraterrestrial gravities were simulated by applying a magnetic body force to the magnetically saturated planetary regolith simulant using a magnetic device consisting of a long solenoid and conjugate short solenoids. The simulated extraterrestrial gravitational acceleration $g_m$ is given by:

$$g_m = g + \mu_0\vartheta_s \frac{\partial H}{\partial z}$$

### Experimental Apparatus
A trapdoor experimental apparatus consisted of a cylindrical model box (inner diameter 85.0 mm), a base seat, and a strip trapdoor (85 x 8 mm). A fiber Bragg grating (FBG) sensing element was used to measure soil pressure without electromagnetic interference.

## Conclusions
1. The unloading rate for soil pressure and the reduction in soil pressure are strongly affected by gravity. Both unload rapidly at lower gravity.
2. The unloading rate for soil pressure increased when gravity increased, while the normalized soil pressure rate decreased.
3. Soil pressure and normalized soil pressure acting on the trapdoor increased when gravity increased at a certain trapdoor displacement.
4. A theoretical solution for soil pressure on trapdoors was proposed, accounting for gravity-induced stress level and stress gradients. The model showed good agreement with experimental results below 2.0g.
