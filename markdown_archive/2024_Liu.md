# Micromechanical testing and property upscaling of planetary rocks: A critical review

## Abstract
Knowledge of the mechanical behavior of planetary rocks is indispensable for space explorations. Scarcity of pristine samples makes macroscale rock mechanics experiments (macro-RMEs) difficult. This review discusses microscale RMEs (micro-RMEs) techniques (nanoindentation, AFM) and upscaling methods to extract mechanical parameters. Applications range from sample return to extraterrestrial construction.

## 1. Introduction
Deep space exploration aims to identify potential habitats and assess resources. Understanding mechanical properties is crucial for establishing space research stations and future space mining. These properties influence material selection and processing methods for In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU).

## 2. Methods of mineralogical and microstructural analyses
- **Microscopy**: Visible light, SEM, and TEM to observe surface features, grains, and internal structures.
- **Energy-dispersive spectroscopy (EDS)**: Identifies elemental composition and chemical variations.
- **X-ray microscopy (XCT)**: Non-destructive 3D visualization of internal structures and porosity.
- **X-ray diffraction (XRD)**: Determines crystalline structure and mineral phases.
- **Electron probe X-ray microanalysis (EPMA)**: High-resolution chemical composition analysis.

## 3. Methods of nondestructive microscale mechanical experiments
### 3.1 Nanoindentation
Used to measure hardness and elastic modulus at the microscale. It is beneficial for precious samples as it requires minimal material and is non-destructive.
### 3.2 Atomic force microscopy (AFM)
Achieves atomic or near-atomic resolution. It measures topography, adhesion, and local mechanical properties like elastic modulus.

## 4. Upscaling of microscale mechanical properties
Bridging the gap between micro-measurements and the strength of the intact planetary body is critical. Methods include:
- **Mori-Tanaka (M-T) scheme**: Considers interaction among inclusions and matrix.
- **Self-consistent scheme (SCS)**: Estimates macroscale moduli of two-phase composites.
- **Accurate grain-based modeling (AGBM)**: Numerical simulation incorporating detailed microstructural and microscale mechanical data.

## 5. Prospective applications
- **Sample return missions**: Designing coring and sampling equipment.
- **Planetary defense**: Predicting asteroid response to impact events.
- **Extraterrestrial construction**: ISRU is the most promising technique for sustainability. Regolith-based construction requires assessing mechanical performance for reliable structures in extreme environments. 3D printing and additive manufacturing are mentioned as promising methods for habitat components.

## 6. Conclusions
Microscale testing (nanoindentation and AFM) coupled with upscaling methods like AGBM provides a scientific basis for space exploration, enabling the prediction of macroscale properties from minimal sample volumes.
