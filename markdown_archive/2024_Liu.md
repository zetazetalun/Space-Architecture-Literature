# Micromechanical testing and property upscaling of planetary rocks: A critical review\n\n## 1. Introduction\nUnderstanding the mechanical properties of planetary rocks is crucial for deep space exploration, including tasks like sampling, establishing research stations, and future space mining. These properties influence in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) strategies, material selection, and processing methods.\n\n## 2. Methods of Mineralogical and Microstructural Analyses\nMicrostructure and mineralogy profoundly influence rock behavior. Techniques discussed include:\n- **Microscopy:** SEM (Scanning Electron Microscopy) and TEM (Transmission Electron Microscopy) for high-resolution imaging of surface topography and internal structures.\n- **EDS (Energy-dispersive spectroscopy):** Elemental composition mapping.\n- **XCT (X-ray computed tomography):** Non-destructive 3D visualization of internal structures, useful for porosity and density assessment.\n- **XRD (X-ray diffraction):** Determining crystalline structure and mineral abundance.\n- **EPMA (Electron probe X-ray microanalysis):** High-precision chemical composition analysis.\n\n## 3. Nondestructive Microscale Mechanical Experiments\n### 3.1. Nanoindentation\nMeasures hardness (H) and elastic modulus (E) at the micro/sub-micro scale. It is particularly beneficial for scarce planetary samples as it allows multiple measurements on small fragments without destroying them.\n### 3.2. Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM)\nProvides nanoscale resolution for topography and mechanical properties. Modes include contact, non-contact, and tapping. PeakForce QNM allows for quantitative nanomechanical mapping.\n\n## 4. Upscaling of Microscale Mechanical Properties\nTo relate micro-scale data to the strength of an intact planetary body, various schemes are used:\n- **Mori-Tanaka (M-T) scheme:** Considers interaction among inclusions in a matrix.\n- **Self-consistent scheme (SCS):** Accounts for stress and strain inhomogeneity in multi-phase composites.\n- **Voigt-Reuss-Hill average:** Simple arithmetic/harmonic average of individual constituent moduli.\n- **Accurate Grain-Based Modeling (AGBM):** Incorporates real microstructure from TIMA analysis into finite element models to predict macroscale behavior.\n\n## 5. Prospective Applications\n- **Sample Return Missions:** Guiding the design of drilling and coring equipment.\n- **Planetary Defense:** Predicting the response of asteroids to kinetic impacts or deflection strategies.\n- **Extraterrestrial Construction:** Assessing regolith-based construction materials and ensuring structural reliability in extreme environments. 3D printing and laser sintering are highlighted as promising ISRU techniques.\n\n## 6. Conclusions\nMicroscale testing is essential due to sample scarcity. Combined with advanced upscaling models like AGBM, researchers can generate accurate mechanical data for planetary exploration and construction planning.
