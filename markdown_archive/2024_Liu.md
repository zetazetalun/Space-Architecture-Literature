# Micromechanical testing and property upscaling of planetary rocks: A critical review

**Authors:** Yiwei Liu, Guoping Zhang, Jiangmei Qiao, Xuhai Tang
**Journal:** International Journal of Mining Science and Technology (2024)
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijmst.2024.08.002

## Abstract
Knowledge of the mechanical behavior of planetary rocks is indispensable for space explorations. The scarcity of pristine samples and the irregular shapes of planetary meteorites make it difficult to obtain representative samples for conventional macroscale rock mechanics experiments (macro-RMEs). This critical review discusses recent advances in microscale RMEs (micro-RMEs) techniques and the upscaling methods for extracting mechanical parameters. Methods of mineralogical and microstructural analyses, along with non-destructive mechanical techniques, have provided new opportunities for studying planetary rocks with unprecedented precision and capabilities.

## 1. Introduction
Understanding the mechanical properties of planetary rocks is crucial for tasks such as planetary sampling, establishing space research stations, planetary defense, and future space mining. These properties guide in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) technologies, influencing material selection, processing methods, and overall resource utilization strategy.

## 2. Methods of Mineralogical and Microstructural Analyses
Understanding the internal mineral constituents, crystalline structures, and morphological features is essential. Microstructure reveals physical and chemical processes like high-temperature melting and cooling crystallization.
- **Microscopy:** SEM (Scanning Electron Microscopy) and TEM (Transmission Electron Microscopy) are used to scan specimen surfaces and internal structures at the atomic level.
- **Energy-dispersive spectroscopy (EDS):** Identifies elemental composition and classifies minerals.
- **X-ray microscopy (XCT):** Visualizes internal structure non-destructively; useful for investigate properties of meteorites, lunar regolith, and materials for 3D printing construction.
- **X-ray diffraction (XRD):** Determines crystalline structure and chemical composition.

## 3. Microscale Mechanical Experiments
- **Nanoindentation:** Measures hardness and elastic modulus by applying controlled loads to small samples. It is particularly beneficial for precious lunar or Martian samples as it is non-destructive.
- **Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM):** Offers nanoscale resolution for surface topography and mechanical properties, including adhesion forces and Young's modulus of interfaces between different minerals.

## 4. Upscaling of Microscale Mechanical Properties
Bridging the gap between microscale measurements and the strength of the parent body (asteroids or planetary crust) is critical.
- **Homogenization Schemes:** Includes Mori-Tanaka (M-T), self-consistent (SCS), and Voigt-Reuss-Hill methods.
- **Grain-Based Modeling (GBM):** Numerical simulations like Accurate Grain-Based Modeling (AGBM) incorporate microstructural data to predict macroscale behavior.

## 5. Prospective Applications
- **Sample Return Missions:** Guides design of drilling and sampling equipment.
- **Planetary Defense:** Informs kinetic impactor or deflection strategies.
- **Extraterrestrial Construction:** Essential for evaluating regolith-based construction materials (e.g., laser sintering, cold sintering, geopolymerization, and 3D printing).

## 6. Conclusions
Micro-RMEs combined with upscaling methods provide a scientific basis for extraterrestrial construction and space exploration. Future work should focus on building comprehensive databases of meteorite mechanical parameters.
