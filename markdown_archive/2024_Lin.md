# Construction Technology of the Curved ‘Mars House’ Digital Hotel Project in China Using Concrete 3D Printing

## Abstract
3D printing technology is one of the most likely construction technologies to be used when building extraterrestrial bases in the future. The Mars 1 Base project in Gansu Province, China simulates the Martian landscape and uses concrete 3D printing technology to construct houses... The 3D-printed hotel is egg-shaped, with a total height of 9 m and a floor area of 46 square meters... This article introduces the structural design, 3D printing process and overall installation and construction of the Mars house.

## 1 Introduction
Significant progress has been made in Mars research and habitat development efforts to improve the feasibility of Mars exploration missions. Building Martian habitats presents numerous challenges, such as extreme environments, limited resources, and the incompatibility of traditional building materials and techniques.

## 2 Architectural Design and Structural Design of Mars House
The egg-shaped structure is designed to withstand harsh Martian conditions (strong winds, radiation, high-temperature differences). 
- **Height:** 9 m
- **Floor Area:** 46 m²
- **Structure:** 3D printed wall as permanent outer shell/mould; reinforced concrete core.
- **Reinforcement:** Steel mesh arranged inside the printing mould; vertical ϕ12 mm reinforcement inserted post-printing.
- **Internal Pouring:** C40 self-compacting concrete.

## 3 3D Printing Construction Technology and Process

### 3.1 Process Overview
1. Model refinement design.
2. Printing at component factory.
3. Embedding lifting points.
4. Hoisting and transport to site (1800 km distance).
5. On-site assembly and decoration.

### 3.2 Equipment and Parameters
- **Equipment:** 2 Industrial-grade concrete 3D printers.
- **Printer Dimensions:** 14.8 m × 8.6 m × 5.5 m.
- **Print Height (Layer):** 15 mm.
- **Print Speed:** 120 mm/s.
- **Extrusion Width:** 40 mm.
- **Material Volume:** 10.6 m³ (printed) + 17.76 m³ (filling) = 28.36 m³ total per house.

### 3.3 Concrete Mix Proportion and Performance

| Raw materials | OPC42.5 cement/% | Artificial sand/% | Admixture/% | Water/% |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Mix proportion | 38 | 42 | 4 | 16 |

**Performance Indicators:**
- **Workability:** 200 mm
- **Setting Time:** 41 min
- **Compressive Strength (Standard Moulding):** 47.9 MPa
- **Compressive Strength (Printed Sample):** 40.3 MPa (16% reduction)
- **Interlayer Tensile Strength:** 3.2 MPa
- **Permeability Grade:** P12

### 3.4 Prefabrication of Components
Components are printed as 4 annular rings. Printing 4 rings (one house) takes 6 days across 2 printers.

## 4 On-Site Assembly and Construction
Ring components are transported and assembled via crane. Installation of the four ring-shaped structures takes one day per house.

## 5 Summary
The project successfully demonstrates that concrete 3D printing can greatly improve construction speed and shorten periods for complex structures. It is currently the largest egg-shaped 3D printed structure globally.
