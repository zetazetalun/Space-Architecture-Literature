# Construction Technology of the Curved 'Mars House' Digital Hotel Project in China Using Concrete 3D Printing

**Authors:** Xiqiang Lin, Hailong Wang, Xiaoyan Sun, Wael Kikhia, Zhennan Wu, Lu Lan, and Jiawei Zhao

## Abstract
3D printing technology is one of the most likely construction technologies to be used when building extraterrestrial bases in the future. The Mars 1 Base project in Gansu Province, China simulates the Martian landscape and uses concrete 3D printing technology to construct houses, creating a unique cultural tourism industry project. In June 2022, the 3D printing construction of five Martian hotel buildings began. The 3D-printed hotel is egg-shaped, with a total height of 9 m and a floor area of 46 square meters, divided into two floors with triangular windows and a skylight on the top. After the concrete components are 3D printed in the factory, they are transported to the project site for assembly and construction. This article introduces the structural design, 3D printing process and overall installation and construction of the Mars house. The five 3D-printed Martian houses and hotels were built in the Gobi Desert and took three months to complete. Based on digital printing and concrete 3D printing construction, the entire printing, installation, and construction process was safe, fast, and accurate, with a complete structural design and installation process. This project is currently the world’s largest egg-shaped 3D printed structure, and its construction process provides experience for more 3D printing designs and is of positive significance for exploring the addition of intelligent building technology.

## 1 Introduction
Mars research has sparked significant interest in Mars colonization and habitat development. These efforts aim to improve the feasibility of Mars exploration missions and enable long-term habitation. Concrete 3D printing presents a promising approach for constructing Martian habitats due to challenges like extreme environments and limited resources, driving the need for innovative building methods and in-situ resource utilization strategies.

## 2 Architectural Design and Structural Design of Mars House
The egg-shaped structure was chosen for its ease of 3D printing and ability to withstand Martian conditions (strong winds, radiation, temperature differences). The house is 9 m high with a floor area of 46 m², featuring two floors, triangular windows, and a skylight.

### Structural Scheme
*   **Outer Shell:** Concrete 3D printed wall used as a permanent mould.
*   **Reinforcement:** Steel mesh arranged inside the printing mould; horizontal reinforcement set every 25 cm; vertical reinforcement (φ12 mm) inserted after printing.
*   **Core:** C40 self-compacting concrete poured inside the shell to form a composite reinforced concrete structure.
*   **Segmentation:** The structure was divided into four rings for printing.

## 3 3D Printing Construction Technology and Process

### 3.1 Printing Process
The workflow involves: Model refinement → Preparation → Factory printing → Pouring and embedding lifting points → Transportation to site → On-site assembly → Decoration.

### 3.2 Personnel, Materials, and Machines
*   **Printers:** 2 industrial-grade concrete 3D printers.
*   **Print Dimensions:** 14.8 m × 8.6 m × 5.5 m.
*   **Parameters:** Layer height: 15 mm; speed: 120 mm/s; extrusion width: 40 mm.
*   **Material Volumes:** 10.6 m³ of printing material; 17.76 m³ of filling concrete per house.
*   **Steel:** ~5 tons of ribbed steel bars (10 mm and 12 mm).

### 3.3 Concrete Mix Proportion and Performance

**Table 1. The concrete mix ratio for 3D printing of the Mars house**
| Raw materials | OPC42.5 cement/% | Artificial sand/% | Admixture/% | Water/% |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Mix proportion | 38 | 42 | 4 | 16 |

**Performance Data:**
*   Workability: 200 mm
*   Setting time: 41 min
*   28-day Compressive Strength (moulded): 47.9 MPa
*   28-day Compressive Strength (printed): 40.3 MPa (16% reduction)
*   Interlayer Tensile Strength: 3.2 MPa
*   Permeability Grade: P12

### 3.4 Prefabrication of Components
Components were printed in a factory to ensure quality control. Each printer could print two ring-shaped structures simultaneously. Total printing for 4 rings of an egg-shaped house took 6 days.

## 4 Mars House On-Site Assembly and Construction
Printed components were transported via truck to the Gobi Desert site. A large crane assembled the components into four rings. Installation of the four ring-shaped structures took one day. Post-assembly, structural columns were formed by welding reinforcement at reserved positions and pouring mortar.

## 5 Summary
The successful application of concrete 3D printing in the Mars-1 Base project demonstrates improved construction speed and efficiency for complex geometries. As the world's largest egg-shaped 3D printed structure, it provides technical experience for future additive intelligent construction in simulated extraterrestrial environments.
