# Construction Technology of the Curved ‘Mars House’ Digital Hotel Project in China Using Concrete 3D Printing

**Authors:** Xiqiang Lin, Hailong Wang, Xiaoyan Sun, Wael Kikhia, Zhennan Wu, Lu Lan, and Jiawei Zhao  
**Publication:** 3DcP 2023, STAM, pp. 241–250, 2024.  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-031-64269-2_30

## Abstract
3D printing technology is a critical candidate for building extraterrestrial bases. This paper introduces the 'Mars House' project in Gansu Province, China, which simulates a Martian landscape. The project involved constructing five egg-shaped buildings using concrete 3D printing. Each house stands 9 m high with a floor area of 46 m², featuring two floors, triangular windows, and a skylight. Components were 3D printed in a factory and assembled on-site in the Gobi Desert. The project demonstrates a safe, fast, and accurate construction process for large-scale curved 3D printed structures.

## 1. Introduction
Research into Martian geology and climate has progressed significantly, sparking interest in long-term human habitation. Construction on Mars presents challenges such as extreme environments and resource limitations, necessitating innovative methods like in-situ resource utilization (ISRU). Various global initiatives (NASA's 3D-Printed Habitat Challenge, ESA's Moon Village) are exploring 3D printing for lunar and Martian habitats. The 'Mars-1 Base' in Jinchang, China, specifically utilizes concrete 3D printing to reduce costs and time while testing architectural designs suitable for Mars.

## 2. Architectural and Structural Design
The egg-shaped form was chosen for its ability to withstand Martian conditions (strong winds, radiation, high-temperature differences) and its ease of fabrication via 3D printing. 
- **Dimensions:** 9 m height, 46 m² floor area.
- **Layout:** Two floors, triangular windows, top skylight.
- **Structure:** Concrete 3D printed outer shell (permanent mould) with an inner core of C40 self-compacting concrete and steel reinforcement.
- **Reinforcement:** 
  - Horizontal steel mesh (every 25 cm).
  - Vertical ϕ12 mm reinforcement inserted into the shell.
  - Structural columns integrated into the ring-shaped modules.

## 3. 3D Printing Construction Technology
### 3.1 Printing Process
The process includes model refinement, factory-based printing of components, embedding lifting points, transportation (1800 km to the site), and on-site assembly.

### 3.2 Equipment and Parameters
- **Printers:** 2 industrial-grade concrete 3D printers.
- **Dimensions:** 14.8 m × 8.6 m × 5.5 m.
- **Layer Height:** 15 mm.
- **Print Speed:** 120 mm/s.
- **Extrusion Width:** 40 mm.
- **Material Volume:** 10.6 m³ of 3D printing material and 17.76 m³ of filling concrete per house.

### 3.3 Concrete Mix and Performance
| Raw Materials | Mix Proportion (%) |
| :--- | :--- |
| OPC42.5 Cement | 38 |
| Artificial Sand | 42 |
| Admixture | 4 |
| Water | 16 |

**Performance Data:**
- **Workability:** 200 mm.
- **Setting Time:** 41 min.
- **Compressive Strength (Cast):** 47.9 MPa.
- **Compressive Strength (Printed):** 40.3 MPa (16% reduction).
- **Interlayer Tensile Strength:** 3.2 MPa.
- **Permeability Grade:** P12.
- **Shrinkage (56 days):** 258 × 10⁻⁶.

### 3.4 Prefabrication and Assembly
Components were printed as annular rings. It took 6 days to print 4 ring-shaped structures for one house using two printers. A 3D printing interface agent was used to ensure bonding between cold joints.

## 4. On-Site Assembly
The printed wall components were transported by truck and assembled using a crane. The assembly of the four main ring-shaped structures took one day. After assembly, vertical reinforcement was welded, and mortar was poured into structural columns to ensure stability.

## 5. Summary
The project successfully demonstrated that factory-prefabricated 3D printed components can significantly shorten construction periods and improve quality control for complex, irregular geometries. It represents one of the largest egg-shaped 3D-printed structures and provides technical experience for future additive intelligent construction in extreme environments.
