# Construction Technology of the Curved ‘Mars House’ Digital Hotel Project in China Using Concrete 3D Printing\n\n**Authors:** Xiqiang Lin, Hailong Wang, Xiaoyan Sun, Wael Kikhia, Zhennan Wu, Lu Lan, and Jiawei Zhao\n\n**Affiliations:** College of Architectural and Civil Engineering, Zhejiang University; Hangzhou Guanli Intelligent Technology Co., Ltd.\n\n## Abstract\n3D printing technology is one of the most likely construction technologies to be used when building extraterrestrial bases in the future. The Mars 1 Base project in Gansu Province, China simulates the Martian landscape and uses concrete 3D printing technology to construct houses, creating a unique cultural tourism industry project. In June 2022, the 3D printing construction of five Martian hotel buildings began. The 3D-printed hotel is egg-shaped, with a total height of 9 m and a floor area of 46 square meters, divided into two floors. After components are 3D printed in the factory, they are transported to the project site for assembly. The project is currently the world’s largest egg-shaped 3D printed structure.\n\n## 1 Introduction\nMars research and habitat development efforts aim to improve the feasibility of long-term human inhabitation. Concrete 3D printing technology presents a promising approach to constructing Martian habitats, addressing challenges such as extreme environments and limited resources through innovative building methods and in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) strategies.\n\n## 2 Architectural and Structural Design of Mars House\nThe egg-shaped design was chosen to withstand Martian conditions like strong winds, radiation, and high-temperature differences. The structure stands 9 m high with a 46 sq. meter floor area. The wall utilizes a concrete 3D printed shell as a permanent mould with steel mesh and C40 self-compacting concrete poured inside to form a reinforced structure. It is printed in four rings with integrated structural columns.\n\n## 3 3D Printing Construction Technology and Process\n### 3.1 Process Overview\nThe process includes model refinement, printing in a component factory, embedding lifting points, hoisting, transportation (1800 km), and on-site assembly.\n\n### 3.2 Equipment and Materials\n- **Printers:** 2 industrial-grade concrete 3D printers.\n- **Dimensions:** 14.8 m × 8.6 m × 5.5 m.\n- **Parameters:** Layer height: 15 mm; speed: 120 mm/s; extrusion width: 40 mm.\n- **Quantities:** 10.6 m³ of 3D printing material and 17.76 m³ of internal filling concrete per house.\n\n### 3.3 Concrete Mix and Performance\n| Raw materials | OPC42.5 cement/% | Artificial sand/% | Admixture/% | Water/% |\n| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |\n| Mix proportion | 38 | 42 | 4 | 16 |\n\n**Performance Indicators:**\n- **Workability:** 200 mm\n- **Setting time:** 41 min\n- **Compressive strength (printed):** 40.3 MPa (after 28 days)\n- **Interlayer tensile strength:** 3.2 MPa\n- **Permeability grade:** P12\n\n## 4 On-Site Assembly\nComponents are assembled into four ring structures using a crane. Assembly of the four rings takes approximately one day per house. Vertical structural columns are connected and reinforced with welded steel bars and mortar.\n\n## 5 Summary\nThe project demonstrates that concrete 3D printing significantly improves construction speed and enables complex geometries. It serves as a technical experience for additive intelligent construction in extreme environments.
