# Construction Technology of the Curved ‘Mars House’ Digital Hotel Project in China Using Concrete 3D Printing\n\n**Authors:** Xiqiang Lin, Hailong Wang, Xiaoyan Sun, Wael Kikhia, Zhennan Wu, Lu Lan, and Jiawei Zhao\n**Affiliations:** Zhejiang University; Hangzhou Guanli Intelligent Technology Co., Ltd.\n**Publication:** 3DcP 2023, STAM, pp. 241–250, 2024.\n\n## Abstract\n3D printing technology is one of the most likely construction technologies for future extraterrestrial bases. The Mars 1 Base project in Gansu Province, China, uses concrete 3D printing to construct five egg-shaped Martian hotel buildings. These structures are 9 m high with a floor area of 46 m², divided into two floors. This article introduces the structural design, 3D printing process, and on-site assembly in the Gobi Desert.\n\n## 1. Introduction\nMars research and exploration missions have sparked interest in habitat development and long-term human inhabitation. Concrete 3D printing offers a promising approach to overcome challenges like extreme environments and limited resources through innovative building methods.\n\n## 2. Architectural and Structural Design\nThe egg-shaped form was selected for its ability to withstand harsh Martian conditions (winds, radiation, temperature differences). The structure uses 3D printed walls as a permanent outer shell/mould, with a steel mesh and poured C40 self-compacting concrete inside to form a reinforced composite structure. The 9m structure is divided into four ring-shaped modules for printing.\n\n## 3. 3D Printing Technology and Process\n### 3.2 Personnel, Materials, and Machines\n- **Printers:** 2 industrial-grade concrete 3D printers (14.8 m × 8.6 m × 5.5 m).\n- **Parameters:** Layer height 15 mm, print speed 120 mm/s, extrusion width 40 mm.\n- **Material Consumption:** 10.6 m³ printing material and 17.76 m³ filling concrete per house.\n\n### 3.3 Concrete Mix and Performance\n| Raw Material | Proportion (%) |\n| :--- | :--- |\n| OPC42.5 Cement | 38 |\n| Artificial Sand | 42 |\n| Admixture | 4 |\n| Water | 16 |\n\n**Performance Metrics:**\n- Compressive strength (moulded): 47.9 MPa\n- Compressive strength (3D printed): 40.3 MPa (16% reduction)\n- Interlayer tensile strength: 3.2 MPa\n\n## 4. On-Site Assembly and Construction\nComponents were prefabricated in a factory, transported 1800 km, and assembled on-site using cranes. The four ring-shaped structures of one house could be assembled in a single day. Structural columns were formed by welding reinforcement cages and pouring mortar at reserved positions.\n\n## 5. Summary\nThe project successfully demonstrated that concrete 3D printing improves construction speed and accuracy for complex, irregular shapes. It serves as a technical precedent for future additive intelligent construction in extreme environments.
