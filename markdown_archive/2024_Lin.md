# Construction Technology of the Curved ‘Mars House’ Digital Hotel Project in China Using Concrete 3D Printing

## Abstract
3D printing technology is one of the most likely construction technologies to be used when building extraterrestrial bases in the future. The Mars 1 Base project in Gansu Province, China simulates the Martian landscape and uses concrete 3D printing technology to construct houses. In June 2022, the construction of five Martian hotel buildings began. The 3D-printed hotel is egg-shaped, with a total height of 9 m and a floor area of 46 square meters. This article introduces the structural design, 3D printing process, and overall installation and construction. The project is currently the world’s largest egg-shaped 3D printed structure.

## 1 Introduction
Advancement in Mars exploration has sparked interest in Mars colonization and habitat development. Concrete 3D printing presents a promising approach to constructing Martian habitats by addressing challenges such as extreme environments and limited resources through innovative building methods and in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) strategies.

## 2 Architectural Design and Structural Design of Mars House
The egg-shaped structure is chosen to withstand harsh Martian conditions, such as strong winds, radiation, and high-temperature differences. The house is 9 m high with a floor area of 46 square meters, divided into two floors with triangular windows and a skylight.

## 3 3D Printing Construction Technology and Process
### 3.1 Mars House 3D Printing Process
The process includes model refinement, factory-based component printing, pouring and embedding lifting points, and on-site hoisting/assembly.

### 3.2 Personnel, Materials, and Machines for 3D Printing
- **Machines:** 2 industrial-grade concrete 3D printers (14.8 m × 8.6 m × 5.5 m).
- **Parameters:** Layer height: 15 mm; print speed: 120 mm/s; extrusion width: 40 mm.
- **Material Consumption:** 10.6 m³ of printing material and 17.76 m³ of internal filling concrete per house.
- **Reinforcement:** Ribbed steel bars (10 mm and 12 mm); ~5 tons total.

### 3.3 3D Printed Concrete Mix Proportion and Performance
**Table 1. The concrete mix ratio for 3D printing of the Mars house**
| Raw materials | OPC42.5 cement/% | Artificial sand/% | Admixture/% | Water/% |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Mix proportion | 38 | 42 | 4 | 16 |

**Performance Results:**
- Compressive strength (printed): 40.3 MPa (16% reduction from 47.9 MPa standard moulding).
- Interlayer tensile strength: 3.2 MPa.
- Interlayer bonding strength: 2.7 MPa.
- Permeability grade: P12.
- Setting time: 41 min.

### 3.4 3D Printing Prefabrication of Components
Components are printed as ring-shaped structures. It takes 6 days to complete the printing of 4 ring-shaped structures for one house. Vertical reinforcement and lifting rings are inserted after printing.

## 4 Mar House On-Site Assembly and Construction
Printed components are transported (1800 km) and assembled using a crane. The lifting and installation of the four ring-shaped structures took one day to complete. Structural stability is ensured by welding reinforcement at reserved column positions and pouring mortar.

## 5 Summary
The project demonstrates that factory prefabrication of 3D-printed components significantly improves construction speed and quality control. This construction process provides technical experience for intelligent construction and additive manufacturing in extreme environments.
