# Ultrasonic tool for the realization of combined action during the drilling of extraterrestrial objects

**Authors:** Vladimir N Khmelev, Roman V Barsukov, Viktor A Nesterov, Dmitry V Genne, Qiquan Quan and Roman N Golykh  
**Publication:** Journal of Physics: Conference Series 1679 (2020) 042033  
**DOI:** 10.1088/1742-6596/1679/4/042033  

## Abstract
The article is devoted to the problem of the efficiency increase of ultrasonic drilling of hard materials for the studies of the soils of the planets, asteroids and other space objects. It is proposed the solution of the problem by the help of developed device, which is able to realize simultaneously with ultrasound the action by low-frequency macro-oscillations and rotational motion of the leading edge of the tool. The tests of the practical construction of the developed device showed the possibility of the speed increase of the ultrasonic drilling due to the additional low-frequency oscillations and pseudo-rotation in up to 1.5 times.

## 1. Introduction
Large and small extraterrestrial objects, such as planets, comets, asteroids are the objects for the studies. As a rule the soil intake is carried out in the form of the cores taken from various depths by the drilling of hollow tubular toll. The depth of the drilling can be several meters at the core diameter of 50 mm. 

Traditional drilling methods (by the rotated drill) under extraterrestrial conditions are not practically applied due to:
* High clamping force (inadmissible for low gravitation objects).
* Heating of the drilling zone (leading to volatilization of water and volatiles).
* High energy consumption.

Ultrasonic drilling is considered an alternative because it does not require large pressing forces, excludes heating, and can drill various materials.

## 2. Construction description
The device combines three mechanisms:
1. **Ultrasonic Action:** Longitudinal mechanical oscillations at ~20 kHz with an amplitude of ~70 µm.
2. **Low-Frequency Oscillations:** Periodic shock action of a loading mass providing vibrations with amplitudes ten times exceeding the ultrasonic action.
3. **Pseudo-Rotational Motion:** Provides periodic motion of the construction to increase cutting productivity.

## 3. Experimental Results
Experiments were conducted on silicate brick, red brick, and foam concrete using various modes: 
* RD (Rotation Only)
* UD (Ultrasound Only)
* UD+LFV (Ultrasound + Low-Frequency Vibrations)
* UD+RD (Ultrasound + Rotation)
* UD+LFV+RD (Combined Combined action)

## 4. Conclusion
The realization of complex action (ultrasound + low frequency + rotation) provided a growth in drilling speed of up to 49% compared to standard ultrasonic drilling. This system allows for the exclusion of energy losses and enables real-time monitoring of soil characteristics based on the electrical parameters of the oscillating system.
