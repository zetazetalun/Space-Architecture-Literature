# Ultrasonic tool for the realization of combined action during the drilling of extraterrestrial objects

**Authors:** Vladimir N Khmelev, Roman V Barsukov, Viktor A Nesterov, Dmitry V Genne, Qiquan Quan and Roman N Golykh

**Published in:** Journal of Physics: Conference Series 1679 (2020) 042033
**DOI:** 10.1088/1742-6596/1679/4/042033

## Abstract
The article is devoted to the problem of the efficiency increase of ultrasonic drilling of hard materials for the studies of the soils of the planets, asteroids and other space objects. It is proposed the solution of the problem by the help of developed device, which is able to realize simultaneously with ultrasound the action by low-frequency macro-oscillations and rotational motion of the leading edge of the tool. The tests of the practical construction of the developed device showed the possibility of the speed increase of the ultrasonic drilling due to the additional low-frequency oscillations and pseudo-rotation in up to 1.5 times.

## 1. Introduction
Large and small extraterrestrial objects, such as planets, comets, asteroids are the objects for the studies. As a rule the soil intake is carried out in the form of the cores taken from various depths by the drilling of hollow tubular tool. Traditional drilling methods (by the rotated drill) under extraterrestrial conditions are not practically applied due to high clamping forces, heating of the drilling zone (leading to volatilization of water/volatiles), and high energy consumption.

Ultrasonic drilling is considered an alternative as it realizes mechanical action at ultrasonic frequency, requiring low pressing force and excluding heating. However, it suffers from low drilling depths due to tool damping by waste material.

## 2. Construction Description
To increase efficiency, a device was developed combining:
1. Ultrasonic drilling (high frequency).
2. Shock low-frequency macro-oscillations.
3. Rotational motion (pseudo-rotation).

The system utilizes a piezoelectric transducer (20 kHz) and a concentrating waveguide providing mechanical oscillations of ~70 µm at the leading edge. A loading mass (13) provides low-frequency shock action when rebounding from the concentrator.

## 3. Results of the Experiments
Experiments were conducted on silicate brick, red construction brick, and foam concrete using different modes: Rotation (RD), Ultrasound (UD), Ultrasound + Low Frequency (UD+LFV), Ultrasound + Rotation (UD+RD), and the combined mode (UD+LFV+RD).

## 4. Conclusion
The realization of complex action (ultrasonic + low frequency + rotation) provided a growth in drilling speed of 49% compared to pure ultrasonic drilling. This method allows for continuous control of electric parameters to define soil characteristics during drilling without energy losses typical of tool separation.
