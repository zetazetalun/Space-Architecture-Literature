# Ultrasonic tool for the realization of combined action during the drilling of extraterrestrial objects

**Journal of Physics: Conference Series 1679 (2020) 042033**

**Authors:** Vladimir N Khmelev, Roman V Barsukov, Viktor A Nesterov, Dmitry V Genne, Qiquan Quan, and Roman N Golykh

**Abstract:** The article is devoted to the problem of the efficiency increase of ultrasonic drilling of hard materials for the studies of the soils of the planets, asteroids and other space objects. It is proposed the solution of the problem by the help of developed device, which is able to realize simultaneously with ultrasound the action by low-frequency macro-oscillations and rotational motion of the leading edge of the tool. The tests showed a speed increase of ultrasonic drilling by up to 1.5 times due to additional low-frequency oscillations and pseudo-rotation.

## 1. Introduction
Large and small extraterrestrial objects (planets, comets, asteroids) are studied via soil intake, usually in the form of cores taken by drilling hollow tubular tools. Traditional rotational drilling methods are impractical in extraterrestrial conditions due to:
- High clamping forces required (problematic in low gravitation).
- Heating of the drilling zone, which causes volatilization of water and low-melting materials.
- High energy consumption.

Ultrasonic drilling is an alternative that uses mechanical action at ultrasonic frequencies. It does not require large pressing forces, excludes significant heating, and can drill any material. However, it faces issues with damping at high depths and removal of waste material.

## 2. Construction description
The developed tool combines three mechanisms:
1. **Ultrasonic High-Frequency Action:** Generated by a piezoelectric transducer at approximately 20 kHz with an amplitude of ~70 µm.
2. **Low-Frequency Macro-Oscillations:** Provided by the shock periodic action of a loading mass, creating oscillations with amplitudes ten times higher than the ultrasonic frequency to prevent damping and increase productivity.
3. **Pseudo-Rotational Motion:** A drive provides periodic rotational motion to the construction, enhancing the cutting action at the leading edge.

## 3. Experimental Results
The device was tested on silicate construction brick, red construction brick, and foam concrete building blocks.

### Table 1: Main parameters of the ultrasonic working tool
| Parameter | Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| Amplitude of oscillations of the leading edge | 70 µm |
| Pressing force to the sample | 5, 10, 20 N |
| Rotation angle of pseudo-rotation system | 100 degrees |
| Frequency of pseudo-rotational motion | 1 Hz |
| Frequency of low-frequency oscillating motion | 20 Hz |
| Pressing force of loading mass | 12 N |

### Summary of Drilling Performance (Foam Concrete)
- **RD (Rotation only):** 5–11 mm/min
- **UD (Ultrasound only):** 55–122 mm/min
- **UD + LFV + RD (Combined):** 102–154 mm/min

## 4. Conclusion
The realization of complex action (ultrasound + low-frequency vibrations + pseudo-rotation) provided a growth in drilling speed of 49% compared to standard ultrasonic drilling. This system allows for continuous control of electric parameters to define soil characteristics during the drilling process without energy loss.
