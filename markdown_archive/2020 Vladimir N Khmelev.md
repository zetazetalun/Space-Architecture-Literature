# Ultrasonic tool for the realization of combined action during the drilling of extraterrestrial objects

**Authors:** Vladimir N Khmelev, Roman V Barsukov, Viktor A Nesterov, Dmitry V Genne, Qiquan Quan, and Roman N Golykh

**Journal:** Journal of Physics: Conference Series 1679 (2020) 042033
**DOI:** 10.1088/1742-6596/1679/4/042033

## Abstract
The article is devoted to the problem of the efficiency increase of ultrasonic drilling of hard materials for the studies of the soils of the planets, asteroids and other space objects. It is proposed the solution of the problem by the help of developed device, which is able to realize simultaneously with ultrasound the action by low-frequency macro-oscillations and rotational motion of the leading edge of the tool. The tests of the practical construction of the developed device showed the possibility of the speed increase of the ultrasonic drilling due to the additional low-frequency oscillations and pseudo-rotation in up to 1.5 times.

## 1. Introduction
Drilling extraterrestrial objects is essential for determining chemical composition, structure, and finding life. Soil intake usually involves cores taken by hollow tubular tools. Traditional drilling methods (rotating drills) are impractical in low gravitation due to high clamping force requirements, drilling zone heating (which volatilizes water), and high energy consumption.

Ultrasonic drilling is an alternative because it does not require large pressing forces, excludes heating, and can drill various materials. However, it faces challenges such as low depth due to tool damping and difficulty in removing waste. Combining ultrasonic action with low-frequency macro-oscillations and rotation can increase efficiency.

## 2. Construction Description
The developed device combines:
1. **Ultrasonic frequency oscillations:** ~20 kHz with ~70 µm amplitude.
2. **Low-frequency macro-oscillations:** Produced by a loading mass that rebounds from the concentrator.
3. **Pseudo-rotational motion:** Provides periodic motion of the construction to increase cutting productivity.

## 4. Conclusion
The research developed an ultrasonic apparatus that applies high-intensity oscillations (70 µm) combined with low-frequency vibrations and pseudo-rotational motion. This complex action increased drilling speed by 49% compared to pure ultrasonic drilling and 3 to 10 times compared to traditional rotation. The system also allows for continuous control of electric parameters to define soil characteristics during drilling.
