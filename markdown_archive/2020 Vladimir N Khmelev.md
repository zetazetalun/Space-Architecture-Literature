# Ultrasonic tool for the realization of combined action during the drilling of extraterrestrial objects

**Vladimir N Khmelev, Roman V Barsukov, Viktor A Nesterov, Dmitry V Genne, Qiquan Quan and Roman N Golykh**

**Abstract.** The article is devoted to the problem of the efficiency increase of ultrasonic drilling of hard materials for the studies of the soils of the planets, asteroids and other space objects. It is proposed the solution of the problem by the help of developed device, which is able to realize simultaneously with ultrasound the action by low-frequency macro-oscillations and rotational motion of the leading edge of the tool. The tests of the practical construction of the developed device showed the possibility of the speed increase of the ultrasonic drilling due to the additional low-frequency oscillations and pseudo-rotation in up to 1.5 times.

## 1. Introduction
Large and small extraterrestrial objects, such as planets, comets, asteroids are the objects for the studies. It is studied both the atmosphere of the extraterrestrial objects and the object as a source of the determination of chemical composition, structure and the finding of different forms of life. As a rule the soil intake is carried out in the form of the cores taken from various depths by the drilling of hollow tubular toll. The depth of the drilling can be several meters at the core diameter of 50 mm.

Recently the attention of the scientists is attracted by small space objects (comets, asteroids of various masses and sizes), which studies allow to receive information on near and far space. However, at the carrying out of studies on the small space objects (with low gravitation) besides the objects drilling it is solved the problem of space apparatuses fastening on them by the anchoring elements placed into the soil to the previously drilled channels.

Traditional drilling methods (by the rotated drill) under extraterrestrial conditions are not practically applied due to the high clamping force that at the drilling of the objects with low gravitation is inadmissible, the heating of the drilling zone leading to volatilization of water and low-melting materials during the study of the soil structure and its analysis. Moreover traditional drilling is characterized by high energy consumption [1].

As an alternative of the drilling method it is considered ultrasonic drilling, at which the working tool realizes mechanical action on the object at the ultrasonic frequency directly or through the layer of abrasive grains [2]. Such method is more efficient in comparison with the traditional one, as it does not require large pressing force, excludes the heating in the drilling zone and allows drilling any materials.

## 2. Construction description
To increase the efficiency of the drilling of hard and brittle soils under extraterrestrial conditions it is proposed and developed the construction of the device of combined ultrasonic drilling, which has ultrasonic drilling with shock low-frequency and rotational motion of the working tool (figure 1).

### Figure 1. Scheme of ultrasonic tool for drilling.
*(Technical diagram showing components 1-15 including working tool, concentrator, piezoelectric transducer, drive of pseudo-rotational motion, and loading mass)*

The drilling process is realized by the ultrasonic oscillating system consisting of the transducer 3, the concentrator 2 and the working tool 1. At the excitation of the ultrasonic transducer 3, the system generates longitudinal mechanical oscillations with the frequency of about 20 kHz. The tool obtains an amplitude of mechanical oscillations of about 70 µm at the leading edge. Low-frequency oscillations (amplitude ten times exceeding the ultrasonic action) are provided by shock periodic action of the loading mass 13 on the concentrator. The drive of the pseudo-rotational motion 8 provides periodic motion of the entire construction.

## 4. Conclusion
The study proposed a construction for drilling hard and brittle soils using complex action: high-intensity ultrasonic oscillations (70 µm), low-frequency vibrations, and pseudo-rotational motion. This realization provided a growth in drilling speed of up to 49% compared to pure ultrasonic drilling. The use of low-frequency vibrations without tool separation excludes energy losses and allows for real-time monitoring of soil characteristics through electrical parameters.
