# Ultrasonic tool for the realization of combined action during the drilling of extraterrestrial objects

**Journal of Physics: Conference Series**
**Volume 1679 (2020) 042033**
**DOI: 10.1088/1742-6596/1679/4/042033**

## Abstract
The article is devoted to the problem of the efficiency increase of ultrasonic drilling of hard materials for the studies of the soils of the planets, asteroids and other space objects. It is proposed the solution of the problem by the help of developed device, which is able to realize simultaneously with ultrasound the action by low-frequency macro-oscillations and rotational motion of the leading edge of the tool. The tests of the practical construction of the developed device showed the possibility of the speed increase of the ultrasonic drilling due to the additional low-frequency oscillations and pseudo-rotation in up to 1.5 times.

## 1. Introduction
Large and small extraterrestrial objects (planets, comets, asteroids) are studied for chemical composition and potential life forms. Traditional rotational drilling methods are impractical in extraterrestrial conditions due to high clamping forces required (problematic in low gravity), heating of the drilling zone (causing volatilization of water/low-melting materials), and high energy consumption. Ultrasonic drilling is considered an alternative as it requires less pressing force, minimizes heating, and can penetrate various hard materials.

## 2. Construction Description
The developed tool combines:
1. **Ultrasonic Oscillations:** 20 kHz longitudinal oscillations generated by a piezoelectric transducer with amplitudes of ~70 µm.
2. **Low-Frequency Macro-Oscillations:** Provided by a shock periodic action of a loading mass (rebound mechanism), with amplitudes exceeding ultrasonic action by ten times.
3. **Pseudo-Rotational Motion:** A drive providing periodic motion to increase productivity at the cutting edge.

## 3. Experimental Results
Experiments were conducted using silicate brick, red brick, and foam concrete as extraterrestrial soil analogues.

## 4. Conclusion
The combined action (ultrasound + low-frequency vibrations + pseudo-rotation) increased drilling speed by up to 49% compared to pure ultrasonic drilling. The device excludes energy losses by maintaining contact with the oscillating system and allows for real-time monitoring of soil characteristics based on the electric parameters of the system.
