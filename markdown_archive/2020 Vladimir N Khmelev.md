# Ultrasonic tool for the realization of combined action during the drilling of extraterrestrial objects

**Vladimir N Khmelev, Roman V Barsukov, Viktor A Nesterov, Dmitry V Genne, Qiquan Quan, and Roman N Golykh**

**Journal of Physics: Conference Series 1679 (2020) 042033**
**DOI: 10.1088/1742-6596/1679/4/042033**

## Abstract
The article is devoted to the problem of the efficiency increase of ultrasonic drilling of hard materials for the studies of the soils of the planets, asteroids and other space objects. It is proposed the solution of the problem by the help of developed device, which is able to realize simultaneously with ultrasound the action by low-frequency macro-oscillations and rotational motion of the leading edge of the tool. The tests of the practical construction of the developed device showed the possibility of the speed increase of the ultrasonic drilling due to the additional low-frequency oscillations and pseudo-rotation in up to 1.5 times.

## 1. Introduction
Large and small extraterrestrial objects, such as planets, comets, asteroids are the objects for the studies. Soil intake is carried out in the form of the cores taken from various depths by the drilling of hollow tubular tool. Traditional drilling methods are not practically applied in low gravitation due to high clamping forces, heating of the drilling zone, and high energy consumption. Ultrasonic drilling is considered an alternative as it does not require large pressing forces and excludes heating.

## 2. Construction description
The device consists of an ultrasonic transducer, a concentrator, and a tubular working tool. To increase efficiency, the design incorporates a loading mass (13) and a spring (12) to generate shock-induced low-frequency macro-oscillations. A drive (8) provides pseudo-rotational motion. This combination ensures continuous contact with the material and additional mechanical work via the teeth on the leading edge.

## 3. Results of the experiments
Experiments compared various modes: Rotation only (RD), Ultrasound only (UD), UD with low-frequency vibration (UD+LFV), UD with rotation (UD+RD), and the combined mode (UD+LFV+RD).

## 4. Conclusion
The complex action (UD+LFV+RD) provided a 49% growth in drilling speed compared to standard ultrasonic drilling. The device excludes energy losses by maintaining tool-system contact and allows for continuous control of soil characteristics via electric parameter monitoring.
