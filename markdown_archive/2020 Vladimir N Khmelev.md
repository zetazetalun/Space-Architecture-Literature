# Ultrasonic tool for the realization of combined action during the drilling of extraterrestrial objects

**Journal of Physics: Conference Series**
**DOI:** 10.1088/1742-6596/1679/4/042033
**Date:** 2020

**Authors:** Vladimir N Khmelev, Roman V Barsukov, Viktor A Nesterov, Dmitry V Genne, Qiquan Quan and Roman N Golykh

## Abstract
The article is devoted to the problem of the efficiency increase of ultrasonic drilling of hard materials for the studies of the soils of the planets, asteroids and other space objects. It is proposed the solution of the problem by the help of developed device, which is able to realize simultaneously with ultrasound the action by low-frequency macro-oscillations and rotational motion of the leading edge of the tool. The tests of the practical construction of the developed device showed the possibility of the speed increase of the ultrasonic drilling due to the additional low-frequency oscillations and pseudo-rotation in up to 1.5 times.

## 1. Introduction
Extraterrestrial objects such as planets, comets, and asteroids are subjects of study for chemical composition and potential life. Soil intake usually involves core samples (up to several meters deep with a 50 mm diameter). On small space objects with low gravitation, drilling also solves the problem of anchoring space apparatuses. Traditional rotary drilling is impractical in low gravity due to high required clamping forces and heat generation that causes volatilization of water/low-melting materials. Ultrasonic drilling is an efficient alternative as it requires low pressing force and minimizes heating.

## 2. Construction Description
A combined ultrasonic drilling device was developed to increase efficiency in hard and brittle soils. It incorporates:
- **Ultrasonic Oscillations:** 20 kHz frequency generating ~70 µm amplitude at the tool edge.
- **Low-frequency Macro-oscillations:** Produced by the shock periodic action of a loading mass rebounding from the concentrator.
- **Pseudo-rotational Motion:** Adds periodic motion to the special cutting leading edge.

## 4. Conclusion
The complex action (ultrasonic + low-frequency + rotation) provided a drilling speed growth of up to 49% compared to pure ultrasonic drilling. The device excludes energy losses by maintaining tool contact with the oscillating system and allows for real-time monitoring of soil characteristics through electric parameter control.
