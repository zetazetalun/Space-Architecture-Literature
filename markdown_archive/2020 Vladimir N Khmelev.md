# Ultrasonic tool for the realization of combined action during the drilling of extraterrestrial objects

**Vladimir N Khmelev, Roman V Barsukov, Viktor A Nesterov, Dmitry V Genne, Qiquan Quan and Roman N Golykh**

*Journal of Physics: Conference Series 1679 (2020) 042033*
*doi:10.1088/1742-6596/1679/4/042033*

## Abstract
The article is devoted to the problem of the efficiency increase of ultrasonic drilling of hard materials for the studies of the soils of the planets, asteroids and other space objects. It is proposed the solution of the problem by the help of developed device, which is able to realize simultaneously with ultrasound the action by low-frequency macro-oscillations and rotational motion of the leading edge of the tool. The tests of the practical construction of the developed device showed the possibility of the speed increase of the ultrasonic drilling due to the additional low-frequency oscillations and pseudo-rotation in up to 1.5 times.

## 1. Introduction
Large and small extraterrestrial objects, such as planets, comets, asteroids are the objects for the studies. As a rule the soil intake is carried out in the form of the cores taken from various depths by the drilling of hollow tubular toll. The depth of the drilling can be several meters at the core diameter of 50 mm. 

Recently the attention of the scientists is attracted by small space objects (comets, asteroids), where drilling solves the problem of space apparatuses fastening by anchoring elements placed into the soil to previously drilled channels. 

Traditional drilling methods (rotated drill) are often inapplicable due to:
- High clamping force required (inadmissible in low gravitation).
- Heating of the drilling zone leading to volatilization of water and low-melting materials.
- High energy consumption.

Ultrasonic drilling is considered as an alternative because it requires less pressing force, excludes heating, and allows drilling any materials.

## 2. Construction description
The proposed device combines:
1. **Ultrasonic oscillations:** Generated at ~20 kHz with a 70 µm amplitude.
2. **Low-frequency oscillations:** Created by a loading mass shock action, with amplitudes ten times exceeding the ultrasonic action.
3. **Pseudo-rotational motion:** Provides periodic motion of the construction to increase cutting productivity.

## 3. Results of the experiments
Testing was conducted on silicate brick, red brick, and foam concrete using various modes: 
- RD (Rotation Drilling)
- UD (Ultrasonic Drilling)
- UD+LFV (Ultrasonic + Low-frequency vibrations)
- UD+RD (Ultrasonic + Rotation)
- UD+LFV+RD (Combined Action)

## 4. Conclusion
The realization of complex action (ultrasonic + low-frequency + rotation) provided a growth in drilling speed of up to 49% compared to pure ultrasonic drilling and 3-10 times compared to traditional rotation. This system allows for continuous control of electric parameters to define soil characteristics during the drilling process.
