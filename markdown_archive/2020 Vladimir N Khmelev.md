# Ultrasonic tool for the realization of combined action during the drilling of extraterrestrial objects

**Journal of Physics: Conference Series**
**APITECH II**
**DOI:** 10.1088/1742-6596/1679/4/042033

## Authors
Vladimir N Khmelev, Roman V Barsukov, Viktor A Nesterov, Dmitry V Genne, Qiquan Quan, and Roman N Golykh

## Abstract
The article is devoted to the problem of the efficiency increase of ultrasonic drilling of hard materials for the studies of the soils of the planets, asteroids and other space objects. It is proposed the solution of the problem by the help of developed device, which is able to realize simultaneously with ultrasound the action by low-frequency macro-oscillations and rotational motion of the leading edge of the tool. The tests of the practical construction of the developed device showed the possibility of the speed increase of the ultrasonic drilling due to the additional low-frequency oscillations and pseudo-rotation in up to 1.5 times.

## 1. Introduction
Extraterrestrial objects like planets, comets, and asteroids are subjects of study for chemical composition and life forms. Soil intake is usually carried out via core drilling. Traditional rotary drilling is impractical in extraterrestrial conditions due to:
- High clamping force requirements (problematic in low gravity).
- Heat generation leading to volatilization of water/low-melting materials.
- High energy consumption.

Ultrasonic drilling is an alternative as it requires less pressing force, excludes heating, and can drill various materials. However, it suffers from low depth capabilities due to damping and waste removal issues.

## 2. Construction Description
The proposed device combines:
1. **Ultrasonic frequency oscillations (~20 kHz):** Generated by a piezoelectric transducer.
2. **Low-frequency macro-oscillations:** Produced by a shock periodic action of a loading mass (impacting the concentrator).
3. **Pseudo-rotational motion:** Provides periodic motion of the entire construction to increase productivity via a special cutting edge.

## 4. Conclusion
The realization of complex action (ultrasound + low-frequency vibrations + rotation) provided a drilling speed growth of approximately 49% compared to standard ultrasonic drilling. The system allows for continuous control of electric parameters to define soil characteristics during the process.
