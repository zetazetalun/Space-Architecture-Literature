# Ultrasonic tool for the realization of combined action during the drilling of extraterrestrial objects

## Abstract
The article is devoted to the problem of the efficiency increase of ultrasonic drilling of hard materials for the studies of the soils of the planets, asteroids and other space objects. It is proposed the solution of the problem by the help of developed device, which is able to realize simultaneously with ultrasound the action by low-frequency macro-oscillations and rotational motion of the leading edge of the tool. The tests of the practical construction of the developed device showed the possibility of the speed increase of the ultrasonic drilling due to the additional low-frequency oscillations and pseudo-rotation in up to 1.5 times.

## 1. Introduction
Drilling extraterrestrial objects like planets, comets, and asteroids is essential for determining chemical composition and searching for life. Traditional rotary drilling is impractical in low-gravity environments due to high clamping force requirements and thermal effects that can volatilize water or low-melting materials. Ultrasonic drilling is an alternative that requires less force and minimizes heating. However, it suffers from low depth capabilities due to material damping. This paper proposes a combined tool incorporating ultrasonic oscillations, low-frequency macro-oscillations (shock impact), and rotational motion.

## 2. Construction Description
The developed device consists of a piezoelectric transducer, a concentrating waveguide (concentrator), and a tubular working tool. Key components include:
- **Ultrasonic System:** Generates 20 kHz oscillations with an amplitude of ~70 µm.
- **Low-Frequency System:** A loading mass (13) provides shock periodic actions, creating vibrations at a frequency of ~20 Hz with amplitudes ten times higher than the ultrasonic ones.
- **Rotational Drive:** Provides pseudo-rotational motion (1 Hz) to prevent jamming and enhance cutting.

## 3. Results of the Experiments
Testing was performed on silicate brick, red construction brick, and foam concrete using various modes:
- RD: Rotation only
- UD: Ultrasound only
- UD+LFV: Ultrasound + Low-frequency vibration
- UD+RD: Ultrasound + Rotation
- UD+LFV+RD: Combined complex action

### Table 1: Main parameters of the tool
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Amplitude of oscillations at leading edge | 70 µm |
| Pressing force | 5, 10, 20 N |
| Rotation angle of pseudo-rotation system | 100 degrees |
| Frequency of pseudo-rotational motion | 1 Hz |
| Frequency of low-frequency oscillations | 20 Hz |
| Pressing force of loading mass | 12 N |

### Results Summary (Speed in mm/min)
- **Silicate Brick (20N):** UD (27), UD+LFV+RD (42)
- **Red Brick (10N):** UD (14), UD+LFV+RD (17)
- **Foam Concrete (20N):** UD (122), UD+LFV+RD (154)

## 4. Conclusion
The complex action (combined UD, LFV, and RD) provided a drilling speed increase of up to 49% compared to pure ultrasonic drilling and up to 3–10 times higher than rotation-only methods. The design allows for continuous control of soil characteristics by monitoring the electrical parameters of the oscillating system without loss of contact.
