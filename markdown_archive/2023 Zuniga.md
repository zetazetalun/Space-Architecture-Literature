# SUITABILITY OF SILICONE FOR SOFT-ROBOTIC EXPLORATION OF TERRESTRIAL AND EXTRATERRESTRIAL OCEAN WORLDS\n\n**Authors:** A. NUNCIO ZUNIGA, W. FINK\n**Journal:** Space Science and Technology, 2023, Vol. 29, No. 3, pp. 47–56\n**DOI:** 10.15407/knit2023.03.047\n\n## Abstract\nThis work revisits mechanical and chemical properties of silicone rubber — Ecoflex — to assess its suitability for use in soft-robotic explorer construction and as a sealant for electronics in extreme planetary liquid environments. Tests included strain at various temperatures, pliability at low temperatures, re-bonding properties for additive manufacturing, and resistance to salt water and hydrocarbons.\n\n## 1. Introduction\nSubsurface oceans on planetary bodies such as Europa, Titan, and Enceladus are prime candidates for astrobiological exploration. Soft robotics offers advantages over rigid frames for navigating small apertures in these liquid environments. This paper examines the suitability of silicone rubber for these extreme conditions.\n\n## 2. Materials and Methods\n### 2.1. Ecoflex Overview\nEcoflex™ 00-30 was selected as a stand-in for polymers due to its resistance to freezing, high elasticity, and ease of casting. It is a two-part silicone rubber (A and B).\n\n### 2.2. Testing Procedures\n- **Strain Testing:** Rectangular prisms were tested at ratios (A:B) of 20:80, 50:50, and 80:20 at temperatures ranging from -5°C to 40°C.\n- **Very Low Temperature Testing:** Samples were cooled to -7.5°C, -20°C, -80°C, and -196°C (liquid nitrogen).\n- **Re-bonding Ability:** Investigated potential for additive manufacturing by curing fresh silicone against already-cured pieces.\n- **Chemical Resistance:** Submersion in salt water (3.5% and saturated) and mineral oil (hydrocarbon stand-in).\n- **Electrical Conductivity:** Testing for electronics encapsulation using a Raspberry Pi unit.\n\n## 4. Results\n- **Mechanical:** Increased compound A led to stiffer material. Strain results were largely temperature-independent within the measured range. Elongation at break for the 50:50 mix was 553.3 ± 20.5%.\n- **Temperature:** Samples froze and shattered at -80°C and -196°C but returned to pre-freezing pliability once thawed. Pliability was maintained down to -20°C.\n- **Re-bonding:** New silicone successfully bonded to cured samples (up to 6 days old), behaving as a single piece.\n- **Chemical/Electrical:** No degradation after 21 days in salt water or 2 months in mineral oil. Encapsulated electronics functioned perfectly while submerged.\n\n## 5. Discussion & Conclusions\nEcoflex 00-30 is an excellent encapsulation and construction option for soft-robotics in terrestrial and some extraterrestrial aqueous missions. While it freezes at extreme cryogenic temperatures (e.g., Titan's lakes at 94 K), it remains robust for missions where liquid water phases exist (Europa/Enceladus subsurface oceans). Its re-bonding properties facilitate additive manufacturing of soft-robotic components.
