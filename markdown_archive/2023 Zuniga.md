# SUITABILITY OF SILICONE FOR SOFT-ROBOTIC EXPLORATION OF TERRESTRIAL AND EXTRATERRESTRIAL OCEAN WORLDS

**Authors:** A. Nuncio Zuniga, W. Fink  
**Journal:** Space Science and Technology (Космічна наука і технологія)  
**Year:** 2023  
**DOI:** 10.15407/knit2023.03.047

## Abstract
This work revisits relevant mechanical and chemical properties of silicone rubber — Ecoflex — to assess its suitability and viability for use in soft-robotic explorer construction and subsequent deployment and as a sealant for communication beacons, sensor pods, and other electronics in extreme planetary liquid environments, such as the depths of Earth’s oceans and extraterrestrial ocean worlds (Europa, Titan, Enceladus). 

## 1. Introduction
Ocean worlds with subsurface liquid environments are prime candidate locales for astrobiological exploration. Conventional rigid-frame underwater vehicles lack the flexibility to maneuver through small apertures. Soft robotics provides a paradigm for flexible exploration. This study experimentally examines the suitability of silicone rubber for constructing these systems and sealing associated electronics against extreme salinity and hydrocarbons.

## 2. Materials and Methods
- **Material:** Ecoflex™ 00-30 silicone rubber.
- **Compound Ratios:** Tested mixing ratios of 20:80, 50:50, and 80:20 (Hardener A : Resin B).
- **Temperature Conditions:** Testing at -5°C to 0°C, 22.4°C, and 39°C to 40°C.
- **Specialized Tests:** Cryogenic testing in liquid nitrogen (-196°C), re-bonding ability for additive manufacturing, and 21-day submersion in saturated saltwater and mineral oil.

## 3. Results
### 3.1. Strain and Mechanical Durability
Increased hardener (compound A) produced stiffer material. Strain performance was found to be minimally dependent on temperature within the terrestrial ocean range (-5°C to 40°C).

### 3.2. Material Failure
At a 50:50 mixing ratio, the average elongation at breaking was 553.3 ± 20.5% at room temperature.

### 3.3. Thermal Resilience
Ecoflex remained pliable down to -20°C. It froze and shattered at -196°C (liquid nitrogen), but properties were fully restored upon returning to room temperature, suggesting reversible freezing behavior.

### 3.4. Additive Manufacturing Potential
Newly curing silicone successfully bonded with already-cured samples (up to 6 days old) without forming a weak interface. This allows for additive-style construction and encapsulation of components.

### 3.5. Chemical and Electrical Insulation
The material is non-polar and resistant to dissolution in water and mineral oil. A Raspberry Pi beacon encapsulated in Ecoflex maintained continuous wireless communication for 21 days while submerged in saturated saltwater.

## 4. Discussion & Conclusions
Ecoflex 00-30 is an excellent encapsulation and construction material for planetary liquid explorers at temperatures above -50°C. While unsuitable for the cryogenic liquid hydrocarbons of Titan (-179°C) due to freezing, it provides a benchmark for developing similar soft materials for extreme subsurface ocean exploration on Europa and Enceladus.
