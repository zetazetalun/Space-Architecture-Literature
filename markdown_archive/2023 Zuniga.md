# SUITABILITY OF SILICONE FOR SOFT-ROBOTIC EXPLORATION OF TERRESTRIAL AND EXTRATERRESTRIAL OCEAN WORLDS

**Authors:** A. NUNCIO ZUNIGA, W. FINK  
**Publication:** Space Science and Technology (Космічна наука і технологія)  
**Year:** 2023  
**DOI:** 10.15407/knit2023.03.047

## Abstract
This work revisits relevant mechanical and chemical properties of silicone rubber — Ecoflex — to assess its suitability for use in soft-robotic explorer construction and as a sealant for communication beacons and sensor pods in extreme planetary liquid environments (e.g., Earth’s oceans, Europa, Titan, Enceladus). Key tests included strain at various temperatures, re-binding properties for additive manufacturing, and dissolution resistance in saltwater and hydrocarbons.

## 1. Introduction
Underwater autonomous vehicles are often restricted by rigid frames. Soft-robotics offers a paradigm shift for exploring restricted apertures in subsurface oceans on planetary bodies like Europa, Titan, and Enceladus. This study re-evaluates Ecoflex 00-30 for its elasticity, low-temperature resilience, re-bonding ability, and chemical resistance.

## 2. Materials and Methods
- **Material:** Ecoflex™ 00-30 silicone rubber (Smooth-On, Inc.).
- **Compound Ratios:** Tested A:B mixing ratios of 20:80, 50:50, and 80:20.
- **Strain Testing:** Measured under clamp forces (5N, 10N, 15N) across temperature ranges: -5°C to 0°C, ~22.4°C, and 39°C to 40°C.
- **Low Temp Testing:** Samples tested down to -196°C (Liquid Nitrogen).
- **Re-bonding:** Testing cured samples joined with newly curing liquid Ecoflex after delays of up to 6 days.
- **Resistance:** Submersion in 3.5% salinity water, saturated saltwater, and mineral oil (hydrocarbon stand-in).

## 3. Results

### 3.1. Strain and Temperature
Increased amounts of compound A (catalyst) led to a stiffer material. Strain performance was found to be largely temperature-independent within the tested range (-5°C to 40°C).

### 3.2. Failure Testing
For the 50:50 mix at room temperature (~23°C), the breaking elongation point was **553.3 ± 20.5%**, significantly lower than the manufacturer-advertised 900% but sufficient for robotic flexibility.

### 3.3. Low Temperature Behavior
- **-20°C:** Remained pliable.
- **-80°C:** Frozen/stiff (reversible upon thawing).
- **-196°C:** Shattered upon impact (reversible upon thawing).

### 3.4. Re-bonding and Additive Manufacturing
Cured Ecoflex successfully bonded with fresh liquid Ecoflex, creating a seamless interface even after the initial piece had been cured for 6 days. This supports incremental construction or additive manufacturing processes.

### 3.5. Chemical and Electrical Resistance
- **Saltwater/Hydrocarbons:** No signs of degradation after 21 days (saltwater) or 2 months (mineral oil).
- **Conductivity:** The material is non-conductive, successfully protecting a submerged Raspberry Pi Zero W and power bank while allowing wireless communication and charging.

## 4. Discussion & Conclusions
Ecoflex 00-30 is an excellent encapsulation option for soft-robotics in aqueous environments. While it freezes at temperatures relevant to Titan's surface (-179°C), it remains functional for terrestrial and potentially water-based subsurface ocean missions (Europa/Enceladus) where brines might depress freezing points. Its re-bonding property allows for complex soft-robotic structural assembly.
