# SUITABILITY OF SILICONE FOR SOFT-ROBOTIC EXPLORATION OF TERRESTRIAL AND EXTRATERRESTRIAL OCEAN WORLDS  **Authors:** A. Nuncio Zuniga, W. Fink   **Affiliation:** Visual and Autonomous Exploration Systems Research Laboratory, University of Arizona  **Journal:** Space Science and Technology (Космічна наука і технологія), 2023, Vol. 29, No. 3, pp. 47–56  **DOI:** 10.15407/knit2023.03.047  ## ABSTRACT This work revisits mechanical and chemical properties of silicone rubber — Ecoflex — to assess its suitability for soft-robotic explorer construction and as a sealant for electronics in extreme planetary liquid environments, such as Earth’s oceans and extraterrestrial ocean worlds (Europa, Titan, Enceladus). Tests included strain under various temperatures, re-bonding properties for additive manufacturing, and resistance to dissolution in salt water and hydrocarbons. Results indicate Ecoflex is an effective non-conducting sealant and pliable material for temperatures above -50 °C.  ## 1. INTRODUCTION Exploration of subsurface oceans on moons like Europa and Enceladus requires flexible explorers capable of navigating small apertures. This paper investigates silicone rubber for constructing these soft-robotic explorers and sealing communication beacons.  ## 2. MATERIALS AND METHODS ### 2.1 Ecoflex Overview Ecoflex 00-30 (Smooth-On, Inc.) was used. It is a proprietary silicone rubber mixed in equal parts (A and B). It cures in 4 hours at room temperature and is known for sub-zero temperature resistance and high elasticity.  ### 2.2 Strain Testing Prisms (1.25 x 1.00 x 7.50 cm) were cast at three mixing ratios (A:B): 20:80, 50:50, and 80:20. Testing was conducted at -5 to 0 °C, ~22.4 °C, and 39 to 40 °C.  ### 2.4 Very Low Temperature Testing Samples were tested at -7.5 °C, -20 °C, -80 °C, and -196 °C (liquid nitrogen) to observe freezing and thawing behavior.  ### 2.5 Re-bonding Ability Cured samples were joined with fresh liquid Ecoflex to test the potential for additive manufacturing and repairs.  ## 4. RESULTS ### 4.1 Strain Performance Increased amounts of compound A resulted in a stiffer material. Strain results were largely temperature-independent within the measured range above freezing.  ### 4.2 Stretching to Failure The 50:50 mix reached a breaking point at an elongation of 553.3 ± 20.5%.  ### 4.3 Low Temperature Results Samples did not freeze at -20 °C but froze at -80 °C. Freezing was fully reversible; samples returned to pre-freezing pliability once thawed. At -196 °C, samples shattered but regained pliability upon returning to room temperature.  ### 4.4 Re-bonding The interface between old and new cured Ecoflex was nearly indistinguishable and did not break under stress, supporting additive manufacturing applications.  ### 4.5 Chemical Resistance Cured Ecoflex showed no degradation after 21 days in salt water or 2 months in mineral oil (hydrocarbon stand-in).  ## 5. CONCLUSIONS Ecoflex 00-30 is an excellent candidate for soft-robotic construction and electronic encapsulation for missions to subsurface oceans. It maintains pliability down to -20 °C (likely -50 °C) and is chemically resistant to aqueous and hydrocarbon environments.
