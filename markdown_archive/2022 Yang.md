# Laboratory construction and curation scheme for returned samples of the chang’e-5 mission

**Authors:** Hongwei Yang, Ruihong Yang, and Qian Wang  
**Journal:** Geoscience Letters (2022) 9:19  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1186/s40562-022-00224-z

## Abstract
The analysis of samples returned from a planetary body is important to understand the origin, composition, evolution, and interactions with space or atmospheric weathering. The Chang’e-5 mission acquired less than 2 kg of lunar samples, including a core sample almost 1 m long. The CLESEC (China Lunar Exploration and Space Engineering Center) is planning to construct at least two laboratories and determine regulations for preserving samples. This paper establishes requirements for constructing laboratories and curation based on Apollo experiences and outlines six potential demands for future sampling missions.

## Introduction
Remote sensing and radar observations are primary approaches for determining planetary surface features. However, impact events result in layered structures (regolith) that can deviate from geological interpretations. Sampling is a key method to identify and understand materials sourced from the surface and deep layers. The Chang’e-5 Sampling Station landed at 43.058 N, 51.916 W, returning 1731 g of samples after 16 days.

## Laboratory Requirements and Conditions

### Contamination Control
*   **Air Quality:** Oxygen < 20 molecules per million; Water < 50 molecules per million (1000x cleaner than standard labs).
*   **Materials:** Storage in sealed glove box cabinets using Teflon, neoprene, aluminum, and stainless steel tools.
*   **Pressure:** Nitrogen gas pressure in cabinets kept higher than the laboratory to prevent leak-in.

### Laboratory Layout
The laboratory comprises eight sub-labs:
1. Change Rooms and Air Shower
2. Pristine Sample Lab
3. Pristine Sample Display Case
4. Core and Saw Room
5. Pristine Corridor and Vault
6. Experiment Lab
7. Return Sample Vault
8. Observation Room

## Sampling and Processing
*   **Lunar Environment:** Vacuum (10^4~2x10^5 particles/cm²), Temperature (-150 °C to 110 °C).
*   **Sawing:** Performed in nitrogen-filled cabinets using stainless steel blades coated with diamonds. No lubricants or cooling liquids are used to prevent contamination.
*   **Digitalization:** All measurements are recorded in digital format to promote multi-disciplinary research.

## Principles for Curation
1. CNSA/CLESEC manages all lunar samples.
2. Samples are stored in multiple locations to prevent catastrophic loss.
3. Usage categories: permanently stored, backup, research, and public presentation.
4. Destructive testing is minimized.

## Future Considerations
1. Developments in sampling techniques and approaches on planetary surfaces.
2. Intensive research on geological settings for landing site candidates.
3. Optimization of the ratio of open to unopened samples for lasting preservation.
