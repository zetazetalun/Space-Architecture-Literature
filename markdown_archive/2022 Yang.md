# Laboratory construction and curation scheme for returned samples of the chang’e-5 mission

**Authors:** Hongwei Yang, Ruihong Yang and Qian Wang
**Journal:** Geoscience Letters (2022) 9:19
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1186/s40562-022-00224-z

## Abstract
The analysis of samples returned from a planetary body is important to understand the origin, composition, evolution, and interactions with space or atmospheric weathering of the planet. The Chang’e-5 lander, the first Chinese science project involving the return of lunar samples, launched on November 24, 2020, and drilled a core sample that is almost 1 m long. The CLESEC (China Lunar Exploration and Space Engineering Center) is planning to construct at least two laboratories and determine regulations for preserving samples. Based on the Apollo experiences, we have established detailed requirements and standardized conditions for constructing laboratories and curation with the aid of advanced technologies.

## Introduction
Remote sensing and radar observations are primary for determining mineralogical features, but they are limited by surface weathering and layered regolith structures. Sampling on planetary surfaces like the Moon is a key method to understand materials sourced from the surface and the deep. On December 1, 2020, the Chang’e-5 Sampling Station landed in the northern part of Oceanus Procellarum, returning 1731 g of samples, including a 1 m drill core.

## Requirements for Chang’e-5 Laboratories
Six requirements were established to magnify the scientific value of the samples:
1. Strict conditions for experimental laboratories.
2. Planned ratio of usage to preservation.
3. Specification of techniques for measurement.
4. Multi-disciplinary measurements.
5. Digitalization of results.
6. Public data access.

### Environmental Control and Contamination Prevention
To protect the samples, the following conditions must be maintained in the laboratory glove boxes:
- **Cleanliness:** 10^4 to 2 × 10^5 particles per centimeter square.
- **Oxygen/Water Levels:** Less than 20 ppm oxygen and 50 ppm water.
- **Pressure:** Nitrogen gas pressure in the cabinet kept slightly higher than the cleanroom to prevent leaks.
- **Temperature:** Low controlled temperatures similar to the lunar surface to decrease chemical reactivity.

### Sample Processing Protocols
- **Tools:** Use of Teflon, stainless steel, and aluminum tools to prevent chemical contamination.
- **Sawing:** Performed in a dry, slow process without cooling lubricants to protect sample integrity.
- **Scanning:** X-ray and CT imaging of sealed cores to determine grain size and internal structure before opening.

## Principles for Curation
1. CNSA is the management authority; CLESEC executes concrete management.
2. Samples are stored in multiple locations for redundancy/safety.
3. Usage types: permanently stored, backup, research, and public presentation.
4. Destructive testing is minimized and subject to strict peer review.

## Discussion and Future Developments
Future efforts focus on advancing sampling techniques, improving curation conditions, and expanding the use of digitalized data. The choice of landing sites for future missions should target pristine bedrock to better understand planetary evolution.
