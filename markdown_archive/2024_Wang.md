# To boldly go where no one has gone before: Sulfur concrete, a promising construction material fulfilling the demands for a sustainable future on celestial objects: A review

**Q. Wang, D. Snoeck**

BATir Department, École Polytechnique de Bruxelles, Université Libre de Bruxelles (ULB), 50 F.D. Roosevelt Ave, CP 194/02, 1050 Brussels, Belgium

## Abstract
The need to build a long-term or even permanent base is now a significant concern with the development of the exploration of extraterrestrial celestial bodies. Sulfur concrete was first proposed as a new building material in the 20th century. Recently, sulfur concrete has attracted much interest, as sulfur is considered one of the most accessible resources on the Moon and Mars, thanks to the in-situ resource utilization methodology. In addition, sulfur concrete is one of the most promising building materials for improving terrestrial sustainability or extraterrestrial exploration. This review paper summarizes the history of sulfur concrete development and different modified sulfur concretes. Previous research on extraterrestrial building materials is also reviewed. The unique advantage of sulfur concrete as an extraterrestrial material is justified, as no water is used during mixing. Lunar and Martian soil simulants are also examined as possible aggregate types. Finally, further improvements are proposed to broaden the application of sulfur concrete and the corresponding treatments. The possibility of recyclability and circularity is discussed from a sustainable development point of view.

## Introduction
Since the 1960s, exploration of the Moon and Mars has accelerated. NASA's Artemis program and ESA's space resource strategy emphasize the need for local resources to sustain human presence. Sulfur, found at the Moon's south pole by Chandrayaan-3, is a critical material for waterless construction. Conventional hydraulic concrete is not ideal for space due to water scarcity; sulfur concrete, which solidifies upon cooling below 140°C, offers a high-strength alternative.

## Sulfur Concrete Characteristics
Sulfur concrete is a thermoplastic material. Its advantages include: a) reduction of CO2 footprint (no cement), b) low price (waste material), c) production at freezing temperatures, d) quick hardening, and e) recyclability (can be re-melted above 120°C).

### Challenges
1. **Thermal Constraints:** Sublimation occurs in a vacuum if temperatures reach 120°C (lunar equator). This can be mitigated by burying structures under 30cm of regolith.
2. **Thermal Expansion:** Differences between sulfur and aggregates can cause debonding during thermal cycling (-191°C to 20°C).
3. **Cracking:** Phase transitions (beta to alpha sulfur) cause shrinkage (7%).

## ISRU and Automated Construction
ISRU reduces transportation costs (estimated at $130,000/kg to the Moon). Sulfur can be extracted from lunar troilite (FeS) or Martian sulfates. 3D printing techniques like Contour Crafting and FDM are proposed for building domes using regolith-sulfur mixtures.

## Future Solutions
- **Phase-Change Materials (PCMs):** Paraffin-based PCMs can help regulate temperature and reduce thermal stress.
- **Self-healing:** Implementing self-healing mechanisms inspired by asphalt technology (thermoplastic behavior) could increase structural longevity.
