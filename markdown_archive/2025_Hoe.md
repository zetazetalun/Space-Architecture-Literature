# Balancing Strength and Extreme Thermal Resilience in Lunar Regolith Composites: The Role of Multi-Walled Carbon Nanotubes

**Authors:** Andrea J. Hoe, Amirreza Tarafdar, Wenhua Lin, Michael R. Fiske, Jennifer E. Edmunson, and Yeqing Wang*

**DOI:** 10.1002/smll.202502220

## Abstract
The National Aeronautics and Space Administration’s (NASA) Artemis program aims to establish sustainable lunar habitats using resilient construction materials with minimal reliance on Earth-based resources. This study explores reinforcing lunar regolith with multi-walled carbon nanotubes (MWCNTs) using minimal water and additives (urea). Composites with 0.00 to 1.00 wt.% MWCNTs were subjected to freeze-thaw cycles, vacuum pressures, and various curing methods. Ambient-cured samples with MWCNTs achieved compressive strengths exceeding 35 MPa (a 44.44% increase). However, thermal cycling revealed limitations due to mismatched coefficients of thermal expansion (CTE), causing microcracking. Vacuum-cured MWCNT-free samples reached 45 MPa, showing that curing protocols significantly influence densification.

## 1. Introduction
Transporting material to orbit costs approximately $10,000 per pound, making In Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) of lunar regolith essential. This study utilizes LSP-1 Lunar South Pole Simulant. Urea, sourced from human waste, is used as a superplasticizer to reduce water requirements (down to 12.48 wt.%). The research addresses the gap in understanding how MWCNT-reinforced regolith behaves under lunar temperature extremes (≈114 to −171 °C) and vacuum conditions.

## 2. Results and Discussion

### 2.1. Mechanical Strength Enhancement
Three processing routes were evaluated:
- **Route 1:** Ambient curing (baseline).
- **Route 2:** Ambient curing + 4 thermal cycles (-80 to 117 °C).
- **Route 3:** Vacuum curing + 4 thermal cycles.

**Key Findings on Composition:**
- **Particle Size:** An 80:20 ratio of fine (<0.25 μm) to coarse (0.25–250 μm) regolith particles was found optimal for packing density.
- **MWCNT Effect:** In Route 1, 0.50 wt.% MWCNTs increased compressive strength to 39 MPa and modulus to 1.98 GPa.

### 2.2. Thermal Cycling Effects
Thermal cycling (Routes 2 and 3) introduced significant inconsistencies. In Route 2, strengths dropped below 20 MPa. The mismatch between the negative CTE of MWCNTs (−1.2 × 10⁻⁵/°C) and the positive CTE of regolith components (e.g., Al₂O₃ at 8.1 × 10⁻⁶/°C) caused interfacial stresses exceeding 207 MPa at −171 °C, leading to delamination and microcracking.

### 2.3. Vacuum Curing Benefits
Route 3 (vacuum curing) for the 0.00 wt.% MWCNT sample achieved 47 MPa. Vacuum pressure promotes accelerated moisture evaporation and better densification in the matrix, although it aggravates the thermal mismatch in MWCNT-reinforced samples.

## 3. Conclusion
MWCNT reinforcement significantly enhances the strength of lunar regolith composites under ambient conditions. However, the thermal expansion mismatch between CNTs and regolith presents a durability challenge under the Moon's extreme temperature swings. Future work must focus on optimizing CTE compatibility or tailoring CNT variants for extreme environments.

## 4. Experimental Section
- **Regolith:** LSP-1 Lunar South Pole Simulant.
- **Additives:** Urea (superplasticizer), Phosphoric acid (pH modifier/binder).
- **Reinforcement:** MWCNTs (>50 nm diameter, 10-20 μm length).
- **Testing:** Compressive strength measured via MTS machine; microstructure analyzed via SEM/EDS and XRD.
