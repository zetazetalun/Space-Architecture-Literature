# Designing Biopolymer-Bound Regolith Composites for Maximum Compressive Strength

**Authors:** I. Rosa, A. Coto, M. I. Allende, M. D. Lepech, and D. J. Loftus
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE

## Abstract
Extraterrestrial construction presents many interesting and new challenges. Transporting large amounts of construction materials from Earth is cost prohibitive. Thus, to take advantage of limited in situ extraterrestrial resources, this work focuses on a novel class of biopolymer-bound soil composite (BSC) materials. These quasi-brittle composites are produced by desiccating a mixture of soil, water, and a biopolymer binder to create a versatile material with uniaxial compressive strength comparable to concrete. 

## 1. Introduction
NASA’s current efforts to develop closed-loop building material systems for long-duration missions in the resource-constrained environment of space serve as a platform for the development of new construction materials that use local resources. Biopolymer-bound Soil Composites (BSC) are a mixture of granular soil aggregate, biopolymer, and water, which is desiccated to form a construction material with compressive strengths similar to Ordinary Portland Cement Concrete (OPCC).

## 2. Experimental Design
Experiments were designed using JSC-1A lunar soil simulant and bovine blood plasma proteins (BSA). Nineteen BSC mixes were tested with varying biopolymer solution concentrations (30% to 53%) and biopolymer-to-soil ratios.

## 4. Experimental Results
Results showed that a maximum compressive strength of 25.4 MPa was obtained at a biopolymer concentration of 48% and a biopolymer-to-soil ratio of 12.3%.

## 7. Conclusions
JSC-1A/AP920 BSC materials exhibited a linear relationship between compressive strength, dry bulk density, and biopolymer-to-soil ratio at saturation ratios less than or equal to unity. This enabling the creation of design tools for future deployment of BSCs in terrestrial and extraterrestrial construction applications such as micrometeoroid shielding or rural airstrips.
