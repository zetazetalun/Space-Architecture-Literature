# Designing Biopolymer-Bound Regolith Composites for Maximum Compressive Strength

**Authors:** I. Rosa, A. Coto, M. I. Allende, M. D. Lepech, and D. J. Loftus
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE

## Abstract
Extraterrestrial construction presents many interesting and new challenges. Transporting large amounts of construction materials from Earth is cost prohibitive. Thus, to take advantage of limited in situ extraterrestrial resources, this work focuses on a novel class of biopolymer-bound soil composite (BSC) materials. These quasi-brittle composites are produced by desiccating a mixture of soil, water, and a biopolymer binder to create a versatile material with uniaxial compressive strength comparable to concrete. 

Nineteen BSC mixes using JSC-1A regolith were tested with varying biopolymer solution concentrations and biopolymer to soil ratios. A maximum compressive strength of 25 MPa was obtained at a biopolymer concentration of 48% and a biopolymer to soil ratio of 12.4%.

## 1. Introduction
The development of new construction materials that use local resources, embody ultra-low amounts of energy, and can be continuously recycled is critical for long-duration space missions. Inspired by protein-based binders, BSC materials demonstrate closed-loop resource consumption and reduced environmental impacts compared to conventional concrete.

## 2. Experimental Design
Experiments were designed using JSC-1A/Biopolymer BSC mixtures at five biopolymer solution concentrations (30%, 38%, 43%, 48%, and 53%). Dried bovine blood plasma proteins (primarily BSA and immunoglobins) were used as the biopolymer.

## 3. Materials and Methods
*   **Inorganic Aggregate:** JSC-1A lunar regolith simulant.
*   **Binder:** AP920 Bovine Plasma.
*   **Manufacturing:** Dry-mixing of sand and protein, adding water, blending, and compaction in cylindrical molds at 5 MPa. Specimens were desiccated at 60°C for 24 hours.

## 4. Experimental Results
*   **Max Compressive Strength ($\\sigma_c$):** 25.4 ± 0.73 MPa (Mix BPS-48_BS-12).
*   **Max Dry Density:** 1.97 ± 0.01 g/cm³.
*   **Finding:** Higher biopolymer concentrations (53%) showed lower strength due to undissolved biopolymer suspension.

## 5. Predictive Models
The study established linear and mixed-effects models to predict dry bulk density and compressive strength based on the biopolymer to soil ratio and solution concentration.

## 6. Conclusions
JSC-1A/AP920 BSC materials exhibit a linear relationship between compressive strength, dry bulk density, and the biopolymer to soil ratio when saturation is $\le 1$. These findings support the creation of design tools for future habitat construction and micrometeoroid shielding.
