# Design and Analysis of 3D-Printable Thin-Shell Dome Structures for Extraterrestrial Habitation\n\n**Authors:** Matthew Troemner, Elham Ramyar, Benton Johnson, and Gianluca Cusatis\n\n## Abstract\nExtreme environmental conditions, unusual loadings, and availability of novel construction techniques dictate the form of Martian habitats. This paper presents the design and analysis of a dome-shaped Martian habitat designed as part of NASA’s 3D-Printed Habitat Challenge. The structure is a novel composite hemispheric-parabolic dome optimized for environmental loads and 3D-printed on an inflatable pressure vessel using Marscrete (Martian regolith and sulfur). The study evaluates performance under wind, sand deposition, and meteorite impact.\n\n## Introduction\nMartian missions require semi-permanent structures utilizing in situ resource utilization (ISRU) and automated fabrication. NASA's 3D-Printed Habitat Challenge specified requirements for a 4-person habitat with 93 sqm living space for one Earth-year.\n\n## Design Basis\nA hemispheric-parabolic dome structure was selected to minimize overhanging regions and maximize structural integrity. The design matching an internal hemispherical pressure vessel allows the membrane to serve as construction support. The parabolic exterior ensures compressive stress under loading.\n\n## Construction Method\n1. Site preparation by autonomous rovers.\n2. Foundation 3D printing via articulated arm.\n3. Deployment and inflation of a collapsible pressure vessel.\n4. 3D printing using Marscrete via a crawler robot circumnavigating the vessel.\n5. Slight deflation of the vessel to allow for thermal deformation.\n\n## Environment and Loading\n- **Gravity:** 3.71 m/s² (38% of Earth).\n- **Wind Load:** Calculated at 9.79 Pa (standard) to 60.0 Pa (high density).\n- **Sand/Dust Load:** Static pressure of 2,780 Pa for unbalanced buildup.\n- **Meteorite Risk:** Assessed via flux equations and impact simulations.\n\n## Structural and Impact Analysis\nFinite element analysis showed deformations < 1.0 mm within the elastic range. Lattice Discrete Particle Model (LDPM) simulations identified a critical meteorite impact mass of 7,390g at 6.29 km/sec (entry mass 12,022g). The probability of such an impact during a one-year mission is 6.9 x 10⁻⁹.\n\n## Conclusion\nThe design surpasses benchmarks for Martian habitation, offering efficient construction without printed supports and resilient structural performance under typical and extreme loading conditions.
