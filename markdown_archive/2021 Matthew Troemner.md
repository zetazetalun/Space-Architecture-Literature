# Design and Analysis of 3D-Printable Thin-Shell Dome Structures for Extraterrestrial Habitation

**Authors:** Matthew Troemner, Elham Ramyar, Benton Johnson, and Gianluca Cusatis
**Source:** Earth and Space 2021 (ASCE), pp. 1335-1341

## Abstract
This paper presents the design and analysis of a dome-shaped Martian habitat developed for NASA’s 3D-Printed Habitat Challenge. The structure utilizes a composite hemispheric-parabolic dome optimized for 3D-printing using 'Marscrete' (a sulfur-regolith concrete). Performance was assessed under Martian wind, gravity, regolith deposition, and meteorite impacts.

## Design Basis
- **Form:** Internal hemispherical dome with an outer parabolic dome.
- **Objective:** Minimize overhanging regions and maximize continuity to reduce 'cold joints' during 3D printing.
- **Material:** Marscrete (primarily Martian regolith and sulfur), which exhibits quasi-brittle behavior.
- **Optimization:** Thickness varies from 21 cm to 108 cm to ensure compressive stress under external loading.

## Construction Method
1. **Site Prep:** Autonomous rovers clear debris.
2. **Foundation:** 3D-printed using an articulated arm.
3. **Inflatable Support:** A collapsible dome pressure vessel is inflated to serve as a backing for printing, eliminating internal supports.
4. **3D Printing:** A small 'crawler' robot circumnavigates the pressure vessel to deposit layers from bottom to top.
5. **Finishing:** An oculus is affixed at the apex; the pressure vessel is slightly deflated to allow for independent thermal expansion.

## Environmental and Loading Parameters

| Load Type | Value / Parameter |
| :--- | :--- |
| Gravity | 3.71 m/s² (38% of Earth) |
| Wind Load | 9.79 Pa (standard) to 60.0 Pa (dust storm) |
| Regolith/Sand Depth | 0.5 m |
| Static Pressure (Sand) | 2,780 Pa (applied to half of structure) |
| Meteorite Critical Mass | 7,390 grams (impact velocity 6.29 km/sec) |

## Key Performance Findings
- **Static Analysis:** Under all load combinations, structural response remained in the elastic region with deformations < 1.0 mm.
- **Meteorite Impact:** The Lattice Discrete Particle Model (LDPM) determined that a 7.39 kg meteorite is the critical failure threshold. The probability of such an impact during a one-year mission is estimated at 6.9 × 10⁻⁹.
