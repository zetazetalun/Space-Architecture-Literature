## Abstract
The design of a permanent human habitat on a planetary body other than the Earth is an idea introduced many decades ago... The scope of this paper is to present a meticulous selection of the most significant publications within the scientific areas related to: (a) geotechnical engineering aspects... (b) ground motions generated by different types of Moonquakes and meteoroid impacts; (c) the different concepts and types of extraterrestrial (ET) structures (generic, inflatable, deployable, 3D-printed).

### 2.4. Anchoring in regolith
Anchoring can be envisaged as a basic foundation method for modular extraterrestrial structures in microgravity environments in order to avoid deep excavations and prevent potential uplifting. Techniques include helical anchoring, suction drilling, circular wedge anchoring, and claw anchoring.

### 2.5. Regolith-based landing pads
Foundations that demand extensive excavations likely would not be preferred. Interest shifts towards free-standing structures using local regolith (ISRU). Concrete made of KOHLS-1 (Lunar simulant) is a candidate material.

## 4. Extraterrestrial structures
- **Inflatable structures:** Dominant lightweight solution, e.g., TransHab, inflatable dome-shaped modules using Kevlar membranes.
- **Deployable structures:** Ability to change shape and size (e.g., folding hinges, origami-inspired).
- **3D-printed structures:** Utilizing AM technologies like Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM), Selective Laser Sintering, or Microwave Sintering of regolith.

## 5. Human-centered concepts for lunar/martian outposts
Focuses on holistic conceptualization (e.g., MALEO strategy - Modular Assembly in Low-Earth Orbit). Initial Manned Lunar Outpost (IMLO) concepts suggest placing modules under a Regolith Support Structure (RSS) for radiation protection.
