# A review towards the design of extraterrestrial structures: From regolith to human outposts

**Authors:** Nicos Kalapodis, Georgios Kampas, Olga-Joan Ktenidou
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 175 (2020) 540–569
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.actaastro.2020.05.038

## Abstract
The design of a permanent human habitat on a planetary body other than the Earth is an idea introduced many decades ago. This paper presents a selection of significant publications related to: (a) geotechnical engineering aspects, including mechanical properties and chemical composition of Lunar and Martian regolith samples and simulants; (b) ground motions generated by moonquakes and meteoroid impacts; (c) different concepts and types of extraterrestrial (ET) structures (generic, inflatable, deployable, 3D-printed).

## 1. Introduction
The concept of creating extraterrestrial habitats predates the 'space age'. Current federal and private missions (Artemis, SpaceX, etc.) necessitate civil engineering for Lunar and Martian structures. Key environmental challenges include low gravity, temperature fluctuations, radiation, lack of atmosphere, meteoroid impacts, and lunar dust.

## 3. Extraterrestrial Ground Motions and Seismic Hazard
Sources of moonquakes include deep moonquakes (tide-related), shallow moonquakes (tectonic-like, most energetic), thermal moonquakes, and meteoroid impacts. Moonquakes have very long durations compared to Earth quakes.

## 4. Extraterrestrial Structures

### 4.1 Inflatable Structures
Lightweight, low-volume for transport. Examples include Goodyear's 1961 toroid, TransHab, and NASA's Bigelow expandable modules. Materials often involve Kevlar membranes.

### 4.2 Deployable Structures
Able to change shape/size. Concepts include folding hinges (IFH) and origami-inspired designs.

### 4.3 3D-Printed Structures
Focuses on In Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU). Methods include:
- **Sintering:** Solar, Laser, and Microwave sintering of regolith.
- **Contour Crafting (CC):** Developed by Khoshnevis; uses extrusion-based 3D printing with sulfur concrete and regolith.
- **D-shape:** Uses binder jetting to solidify regolith simulant.

## 5. Human-centered Concepts
Strategies like MALEO (Modular Assembly in Low-Earth Orbit) and IMLO (Initial Manned Lunar Outpost). Foster + Partners' concept utilizes D-shape technology to create a protective dome over inflatable modules using lunar regolith.

## 6. Discussion
Successful ET construction requires multi-hazard analysis and interdisciplinary collaboration. The trend is moving from Earth-prefabricated modules (Class I/II) towards autonomous, ISRU-based 3D printing (Class III).
