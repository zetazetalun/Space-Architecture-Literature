# A review towards the design of extraterrestrial structures: From regolith to human outposts

**Authors:** Nicos Kalapodis, Georgios Kampas, Olga-Joan Ktenidou  
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 175 (2020) 540–569  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.actaastro.2020.05.038

## Abstract
The design of a permanent human habitat on a planetary body other than the Earth is an idea introduced many decades ago... The scope of this paper is to present a meticulous selection of the most significant publications within the scientific areas related to: (a) geotechnical engineering aspects... (b) ground motions generated by different types of Moonquakes and meteoroid impacts; (c) the different concepts and types of extraterrestrial (ET) structures (generic, inflatable, deployable, 3D-printed), as well as overall views of proposed ET habitats.

## 1. Introduction
The concept of creating extraterrestrial habitats predates the “space age”... there will soon be a need to expand civil engineering towards the design and construction of Lunar and Martian structures, habitats and outposts.

### 2.5. Regolith-based landing pads
Foundations requiring extensive excavations are unlikely. Lee et al. (2012) presented an ISRU-based technique using Lunar concrete (KOHLS-1). Kelso et al. (2016) demonstrated a 20-m-diameter vertical-takeoff-vertical-landing (VTVL) pad prototype made of basalt.

## 3. Extraterrestrial ground motions and seismic hazard considerations
Moonquakes are classified into four types: deep Moonquakes, shallow Moonquakes, thermal Moonquakes, and meteoroid impacts. Shallow Moonquakes are the most energetic, with magnitudes up to 4.8 mb.

## 4. Extraterrestrial structures
Structures are categorized into:
- **Inflatable structures:** Lightweight, occupy minimal space during transport (e.g., TransHab).
- **Deployable structures:** Can change shape and size, suitable for mass reduction (e.g., Kevlar membranes).
- **3D-printed structures:** Utilize ISRU to overcome cost and volume limitations (e.g., Contour Crafting, D-shape).

## 6. Discussion
The range of information spans a wide spectrum from regolith properties to structural analysis. While early concepts (1990s) focused on inflatable or truss systems, the last decade (2010s) shifted focus toward 3D-printing using regolith in an ISRU framework.
