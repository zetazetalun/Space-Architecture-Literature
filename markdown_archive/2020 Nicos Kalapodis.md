# A review towards the design of extraterrestrial structures: From regolith to human outposts

## Abstract
The design of a permanent human habitat on a planetary body other than the Earth is a concept gaining momentum through missions like InSight and Artemis. This paper reviews scientific areas related to: (a) geotechnical engineering aspects of Lunar and Martian regolith; (b) ground motions from moonquakes and impacts; (c) extraterrestrial (ET) structure types including generic, inflatable, deployable, and 3D-printed concepts.

## 1. Introduction
Civil engineering must expand towards ET structures. Key environmental challenges include low gravity, temperature fluctuations (up to 280 K on the Moon), radiation (GCR/SEP), meteoroid impacts (2.4 to 72 km/s), and pervasive Lunar dust. Gravity on the Moon is 0.17g and on Mars is 0.38g.

## 2. Aspects of Geotechnical Engineering
### 2.1. Lunar Regolith
Lunar regolith results from meteoroid impacts. Key properties for engineering include relative density (Dr), apparent cohesion (c'), and internal friction angle (phi').

### 2.2. Martian Regolith
Martian regolith is composed of basaltic sand and rocks. Viking and Phoenix landers provided in-situ data. Minimum cohesion estimates are 1–2 kPa.

## 3. Extraterrestrial Ground Motions
Moonquakes are classified into deep, shallow, thermal, and meteoroid impacts. Shallow moonquakes are the most energetic (up to 4.8 mb) and pose a significant risk to structure stability. Attenuation on Mars is roughly three times higher than on the Moon.

## 4. Extraterrestrial Structures
### 4.1. Generic and Inflatable Structures
- **Inflatable:** High volume-to-mass ratio. Examples include Goodyear's 1961 toroid and NASA's TransHab.
- **Deployable:** Compact for transport, including folding mechanisms (e.g., integral folding hinges).
- **3D-Printed (ISRU-based):** Utilizing regolith with technologies like Selective Laser Sintering (SLS), Microwave Sintering, or Contour Crafting (CC). Sintered regolith mixtures can reach compressive strengths of 55.16 MPa.

## 5. Human-Centered Concepts
Concepts such as the Modular Assembly in Low-Earth Orbit (MALEO) suggest assembling base components in orbit before landing. The 'Lunar Outpost' (Foster + Partners) proposes a 3D-printed regolith dome over inflatable modules for shielding.

## 6. Discussion
Habitats must primarily address radiation protection, internal pressurization, and material sustainability. The 2010s marked a shift toward 3D printing and ISRU as the primary manufacturing strategy for permanent outposts.
