## Abstract
The design of a permanent human habitat on a planetary body other than the Earth is an idea introduced many decades ago... The scope of this paper is to present a meticulous selection of the most significant publications within the scientific areas related to: (a) geotechnical engineering aspects... (b) ground motions generated by different types of Moonquakes and meteoroid impacts; (c) the different concepts and types of extraterrestrial (ET) structures (generic, inflatable, deployable, 3D-printed).

## 4. Extraterrestrial structures
Various researchers have proposed a plethora of concepts regarding different structural systems in ET environments. These are categorized into:
- **Inflatable structures:** Dome-shaped structures that withstand high tensile forces and occupy minimal space during transport.
- **Deployable structures:** Small, lightweight structures like antennae, though complex habitat concepts also exist.
- **3D-printed structures:** Utilizing in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) to overcome material transportation limitations from Earth.

## 6. Discussion
The range of information spans a very wide spectrum, from Lunar and Martian regolith properties to structural analysis and design. While early engineers envisioned strong inflatable or truss structures, the 2010s have seen a shift towards 3D-printed structures using regolith in an ISRU framework.
