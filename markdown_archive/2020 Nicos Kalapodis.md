# A review towards the design of extraterrestrial structures: From regolith to human outposts

**Authors:** Nicos Kalapodis, Georgios Kampas, Olga-Joan Ktenidou
**Published in:** Acta Astronautica 175 (2020) 540–569

## Abstract
The scope of this paper is to present a meticulous selection of the most significant publications within the scientific areas related to: (a) geotechnical engineering aspects, including the mechanical properties and chemical composition of Lunar and Martian regolith; (b) ground motions generated by Moonquakes and meteoroid impacts; (c) concepts and types of extraterrestrial (ET) structures (inflatable, deployable, 3D-printed).

## 1. Introduction
- The need for civil engineering to expand towards the design and construction of Lunar and Martian structures.
- Key environmental factors: low gravity, radiation, temperature fluctuations, and lack of atmosphere.

## 2. Aspects of Geotechnical Engineering
### 2.1 Lunar Regolith
- Review of physical and mechanical properties from Apollo and Luna missions.
- Discussion of Lunar simulants (JSC-1, FJS-1, MLS-1, etc.) and their chemical compositions.
### 2.2 Martian Regolith
- Data provided by Viking, Phoenix, and Mars Exploration Rovers.
- Simulants such as JSC Mars-1 and MMS.
### 2.4/2.5 Foundations and Landing Pads
- Challenges of anchoring in microgravity.
- Regolith-based landing pads to mitigate dust problems and plume effects.
- Use of ISRU frameworks for lunar concrete pads.

## 3. Extraterrestrial Ground Motions
- Analysis of Moonquakes: deep, shallow, and thermal.
- Hazard assessment for prospective ET bases.
- Meteorite impact data and seismic risk.

## 4. Extraterrestrial Structures
### 4.1 Generic Structural Concepts
- Truss systems (Benaroya & Ettouney).
- Cable structures and tensegrity systems.
- Semicircular arches vs. parabolic arches for internal pressure optimization.
### 4.2 Inflatable Structures
- Dominant category due to lightweight and high volume potential.
- Concepts like TransHab and Bigelow Aerospace modules.
### 4.3 Deployable Structures
- Focus on mass reduction and compact stowing.
- Origami-inspired designs and folding hinges.
### 4.4 3D-printed ET Structures
- Sintering methods (laser, solar, microwave).
- Contour Crafting (Khoshnevis) and D-shape (Cesaretti) technologies.

## 5. Human-centered Concepts
- Integration of ISRU, robotics, and inflatable volumes (e.g., De Kestelier et al.).
- Use of regolith shielding (RSS) for radiation and thermal protection.

## 6. Discussion
- Variability in regolith shear strength.
- The transition from the 1990s focus on inflatables/trusses to the 2010s focus on 3D-printed ISRU structures.
- Importance of Soil-Structure Interaction (SSI) in low-gravity environments.
