# A review towards the design of extraterrestrial structures: From regolith to human outposts

**Authors:** Nicos Kalapodis, Georgios Kampas, Olga-Joan Ktenidou  
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 175 (2020) 540–569  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.actaastro.2020.05.038

## Abstract
The design of a permanent human habitat on a planetary body other than the Earth is an idea introduced many decades ago... The scope of this paper is to present a meticulous selection of the most significant publications within the scientific areas related to: (a) geotechnical engineering aspects... (b) ground motions generated by different types of Moonquakes and meteoroid impacts; (c) the different concepts and types of extraterrestrial (ET) structures (generic, inflatable, deployable, 3D-printed).

## 1. Introduction
The expansion of civil engineering towards the design and construction of Lunar and Martian structures is now a necessity due to interests from both federal bodies (NASA, ESA) and private firms (SpaceX, Blue Origin). Key challenges include low gravity, temperature fluctuations, radiation, lack of atmosphere, and meteorite impacts.

## 2. Aspects of Geotechnical Engineering
This section reviews the mechanical and chemical properties of Lunar and Martian regolith. 

### 2.1 Lunar Regolith
- **Relative Density ($D_R$):** Increases abruptly between 10 and 20 cm depth.
- **Internal Friction Angle:** Ranges from 35° to 45° for Apollo 11 samples, reaching up to 51.5° for Apollo 15.
- **Simulants:** Materials like JSC-1, FJS-1, and MLS-1 are used to approximate original regolith properties for Earth-based testing.

### 2.2 Martian Regolith
Properties are primarily derived from in-situ testing by Viking, Phoenix, and various rovers. The InSight mission's HP3 (Heat Flow and Physical Properties Package) represents the latest effort to constrain soil properties.

### 2.5 Regolith-based landing pads
Landing pads mitigate dust problems and plume effects. The paper discusses ISRU-based construction of pads using Lunar concrete (KOHLS-1) and sintered regolith tiles.

## 3. Extraterrestrial Ground Motions
Reviews seismic hazards including deep moonquakes (tide-related), shallow moonquakes (tectonic), thermal moonquakes, and meteorite impacts. Shallow moonquakes can reach magnitudes of $4.8 \, m_b$ and represent a significant structural hazard.

## 4. Extraterrestrial Structures
The review categorizes structures into three main types:
- **Inflatable Structures:** Lightweight, high volume-to-mass ratio, effective against internal pressure.
- **Deployable Structures:** Compact for transport, including folding hinges and origami-inspired designs.
- **3D-printed Structures:** Utilizing ISRU (In Situ Resource Utilization) to minimize Earth-to-orbit transportation costs.
