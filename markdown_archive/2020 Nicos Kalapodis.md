# A review towards the design of extraterrestrial structures: From regolith to human outposts

## Abstract
The design of a permanent human habitat on a planetary body other than Earth is a decades-old idea. Today's technological advances (Artemis, InSight) make space colonization more realistic. This paper reviews the scientific areas related to: (a) geotechnical engineering aspects of Lunar and Martian regolith; (b) ground motions from moonquakes and meteoroid impacts; and (c) concepts for extraterrestrial (ET) structures (inflatable, deployable, 3D-printed).

## 1. Introduction
The expansion of civil engineering towards Lunar and Martian structures is becoming essential. Understanding environmental conditions (low gravity, temperature fluctuations, radiation, meteoroids, and dust) is the first step toward urban development on other bodies.

Martian soil is characterized by high variability. The paper provides data from Viking, Pathfinder, and InSight missions, comparing original materials with various simulants (JSC Mars-1, MMS, etc.).

### 2.4. Anchoring and Landing Pads
Anchoring is a fundamental foundation method in microgravity to avoid deep excavations. ISRU-based landing pads (using Lunar concrete or sintered tiles) are essential to prevent plume effects and cratering during rocket landing.

## 3. Ground Motions and Seismic Hazard
The paper discusses the four types of moonquakes: deep, shallow, thermal, and meteoroid impacts. Shallow moonquakes are the most energetic (up to 4.8 mb) and pose the greatest hazard to structures due to low attenuation in the Lunar crust.

## 4. Extraterrestrial Structures
### 4.1. Generic Structural Concepts
- **Inflatable structures:** Prevalent due to high strength-to-weight ratios and compact stowage.
- **Deployable structures:** Useful for smaller, lightweight applications like antennae.
- **3D-printed structures:** Focuses on the ISRU framework, using indigenous regolith to build habitats.

## 5. Human-centered Concepts
Strategies like MALEO (Modular Assembly in Low-Earth Orbit) and IMLO (Initial Manned Lunar Outpost) shift focus from individual structures to holistic outposts involving robotics and multi-layered shielding.

## 6. Discussion and Conclusion
Future design must involve multiphysics frameworks coupling thermal and static/dynamic analysis. Automated construction, interlocking regolith bricks, and sintering techniques are the path forward for sustainable ET habitation.
