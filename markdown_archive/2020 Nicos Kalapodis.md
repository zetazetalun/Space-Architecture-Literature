# A review towards the design of extraterrestrial structures: From regolith to human outposts

**Authors:** Nicos Kalapodis, Georgios Kampas, Olga-Joan Ktenidou
**Published in:** Acta Astronautica 175 (2020) 540–569

## Abstract
The design of a permanent human habitat on a planetary body other than the Earth is an idea introduced many decades ago... The scope of this paper is to present a meticulous selection of the most significant publications within the scientific areas related to: (a) geotechnical engineering aspects, including the mechanical properties and chemical composition of Lunar and Martian regolith samples and simulants... (b) ground motions generated by different types of Moonquakes and meteoroid impacts; (c) the different concepts and types of extraterrestrial (ET) structures (generic, inflatable, deployable, 3D-printed).

## 1. Introduction
The concept of creating extraterrestrial habitats predates the “space age.” There is an evident need to expand civil engineering towards the design and construction of Lunar and Martian structures, habitats and outposts. Key environmental challenges include low gravity, temperature fluctuations, radiation, meteoroid impacts, and Lunar dust.

## 2. Aspects of Geotechnical Engineering
### 2.1 Lunar Regolith
Regolith is the layer of fragmented rock materials. Key mechanical properties include bulk density, relative density ($D_R$), apparent cohesion ($c$), and internal friction angle ($\phi'$).

### 2.2 Martian Regolith
Martian regolith is a mix of weathered and windblown material. Data primarily comes from in-situ testing by Viking landers, Phoenix, and rovers (Sojourner, Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity).

## 3. Extraterrestrial Ground Motions and Seismic Hazard
Seismometers installed during Apollo missions recorded over 12,000 events. Sources include:
- **Deep Moonquakes:** Occur at depths of 700-1100 km.
- **Shallow Moonquakes:** Rarest but most energetic (up to 4.8 $m_b$).
- **Thermal Moonquakes:** Caused by temperature variations.
- **Meteorite Impacts:** Significant source of external vibration.

## 4. Extraterrestrial Structures
### 4.1 Inflatable Structures
Advantages: Effectively withstand internal pressure, minimal space during transport. Examples include Goodyear (1961), TransHab, and Bigelow Expandable Activity Module.

### 4.2 Deployable Structures
Capable of changing shape and size. Potential for mass reduction and compact stowage during launch.

### 4.3 3D-printed ET Structures
Focus on ISRU frameworks utilizing local regolith. Techniques include:
- **Sintering:** Selective Laser Sintering, Microwave sintering, and Solar sintering.
- **Contour Crafting:** Automated extrusion of regolith-based concrete.
- **D-shape:** Agglomeration process using a binder fluid.

## 5. Human-Centered Concepts
Focus shifts to holistic conceptualization, such as the Modular Assembly in Low-Earth Orbit (MALEO) strategy or the Initial Manned Lunar Outpost (IMLO) proposal by Bell et al., involving regolith support structures (RSS) for shielding.
