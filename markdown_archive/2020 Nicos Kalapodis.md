# A review towards the design of extraterrestrial structures: From regolith to human outposts

## Abstract
The design of permanent human habitats on planetary bodies is a vision gaining momentum with missions like Artemis and Insight. This paper reviews scientific areas related to: (a) geotechnical engineering aspects (regolith properties, simulants, anchoring); (b) ground motions (moonquakes, impacts); (c) concepts of extraterrestrial (ET) structures (generic, inflatable, deployable, 3D-printed).

## 2. Aspects of geotechnical engineering
Regolith is the layer of fragmented rock materials on planetary surfaces. Lunar regolith is produced by meteoroid impacts and charged particle bombardment. Martian regolith is weathered and windblown.

### 2.5. Regolith-based landing pads
Foundations requiring extensive excavation are impractical. Landing pads utilizing local regolith (ISRU) can be used as rigid foundation rafts. Lee et al. proposed Lunar concrete (KOHLS-1 simulant). Kelso et al. demonstrated a 20-m VTVL pad prototype using robotic pavers.

## 4. Extraterrestrial structures
Structures are categorized into:
- **Inflatable structures:** Prevalent due to low stowage volume and ability to withstand internal pressure (e.g., TransHab, Bigelow).
- **Deployable structures:** Focus on small lightweight structures like antennae or trusses.
- **3D-printed structures:** Utilize ISRU to reduce transport costs. Methods include sintering (Solar, Laser, Microwave) and Contour Crafting.

## 6. Discussion
Review findings indicate Lunar/Martian regolith cohesion is generally < 6 kPa, but friction angles can exceed 40°. High silicate, iron, and aluminum oxide content in regolith facilitates the formation of strong structural materials via sintering. A 3m regolith shield is identified as sufficient for insulation and radiation protection.
