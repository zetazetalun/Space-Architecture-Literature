# A review towards the design of extraterrestrial structures: From regolith to human outposts

**Authors:** Nicos Kalapodis, Georgios Kampas, Olga-Joan Ktenidou
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 175 (2020) 540–569
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.actaastro.2020.05.038

## Abstract
The design of a permanent human habitat on a planetary body other than the Earth is an idea introduced many decades ago... The scope of this paper is to present a meticulous selection of the most significant publications within the scientific areas related to: (a) geotechnical engineering aspects... (b) ground motions generated by different types of Moonquakes and meteoroid impacts; (c) the different concepts and types of extraterrestrial (ET) structures (generic, inflatable, deployable, 3D-printed), as well as overall views of proposed ET habitats.

## 1. Introduction
Following the space boom originated by federal bodies (NASA, ESA, ISRO, etc.) and private firms, it has become evident that there will soon be a need to expand civil engineering towards the design and construction of Lunar and Martian structures.

### 2.4. Anchoring in regolith
Anchoring can be envisaged as a basic foundation method for modular extraterrestrial structures in microgravity environments to avoid deep excavations and prevent potential uplifting.

### 2.5. Regolith-based landing pads
Foundations that demand extensive excavations are not preferred. Interest shifts towards free-standing structures using local regolith for landing pads/foundation rafts. Lee et al. [93] presented a construction technique using Lunar concrete made of KOHLS-1 simulant.

## 4. Extraterrestrial structures
### 4.2. Inflatable structures
Inflatable dome-shaped structures are prevalent because they effectively withstand high tensile forces from internal pressure and occupy minimal space during transport.

### 4.4. 3D-printed ET structures
Additive Manufacturing (AM) is receiving attention for its potential to produce complex structures using local materials via sintering (Solar, Laser, or Microwave) or binder jetting. The most effective AM fabrication method is based on sintering local materials.

## 5. Human-centered concepts for lunar/martian outposts
Holistic conceptualization involves combinations of AM, ISRU, and robotics. Examples include the MALEO strategy (Modular Assembly in Low-Earth Orbit) and the 3D-printed regolith shell by De Kestelier et al. (2015).
