# Solar-Powered Additive Manufacturing in Extraterrestrial Environments ## Authors Steven D. Anderson, Jekan Thangavelautham ## Abstract Building a space economy requires infrastructure like habitats and mining bases constructed from in-situ resources. This paper proposes replacing the laser in Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) with a Fresnel lens to utilize solar energy for sintering. The research analyzes the conceptualization, design, and prototype construction of a solar 3D printer tailored for the Moon, Mars, and asteroids. ## Introduction Sustainable construction methods are vital for long-duration missions. Exporting materials from Earth is costly, making ISRU and additive manufacturing (AM) paramount. ## Challenges to Extreme Environment Additive Manufacturing Key challenges include material availability (requiring ISRU), the need for refining processes (e.g., pelletizing), and high power consumption for heating and operations. ## Objective The goal is to develop an AM method that is low-cost, low-energy, adaptable to various environments, adheres to ISRU, and is suitable for autonomous operation. ## Design Method While FDM is common, SLS is promising due to lower energy requirements and minimal material pre-processing. A standard FDM process consumes ~115 kWh/kg, while SLS consumes ~40 kWh/kg for polymers. By using a Fresnel lens, the power system can rely on renewable solar energy. ## Feasibility and Calculations Solar irradiance varies across the solar system (Moon ~1395 W/m2, Mars ~597.9 W/m2). Calculations for a 1m2 lens with 50% efficiency show achievable power: Earth Surface (393 W), Moon (548 W), and Mars (235 W). Required sinter power for regolith on the Moon is 450 W at 1.00 mm/s feed rate; for basalt on Mars, it is 236 W at 4.50 mm/s. ## Employment and Technical Considerations Solar sintering is ideal for autonomous robotic fleets constructing foundations and roads prior to human arrival. It is less suited for tall structures requiring significant vertical travel. ## Experimental Design A mid-scale prototype (75cm x 75cm) using a 1m2 Fresnel lens is under development. It features a print bed moving in X-Y-Z axes via Gcode commands while the solar beam remains fixed. ## Conclusion Solar sintering reduces energy demands and material processing needs, offering a versatile solution for extraterrestrial construction where power conservation is a priority.
