# 3D printable regolith filled shape memory vitrimer composite for extraterrestrial application

**Journal:** Journal of Composite Materials  
**Volume/Issue:** 58(24) 2639–2654  
**Date:** 2024  
**Authors:** Kingsley Yeboah Gyabaah, John Konlan, Obed Tetteh, Maryam Jahan, Enrique Jackson, Patrick Mensah, and Guoqiang Li  
**DOI:** 10.1177/00219983241274544

## Abstract
This study investigates a neoteric approach in manufacturing lunar regolith-filled shape memory vitrimer (SMV) composites for extraterrestrial applications. A SMV with robust mechanical properties was combined with locally available lunar regolith to form a composite material. Compressive strength, flexural strength, and impact resistance of the composite were tested at both room and elevated temperatures. A compressive strength of 96.0 MPa and 5.4 MPa were recorded for composite with 40 wt% regolith ratio at room and elevated temperatures, respectively. The glass fabric reinforced SMV-regolith laminate exhibited a bending strength of 232.7 MPa and good healing efficiency. The 3D printed SMV-regolith composite using a liquid crystal display (LCD)-based printer exhibited a compressive strength of 139.16 MPa and good shape memory effect.

## Introduction
Developing independent extraterrestrial settlements requires in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) to reduce the cost of shipping materials from Earth. Lunar regolith is a primary feedstock for construction. Traditional consolidation methods like sintering require high energy (1200–1500 °C) and result in high porosity. This study proposes using shape-memory vitrimers (SMV) as a binder, combining the resilience of thermosets with the recyclability of thermoplastics.

## Experimental Methods
### Materials
- **Binder:** Bisphenol A glycerolate dimethacrylate (BPAGMA).
- **Photoinitiator:** 2-hydroxy-4-(2-hydroxyethoxy)-2-methylpropiophenone.
- **Regolith:** Lunar Regolith Simulant (LRS) from Exolith Lab.
- **Reinforcement:** Bidirectional plain woven glass fabric.

### Fabrication
1. **Conventional:** Mixing BPAGMA with LRS (10-40 wt%) and UV-curing for 20s, followed by post-curing at 180°C.
2. **3D Printing:** LCD-based vat photopolymerization using an Anycubic Photon Mono 3D printer (405 nm LED).
3. **Laminates:** Hand layup process with glass fabric (35% volume fraction).

## Conclusions
The SMV-regolith composite demonstrates excellent potential for lunar base construction due to its simple manufacturing, low energy consumption, and high mechanical performance. The inclusion of glass fabric enhances impact resistance and allows for structural healing.
