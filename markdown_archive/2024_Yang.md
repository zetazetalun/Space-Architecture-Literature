# Cross-section extraction and model reconstruction of lava tube based on L1-medial skeleton

**Authors:** Qiao Yang, Zhizhong Kang, Teng Hu, Zhen Cao, Chenming Ye, Dongming Liu, Haoxiang Hu, Shuai Shao  
**Journal:** International Journal of Applied Earth Observation and Geoinformation  
**Volume:** 132 (2024) 104062  
**Date:** Received 4 March 2024; Accepted 23 July 2024; Available online 26 July 2024  

## Abstract
Lunar lava tube caves are prime candidates for lunar base site selection due to their geographical structure and potential as natural shelters. However, direct data acquisition from lunar interiors is currently difficult, necessitating the study of terrestrial analogues. This paper explores a method for extracting lava tube parameters and constructing 3D models using LiDAR point cloud data. The approach involves data preprocessing (voxelization), axis construction using an iterative L1-medial skeleton algorithm, cross-sectional feature extraction, and 3D mesh reconstruction. The method achieves high accuracy, with 99% of positional errors falling within 0.15 m, providing technical support for extraterrestrial base planning.

## 1. Introduction
Lava tubes are roofed passages formed by flowing lava. Their presence has been confirmed on the Moon and Mars via gravity detection and satellite imagery (skylights). These natural shelters offer protection from extreme temperature fluctuations, cosmic radiation, and micrometeorites, making them suitable for lunar base construction.

## 2. Methodology
The study employs an approach for lava tube parameter extraction based on the L1-medial skeleton, comprising four steps:
1.  **Data Preprocessing:** Voxelization (0.05 m size) to reduce noise and file size while maintaining internal details.
2.  **Axis Extraction:** A novel iterative algorithm based on the L1-Medial Skeleton to simplify 3D points into a 1D set of cluster centers representing the central axis.
3.  **Cross-section Extraction:** Sampling every 0.5 m along the axis to extract geometric parameters like area, length, and width.
4.  **3D Model Reconstruction:** Building a continuous 3D mesh model by establishing topological relationships between cross-sectional points.

## 3. Results
- **Accuracy:** 99% of extracted cross-sectional points exhibited an error < 0.12 m. 99% of the final model's positional errors were within 0.15 m.
- **Efficiency:** Voxelization reduced the dataset from 6.44 GB to 9 MB, significantly improving computational efficiency.
- **Volume Estimation:** The method allowed for the calculation of internal volumes for different tube segments.

## 4. Discussion and Application to Space Architecture
- **Base Selection:** High-precision models facilitate layout planning and internal deformation monitoring for lunar bases.
- **Structural Integrity:** Internal volume and surface geometry are vital for assessing the feasibility of inflating or sealing tubes for human habitation.
- **Robotic Exploration:** The methodology can be integrated into cave-exploring robots for unmanned data collection on the Moon or Mars.
