# Cross-section extraction and model reconstruction of lava tube based on L1-medial skeleton ## Abstract Lunar lava tube caves, due to their unique geographical structure and potential as shelters, have repeatedly emerged as prime candidates or priority areas during discussions on lunar base site selection. However, obtaining detailed data directly from the interiors of lunar lava tubes poses a significant challenge. This paper explores a method for extracting lava tube parameters and constructing models using LiDAR point cloud data. The approach encompasses four key steps: data preprocessing, axis construction of the lava tube, cross-sectional feature extraction, and 3D model reconstruction. Remarkably, this method achieves a high degree of accuracy, with 99% of the extracted cross-sectional points exhibiting an error of less than 0.12 m, and 99% of the model's positional errors falling within 0.15 m. ## Introduction Lava tubes, also known as lava tunnels, are roofed passages formed by flowing lava. On the Moon and Mars, deep skylights similar to those on Earth's lava tubes have been discovered. These natural shelters offer exceptional protection from the Moon's harsh environmental conditions (extreme temperature fluctuations, cosmic radiation, lunar dust, and meteorites), making them suitable for lunar base construction. ## Research Area The experiment focuses on the Shenyang Cave lava tube in the Jingpo Lake Geopark, China. This terrestrial analogue is relatively dry, resembling more closely the internal environment of lunar lava tubes. ## Methodology 1. Data preprocessing: Voxelization (0.05m size) to remove noise and reduce data volume. 2. Lava tube axis extraction: Iterative algorithm based on the L1-Medial Skeleton to identify the central axis of the irregular tunnel. 3. Extraction of cross-sections: Sampling every 0.5m along the axis, projecting points onto a 2D plane, and using statistical interpolation to fill voids/gaps. 4. Mesh model construction: Establishing topological relationships between points to create a continuous 3D model. ## Results The statistical graph demonstrates excellent reconstruction accuracy for the Jingpo Lake lava tube, with 99% of points falling within a 0.15 m error margin. Calculated internal volumes for surveyed segments: Segment 1 (421,624 m³), Segment 2 (725,942 m³), Segment 3 (614,921 m³). ## Summary and Discussion This methodology provides data support for understanding lava tube morphology and growth trends, which is significant for lunar base construction planning, layout design, and safety assessments.
