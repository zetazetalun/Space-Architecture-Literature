# Cross-section extraction and model reconstruction of lava tube based on L1-medial skeleton

**Journal:** International Journal of Applied Earth Observation and Geoinformation 132 (2024) 104062  
**Authors:** Qiao Yang, Zhizhong Kang, Teng Hu, Zhen Cao, Chenming Ye, Dongming Liu, Haoxiang Hu, Shuai Shao  
**Received:** 4 March 2024; **Revised:** 10 June 2024; **Accepted:** 23 July 2024; **Available online:** 26 July 2024

## Abstract
Lunar lava tube caves, due to their unique geographical structure and potential as shelters, have repeatedly emerged as prime candidates or priority areas during discussions on lunar base site selection. However, obtaining detailed data directly from the interiors of lunar lava tubes poses a significant challenge. This paper explores a method for extracting lava tube parameters and constructing 3D models using LiDAR point cloud data. The approach encompasses data preprocessing, axis construction using the L1-medial skeleton, cross-sectional feature extraction, and 3D model reconstruction. The method achieves high accuracy, with 99% of model positional errors falling within 0.15 m, providing technical support for the study of extraterrestrial lava tube formation and habitat development.

## 1. Introduction
Lava tubes are roofed passages formed by flowing lava. On the Moon and Mars, similar structures have been identified via 'skylights' and gravity detection. These natural shelters offer protection from extreme temperature fluctuations, cosmic radiation, lunar dust, and meteorites, making them suitable for lunar base construction.

## 2. Related Works
### 2.1. State-of-the-art of lava tube detection
Lunar lava tubes are significantly larger and deeper than terrestrial ones, remaining structurally stable despite their volume. They present an opportunity for underground exploration and stable environments for potential settlement.

### 2.2. Construction and application of 3D models
Modeling irregular natural tunnels like lava tubes is challenging compared to artificial tunnels. Current research focuses on cross-section-based and geometry-based modeling to simulate and analyze these 3D environments.

## 3. Research Area
The study uses the Shenyang Cave in Jingpo Lake Geopark, China, as a terrestrial analogue. This region features Holocene volcanic rocks and various lava tubes that are relatively dry, resembling lunar environments.

## 4. Methodology
The workflow includes:
1. **Data Preprocessing:** Voxelization (0.05 m size) to reduce noise and point cloud volume while preserving details. The Cloth Simulation Filter (CSF) algorithm is used for ground segmentation.
2. **Lava Tube Axis Extraction:** Utilizing a novel algorithm based on the L1-Medial Skeleton to simplify the 3D point set into a 1D set of cluster centers.
3. **Cross-section Extraction:** Sampling every 0.5 m along the axis and projecting points onto a 2D plane.
4. **3D Mesh Model Reconstruction:** Creating topological relationships between cross-sectional points to form a continuous 3D mesh.

## 5. Results and Discussion
### 5.1. Accuracy Evaluation
The Hausdorff distance was used to calculate similarity between the model and the original point cloud. 99% of points fell within a 0.15 m error margin.

### 5.2. Level of Detail (LoD)
The method allows for flexible adjustment of cross-section density. Simplified models (low LoD) are suitable for equipment installation planning, while high LoD models are used for deformation monitoring.

## 6. Summary and Discussion
The methodology provides a robust tool for extraterrestrial exploration. High-accuracy 3D models can mitigate point cloud gaps from rockfalls and support layout design for future lunar bases. LiDAR-equipped robots could automate this data collection on the Moon.
