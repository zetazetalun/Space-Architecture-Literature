# 3D printed sulfur-regolith concrete performance evaluation for waterless extraterrestrial robotic construction

**Authors:** Ilerioluwa Giwa, Mary Dempsey, Michael Fiske, Ali Kazemian  
**Journal:** Automation in Construction  
**Volume:** 165 (2024) 105571  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.autcon.2024.105571

## Abstract
By leveraging the capabilities of construction 3D printing, building structures in harsh extraterrestrial environments is conceivable. Sulfur concrete is a waterless construction material that offers great potential to replace Portland cement concrete (PCC) in extraterrestrial construction. This study characterizes the fresh-state properties, mechanical properties, and microstructure of 3D printed sulfur-regolith concrete (SRC) specimens intended for Mars and Moon applications.

## Materials and Methods
- **Binder:** Elemental sulfur powder (99.9% purity).
- **Aggregate:** Mars Global Simulant (MGS-1), high-fidelity basaltic soil simulant.
- **Additive:** Dicyclopentadiene (DCPD) used as a chemical modifier.
- **Printing System:** Custom gantry-type high-temperature extrusion system.
- **Testing Conditions:** Room temperature and vacuum chamber (0.07 Torr) at 20°C and 55°C.

## Key Results
1. **Strength Development:** SRC specimens gained 85% of ultimate strength in 12 hours, significantly faster than PCC.
2. **Temperature Sensitivity:** Lower substrate temperatures improved shape stability but reduced flexural strength by up to 53% due to cold joints.
3. **DCPD Modification:** Modification with 10% DCPD increased compressive strength by 44% and flexural strength by 34%. It also reduced the sublimation rate in vacuum by an order of magnitude.
4. **Vacuum Performance:** SRC outperformed PCC in vacuum. PCC experienced significant moisture loss and strength degradation, while SRC maintained integrity and continued strength development.
5. **Recyclability:** SRC and MSRC specimens were successfully remelted and reprinted without significant loss in flowability or mechanical strength, demonstrating cradle-to-cradle sustainability.
