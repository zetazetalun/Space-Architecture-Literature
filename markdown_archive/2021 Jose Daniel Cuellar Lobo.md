# Building Information Modelling- (BIM-) Based Generative Design for Drywall Installation Planning in Prefabricated Construction\n\n**Authors:** Jose Daniel Cuellar Lobo, Zhen Lei, Hexu Liu, Hong Xian Li, and SangHyeok Han\n**Publication:** Advances in Civil Engineering, Vol 2021, Article ID 6638236\n**Date:** July 14, 2021\n**Publisher:** Hindawi\n\n## Abstract\nIn prefabricated construction, components are manufactured off-site. This study proposes a generative design (GD) approach utilizing BIM and optimization algorithms to improve drywall installation layout efficiency. The framework includes a decision support module considering environmental, cost, and aesthetic aspects. Results showed design improvements of 37.5% for environmental factors, 7% for cost, and 54% for aesthetics.\n\n## 1. Introduction\nPrefabricated construction improves efficiency but requires precise design. Sheathing materials (gypsum, plywood) often generate scrap waste due to standard sizes. This research develops a BIM-based approach to optimize sheathing layouts to reduce waste and evaluate alternatives through a fuzzy analytical hierarchy process (AHP).\n\n## 2. Methodology\nThe methodology consists of five modules:\n1. **Data Extraction:** Geometric information (coordinates) and material properties are extracted from BIM (Revit) to XML.\n2. **Simulation-Based Design Algorithm:** Generates multiple feasible layout alternatives based on design rules (orientation, sequence, stud alignment).\n3. **Heuristic Optimization:** A greedy algorithm locally optimizes material waste for each design alternative by reusing cuts.\n4. **Decision-Making Support:** Uses fuzzy AHP to rank alternatives based on three criteria:\n   - **Environmental:** Material waste percentage.\n   - **Cost:** Material, installation, and waste processing costs.\n   - **Aesthetics:** Total length of joints (seams).\n5. **Visualization:** Optimized layouts are returned to Dynamo/Revit for visualization.\n\n## 3. Implementation and Case Study\nA two-story wood residential house with 53 wood frames was used. Design rules included vertical/horizontal orientation checks, aligning edges with studs, staggered seams, and avoiding door/window openings for joints.\n\n## 4. Evaluation and Discussion\nThe model achieved a minimum of 10% material waste. Compared to median results of non-optimized simulations, the best scenarios achieved significant reductions across all management criteria. Traditional design methods are time-consuming; GD allows rapid exploration of multiple alternatives.\n\n## 5. Conclusions\nThe approach is effective for planning drywall in prefabricated construction. Improvements include a 37.5% reduction in waste, 7% in cost, and 54% in seam length. Future work includes real-time field data integration and supply chain analysis.
