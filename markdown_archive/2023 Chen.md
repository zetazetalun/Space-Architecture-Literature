# Genetic Algorithm–Based Multiobjective Optimization for 3D Printable Design of a Double-Shell Lunar Habitat Structure

**Authors:** Quanyao Chen, Yuyue Gao, Lieyun Ding, Cheng Zhou, Wenbin Han, Yan Zhou, and Yusheng Shi  
**Publisher:** Journal of Aerospace Engineering (ASCE)  
**DOI:** 10.1061/JAEEEZ.ASENG-4755  
**Date:** July 2023

## Abstract
The establishment of lunar habitats is significant for humans to explore the Moon. Constructing lunar habitats through additive manufacturing using lunar in situ resource is a promising solution. This study proposed a parametric design and multiobjective optimization approach based on genetic algorithms for the structure of lunar habitats. Optimization objectives included material mass consumption, space efficiency, and resistance ability for extreme extraterrestrial environments. Finite-element analysis (FEA) and 3D printing of scaled-down models were used to validate the optimized designs.

## Methodology
### 1. Parametric Modeling
A conceptual double-shell structure named 'Lunar Ampullae' was designed using Grasshopper and Karamba 3D. The structure consists of an inner shell, middle rib, outer shell, and catenary roof. Nine design parameters were identified (e.g., surface major/minor axes, thickness, heights).

### 2. Optimization Goals
- **Maximize available volume (V)**
- **Minimize mass (W)**
- **Maximize thermal insulation performance (T)**

### 3. Optimization Algorithms Compared
- NSGA-II (Nondominated Sorting Genetic Algorithm II)
- PESA-II (Pareto Envelope-Based Selection Algorithm II)
- SPEA2 (Strength Pareto Evolutionary Algorithm 2)

## Results and Findings
- **Hypervolume (HV) Index:** NSGA-II demonstrated the best performance with an HV index of 0.8022 when PC = 0.9 and PM = 0.06.
- **Structural Performance:** The optimal solution (No. 25) from the Pareto set was selected based on FEA. It showed a maximum tensile stress of 3.33 MPa and a maximum displacement of 0.0272 cm under internal pressure (0.1 MPa) and lunar gravity (1/6 G).
- **Physical Validation:** A scaled-down model was successfully printed using concrete additive manufacturing to demonstrate printability.

## Conclusions
The GA-based multiobjective optimization framework effectively balances competing requirements for lunar habitat design. The double-shell configuration provides redundant protection and improved thermal insulation, making it a viable candidate for future robotic ISRU construction.
