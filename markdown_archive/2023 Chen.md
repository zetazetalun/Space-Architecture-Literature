# Genetic Algorithm–Based Multiobjective Optimization for 3D Printable Design of a Double-Shell Lunar Habitat Structure

**Authors:** Quanyao Chen, Yuyue Gao, Lieyun Ding, Cheng Zhou, Wenbin Han, Yan Zhou, and Yusheng Shi
**Publication:** Journal of Aerospace Engineering, Vol. 36, Issue 6, 2023
**DOI:** 10.1061/JAEEEZ.ASENG-4755

## Abstract
The establishment of lunar habitats is significant for humans to explore the Moon. Constructing lunar habitats through additive manufacturing using lunar in situ resource is a promising solution. This study proposed a parametric design and multiobjective optimization approach based on genetic algorithms for the structure of lunar habitats. Three kinds of genetic algorithm–based multiobjective optimization methods (NSGA-II, SPEA2, PESA-II) were used and compared. The optimized structure was determined based on maximum stress and displacement via Finite Element Analysis (FEA), and a scaled physical model was produced through additive manufacturing.

## Methodology

### 1. Parametric Modeling
The proposed habitat, named "Lunar Ampullae," is a 3D-printable double-shell structure consisting of:
- **Inner Shell**
- **Middle Rib** (filled with braided ribbed parts)
- **Outer Shell**
- **Catenary Roof**

The structure is defined by nine design parameters including surface major/minor axes, cross-section axes, shell thickness ($t_i, t_o$), and the distance between shells ($d$).

### 2. Optimization Objectives
Three conflicting objectives were selected for optimization:
- **Maximize Available Volume ($V$):** Based on crew requirements (min 12 $m^3$/crew).
- **Minimize Material Mass ($W$):** To reduce construction/transport costs.
- **Maximize Thermal Insulation ($T$):** Represented by heat transfer thermal resistance to withstand lunar temperature cycles.

### 3. Optimization Algorithms
The study compared three Multi-Objective Parametric Tools (MOPT):
- **NSGA-II** (Non-dominated Sorting Genetic Algorithm II)
- **SPEA2** (Strength Pareto Evolutionary Algorithm 2)
- **PESA-II** (Pareto Envelope-based Selection Algorithm II)

## Key Findings
- **Algorithm Performance:** NSGA-II demonstrated the best diversity and convergence performance for the three optimization objectives compared to SPEA2 and PESA-II.
- **Structural Analysis:** Model No. 25 was selected as the optimal design from the Pareto solution set, exhibiting a maximum tensile stress of 3.33 MPa and a maximum displacement of $2.72 \times 10^{-2}$ cm under one standard atmosphere internal pressure and 1/6 G gravity.
- **Validation:** A partially scaled-down physical model was successfully 3D printed using concrete-based additive manufacturing to demonstrate the feasibility of the "Lunar Ampullae" geometry.
