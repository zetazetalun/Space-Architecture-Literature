# Genetic Algorithm–Based Multiobjective Optimization for 3D Printable Design of a Double-Shell Lunar Habitat Structure

**Authors:** Quanyao Chen, Yuyue Gao, Lieyun Ding, Cheng Zhou, Wenbin Han, Yan Zhou, and Yusheng Shi  
**Journal:** Journal of Aerospace Engineering, Vol. 36, No. 6, 2023.  
**DOI:** 10.1061/JAEEEZ.ASENG-4755  

## Abstract
The establishment of lunar habitats is significant for humans to explore the Moon and carry out scientific work. Constructing lunar habitats through additive manufacturing using lunar in situ resource is a promising solution. This study proposed a parametric design and multiobjective optimization approach based on genetic algorithms for the structure of lunar habitats. Structural shape was translated into design parameters, which were optimized during the design phase. The optimization objectives were determined considering the material mass consumption, space efficiency, and resistance ability for extreme extraterrestrial environment of the structure. Three kinds of genetic algorithm–based multiobjective optimization methods were used and compared to optimize the structural parameters. The optimized Pareto solution set containing multiple solutions based on the hypervolume index are obtained. Finite-model analysis was performed on the structures of the Pareto solution set and the optimal structure was determined based on the results of maximum stress and displacement. Finally, partially scaled-down physical models were produced through additive manufacturing to demonstrate the optimized double-shell habitat structure.

## Introduction
The next target of human exploration in space is the Moon. To realize exploration projects, humans need to establish habitats. Robotic construction based on in situ resource utilization (ISRU) is considered the most feasible way due to transportation costs. Additive manufacturing (3D printing) offers advantages such as fast construction speed and high accuracy. This paper focuses on the parametric design and optimization of a 3D-printable double-shell lunar habitat named "Lunar Ampullae."

## Methodology
The proposed framework involves three parts: parametric model creation (Rhino & Grasshopper), optimization methods (Genetic Algorithms), and additive manufacturing validation.

### Design Objectives
1.  **Maximize Available Volume (V):** To ensure habitable space (aiming for ~12 m³/crew).
2.  **Minimize Mass (W):** To reduce construction material consumption.
3.  **Maximize Thermal Insulation (T):** Represented by heat transfer thermal resistance.

## Optimization Methods
Three Multi-Objective Parametric Tools (MOPT) were compared:
*   **NSGA-II:** Nondominated Sorting Genetic Algorithm II
*   **SPEA2:** Strength Pareto Evolutionary Algorithm 2
*   **PESA-II:** Pareto Envelope-based Selection Algorithm II

## Results

### Comparison of Algorithms
NSGA-II demonstrated superior performance in terms of diversity and convergence (Hypervolume index) and had the lowest time cost.

### Finite Element Analysis (FEA)
FEA was performed using the "Karamba 3D" plug-in for Grasshopper. The structure was tested under internal pressure (0.1 MPa) and lunar gravity (1/6 G). The 25th structure model was selected as optimal, exhibiting the smallest maximum stress (3.33 MPa) and displacement (2.72 × 10⁻² cm).

## Conclusion
The study successfully demonstrates a method for optimizing the design of lunar habitats for 3D printing. The NSGA-II algorithm proved most effective for the multi-objective optimization of the "Lunar Ampullae" structure. A scaled physical model confirmed the printability of the design.
