# Genetic Algorithm–Based Multiobjective Optimization for 3D Printable Design of a Double-Shell Lunar Habitat Structure

**Authors:** Quanyao Chen, Yuyue Gao, Lieyun Ding, Cheng Zhou, Wenbin Han, Yan Zhou, and Yusheng Shi  
**Published in:** Journal of Aerospace Engineering (ASCE), Volume 36, Issue 6 (2023)  
**DOI:** 10.1061/JAEEEZ.ASENG-4755

## Abstract
The establishment of lunar habitats is significant for humans to explore the Moon. Constructing habitats through additive manufacturing using lunar in situ resources (ISRU) is a promising solution. This study proposes a parametric design and multiobjective optimization approach based on genetic algorithms for a "Lunar Ampullae" structure. Optimization objectives include material mass consumption, space efficiency, and thermal insulation performance. The study uses the NSGA-II algorithm to find an optimal Pareto solution set, validated through finite-element analysis (FEA) and a 3D-printed scaled physical model.

## Introduction
Robotic construction based on ISRU is identified as the most feasible way for lunar development due to high transportation costs. Additive manufacturing offers advantages in speed and accuracy. The paper emphasizes that structural design must account for extreme lunar conditions: low gravity, high vacuum, temperature variations, radiation, and meteorite impacts.

## Methodology
The "Lunar Ampullae" is a double-shell structure designed for high internal space utilization and uniform force distribution. 

### Optimization Parameters
Nine parameters define the structure (Table 1 summary):
- **Surface major/minor axes (A, B):** [12,14]m and [4,6]m
- **Cross section axes (a, b):** [1,3]m
- **Distance (d):** Distance between inner and outer shell [0.4, 0.6]m
- **Heights (Hb, Ht):** Bottom and top cut heights
- **Thickness (ti, to):** Inner and outer shell thicknesses [0.2, 0.3]m

### Optimization Framework
1. **Parametric Model Creation:** Developed in Rhino/Grasshopper.
2. **Optimization Algorithms:** Comparison of NSGA-II, SPEA2, and PESA-II.
3. **Evaluation Indicators:** Hypervolume (HV) index to assess convergence and diversity.
4. **FEA Analysis:** Performed using the "Karamba 3D" plug-in, subjecting the structure to 0.1 MPa internal pressure and 1/6 G gravity.

## Key Results
- **NSGA-II Performance:** NSGA-II with PC=0.9 and PM=0.06 demonstrated the best diversity and convergence.
- **Optimal Solution:** The 25th structure model from the Pareto set showed the smallest maximum stress (3.33 MPa) and displacement (2.72 × 10⁻² cm).
- **Physical Validation:** A scaled-down model was successfully printed using concrete additive manufacturing to prove constructability.

## Conclusion
The study successfully integrates parametric design with multiobjective optimization for space architecture. Future work will focus on incorporating thermal stress analysis, seismic performance (moonquakes), and more advanced ISRU material simulants like geopolymer concrete.
