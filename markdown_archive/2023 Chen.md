# Genetic Algorithm–Based Multiobjective Optimization for 3D Printable Design of a Double-Shell Lunar Habitat Structure

**Authors:** Quanyao Chen, Yuyue Gao, Lieyun Ding, Cheng Zhou, Wenbin Han, Yan Zhou, and Yusheng Shi
**Published in:** Journal of Aerospace Engineering, Vol. 36, No. 6 (2023)
**DOI:** 10.1061/JAEEEZ.ASENG-4755

## Abstract
The establishment of lunar habitats is significant for humans to explore the Moon and carry out scientific work. Constructing lunar habitats through additive manufacturing using lunar in situ resource is a promising solution. This study proposed a parametric design and multiobjective optimization approach based on genetic algorithms for the structure of lunar habitats. Structural shape was translated into design parameters, which were optimized during the design phase. The optimization objectives were determined considering the material mass consumption, space efficiency, and resistance ability for extreme extraterrestrial environment of the structure.

## Introduction
Robotic construction based on in situ resource utilization (ISRU) appears to be the most feasible way for lunar habitation. Construction through additive manufacturing has advantages including fast construction speed and high forming accuracy. This study applies a genetic algorithm (GA)–based multiobjective optimization (MOPT) approach to the structural design of a 3D printable double-shell lunar habitat named 'Lunar Ampullae.'

## Methodology
### Parametric Modeling
The 'Lunar Ampullae' is a double-shell structure designed for additive manufacturing using lunar ISRU. The design parameters include:
- Surface major and minor axes (A, B)
- Cross section major and minor axes (a, b)
- Distance between shells (d)
- Heights of top and bottom endpoints (Ht, Hb)
- Inner and outer shell thickness (ti, to)

### Optimization Objectives
1. **Maximize Available Volume (V)**: Integrating cross-sectional areas between boundary heights.
2. **Minimize Mass (W)**: Calculated based on regolith density and surface area/thickness.
3. **Maximize Thermal Insulation (T)**: Represented by heat transfer thermal resistance.

### Optimization Algorithms
Three GA-based methods were compared: NSGA-II, SPEA2, and PESA-II. NSGA-II was found to have the best diversity and convergence performance.

## Results and Analysis
### Optimal Solution Sets
The NSGA-II algorithm with a population of 100 and 1,000 iterations identified the Pareto solution set. It was observed that mass, volume, and thermal insulation are positively correlated.

### Finite Element Analysis (FEA)
FEA was performed using 'Karamba 3D' on the structures in the Pareto set. The 25th structure model was selected as the optimal solution because it exhibited the smallest maximum stress and displacement under 0.1 MPa internal pressure and 1/6 Earth gravity.

### Additive Manufacturing Experiment
A scaled-down physical model was printed using putty powder and a robotic arm to demonstrate construction potential. The model demonstrated that the 'Lunar Ampullae' design is printable without additional supports in its main sections.

## Conclusion
1. NSGA-II provides superior Pareto solutions for lunar habitat design compared to other GA variants.
2. The double-shell structure offers enhanced protection and insulation.
3. Additive manufacturing of complex parametric designs using ISRU is feasible and structurally sound for lunar environments.
