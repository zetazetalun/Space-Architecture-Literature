# Genetic Algorithm–Based Multiobjective Optimization for 3D Printable Design of a Double-Shell Lunar Habitat Structure

**Authors:** Quanyao Chen, Yuyue Gao, Lieyun Ding, Cheng Zhou, Wenbin Han, Yan Zhou, and Yusheng Shi  
**Publication:** Journal of Aerospace Engineering, Vol. 36, No. 6, 2023  
**DOI:** 10.1061/JAEEEZ.ASENG-4755

## Abstract
The establishment of lunar habitats is significant for humans to explore the Moon. Constructing lunar habitats through additive manufacturing using lunar in situ resource is a promising solution. This study proposed a parametric design and multiobjective optimization approach based on genetic algorithms for the structure of lunar habitats. The optimization objectives were determined considering the material mass consumption, space efficiency, and resistance ability for extreme extraterrestrial environment. Three kinds of genetic algorithm–based multiobjective optimization methods (NSGA-II, SPEA2, and PESA-II) were used and compared. The optimal structure was determined based on finite-element analysis (FEA) of the Pareto solution set. Finally, partially scaled-down physical models were produced through additive manufacturing.

## Methodology

### 1. Parametric Modeling
The proposed habitat, named "Lunar Ampullae," is a double-shell structure including four parts: inner shell, middle rib, outer shell, and catenary roof. The shape is defined by nine parameters:
- Surface major axis (A)
- Surface minor axis (B)
- Cross section major axis (a)
- Cross section minor axis (b)
- Distance (d)
- Height of the bottom endpoint (Hb)
- Height of the top endpoint (Ht)
- Inner shell thickness (ti)
- Outer shell thickness (to)

### 2. Optimization Objectives
- **Objective 1 (Maximize Volume):** To ensure maximum habitable space (V).
- **Objective 2 (Minimize Mass):** To reduce transport and construction costs (W).
- **Objective 3 (Maximize Thermal Insulation):** To improve performance in extreme thermal environments (T).

## Results and Discussion

### Comparison of Algorithms
| Method | Hypervolume (Mean) | Time cost (s) (Mean) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| SPEA2 | 0.7485 | 477.476 |
| PESA-II | 0.5465 | 613.162 |
| **NSGA-II** | **0.8022** | **77.685** |

NSGA-II demonstrated the best performance regarding convergence, diversity of the solution set, and computational efficiency.

### Structural Performance (Solution 25)
- **Max Tensile Stress:** 3.33 MPa
- **Max Displacement:** 2.72 x 10^-2 cm
- **Loading Conditions:** Internal pressure (0.1 MPa) and lunar gravity (1/6g).

## Conclusion
The study provides a framework for integrating parametric design with metaheuristic optimization for extraterrestrial construction. The double-shell structure offers redundancy and space for radiation shielding materials between layers. The physical model demonstration confirms the printability of the complex geometric forms derived from the optimization process.
