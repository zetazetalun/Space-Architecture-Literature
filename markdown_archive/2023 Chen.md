# Genetic Algorithm–Based Multiobjective Optimization for 3D Printable Design of a Double-Shell Lunar Habitat Structure

**Authors:** Quanyao Chen, Yuyue Gao, Lieyun Ding, Cheng Zhou, Wenbin Han, Yan Zhou, and Yusheng Shi
**Journal:** Journal of Aerospace Engineering, ASCE
**Year:** 2023
**DOI:** 10.1061/JAEEEZ.ASENG-4755

## Abstract
The establishment of lunar habitats is significant for humans to explore the Moon. Constructing lunar habitats through additive manufacturing using lunar in situ resource is a promising solution. This study proposed a parametric design and multiobjective optimization approach based on genetic algorithms for the structure of lunar habitats. Structural shape was translated into design parameters, which were optimized during the design phase. The optimization objectives were material mass consumption, space efficiency, and thermal insulation performance. Finite-model analysis was performed on the optimized structures, and a scaled-down physical model was produced through additive manufacturing.

## Introduction
Robotic construction based on in situ resource utilization (ISRU) appears to be the most feasible way to build lunar habitats. Sintered lunar soil brick assembly and additive manufacturing are two primary ISRU construction methods. This study introduces 'Lunar Ampullae,' a double-shell habitat structure designed using parametric tools (Grasshopper) and optimized via Genetic Algorithms (GA) to withstand extreme lunar environments (low gravity, vacuum, temperature swings, and radiation).

## Methodology
### Parametric Modeling
The 'Lunar Ampullae' is a double-shell structure including an inner shell, middle rib, outer shell, and catenary roof. It is defined by nine parameters:
- **A, B:** Surface major/minor axes
- **a, b:** Cross section major/minor axes
- **d:** Distance between inner and outer shells
- **Hb, Ht:** Heights of bottom and top endpoints
- **ti, to:** Inner and outer shell thicknesses

### Optimization Objectives
1. **Maximize Volume (V):** Ensuring habitable space for astronauts.
2. **Minimize Mass (W):** Reducing material consumption and transportation/robotic labor costs.
3. **Maximize Thermal Resistance (T):** Enhancing thermal insulation against lunar temperature cycles.

### Optimization Framework
The study utilized the Non-dominated Sorting Genetic Algorithm II (NSGA-II) and compared it with PESA-II and SPEA2. Finite-element analysis (FEA) was performed using the 'Karamba 3D' plug-in to evaluate structural integrity under internal pressure (0.1 MPa) and lunar gravity (1/6g).

## Results and Discussion
### Comparison of Algorithms
- **NSGA-II:** Demonstrated the best convergence and diversity with a hypervolume (HV) index of 0.8022.
- **PESA-II:** Produced the largest mean volume but had lower overall performance indices.
- **SPEA2:** Showed the smallest mean mass (material efficiency) but was outperformed in convergence time by NSGA-II.

### Optimal Solution (No. 25)
From the Pareto solution set, Solution 25 was selected as the optimal structural design:
- **Maximum Tensile Stress:** 3.33 MPa
- **Maximum Displacement:** 2.72 × 10⁻² cm
- **Structural Safety:** These values are significantly below the ultimate bearing capacity of concrete/regolith composites, indicating low risk of structural failure.

### Additive Manufacturing Verification
A scaled-down model was printed using putty powder to demonstrate the printability of the double-shell geometry. The printing path was planned via Simplify3D, and the structure was realized using a robotic arm with a 2.6 m span.

## Conclusion
The study provides a robust framework for combining parametric design and multiobjective optimization for extraterrestrial structures. Future work will include thermal stress analysis and radiation shielding optimization.
