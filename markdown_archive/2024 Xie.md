# The first biological experiment on lunar surface for Humankind: Device and results

**Authors:** GengXin Xie, JingHang Ding, YuanXun Zhang, MaoZhi Ren, Dan Qiu, Xi Wang
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 214 (2024) 216–223
**Published:** Elsevier Ltd (Available online October 17, 2023)

## Abstract
The Bioregenerative Life Support System (BLSS) is an indispensable part of extraterrestrial bases. The Chinese Chang’e 4 lunar lander carried a micro-ecosystem called Biological Experiment Payload (BEP), developed by Chongqing University. The payload (2.608 kg) contained Cotton seeds, Potato seeds, Arabidopsis seeds, Rape seeds, Fly eggs, and Yeast. On January 3, 2019, BEP arrived on the back of the Moon. After 22 h of watering, cotton seed germination was captured. This experiment demonstrated that plants could grow on the lunar surface under intense radiation, low gravity (1/6 g), and prolonged light using sunlight for photosynthesis.

## 1. Introduction
Building lunar bases requires self-sufficiency due to high transportation costs. BLSS, an artificial ecosystem of producers, consumers, and decomposers, is a critical technology. This paper introduces the BEP device, summarizes results from the lunar surface, and provides an outlook for future lunar bases.

## 2. The Biological Experiment Payload (BEP)
BEP was hung independently inside the Chang’e-4 lander. It featured:
- **Structure:** Cylindrical aluminum shell (173 × 198 mm).
- **Thermal Control:** Multilayer insulation and thermoelectric coolers (TEC) to maintain temperatures between -52 °C and 38 °C.
- **Lighting:** Light-guiding fibers to introduce natural lunar sunlight.
- **Sealing:** Leakage rate < 1.9 × 10⁻⁵ Pa·cm²/s.
- **Biological Seedbed:** Contained producers (Cotton, Potato, Arabidopsis, Rape), consumers (Drosophila), and decomposers (Yeast).

## 3. Preparatory Works and Simulations
Ground tests included light conduction, heat transfer, and radiation tolerance. Simulations showed that under minimum light, 1.91 × 10²⁰ light quanta per hour entered the chamber, sufficient for plant growth.

## 4. Results of Biological Experiments on the Lunar Surface
- **Landing:** January 3, 2019, Von Kármán crater.
- **Germination:** Cotton seed germinated 22 h after watering (vs. 53 h on Earth).
- **Morphology:** Lunar seedlings showed curved stems, fat nodes, and wrapped leaves.
- **Duration:** The payload worked for 1300 h over 5 lunar days.
- **Environmental Data:** Internal pressure ranged from 98 to 104 kPa; temperatures were 16.7–36.5 °C during active periods.

## 5. Discussion
The experiment proved that cultivation on the Moon is possible. Key design successes included the use of water-soluble fiber to secure the substrate and natural light guiding. Challenges remain regarding extreme night temperatures and the need for polar locations to ensure continuous sunlight for sustainable bases.
