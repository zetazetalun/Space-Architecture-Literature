# The first biological experiment on lunar surface for Humankind: Device and results

**Authors:** GengXin Xie, JingHang Ding, YuanXun Zhang, MaoZhi Ren, Dan Qiu, Xi Wang  
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 214 (2024) 216–223  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.actaastro.2023.10.023

## Abstract
The Bioregenerative Life Support System (BLSS) is an indispensable part of extraterrestrial bases. The Chinese Chang’e 4 lunar lander carried a micro-ecosystem called Biological Experiment Payload (BEP) to the back of the Moon to assess effects of low gravity, radiation, and light. The payload contained seeds of cotton, potato, arabidopsis, and rape, plus fly eggs and yeast. Results showed cotton seeds germinated faster on the Moon (22 h) than on Earth (53 h) and demonstrated that plants could grow using sunlight from space for photosynthesis under lunar conditions.

## 1. Introduction
Building a lunar base requires self-sufficiency due to high transportation costs. BLSS, an artificial ecosystem with producers, consumers, and decomposers, is a critical technology. Previous experiments (BIOS-3, Lunar Palace 1) occurred on Earth; the BEP is the first to test these systems in the actual lunar environment (1/6 g, high radiation).

## 2. The Biological Experiment Payload (BEP)
- **Dimensions:** 173 x 198 mm (Diameter x Height)
- **Mass:** 2.608 kg
- **Internal Volume:** 0.82 L (0.42 L bio-activity space)
- **Components:** Biological seedbed, watering system, lighting system (light-guiding fibers), monitor system (CCD cameras), and temperature control system (semiconductor coolers and thermistors).

### 2.1. Biological Seedbed
Producers included Brassica napus, Potato, Cotton, and Arabidopsis. Drosophila acted as consumers, and Saccharomyces served as food for the flies. The substrate was vermiculite.

## 4. Results of biological experiments on the lunar surface
- **Landing:** Jan 3, 2019, Von K&#225;rm‒n crater.
- **Germination:** Cotton seed germinated 22 h after watering.
- **Morphology:** Lunar cotton seedlings showed curved stems, fat streamlined nodes close to the substrate, and curled leaves.
- **Survival:** Seedlings survived the lunar night (-52&#176;C internal) and remained green initially upon waking, unlike terrestrial controls.
- **Operational Life:** The BEP worked for 1300 h over five lunar days.

## 5. Discussion
The experiment proved that plant cultivation on the Moon is possible. Key observations include the use of natural sunlight via fiber optics to reduce energy consumption and the surprisingly fast germination rate. Future ISRU research must address soil improvement, using lunar rocks for construction, and leveraging lava tubes.
