# The first biological experiment on lunar surface for Humankind: Device and results

**Authors:** GengXin Xie, JingHang Ding, YuanXun Zhang, MaoZhi Ren, Dan Qiu, Xi Wang
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 214 (2024) 216–223
**Published:** 17 October 2023

## Abstract
The Bioregenerative Life Support System (BLSS) is an indispensable part of extraterrestrial bases. The Chinese Chang’e 4 lunar lander carried a micro-ecosystem called Biological Experiment Payload (BEP) to the back of the Moon. This payload (2.608 kg, 198 mm height, 173 mm diameter) contained cotton seeds, potato seeds, Arabidopsis seeds, rape seeds, fly eggs, and yeast. The experiment demonstrated that plants could germinate and grow on the lunar surface under low gravity (1/6 g) and intense radiation using natural sunlight.

## 1. Introduction
Building a lunar base requires self-sufficiency due to high transportation costs. BLSS is critical for supplying food, oxygen, and water. While previous experiments occurred on the ISS or in Earth-based facilities (BIOS-3, Lunar Palace 1), this mission represents the first biological experiment on a low-gravity planetary surface.

## 2. The Biological Experiment Payload (BEP)
### 2.1. Device Specifications
- **Weight:** 2.608 kg
- **Dimensions:** 173 mm (diameter) × 198 mm (height)
- **Biological Chamber:** 128 mm (diameter) × 72 mm (height)
- **Air Leakage Rate:** < 1.9 × 10⁻⁵ Pa·cm²/s
- **Power Limit:** 15 W

### 2.2. Components
- **Producers:** Cotton, Potato, Arabidopsis, and Rape seeds.
- **Consumers:** Drosophila (fruit fly) eggs.
- **Decomposers:** Yeast (Saccharomyces).
- **Substrate:** Vermiculite.
- **Watering System:** 18 ml of water stored in storage bags with a thermal valve (petroleum jelly).
- **Lighting:** Natural sunlight guided via fiber optics.

## 3. Ground Testing
Extensive ground simulations were conducted to test temperature tolerance, radiation resistance, and the 90-day diapause (dormancy) required during the pre-launch and flight phases. Results showed that humidity control and pre-freezing successfully delayed development for 4 months.

## 4. Results on the Lunar Surface
- **Landing Site:** Von Karman crater, January 3, 2019.
- **Germination:** Cotton seed germinated 22 hours after watering on the Moon (vs. 53 hours on Earth).
- **Morphology:** Lunar cotton seedlings showed curved stems, fat nodes, and curled leaves.
- **Survival:** Seedlings remained green and upright after surviving the lunar night (-52°C internal temperature), whereas terrestrial controls died.
- **Duration:** The BEP worked for 1300 hours over 5 lunar days, sending back >600 photos.

## 5. Conclusion
The experiment proved that plant cultivation is possible on the Moon. Future research should address long-term survival through the lunar night and the effects of intense radiation on health safety.
