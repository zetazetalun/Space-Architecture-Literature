# The first biological experiment on lunar surface for Humankind: Device and results

**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 214 (2024) 216–223  
**Authors:** GengXin Xie, JingHang Ding, YuanXun Zhang, MaoZhi Ren, Dan Qiu, Xi Wang

## Abstract
The Bioregenerative Life Support System (BLSS) is an indispensable part of extraterrestrial bases as it can provide sufficient food, fresh air, recycle waste, and create a safe environment for humans. The Chinese Chang’e 4 lunar lander carried a micro-ecosystem called Biological Experiment Payload (BEP) to assess the effects of low gravity, intense radiation, and light on the lunar surface. Cotton seeds, Potato seeds, Arabidopsis seeds, Rape seeds, Fly eggs, Yeast, and 18 ml of water were placed in the BEP. The experiment demonstrated that plants could grow on the lunar surface using sunlight from space to power photosynthesis.

## 1. Introduction
Building a lunar base requires self-sufficiency due to high transportation costs. BLSS is an artificial ecosystem consisting of producers, consumers, and decomposers following Earth's biosphere principles. This paper introduces the BEP device and summarizes the results of the first human biological experiment on the lunar surface.

## 2. The Biological Experiment Payload (BEP)
*   **Dimensions:** 173 × 198 mm (Diameter × Height).
*   **Mass:** 2.608 kg.
*   **Total Volume:** 0.82 L (0.42 L bio-activity space).
*   **Sealing:** Air leakage rate less than $1.9 \times 10^{-5} \text{ Pa} \cdot \text{cm}^2/\text{s}$.
*   **Substrate:** Vermiculite (mineral).
*   **Monitoring:** CCD cameras, temperature and pressure sensors.
*   **Lighting System:** Light-guiding fibers to utilize natural lunar sunlight.

## 3. Key Results
*   **Germination:** Cotton seeds germinated 22 hours after watering on the lunar surface, significantly faster than the 53 hours observed in ground control experiments.
*   **Morphology:** Seedlings on the Moon showed curved stems, fat-flowing nodes close to the substrate, and curled leaves that did not unfold.
*   **Survivability:** Seedlings survived the lunar night temperatures (reaching $-52^\circ\text{C}$ inside the payload) and remained green, whereas ground controls died under simulated conditions.
*   **Duration:** The experiment worked for 1300 hours over five lunar days before decommissioning.

## 4. Discussion and ISRU
The study notes that utilizing natural light reduces system energy usage. Future research directions include improving lunar soil for cultivation, utilizing lunar rocks as construction materials, and using lunar lava tube caves for building bases.
