# The first biological experiment on lunar surface for Humankind: Device and results

**Authors:** GengXin Xie, JingHang Ding, YuanXun Zhang, MaoZhi Ren, Dan Qiu, Xi Wang
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 214 (2024) 216–223
**Published:** October 2023 (Online), 2024 (Volume)

## Abstract
The Bioregenerative Life Support System (BLSS) is an indispensable part of extraterrestrial bases. The Chinese Chang’e 4 lunar lander carried a micro-ecosystem called Biological Experiment Payload (BEP) to the back of the Moon to assess the effects of low gravity, intense radiation, and light on living things. The payload (2.608 kg) contained cotton, potato, Arabidopsis, and rape seeds, plus fly eggs and yeast. Results showed that cotton seeds germinated faster on the Moon (22 h) than on Earth (53 h) and demonstrated that plants could survive extreme temperature drops during the lunar night if properly managed.

## 1. Introduction
Building a lunar base requires self-sufficiency due to high transportation costs. BLSS, using producers, consumers, and decomposers, is a key technology for food, oxygen, and water supply. While facilities like BIOS-3 and Biosphere-2 have tested these on Earth, the effects of 1/6 gravity and lunar radiation remained uncertain.

## 2. The Biological Experiment Payload (BEP)
### 2.1. Specifications
- **Weight:** 2.608 kg
- **Dimensions:** 173 mm (Diameter) × 198 mm (Height)
- **Inside Space:** 0.82 L total; 0.42 L bio-activity space.
- **Structure:** Cylindrical aluminum shell with multilayer insulation and thermoelectric coolers (TEC).
- **Components:** Water tank (18 ml), biological chamber with vermiculite substrate, light guide tube, two CCD cameras, and control chips.

### 2.2. Biological Material
- **Producers:** Cotton, Potato, Arabidopsis, Rape.
- **Consumer:** Drosophila (Fly eggs).
- **Decomposer:** Saccharomyces (Yeast).

## 3. Ground Testing and Reliability
- **Sealing:** Air leakage rate < 1.9 × 10⁻⁵ Pa·cm²/s.
- **Thermal:** Simulated ranges from -52 °C to 52 °C (passive) or 38 °C (active cooling).
- **Radiation:** Tested tolerance to X-rays 10⁴~10⁶ times lunar intensity; most organisms survived except fly eggs at highest doses.

## 4. Lunar Surface Results
- **Landing:** January 3, 2019, Von Kármán crater.
- **Germination:** Cotton seed germinated 22 h after watering (vs. 53 h on Earth).
- **Morphology:** Lunar cotton seedlings had curved stems, fat streamlined nodes close to the substrate, and curled leaves.
- **Environmental Data:** Internal temperature 16.7–36.5 °C during day; dropped to -52 °C during night. Internal pressure: 98–104 kPa.
- **Mission Duration:** Worked for 1300 h over five lunar days; decommissioned May 9, 2019.

## 5. Discussion and ISRU Implications
The experiment proved plant cultivation on the Moon is possible. The authors note that future research should focus on:
- Improving lunar soil for cultivation.
- Utilizing lunar rocks as construction materials.
- Using lunar lava tubes for base construction.
- Producing oxygen and water from lunar minerals.
