# The first biological experiment on lunar surface for Humankind: Device and results

**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 214 (2024) 216–223  
**Authors:** GengXin Xie, JingHang Ding, YuanXun Zhang, MaoZhi Ren, Dan Qiu, Xi Wang  
**Available Online:** 17 October 2023

## Abstract
The Bioregenerative Life Support System (BLSS) is an indispensable part of extraterrestrial bases as it can provide sufficient food, fresh air, recycle waste, and create a safe environment for humans. The Chinese Chang’e 4 lunar lander carried a micro-ecosystem called Biological Experiment Payload (BEP) to assess the effects of low gravity, intense radiation, and light on the lunar surface. The payload (2.608 kg) contained seeds of cotton, potato, Arabidopsis, and rape, alongside fly eggs and yeast. Results demonstrated that cotton seeds could germinate and grow on the lunar surface, showing faster germination than Earth-based controls but altered morphology. The experiment proved that plants can adapt to the lunar environment, including the extreme cold of the lunar night, provided internal thermal controls are maintained.

## 1. Introduction
Building a lunar base is a major goal for national space agencies. Due to high transportation costs, bases must be self-sufficient through Bio-regenerative life support systems (BLSS). BLSS uses producers, consumers, and decomposers to sustainably supply food, oxygen, and water. While terrestrial experiments like BIOS-3 and Biosphere-2 have shown success, the impact of real lunar conditions (1/6 g, radiation) remained unknown until this experiment.

## 2. The Biological Experiment Payload (BEP)
The BEP was a cylindrical aluminum shell (173 × 198 mm) designed to maintain an internal environment against the lunar vacuum. 

### 2.1. Biological seedbed
The payload included Brassica napus (Rape), Solanum tuberosum (Potato), Gossypium hirsutum (Cotton), and Arabidopsis thaliana as producers. Drosophila melanogaster served as a consumer, and Saccharomyces fermentati served as food for the drosophila. Vermiculite was used as the substrate.

### 2.2. Watering system
A system involving 18 ml of nutrient-rich water was controlled by heating a petroleum jelly valve. Upon activation, an electromagnetic pump delivered water to dissolve water-soluble fiber and penetrate the substrate.

### 2.3. Lighting system
Sunlight from the lunar surface was introduced via light-guiding fibers (10 mm diameter) to power photosynthesis without relying on artificial light sources.

### 2.4. Monitor and Temperature Control Systems
Two CCD cameras captured growth images. A complex thermal control system used multilayer insulation and thermoelectric coolers (semiconductor chips) to manage temperatures between -52 °C and 38 °C.

## 3. Preparatory Ground Works
Stability tests included light conduction, heat transfer, and leakage. Biological samples were tested for temperature and radiation tolerance (X-ray exposure at 10^4~10^6 times lunar intensity). Experiments showed that most samples except fly eggs could survive high radiation.

## 4. Results on the Lunar Surface
- **Landing:** January 3, 2019, at Von Kármán crater.
- **Germination:** Cotton seed germinated 22 hours after watering (faster than the 53 hours on Earth).
- **Morphology:** Lunar seedlings grew curved stems with fat streamlined nodes close to the substrate; leaves were curled and did not fully unfold.
- **Survivability:** Seedlings remained green and upright even after 18 days of the first lunar night at -52 °C, showing unexpected resilience compared to Earth controls which died.
- **End of Mission:** After 5 lunar days (1300 hours), the payload was decommissioned in May 2019.

## 5. Discussion
The experiment successfully used natural sunlight via light guides to reduce energy burdens. It suggests that lunar resources (ISRU) such as lunar rocks for construction materials and lava tube caves for habitat locations are critical next steps. The phenotypic changes in plants (curved stems) agree with previous microgravity studies, but the resilience to low temperatures under 1/6 g was a novel finding.
