# Materials and Design Concepts for Space-Resilient Structures

**Authors:** Mohannad Z. Naser, Alaa I. Chehab  
**Journal:** Progress in Aerospace Sciences 98 (2018) 74–90  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.paerosci.2018.03.004

## Abstract
This paper presents a state-of-the-art literature review on recent developments of “space-native” construction materials and evolutionary design concepts for “space-resilient” structures. It explores the feasibility of In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU), harvesting elements from space bodies, and the emergence of enabling technologies such as lab-grown construction materials.

## 1. Introduction
The high cost of transporting materials from Earth ($20,000/kg to the Moon) necessitates the use of in-situ resources. Composite materials, such as concrete, are identified as potentially the most suitable for space fabrication due to their performance under radiation and extreme temperatures.

## 4. Space Construction Materials
### 4.1 Concrete Derivates
- **Ordinary Concrete:** Viable but requires water, which is scarce.
- **Sulfur Concrete:** Created by melting sulfur (120-150°C) and mixing with regolith. Compressive strengths range from 39 to 75.7 MPa (Lunar) and 20 to 63 MPa (Martian).
- **Geopolymers:** Produced via aluminosilicate precursors and alkaline solutions; can provide radiation protection.

### 4.2 Metals and Alloys
Aluminum (Al), Magnesium (Mg), Iron (Fe), and Titanium (Ti) are identified as key elements available for mining and processing into structural components.

## 7. Structural Concepts
1. **Terrestrial-like bases:** Rigid structures (precast or sulfur concrete).
2. **Inflatable bases:** High packing coefficient, lightweight, but vulnerable to punctures.
3. **Cable bases:** Tension-based framing systems utilizing natural craters.
4. **Underground bases:** Utilization of 'lava tubes' (30m+ diameter) to provide natural shielding from radiation and meteoroids.

## 8. Research and Future Needs
- **Additive Printing:** 3D printing of regolith concrete (e.g., 'D_SHAPE' technology).
- **Robo-engineers:** Requirement for fully autonomous construction and repair systems.
