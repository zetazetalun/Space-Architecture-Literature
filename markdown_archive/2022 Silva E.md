# Eco-friendly additive construction: Analysis of the printability of earthen-based matrices stabilized with potato starch gel and sisal fibers ## Abstract 3D printing for construction is a feasible alternative for social housing and recently for extraterrestrial habitats. This article studies the printable capabilities of earthen-based matrices using potato starch as a natural stabilizer and sisal fibers to control shrinkage. Results indicate printable earthen-based matrices with 1% sisal fibers and 5% starch gel showed improved workability and minor cracking. ## 1. Introduction 3D printing in construction reduces waste, improves safety, and allows for complex bio-inspired structures. The process involves delivery, deposition, motion, and control systems. ## 2. Additive construction using earthen-based matrices ### 2.1 Summary of recent advances Recent interest in soil-based 3D printing is driven by environmental benefits. Two subtopics: (1) earthen-based 3D printing for terrestrial use and (2) regolith-based 3D printing for extraterrestrial construction (46% of publications). Regolith studies explore Lunar/Martian soil simulants using sintering or binders. ## 3. Materials and Methods ### 3.1 Base Soil Classified as low plasticity sandy clay (CL) with 58.6% SiO2 and 15.6% Al2O3. ### 3.1.2 Natural Stabilizer Food-grade potato starch (amylose and amylopectin) used as a binder. ### 3.1.3 Fiber Reinforcement Sisal fibers (Agave sisalana) used to control shrinkage. ## 4. Results and Discussion ### 4.1 Fresh State Matrices with higher starch required more liquid. Fiber addition allowed for lower liquid percentages. Optimum printing speed identified as 15-30 mm/s. ### 4.2 Hardened State EFS5.0%(27) achieved a compressive strength of 2.2 MPa at 28 days (157% increase over control). Sisal fibers reduced maximum crack width from 8.49 mm to 0.60 mm. ## 5. Conclusions The study successfully formulated a soil/starch/fiber composite for 3D printing. The starch significantly influences stiffening and strength, while fibers control shrinkage.
