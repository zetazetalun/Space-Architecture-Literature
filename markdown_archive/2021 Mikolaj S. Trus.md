## ABSTRACT
The research facility on the Moon is an experimental, structural design for space exploration. It was developed as the Student Master Thesis at Cracow University of Technology, Faculty of Architecture. The main goal was to introduce innovative extraterrestrial architecture. Modularity, multifunctionality, and simplicity of the concept were key objectives. It was crucial to ensure the safety of the lunar crew. The facility is formed from seven modules. The construction of each of them consists of repetitive, easy to transport components. Project is prepared to host twelve astronauts, but it is expected to expand in the future.

## INTRODUCTION

### Historical background
Research on Moon facilities, transport possibilities, 3D-printing technology, new materials, and space ergonomics has taken a faster turn. Architecture and studies in this field can freely go beyond the borders of reality and break away from the planet's surface.

### Concept
The Moon environment is hostile: gravity is six times smaller than on Earth, there is no atmosphere, and solar radiation is unfiltered. Large temperature amplitudes are a challenge for the designer. This project identifies three major development directions: innovative structural solutions, protection from external damage, and functional proposal.

## METHODS
1.  **Hand drawing:** Focused on visual stimuli and defining spherical shapes as most efficient.
2.  **Virtual model:** Created in Blender 3D; defined truncated icosahedrons as the most useful modular geometry.
3.  **3D printing:** Used to verify virtual design results via a physical scale model.
4.  **Technical drawings:** Created sections and floorplans to determine functional interiors.

## RESULTS

### Construction
Rigid assembly elements were chosen over inflatable structures for predictability in altered gravity. The form is based on a truncated icosahedron, consisting of twelve regular pentagons and twenty regular hexagons. This solid was further triangulated.

*   **Exoskeleton:** A light rod construction system reinforced with internal support bars.
*   **Modularity:** The sphere can be divided into twenty repetitive triangular elements to solve transport issues.
*   **Corridors:** Designed as elongated antiprisms (pentagonal or hexagonal) serving as connection nodes.

### Protection
A multilayer shell was designed using vacuum as a thermal insulator:
*   **Internal Layer:** 20 cm thick thermal insulation to stop heat escape.
*   **Vacuum Space:** 5 cm gap between layers for insulation.
*   **External Layer:** 5 cm thick to absorb radiation and temperature differences.
*   **Regolith Cover:** Final step involves covering the structure with lunar regolith for radiation absorption and meteor protection.

### Function
The basic concept includes seven modules:
*   **Top Modules (3):** Living and sleeping areas (4 astronauts per module).
*   **Bottom Modules (3):** Diverse functions: Dining/Kitchen, Laboratory, and Gym.
*   **Central Module (1):** Common space and communication node.

## DISCUSSION
The concept utilizes repetitive rigid components compared to the inflatable/3D-printed regolith foam approach of Foster + Partners. The design allows for additive growth and expansion through regular polyhedral geometry.

## CONCLUSION
The Research Facility on the Moon addresses transport issues through modular, repetitive components and environmental challenges through a multilayer shell and regolith shielding. The structural concept is "multipotential," adaptable to various extraterrestrial conditions.
