# The Research Facility on the Moon

**Author:** Mikolaj S. Trus
**Affiliation:** Graduate of Architecture Studies, Cracow Univ. of Technology
**Published in:** Earth and Space 2021

## Abstract
The research facility on the Moon is an experimental structural design for space exploration, developed as a Master's Thesis. The concept emphasizes modularity, multifunctionality, and simplicity, utilizing seven modules to host twelve astronauts. The design addresses environmental threats through specific structural geometries and multi-layered shielding.

## Introduction
The study highlights the challenges of the lunar environment, including 1/6th gravity, lack of atmosphere, meteoroid impacts, solar radiation, and extreme temperature amplitudes. It references established concepts like Foster + Partners' lunar habitation projects to frame the design objectives: innovative structural solutions, protection from external damage, and functional proposal.

## Methods
1. **Hand Drawing:** Used for initial visualization of spherical concepts.
2. **Virtual Modeling:** Developed in Blender 3D using truncated icosahedrons and platonic solids to establish modular patterns.
3. **3D Printing:** Physical scale models (1:200) were produced to verify the results of the virtual design.
4. **Technical Drawings:** CAD-based sections and floor plans to determine functional interior arrangements.

## Results

### Construction
- **Form:** Truncated icosahedrons consisting of 12 regular pentagons and 20 regular hexagons.
- **Triangulation:** Each face is triangulated for structural rigidity.
- **System:** A light rod exoskeleton reinforced with internal support bars.
- **Modularity:** Modules can be divided into 20 repetitive elements, solving transport volume constraints. 

### Protection
- **Internal Layer:** 20 cm thick thermal insulation.
- **Insulation Gap:** 5 cm narrow space filled with vacuum.
- **External Layer:** 5 cm thin layer for radiation and temperature absorption.
- **Regolith Covering:** The final stage involves covering the structure with lunar regolith for maximum radiation and meteoroid protection.

### Function and Dimensions
- **Module Size:** 10.02 m diameter; 520 m³ accessible volume.
- **Corridors:** Pentagonal (4 m long) and Hexagonal (2.5 m long) modules serving as connection nodes.
- **Layout:** A central module serving as a common node with six peripheral modules arranged in a circle (three high, three low).
- **Crew:** Designed for 12 astronauts, including living quarters, labs, gyms, and dining areas.

## Conclusion
The project demonstrates the potential of regular polyhedrons in extraterrestrial architecture. The design is 'multipotential,' meaning the structural concept can be adjusted to various environmental conditions beyond the Moon.
