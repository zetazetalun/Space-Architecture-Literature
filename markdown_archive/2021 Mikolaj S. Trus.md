# The Research Facility on the Moon

**Author:** Mikolaj S. Trus  
**Affiliation:** Graduate of Architecture Studies, Cracow Univ. of Technology, Kraków, Poland.  
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE

## Abstract
The research facility on the Moon is an experimental, structural design developed as a Student Master Thesis. The concept emphasizes modularity, multifunctionality, and simplicity. The facility consists of seven modules formed from repetitive, easy-to-transport components, designed to host twelve astronauts with future expansion potential. This universal concept is intended to solve threats related to the lunar environment while ensuring crew safety.

## Introduction
The hostile lunar environment (low gravity, lack of atmosphere, micrometeoroids, solar radiation, and extreme temperature amplitudes) defines strict requirements for mass, dimensions, and form. The study examines previous projects by Foster + Partners (2012, 2015) and sets objectives for innovative structural solutions, environmental protection, and functional proposals.

## Methods
1.  **Hand Drawing:** Used for rapid visualization and identifying the spherical shape as the most efficient form.
2.  **Virtual Modeling:** Developed in Blender 3D using platonic solids (truncated icosahedrons) as the basis for modularity.
3.  **3D Printing:** Physical scale models (1:200) were produced to verify the results of the virtual design.
4.  **Technical Drawings:** CAD-based sections and floor plans were created to define functional interiors.

## Results

### Construction
*   **Form:** Spherical and cylindrical shapes were selected for optimal pressure distribution and volume-to-surface ratio.
*   **Geometry:** The design utilizes the **truncated icosahedron** (12 regular pentagons and 20 regular hexagons).
*   **Assembly:** A light rod exoskeleton reinforced with internal support bars. Each module is divided into 20 repetitive triangular elements to solve transport constraints.
*   **Dimensions:** 
    *   Edge length: 2.10 m
    *   Spherical module diameter: 10.02 m
    *   Accessible volume: 520 m³ per module
    *   Corridor modules: Pentagonal (4 m long, 30.35 m³) and Hexagonal (2.5 m long, 28.64 m³).

### Protection
*   **Multilayer Shell:** A three-layer approach using vacuum as a thermal insulator.
    *   **Internal layer:** 20 cm thick thermal insulation.
    *   **Vacuum gap:** 5 cm space between layers.
    *   **External layer:** 5 cm thick to absorb temperature differences and radiation.
*   **Regolith Shielding:** The final stage involves covering the structure with lunar regolith for radiation absorption and meteoroid protection.

### Function
*   **Layout:** Seven modules (six in a circle, one central). 
*   **Allocation:** Three upper modules for living/sleeping (4 astronauts each); three lower modules for dining, laboratories, and physical activity (gym).
*   **Power:** Solar energy is the primary source.

## Discussion and Conclusion
The design utilizes regular polyhedrons to allow for additive growth and unlimited expansion. Unlike the inflatable concepts proposed by Foster + Partners, this concept relies on repetitive rigid components that provide predictability in altered gravity. The study concludes that modular truncated icosahedrons meet the structural and physical requirements of the lunar environment while solving transport logistics.
