# A new deployable pantographic lunar habitat

**Authors:** Amin Lak, Maziar Asefi
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 192 (2022) 351–367
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.actaastro.2021.12.049

## Abstract
Deployable lunar structures are being developed as a solution to the volumetric limitations of cargo mission flights. This paper introduces a deployable lunar habitat consisting of linear and planar elements connected through simple movable joints. The structure utilizes modified polar scissor-like elements (SLE) and closed-loops of angulated SLEs. Evaluation criteria for transformable lunar structures are derived and concepts are evaluated. The proposed hybrid structure combines rigid carbon composite plates with an internal inflatable membrane, providing high protection against environmental hazards and reducing required extravehicular activity (EVA) time due to its quick deployment mechanism.

## 1. Introduction
Space habitats are classified into three categories: 
- **Class I:** Pre-integrated, hard-shell modules.
- **Class II:** Prefabricated, destination assembled (deployable).
- **Class III:** In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) derived structures.

This research focuses on Class II, specifically a hybrid system using scissor-hinge mechanisms and membranes.

## 2. Design Considerations
Key environmental hazards include micrometeoroids, radiation, and vacuum phenomena. Design guidelines include:
- Simple mechanical connections (no welding required).
- Common components for easy replacement.
- Modular design concepts.
- Stable footprint and center-of-gravity for landing.

## 3. Concept Assessment
Three existing Class II concepts were evaluated:
1. **Trigon:** Robotically assembled triangular/square panels.
2. **Inflated-Torus:** Hybrid rigid core with inflatable shell (based on TransHab).
3. **Moon Base Two:** Automatically deploying dome with regolith shielding.

## 4. Scissor-Hinge Systems
Scissor Structural Mechanisms (SSM) are formed by connecting Scissor Units (SU). 
- **Translational Units:** Pivot in the middle, struts parallel.
- **Polar Units:** Pivot not in middle, axes intersect.
- **Angulated Units:** Two straight bars connected through a kink angle (α).

## 5. Design and Mechanism
The proposed habitat is cylindrical, formed by extruding a closed SLE loop consisting of 20 angulated units.

### Habitat Dimensions
- **Target:** 40 m³ total habitable volume per crewmember for a crew of 4 (Total 160 m³).
- **Floor Area:** 45.6 m².
- **Nominal Internal Volume:** ~170 m³.

### Structural Components
- **Closed Loops:** 20 angulated units providing the cylindrical perimeter.
- **Central Core:** 1.10 m diameter; houses the telescopic jack for actuation.
- **Roof Pattern:** Modified Polar Scissor-Like Element (MP-SLE) Type 2 chosen for better packaging volume.
- **Flooring:** Simple Interconnected Linear Element (SILE) units.

### Material Selection
- **Rigid Panels:** Carbon composite (high radiation shielding, thermal insulation).
- **Membrane:** Multi-layer (Dacron seam filler, Vectran or Kevlar restraint layer, redundant pressure bladders, Nomex scuff protection).

## 6. Conclusions
The system combines the quick deployment of inflatable structures with the protection of rigid structures. The pre-assembled rigid elements allow for a simple central actuation system (telescopic jack) to expand the habitat to its full volume with minimal EVA.
