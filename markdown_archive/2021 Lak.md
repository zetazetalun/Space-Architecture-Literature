# A new deployable pantographic lunar habitat

**Authors:** Amin Lak, Maziar Asefi, PhD
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 192 (2022) 351–367
**Available online:** 30 December 2021
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.actaastro.2021.12.049

## Abstract
Deployable lunar structures are being developed as a solution to the volumetric limitations of cargo flights. A new habitat consisting of linear and planar elements connected through movable joints is introduced, utilizing modified polar and angulated scissor-like elements (SLE). The design considers evaluation criteria for transformable habitats, protection against environmental hazards, and rapid deployment to reduce EVA time. A hybrid approach combining rigid carbon-composite plates with an inflatable membrane is proposed to optimize protection and packaging volume.

## 1. Introduction
Habitation systems on the lunar surface are essential for crewed missions. Concepts are classified into Class-I (pre-integrated), Class-II (prefabricated/deployable), and Class-III (ISRU-derived). Deployable structures (Class-II) offer advantages in low packaging volume and reduced transportation costs. This study explores a hybrid Class-II structure using scissor-hinge systems.

## 2. Design Considerations
Key environmental hazards include micrometeoroids, ionizing radiation, and vacuum conditions. Design guidelines include:
- Simple connections (mechanical joints over welding).
- Common components for easy replacement.
- Modular design concepts.
- Stable footprint and center of gravity.

## 4. Scissor-hinge systems
Scissor-structural mechanisms (SSM) allow transformation from compact to deployed states. Types include translational, polar, and angulated units. Angulated units are particularly useful for forming closed-loop deployable structures.

## 5. Design

### 5.1. Habitat Dimensions
NASA standards suggest a minimum Net Habitable Volume (NHV) of 25 m³ per crewmember for long missions. For a crew of four, the total floor area needed is approximately 45.6 m², with a ceiling height of 3.5 m.

### 5.3. Mechanism Design
The proposed structure consists of:
- Two identical loops of angulated SLE units.
- A central structural core.
- Modified polar SLE (MP-SLE) units for the roof.
- Rigid panels for shielding and flooring.
- A thin pressure barrier (inflatable membrane).

### 5.3.1. Closed loops (Table 4)
- **Number of units:** 20
- **Folded radius:** 1.60 m
- **Deployed radius:** 3.80 m
- **Maximum area:** 45.40 m²

### 5.3.9. Material Selection
- **Rigid parts:** Carbon composites (high radiation shielding, low mass).
- **Pressure barrier:** Multilayered membrane (MLI, Vectran/Kevlar restraint layer, Combitherm bladders, Nomex scuff layer).

## 6. Conclusions
The design provides a nominal internal volume of ~170 m³ for four astronauts. The hybrid system combines the quick deployment of inflatables with the protection of rigid structures. The pre-assembled configuration improves the deployment process via a simple central actuation system (telescopic jack).
