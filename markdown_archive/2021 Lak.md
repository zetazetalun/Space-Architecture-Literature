# A new deployable pantographic lunar habitat

**Authors:** Amin Lak, Maziar Asefi, PhD
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 192 (2022) 351–367
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.actaastro.2021.12.049

## Abstract
Deployable lunar structures are being developed as a solution to cargo volume limitations. This paper introduces a new habitat consisting of linear and planar elements connected via movable joints, utilizing modified polar scissor-like elements (SLE) and closed-loop angulated scissor-like elements. Evaluation criteria for transformable lunar structures are derived, and an experimental study assesses deployment strategies. The proposed structure combines rigid plates with a membrane for environmental protection, reducing EVA time for assembly through a quick deployment mechanism.

## 1. Introduction
Space habitats are classified into three categories:
- **Class I:** Pre-integrated, hard-shell modules (rigid).
- **Class II:** Prefabricated, destination assembled (deployable).
- **Class III:** In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) derived structures.

This paper focuses on Class II, aiming to reduce mission costs by minimizing launch volume while maximizing deployed habitable space.

## 2. Design Considerations
Key environmental hazards addressed include:
- Micrometeoroids
- Ionizing and non-ionizing radiation
- Vacuum/pressurization requirements
- Thermal extremes

Design guidelines prioritize simple mechanical connections, modularity, and maximized commonality of components to simplify maintenance and fabrication.

## 3. Structural Concepts Assessment
Three concepts were evaluated as baselines:
1. **Trigon:** Robotic infrastructure generating network using triangular/square panels.
2. **Inflated-Torus:** Derived from NASA TransHab, featuring a rigid core and multilayered inflatable fabric.
3. **Moon Base Two:** An inflatable dome utilizing regolith shielding.

## 4. Scissor-Hinge Systems
Scissor-like elements (SLE) allow transformation from compact to deployed states. Three types are noted:
- Translational units
- Polar units
- Angulated units

## 5. Proposed Design
### 5.1 Habitat Dimensions
- **Target Crew:** 4 astronauts.
- **Mission Duration:** 180 days.
- **Required Floor Area:** ~45.6 m².
- **Net Habitable Volume (NHV):** 40 m³ per crewmember.

### 5.2 Component Mechanisms
- **Closed Loops:** 20 angulated SLE units forming the cylindrical wall.
- **Roof Patterns:** Modified Polar Scissor-Like-Elements (MP-SLE Type 2 selected for better packaging).
- **Flooring:** Simple Interconnected Linear Elements (SILE) coupled with multi-stage jacks.
- **Pressure Vessel:** A thin pressure barrier (Dacron) stowed around the core, protected by external rigid carbon composite panels.

### 5.3 Material Selection
- **External Panels:** Carbon composite (high radiation shielding, thermal insulation, low mass).
- **Internal Membrane:** Multi-layer approach including MLI (thermal protection), Vectran or Kevlar (structural restraint), and redundant pressure bladders.

## 6. Conclusions
The design provides a nominal internal volume of ~170 m³ for four astronauts. The system combines the quick deployment of inflatable structures with the high protection of rigid shielding. The pre-assembled configuration improves deployment via a central actuation system located in the central core.
