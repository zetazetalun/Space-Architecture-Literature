# A new deployable pantographic lunar habitat

**Authors:** Amin Lak, Maziar Asefi
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 192 (2022) 351–367
**Received:** 8 September 2021; **Accepted:** 27 December 2021

## Abstract
Deployable lunar structures are being developed as a solution to the volumetric limitations of cargo mission flights. This paper introduces a new deployable lunar habitat consisting of linear and planar elements connected through simple movable joints. The structure utilizes modified polar scissor-like elements (SLE) and a closed-loop of angulated scissor-like elements. Through parametric design and physical modeling, the study evaluates transformation strategies to meet design considerations for a crew of four, providing protection against environmental hazards using a combination of rigid plates and membranes while reducing EVA time for assembly.

## 1. Introduction
Space habitats are classified into three categories:
- **Class-I:** Pre-integrated, hard-shell module (rigid).
- **Class-II:** Prefabricated, destination assembled (deployable).
- **Class-III:** In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) derived structure.

This research focuses on a Class-II hybrid structure combining rigid elements with a pressurized membrane.

## 2. Design Considerations
Key environmental hazards identified include micrometeoroids, radiation, and vacuum. Governing design criteria for the proposed habitat include:
- Simple mechanical connections (no welding).
- Use of common components to allow swapping.
- Modular design concept.
- Pinned joints for conservative design and easier analysis.

## 4. Scissor-Hinge Systems
The design employs Scissor Structural Mechanisms (SSM). Three basic unit types are identified:
1. **Translational Units:** Pivot point at the middle, major axes parallel.
2. **Polar Units:** Pivot point not in the middle, major axes intersect.
3. **Angulated Units:** Formed by two identical angulated elements with a kink angle α.

## 6. Conclusions
The proposed system combines the quick deployment of inflatable structures with the shielding of rigid structures. The use of a central telescopic jack allows for simplified deployment, improving the deployed-to-packaged volume ratio while covering the overhead hemisphere with protective carbon composite panels.
