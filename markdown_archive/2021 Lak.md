# A new deployable pantographic lunar habitat

**Authors:** Amin Lak, Maziar Asefi
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 192 (2022) 351–367
**Received:** 8 September 2021; **Accepted:** 27 December 2021

## Abstract
Deployable lunar structures are being developed to overcome the volumetric limitations of cargo mission flights. This paper introduces a new structure consisting of linear and planar elements connected through movable joints, utilizing two types of scissor-like elements (SLE): modified polar SLEs and a closed-loop of angulated SLEs. Evaluation criteria for transformable lunar structures are derived, and a design is selected that combines rigid plates with a membrane to provide protection against environmental hazards while reducing required EVA time for assembly.

## 1. Introduction
Habitation on the lunar surface is essential for crewed missions. Habitat designs range from class-I (pre-integrated hard-shell) to class-II (deployable) and class-III (ISRU-derived). Deployable structures are advantageous due to low packaging volume. This study explores a hybrid approach combining rigid elements with an inflatable membrane.

## 2. Design Considerations
Main hazards include micrometeoroids, radiation, and vacuum. Design guidelines require:
- Simple mechanical connections (no welding if possible).
- Common components for easy replacement.
- Modular design concepts.
- Pinned joints for easier analysis.

## 3. Concepts Assessment
### 3.1 Selected structural concepts
- **Trigon:** Robotic infrastructure generating stable geometries from triangular panels.
- **Inflated-torus:** Lightweight rigid core with a multilayered inflatable shell (TransHab derivation).
- **Moon Base Two:** Dome-shaped inflatable habitat with regolith shielding.

## 4. Scissor-hinge systems
Deployable structures provide transformation from compact to deployed states. This study applies Scissor-Like Element (SLE) mechanisms, specifically translational, polar, and angulated units.

## 5. Design
### 5.1 Habitat dimensions
NASA standards suggest a minimum Net Habitable Volume (NHV) of 25 m³ per crewmember. For a 180-day mission, a total floor area of 45.6 m² is estimated for a crew of four.

### 5.3 Mechanism design
Constituent elements include:
- Two loops of angulated SLE units.
- Central structural core (1.10 m diameter).
- Modified polar SLE (MP-SLE) units for the roof.
- Rigid panels for shielding and flooring.
- Pressure barrier (internal membrane).

## 6. Conclusions
The proposed system combines quick deployment with the protection characteristics of rigid structures. The design utilizes a simple central actuation system to expand rigid elements, reducing the required EVA time and providing a high degree of overhead shielding for crewmembers.
