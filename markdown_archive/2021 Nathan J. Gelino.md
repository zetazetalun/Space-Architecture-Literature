# Off Earth Landing and Launch Pad Construction—A Critical Technology for Establishing a Long-Term Presence on Extraterrestrial Surfaces

**Authors:** Nathan J. Gelino, Robert P. Mueller, Robert W. Moses, James G. Mantovani, Philip T. Metzger, Brad C. Buckles, and Laurent Sibille
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE

## Abstract
A long-term presence on the Moon will require numerous lunar landings and launches. Interactions between rocket engine plumes and the unprepared lunar surface lead to erosion and high-velocity ejecta, posing risks to sensors, surface assets, and orbital infrastructure. This paper summarizes previous work on off-Earth landing/launch pad (LLP) construction, identifies technology gaps, and establishes metrics for trade studies to guide future development.

## Introduction
Launch and landing infrastructure, termed a Launch & Landing Complex (LLC), is well-defined on Earth but requires new technological solutions for Moon and Mars missions. Repeated returns to specific sites necessitate functional equivalents to mitigate risks from cratering, soil instability, and dust clouds.

## Preliminary Engineering Assessment
*   **Artemis Program:** Targets 2024 for human return; 2028 for sustained infrastructure.
*   **Erosion Scales:** Apollo engines displaced ~2.6 metric tons of regolith. Larger Human Landing System (HLS) landers (100 kN thrust) could displace ~16 tons of regolith at velocities exceeding lunar escape velocity.
*   **Hazards:** Ejecta causes hardware damage, sensor obscuration, and landing gear instability.

## State of the Art and Past Work
*   **TRL Level:** Currently approximately TRL-2.
*   **Sintered Pavers:** Robotic construction of VTVL pads using sintered basalt pavers (Swamp Works/PISCES). Challenges include seam weakness and thermal cracking.
*   **Soil Stabilization:** Investigation of rock beds and pavers (Van Susante and Metzger, 2016).
*   **Lunar Concrete:** Use of binders (KOHLS-1/KAUMLS) and glass fiber at atmospheric pressure.

## Trade Study Criteria
Table 1 outlines 3 phases for evaluation:
1.  **Preparation and Staging:** Up-mass, difficulty of ISRU collection/processing, site preparation effort.
2.  **Construction Phase:** Constructability, versatility, construction time, robotic autonomy.
3.  **Operations and Maintenance:** Performance as a surface, expected life, ease of repairability, lifecycle cost.

## Conclusion
Off-Earth spaceports will eventually need Launch and Landing Pads (LLP) to reduce mission risks. While various concepts exist, all remain at a low TRL status. Future work must focus on prototyping and exposure to simulated lunar conditions (thermal vacuum, radiation, and reduced gravity).
