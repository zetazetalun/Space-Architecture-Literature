# Deep learning technology for construction machinery and robotics

**Authors:** Ke You, Cheng Zhou, Lieyun Ding
**Journal:** Automation in Construction 150 (2023) 104852
**Published:** 3 April 2023

## Abstract
Construction machinery and robots are essential equipment for major infrastructure. The application of deep learning technology can improve construction quality and alleviate labor shortages. This paper conducts a systematic review of deep learning applied to (1) perception, (2) navigation and planning, (3) control, and (4) human-robot interaction (HRI). It identifies challenges such as dataset limitations and lack of interpretability, while highlighting future outlooks like generative AI and extraterrestrial construction.

## 1. Introduction
Robots have the potential to perform repetitive tasks, reducing manual labor and improving safety. Autonomous control is particularly vital for upcoming lunar and Mars construction where long-distance communication makes real-time intervention difficult. Deep learning algorithms like DCNN, ResNet, LSTM, and DRL are replacing traditional rule-based methods to handle the complexity of construction environments.

## 2. Research Approach and Scientometric Analysis
The review combines keyword co-occurrence and cluster-based topic analysis to identify application hotspots. Hotspots include earth-moving machinery, 3D printing of concrete (additive manufacturing), and shield machines (TBM).

## 3. Perception
Real-time and accurate perception ensures safety and quality. Sensors like cameras (vision-based) and LiDAR are standard. Deep learning models like SegNet and DeepLabV3+ enable semantic segmentation, while VoxelNet and PointNet++ facilitate 3D object detection from point clouds. 

## 4. Navigation and Planning
Navigation requires high-precision positioning (RTK-GPS, IMU). SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) is used where GPS is missing. For lunar construction, DRL-based obstacle avoidance planning and SLAM in low-light environments are key research areas.

## 5. Control
Control methods are evolving from classical schemes to data-driven and hybrid models. DRL (Deep Reinforcement Learning) allows machines like wheel loaders and excavators to learn scooping or driving tasks through trial and error, often outperforming human operators.

## 6. Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)
HRI remains necessary as full autonomy is a long-term goal. Techniques include teleoperation (using VR/AR), Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI) for hands-free control, and imitation learning from expert operators.

## 7. Discussion and Extraterrestrial Construction
Section 7.2.4 focuses on planetary exploration. Lunar and Martian construction are identified as critical nodes. Challenges include varying gravity, atmospheric composition, and radiation. Deep learning is expected to play a major role in autonomous in-situ construction (e.g., Xuanwu lunar habitation).

## 8. Conclusions
Deep learning is robust for unstructured construction environments. Future development hinges on higher-quality datasets, trustworthy/explainable AI, and adapting machinery dynamics for extraterrestrial environments.
