# Deep learning technology for construction machinery and robotics

## Abstract
Construction machinery and robots are essential equipment for major infrastructure. The application of deep learning technology can improve the construction quality and alleviate the shortage of skilled workers. This paper conducts a systematic review from perspectives including: (1) perception; (2) navigation and planning; (3) control; and (4) human-robot interaction. On the basis of a systematic analysis, the study identifies challenges such as dataset limitations and lack of interpretability, while highlighting future outlooks such as generative deep learning and extraterrestrial construction.

## 1. Introduction
Robots have the potential to perform repetitive operation tasks instead of workers, improving safety and efficiency. Autonomous control of unmanned construction machinery has broad application prospects for upcoming lunar and Mars construction, especially considering the difficulty of long-distance real-time communication.

## 3. Perception
Real-time and accurate perception of construction scenes ensures safety. Key sensors include Camera and LiDAR. The paper discusses vision-based semantic segmentation (CNNs) and 3D object detection from point clouds.

## 4. Navigation and Planning
Requires high-precision positioning (RTK-GPS, IMU, depth cameras). Deep reinforcement learning (DRL) is noted for its ability to realize autonomous decision-making control for lunar construction robots (e.g., [68]).

## 5. Control
Analyzes classical control, data-driven methods (CNNs, RNNs, DRL), and hybrid methods. DRL allows machines to learn autonomous driving/operation through reward/punishment mechanisms.

## 6. Human-robot interaction (HRI)
Focuses on teleoperation (VR/AR/BCI), imitation learning from expert operators, and collective construction involving swarms of robots.

## 7. Discussion and Future Outlook
### 7.2.4 Extraterrestrial Construction
Planetary exploration requires in situ construction under different gravity conditions, atmospheric compositions, and radiation. AI and deep learning will be critical for reconstructing machinery dynamics models and designing autonomous control methods for lunar and Martian environments.

## 8. Conclusions
Deep learning is a robust method for addressing knowledge gaps in unstructured construction environments. Future research must focus on dataset quality with expert knowledge, trustworthy AI, and applying these technologies to space-based construction scenarios.
