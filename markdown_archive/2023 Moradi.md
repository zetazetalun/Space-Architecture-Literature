# Space-technological and architectural methodology and process towards design of long-term habitats for scientific human missions on mars

**Authors:** Kasra Amini, Mojgan Moradi, Bahareh Vossoughi, Ehsan Dehghani Janabadi  
**Journal:** MethodsX 11 (2023) 102270  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.mex.2023.102270

## Abstract
Centered on the core idea of long duration habitat design for research crew on Mars, the Martian Habitat Units (MHUs) are designed as a cluster of 10 units each with the maximum capacity of 9 crew members. This manuscript provides detailed insight on the lessons learned of the comprehensive design attempt including concerns for redundancy, modularity, and construction methods.

## Method Details
### Iterative Design
The design process follows an iterative methodology, moving from design criteria and mission specifications to spatial layout, composition strategies, and output design. The process integrates architectural frameworks with extreme environmental constraints.

### Martian Habitat Units (MHUs)
- **Modularity:** Calculated based on a module of 4 x 4 x 4 m³ growing in steps for 4 to 9 residents.
- **Hierarchy:** Interconnected hexagonal modules arranged in a rhombus pattern of 4 by 4.
- **Construction:** AI-assisted 3D printing using granulated Martian regolith (in-situ resources).
- **Atmospheric Protection:** Double-layered pressure vessel with an interspace at 80% of 1 atm.
- **Anti-Dust Settlement Membrane (ADSM):** An inflatable elastic membrane on top of units to control flow fields and prevent dust accumulation on photovoltaics.

### Power Generation
1. **Primary:** Nuclear fission reactors (NASA Kilopower-class 10 kWe units).
2. **Secondary:** Solar farm (multi-junction photovoltaic cells).
3. **Tertiary:** Wind turbines (HAWT) for use during dust storms.

## Conclusion
The MHUs are designed for long-term settlement beyond mere survival. By utilizing modularity and 3D printing with ISRU, the design reduces payload requirements while ensuring safety through redundancy in power and life support systems.
