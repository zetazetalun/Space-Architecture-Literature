# StarCrete: A starch-based biocomposite for off-world construction

**Aled D. Roberts and Nigel S. Scrutton**
*Corresponding author: Nigel S. Scrutton, The University of Manchester*

**Published:** 2023, Open Engineering
**DOI:** 10.1515/eng-2022-0390

## Abstract
Robust and affordable technology capabilities are needed before a sustained human presence on the lunar and Martian surfaces can be established. A key challenge is the production of high-strength structural materials from in situ resources. We demonstrate the use of ordinary starch as a binder for simulated extraterrestrial regolith to produce a high-strength biocomposite material, termed StarCrete. Lunar and Martian StarCrete achieved compressive strengths of 91.7 and 72.0 MPa, respectively, surpassing most other proposed technology solutions.

## 1 Introduction
Stabilizing loose regolith into a solid material provides radiation shielding and allows for inflatable habitats. Most proposed solutions have drawbacks like high energy/water use or high-mass equipment requirements. Biopolymers produced under mild conditions could overcome these. StarCrete utilizes surplus starch (from food production) as a binder.

## 2 Results and Discussion

### Starch Source Screening
Several starch sources were screened (Table 1). Potato starch was the most effective binder.

**Table 1: Properties of native starch and resulting ERB UCS values**
| Starch source | UCS (MPa) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Maize | 2.28 ± 0.80 |
| Waxy maize | 3.69 ± 0.33 |
| Tapioca | 1.43 ± 0.07 |
| Potato | 17.7 ± 2.1 |
| Wheat | 11.5 ± 1.6 |
| Rice | 1.36 ± 0.13 |
| Waxy rice | 8.35 ± 0.59 |

### Additive Screening
Urea and acetic acid were found to have a strong positive effect on UCS (Table 2).

**Table 2: Effect of additive incorporation on UCS**
| Additive | Relative UCS (%) |
| :--- | :--- |
| None | 100 |
| Urea | 152.5 ± 19.6 |
| MgCl2 | 69.1 ± 24.4 |
| Acetic acid | 150.2 ± 42.6 |

### Process Optimization
Using Design of Experiments (DoE), the process was optimized. Higher gelatinization temperatures (up to 120°C) and specific rehydration extents (5%) were critical.

**Table 3: Mechanical property data of optimized StarCrete**
| Regolith | UCS (MPa) | Compressive modulus (GPa) | Flexural strength (MPa) | Flexural modulus (MPa) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| MGS-1 (Mars) | 71.95 ± 1.45 | 4.12 ± 0.27 | 8.41 ± 0.60 | 658.4 ± 43.7 |
| LHS-1 (Moon) | 91.68 ± 2.69 | 5.66 ± 0.09 | 2.14 ± 0.22 | 137.3 ± 37.7 |

## 3 Conclusions and outlook
StarCrete is a relatively simple solution with high technical readiness. Advantages include:
1. Risk reduction (edible binder).
2. Practicality (simple solution).
3. System integration (linked to food/oxygen systems).
4. Resourcefulness (low energy, recoverable water).
5. Resource locality (on-site production).
