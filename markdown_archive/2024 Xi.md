# Bearing capacity of lunar soil ground under extraterrestrial environmental effects

**Journal:** Computers and Geotechnics  
**Volume:** 165 (2024) 105923  
**Authors:** Banglu Xi, Mingjing Jiang, Pinqiang Mo, Jiaqiang Yang, Zhenhua Zhang

## Abstract
Lunar environment features a low gravity field and ultra-high vacuum which shows significant effects on the bearing behavior of lunar soil ground for future lunar habitation. Therefore, the 2D Discrete Element Method was employed to simulate the plate load tests under different gravity fields in non-vacuum (i.e., excluding the van der Waals force) and high-vacuum (i.e., including the van der Waals force) environments, analyzing the bearing behavior and soil response under extraterrestrial environmental effects. The results show that a bilinear line bounded by 1g can be employed to describe the gravity effect on the gravity factors for ultimate bearing capacity and coefficient of subgrade reaction.

## 1. Introduction
Lunar habitation plays an important role in the future of human society, working as a supply center of in-situ resources. From a perspective of geotechnical engineering, it requires an accurate understanding of the lunar soil-tool interaction on the Moon. The lower gravity field affects soil-plate interaction due to low initial stress, and the ultra-high vacuum leads to an apparent increase in internal friction angle and cohesion due to van der Waals forces.

## 6. Concluding Remarks
1. A bilinear line bounded by 1g describes the gravity effect on gravity factors for ultimate bearing capacity ($s_{ng}^{qu}$) and subgrade reaction ($s_{ng}^K$).
2. High-gravity effects in centrifuges cannot be directly applied to predict low-gravity behavior due to different slopes in the bilinear relationship.
3. The affected area under foundations becomes shallower and wider with decreasing gravity in high-vacuum conditions.
