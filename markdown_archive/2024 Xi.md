# Bearing capacity of lunar soil ground under extraterrestrial environmental effects

**Authors:** Banglu Xi, Mingjing Jiang, Pinqiang Mo, Jiaqiang Yang, Zhenhua Zhang
**Journal:** Computers and Geotechnics 165 (2024) 105923
**Available online:** 12 November 2023

## Abstract
Lunar environment features a low gravity field and ultra-high vacuum which shows significant effects on the bearing behavior of lunar soil ground for future lunar habitation. Therefore, the 2D Discrete Element Method was employed to simulate the plate load tests under different gravity fields in non-vacuum and high-vacuum environments. The results show that a bilinear line bounded by 1g can be employed to describe the gravity effect on the gravity factors for ultimate bearing capacity and coefficient of subgrade reaction. The slopes are different in low and high-gravity fields, indicating that the high-gravity effect in a centrifuge cannot be directly applied to predict the bearing behavior of lunar soil ground in a low-gravity field.

## 1. Introduction
Lunar habitation plays an important role in the future of human society, working as a supply center of in-situ resources. From a perspective of geotechnical engineering, it requires an accurate understanding of the lunar soil-tool interaction on the Moon. The lunar environment features a lower gravity field and ultra-high vacuum. The lower gravity field will affect soil-plate interaction due to the low initial stress, and the ultra-high vacuum leads to an apparent increase in internal friction angle and cohesion due to van der Waals forces.

## 3. Bearing behavior
### 3.1. Ultimate bearing capacity
The ultimate bearing capacity (qu) increases non-linearly with the increasing gravity field. An increase in ultimate bearing capacity is observed in the high vacuum environment compared to non-vacuum conditions due to higher shear strength. 

To describe the gravity effect, the gravity factor (s_ng^qu) is proposed:
$$s_{ng}^{qu} = q_u^{ng} / q_u^{1g}$$

### 3.2. Coefficient of subgrade reaction
The coefficient of subgrade reaction (K) increases with the gravity level significantly. A bilinear line bounded by 1g is employed to capture the gravity effect on the gravity factors for the coefficient of subgrade reaction.

## 4. Concluding remarks
1. A bilinear line bounded by 1g describes the gravity effect on bearing capacity factors. Centrifuge tests (high-g) cannot be directly extrapolated to low-g lunar conditions.
2. The particle velocity field reveals a dynamic balance between flowing out to the surface and moving downward. This balance is shifted by the vacuum and gravity levels.
3. Vacuum conditions increase the soil heap height and steepness compared to non-vacuum environments, especially in low gravity (0.01g and 1/6g).
