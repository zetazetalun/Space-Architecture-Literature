# Bearing capacity of lunar soil ground under extraterrestrial environmental effects

**Authors:** Banglu Xi, Mingjing Jiang, Pinqiang Mo, Jiaqiang Yang, Zhenhua Zhang  
**Journal:** Computers and Geotechnics 165 (2024) 105923  
**Date:** Available online 12 November 2023; Published 2024

## Abstract
Lunar environment features a low gravity field and ultra-high vacuum which shows significant effects on the bearing behavior of lunar soil ground for future lunar habitation. Therefore, the 2D Discrete Element Method (DEM) was employed to simulate the plate load tests under different gravity fields in non-vacuum (excluding van der Waals force) and high-vacuum (including van der Waals force) environments. Results show that a bilinear line bounded by 1g describes the gravity effect on ultimate bearing capacity and subgrade reaction. Findings indicate that high-gravity centrifuge effects cannot be directly applied to predict lunar low-gravity behavior. Observations provide advice for developing analytical models for the design of foundations for lunar habitation.

## 1. Introduction
Lunar habitation is crucial for future human society, serving as a supply center for in-situ resources (ISRU) and a platform for scientific research. Accurate understanding of lunar soil-tool and soil-foundation interaction is vital for safe and economical construction. The lunar environment differs from Earth due to lower gravity (affecting initial stress) and ultra-high vacuum (leading to van der Waals forces that increase internal friction and cohesion).

## 2. DEM Model of Lunar Regolith
The study uses 2D Discrete Element Method (DEM) to simulate the mechanical behavior of lunar regolith, accounting for:

## 3. Key Findings on Bearing Behavior
### 3.1 Gravity and Vacuum Effects
- **Vacuum Effect:** Soils in lunar environments exhibit greater strength, more apparent strain softening, and shear dilatancy compared to Earth conditions due to inter-particle cohesion from van der Waals forces.
- **Gravity Factor (s_ng^qu):** The ultimate bearing capacity increases non-linearly with the gravity field. The relationship is described by a bilinear model with a transition point at 1g.
- **Subgrade Reaction (K):** The coefficient of subgrade reaction increases with gravity levels and is significantly higher in high-vacuum environments.

### 3.2 Failure Mechanisms
- In non-vacuum environments, decreasing gravity leads to deeper but narrower affected areas (wedge-shaped flow).
- In high-vacuum environments, decreasing gravity leads to deeper and wider affected areas.
- The van der Waals force is non-ignorable in low gravity (0.01g, 1/6g) but becomes negligible in high-gravity fields where self-weight forces dominate.

## 4. Concluding Remarks
1. High-gravity centrifuge test results cannot be directly extrapolated to low-gravity lunar scenarios due to the bilinear nature of gravity factors.
2. Lunar soil can bear a larger mass relative to Earth because the reduced gravity affects the base load more significantly than it reduces the bearing capacity of the soil.
3. The affected soil zone becomes shallower in depth and wider in width as gravity decreases in high-vacuum conditions.
