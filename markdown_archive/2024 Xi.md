### Abstract
Lunar environment features a low gravity field and ultra-high vacuum which shows significant effects on the bearing behavior of lunar soil ground for future lunar habitation. Therefore, the 2D Discrete Element Method (DEM) was employed to simulate the plate load tests under different gravity fields in non-vacuum and high-vacuum environments. The results show that a bilinear line bounded by 1g can be employed to describe the gravity effect on factors for ultimate bearing capacity and coefficient of subgrade reaction. High-gravity effects in a centrifuge cannot be directly applied to predict low-gravity lunar soil behavior. The affected area becomes deeper and wider with decreasing gravity level in high-vacuum environments.

### 1. Introduction
Lunar habitation plays an important role in the future of human society as a supply center of in-situ resources. Design of suitable foundations requires accurate understanding of lunar soil-tool interaction. The lunar environment's low gravity and ultra-high vacuum (leading to van der Waals forces between particles) present challenges for Earth-based geotechnical modeling. This study uses DEM to analyze these extraterrestrial effects on bearing behavior.

### 3. Bearing Behavior Analysis
- **Ultimate Bearing Capacity (qu):** Determined at s = 2.5 mm. Increases non-linearly with increasing gravity. High-vacuum conditions result in higher bearing capacity than non-vacuum due to van der Waals forces enhancing shear strength.
- **Bilinear Gravity Effect:** A bilinear fitting curve bounded by 1g is proposed for gravity factors. The slopes differ in low and high gravity regimes, suggesting centrifuge testing (high-g) does not directly translate to lunar conditions (low-g).
- **Subgrade Reaction (K):** Increases with gravity level. Coefficients are larger in high-vacuum environments than non-vacuum at identical gravity levels.

### 4. Soil Response and Failure Modes
- **Heaving:** Ground heaving increases with settlement and is more pronounced in high-vacuum environments.
- **Affected Area:** In high-vacuum (lunar-like), as gravity decreases, the failure zone becomes deeper and wider. In non-vacuum, it becomes deeper but narrower.

### 5. Conclusions
1. A bilinear relationship describes the gravity effect on bearing capacity factors.
2. Van der Waals forces in ultra-high vacuum are non-ignorable in low gravity fields.
3. The affected area and failure mechanisms vary significantly between vacuum and non-vacuum conditions, providing a basis for new analytical models for lunar foundation design.
