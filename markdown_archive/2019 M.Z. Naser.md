**M.Z. Naser**

## Abstract
A successful space exploration requires establishing permanent and earth-independent infrastructure that are not only resilient to the extreme environment of space but also preferably made of sustainable and indigenous “space-native” construction materials. This review covers feasibility of exploiting in-situ lunar and Martian resources as well as harvesting of elements and compounds, from near Earth objects (NEOs), to produce extraterrestrial materials suitable for construction of space-based infrastructure. This review also details material features and characteristics required to withstand the unique, and harsh, effects of space environment. In essence, this paper reviews past and recent advancements in construction-based materials that could be used in the design and development of space human bases and highlights design consideration for sustainable human settlements. Towards the end of this review, practical and technological challenges associated with development of lunar and Martian indigenous construction materials are identified and examined.

## 3. Properties of space-native construction materials
### 3.1. Concrete-like hybrids
*   **Sulfur Concrete:** Waterless, rapid curing. Issues include sublimation in vacuum and mass loss over time.
*   **Polymer Concrete:** Uses polymeric resin binders (epoxy, thermoplastics). Compressive strength varies from 17 to 60 MPa.
*   **Geopolymers:** Formed by interaction between aluminosilicate and alkaline solutions. Compressive strength between 16.6 and 33.1 MPa.

### 3.2. Metals and alloys
Extraction of Al, Mg, Fe, and Ti from regolith. Al and Mg are suitable for lightweight members. Magnesium offers electromagnetic shielding and high damping.

## 4. Design considerations for sustainable space infrastructures
*   **Gravity effects:** Moon and Mars gravity (17% and 38% of Earth) allow for larger spans and thinner members (3-6 times the mass on Earth).
*   **Shielding:** Burial in regolith (1-6 m depth) is proposed for radiation and meteorite protection.
*   **Foundations:** Cohesion and bearing capacity are influenced by the lack of moisture and different mineralogy.

## 6. Summary and conclusions
*   Extreme environment presents unique challenges for extraterrestrial construction.
*   Abundance of SiO2 in regolith enables cementitious materials.
*   ISRU leads to sustainable infrastructure with minimal Earth-based supply.
