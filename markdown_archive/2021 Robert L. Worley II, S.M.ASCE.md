# Investigation into Measuring Low Levels of Cohesion of Regolith Simulants

**Authors:** Robert L. Worley II, Laura Obregon, Lane Feldeisen, Brandon Nimberger, Colin Palmer, and Mandar M. Dewoolkar

**Affiliation:** College of Engineering and Mathematical Sciences, Dept. of Civil and Environmental Engineering, Univ. of Vermont, Burlington, VT.

## ABSTRACT
Lunar and Martian regoliths are similar to terrestrial soils in that they appear granular but are expected to contain a small amount of cohesion. As such, cohesion in extraterrestrial regoliths pose challenges for future space operations such as in situ resource utilization (ISRU). ISRU requires a better understanding of mechanical properties of regoliths and to recreate those conditions for physical modeling on Earth. The specific goal of this study was to evaluate two methods—vertical cut and simple direct shear testing—and assess their efficacy in reliably measuring small amounts of cohesion in lunar simulants: JSC-1A and GRC-3. Findings indicate that vertical cut testing cohesion estimates for JSC-1A at relative densities 0%–80% ranged between 0.115 and 0.971 kPa and cohesion estimates for GRC-3 at relative densities 0%–80% ranged between 0.190 and 1.872 kPa. Vertical cut testing provides monotonically increasing cohesion estimates with increased relative density, but simple direct shear testing did not establish any particular trend.

## INTRODUCTION
Understanding surface regoliths influences humans' ability to explore or inhabit extraterrestrial bodies. Reliable simulation of regolith properties is essential for geotechnical aspects of space exploration such as landing, walking, and investigating potential utilization of lunar resources to develop extraterrestrial habitats. Soil shear strength is expressed by the Mohr-Coulomb failure criterion:

$$\tau_f = \sigma \tan \phi + c$$ (Eq. 1)

where $\tau_f$ is shear stress at failure, $\sigma$ is normal stress, $\phi$ is the internal friction angle, and $c$ is cohesion.

### Testing Methods
1.  **Vertical Cut Test:** Measures the "critical height" ($H_c$) at which an unsupported vertical slope of soil fails. Cohesion is estimated as $c = 0.5 \gamma H_c$ where $\gamma$ is unit weight.
2.  **Simple Direct Shear Test:** A modified shear test allowing for very small normal loads (0 to 10 kPa) to improve resolution for low-cohesion materials.

## CONCLUSIONS
Vertical cut testing showed efficacy in estimating low levels of cohesion that align with Apollo model best estimates (0.1–1.0 kPa). Cohesion increased with relative density, supporting the hypothesis that apparent cohesion in lunar regolith is largely due to mechanical interlocking of irregular particles. Simple direct shear testing, while useful for friction angle, lacked the fidelity required for measuring very low cohesion levels in dry granular soil.
