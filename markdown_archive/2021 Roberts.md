# Blood, sweat, and tears: extraterrestrial regolith biocomposites with in vivo binders

**Authors:** A.D. Roberts, D.R. Whittall, R. Breitling, E. Takano, J.J. Blaker, S. Hay, N.S. Scrutton
**Published in:** Materials Today Bio 12 (2021) 100136
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.mtbio.2021.100136

## Abstract
The exploitation of resources in situ — including regolith, water deposits, and atmospheric gases — will be an important part of any crewed mission to the Red Planet. Yet there is one significant source of natural resources that will also be available: the crew themselves. In this work, we explore the use of human serum albumin (HSA) — a common protein obtained from blood plasma — as a binder for simulated Lunar and Martian regolith to produce 'extraterrestrial regolith biocomposites (ERBs).' HSA-based ERBs displayed compressive strengths as high as 25.0 MPa. The incorporation of urea could further increase the compressive strength by over 300%, with the best-performing formulation reaching 39.7 MPa. We also demonstrate that HSA-ERBs have the potential to be 3D-printed.

## 1. Introduction
Early extraterrestrial colonies will likely exploit loose regolith as a bulk material for radiation and meteor shielding. Mechanical stabilization is required to prevent erosion and create pressurized structures. Biological binders (biopolymers) are proposed as an alternative to transporting heavy cement-making equipment from Earth.

## 2. Materials and Methods
- **Simulants:** Lunar Highlands Simulant 1 (LHS-1) and Martian Global Simulant 1 (MGS-1).
- **Binders:** Human Serum Albumin (HSA), Bovine Serum Albumin (BSA), recombinant spider silk, and urea.
- **Fabrication:** Binder solution infusion into packed regolith followed by dehydration at 65 °C.
- **Testing:** Uniaxial compression tests (ASTM C39/C39M-20) and single-lap shear adhesion tests.

## 3. Results and Discussion

### 3.2 Compressive Strength
HSA concentration positively correlates with Ultimate Compressive Strength (UCS) up to 35 wt%. Beyond this, viscosity impedes infusion. 
- **MGS-1 (35 wt% HSA):** 9.3 MPa
- **LHS-1 (35 wt% HSA):** 25.0 MPa

### 3.4 Incorporation of Urea
Urea (found in urine/sweat) acts as a protein denaturant, facilitating the reorganization of HSA into a dense β-sheet network. 
- **LHS-1 (30 wt% HSA + 3M Urea):** 39.7 MPa

### 3.6 3D-Printing
A simple square wall was 3D-printed via extrusion of MGS-1 and HSA (15 wt%). The structure sustained a force of 600 N (equivalent to 162 kg on Mars).

## 4. Conclusion
HSA and urea can produce concrete-like biocomposites with significant strength using minimal equipment. While individual crew members produce limited volumes of HSA, the material could serve as a critical mortar for sandbag-based construction or emergency repairs in situ.
