**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ynexs.2024.100030

## SUMMARY
Migration to Mars and building livable habitation gradually become prosperous and arduous roads for human progress. The Mars habitation module serves as humans' first barrier from harsh extraterrestrial conditions. This review provides a systemic outlook of promising Mars habitation environment control technologies for the sustainability of both habitation resources and occupant health, moving from "habitation design to operation."

## INTRODUCTION
Mars exploration faces tougher challenges in resource availability and human health compared to the Moon or ISS. Successful Mars dwelling involves two key aspects: resource sustainability (efficient construction and operation) and human sustainability (healthy survival). Experiences are gleaned from habitat chambers in extreme environments like the poles and deep sea.

## MARS HABITATION TECHNOLOGIES: FROM DESIGN TO OPERATION

### Design of Mars habitation module
Habitation designs are categorized into:
1. **Above-ground:** Prefab pressurized (often inflatable) cores with outer shells (e.g., Mars Ice House, Marsha).
2. **Underground/Semi-underground:** Utilizing Martian lava tubes or caverns to protect against radiation and storms.

### Construction Materials & ISRU
*   **Martian Basalt:** High radiation resistance and low permeability.
*   **Sulfur-based Concrete:** Suitable due to ease of operation and fast solidification in cold environments. Sulfur content in Mars soil averages 6 wt% (up to 37 wt%).
*   **Compressive Strength:** Sulfur-based Martian concrete has achieved 4.62 kgf/mm².
*   **3D Printing:** Robotic construction systems like "Chinese Super Mason" and Team Zopherus use 3D printing for autonomous module assembly.

### Environmental Control & Operation
*   **Thermal Control:** Use of multi-layer insulation (MLI), aerogels (ultra-low thermal conductivity), and phase change materials.
*   **Air Support:** Electrolyzed water and pressurized oxygen tanks; regenerative life support systems (CO2 removal and waste air recovery).
*   **Lighting:** Rhythmic artificial lighting to simulate natural Earth cycles and support human circadian rhythms.

## NEW PATHS OF MARS HABITATION DESIGN
*   **Performance-centered building structure:** Utilizing AI and genetic algorithms for parametric design to optimize energy consumption (reductions of ~15.8% demonstrated in terrestrial analogs).
*   **Building Integrated Photovoltaics (BIPV):** Using PV windows and walls to maximize energy collection.

## CONCLUSIONS
Mars migration faces five critical issues: module form, construction methods, materials, energy sources, and human health. A multidisciplinary approach integrating architecture, materials science, and physiology is required for long-term sustainable survival.
