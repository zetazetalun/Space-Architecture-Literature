# 3D-printed Lunar regolith simulant-based geopolymer composites with bio-inspired sandwich architectures

**Journal:** Journal of Advanced Ceramics  
**Year:** 2023, 12(3): 510–525  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.26599/JAC.2023.9220700

## Abstract
Over time, natural materials have evolved to be lightweight, high-strength, tough, and damage-tolerant due to their unique biological structures. This study reports the application of an ink-based 3D printing strategy to the structural design of a Lunar regolith simulant-based geopolymer (HIT-LRS-1 GP). High-precision carbon fiber/quartz sand-reinforced biomimetic patterns inspired by the cellular sandwich structure of plant stems were fabricated. The results showed that these architectures exhibited stable non-catastrophic fracture characteristics and effective damage tolerance. Compressive strengths of honeycomb sandwich patterns reached 46.7 MPa in the Z-axis direction with a maximum fracture strain of 12.1%.

## 1. Introduction
Human exploration of the Moon necessitates the use of In Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) to minimize transportation costs. Geopolymers are potential alternatives to cement for Moon construction because raw materials can be obtained from Lunar regolith. To overcome the inherent brittleness of geopolymers, this study explores extrinsic structural design using biomimetic patterns (honeycomb, triangular, wave, and rectangular) to improve mechanical properties and damage tolerance.

## 2. Materials and Methods

### 2.1 Preparation of Lunar regolith simulant (HIT-LRS-1)
The simulant was designed to match the regolith of the Lunar Maria terrain, referencing JSC-1A. It consists of basalt powder (40 wt%), plagioclase (20 wt%), forsterite (10 wt%), and other minerals. 

### 2.2 3D printing of geopolymer inks
Inks were prepared using HIT-LRS-1 powder, alkaline silicate solution, short carbon fibers, and quartz sand. Direct Ink Writing (DIW) was used for fabrication with a 0.82 mm diameter needle.

### 2.3 Design of biomimetic structures
Four regular cellular sandwich architectures were constructed:
1. Honeycomb
2. Triangular
3. Wave
4. Rectangular

## 4. Conclusions
The study demonstrated successful 3D printing of Lunar regolith-based geopolymer composites. The bio-inspired architectures provided a new degree of freedom for designing lightweight structural materials with tailorable strength and non-catastrophic failure characteristics, making them robust candidates for Lunar base construction.
