# 3D-printed Lunar regolith simulant-based geopolymer composites with bio-inspired sandwich architectures

## Abstract
This study reports on an ink-based 3D printing strategy for Lunar regolith simulant-based geopolymers (HIT-LRS-1 GP). It explores carbon fiber/quartz sand-reinforced biomimetic patterns inspired by plant stem cellular structures. The results demonstrate how different cellular architectures balance structure–property relationships to achieve damage tolerance in geopolymer composites.

## 1. Introduction
Lunar base construction via In Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) is essential to reduce transportation costs. Geopolymers are potential alternatives to cement because they can be synthesized using Lunar regolith (Si and Al sources) with minimal Earth-delivered materials. However, their inherent brittleness is a challenge. Bio-inspired structural designs (e.g., Bouligand-type, cellular sandwich) offer paths to improve toughness and saving consumables.

## 2. Materials and Methods
### 2.1 Preparation of Lunar regolith simulant
- **Simulant**: HIT-LRS-1 (consistent with JSC-1A).
- **Composition**: Basalt (40 wt%), plagioclase (20%), forsterite (10%), albite (5%), ilmenite (5%), hematite (5%), silica (5%), alumina (5%), and cristobalite (5%).
- **Preparation**: Ball milled at 400 r/min for 2 h, then dried at 80°C.

### 2.2 3D printing of geopolymer inks
- **Ink**: Short carbon fiber/quartz sand reinforced HIT-LRS-1 geopolymer.
- **Process**: Direct Ink Writing (DIW).
- **Curing**: Room temperature for 7 d, then 60°C for 2 d.

### 2.3 Design of biomimetic structures
Four regular cellular architectures were constructed: (1) honeycomb, (2) triangular, (3) wave, and (4) rectangular.

## 3. Results and Discussion
### 3.1 Mechanical Properties (Compression)
| Structure | Y-axis Strength (MPa) | Z-axis Strength (MPa) | Max Strain Y (%) | Max Strain Z (%) |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Honeycomb | 15.6 | 46.7 | 10.2 | 12.1 |
| Triangular | 17.9 | 26.5 | 6.7 | 13.7 |
| Wave | 11.3 | 23.8 | 5.8 | 13.6 |
| Rectangular | 20.1 | 34.4 | 5.9 | 13.9 |

### 3.2 Key Findings
- **Rheology**: Addition of quartz sand and carbon fibers significantly improves printability by increasing yield stress and viscosity.
- **Fiber Alignment**: Carbon fibers align parallel to the extrusion direction, enhancing flexural strength via pull-out effects.
- **Damage Tolerance**: Bio-inspired designs exhibit non-catastrophic failure modes, maintaining structural integrity even after initial cracking.

## 4. Conclusions
The research successfully demonstrated high-precision 3D printing of lunar regolith geopolymers. The Z-axis compressive strengths significantly outperformed standard 3D-printed cement/geopolymer materials, making these patterns robust candidates for lunar base construction.
