# 3D-printed Lunar regolith simulant-based geopolymer composites with bio-inspired sandwich architectures

**Journal:** Journal of Advanced Ceramics
**Year:** 2023
**DOI:** 10.26599/JAC.2023.9220700

## Abstract
Natural materials have evolved to be lightweight and damage-tolerant. This study reports an ink-based 3D printing strategy (Direct Ink Writing) for Lunar regolith simulant-based geopolymers (HIT-LRS-1 GP). High-precision carbon fiber/quartz sand-reinforced biomimetic patterns inspired by plant stems were fabricated. The results demonstrate that these architectures balance structure–property relationships, achieving high damage tolerance and non-catastrophic fracture characteristics.

## 1. Introduction
Space exploration necessitates the use of In Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) to minimize transportation costs. Geopolymers are identified as promising building materials for Moon bases because their raw materials can be obtained from Lunar regolith. To overcome inherent brittleness, structural design and fiber reinforcement are employed.

## 2. Materials and Methods
### 2.1 Lunar Regolith Simulant (HIT-LRS-1)
- **Reference:** JSC-1A simulant.
- **Composition:** 40 wt% basalt, 20 wt% plagioclase, 10 wt% forsterite, and other oxides (SiO2, Al2O3, Fe2O3, etc.).
- **Particle Size:** D50 = 47.3 μm.

### 2.2 3D Printing Ink Formulation
- **Matrix:** Lunar regolith simulant-based geopolymer.
- **Reinforcements:** Chopped carbon fibers (0.50–1.25 vol%) and quartz sand (10–60 wt%).
- **Method:** Direct Ink Writing (DIW) using a 0.82 mm diameter needle.

### 2.3 Biomimetic Architectures
Four cellular sandwich architectures were designed based on plant rhizomes:
1. Honeycomb (B1)
2. Triangular (B2)
3. Wave (B3)
4. Rectangular (B4)

## 3. Results and Discussion
### 3.1 Rheological Properties
Adding quartz sand and carbon fibers increased initial viscosity by 2–3 orders of magnitude. The 1Csf/20QS@HIT-LRS-1 GP ink exhibited optimal printability with a shear yield stress (τ0) of ~8628 Pa.

### 3.2 Mechanical Properties
- **Flexural Strength:** Maximum of 41.6 MPa reached with 1.00 vol% carbon fiber (50.7% increase over unreinforced geopolymer).
- **Compression Anisotropy:** Compressive strengths were significantly higher in the Z-axis (printing direction) compared to the Y-axis.

| Structure | Compressive Strength Y-axis (MPa) | Compressive Strength Z-axis (MPa) | Max Strain Y (%) | Max Strain Z (%) |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Honeycomb | 15.6 | 46.7 | 10.2 | 12.1 |
| Triangular | 17.9 | 26.5 | 6.7 | 13.7 |
| Wave | 11.3 | 23.8 | 5.8 | 13.6 |
| Rectangular | 20.1 | 34.4 | 5.9 | 13.9 |

## 4. Conclusions
The study successfully demonstrated DIW of HIT-LRS-1 based geopolymer composites. The bio-inspired sandwich patterns provided stable non-catastrophic failure characteristics and excellent energy absorption, making them robust candidates for Lunar base construction.
