# 3D-printed Lunar regolith simulant-based geopolymer composites with bio-inspired sandwich architectures

**Journal:** Journal of Advanced Ceramics  
**Volume/Issue:** 2023, 12(3): 510–525  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.26599/JAC.2023.9220700  

## Abstract
Over time, natural materials have evolved to be lightweight, high-strength, tough, and damage-tolerant due to their unique biological structures. This study reports an ink-based three-dimensional (3D) printing strategy for the structural design of a Lunar regolith simulant-based geopolymer (HIT-LRS-1 GP). High-precision carbon fiber/quartz sand-reinforced biomimetic patterns inspired by plant stem cellular sandwich structures were fabricated. Components exhibited stable non-catastrophic fracture characteristics and significant anisotropy in mechanical properties. Compressive strengths in the Z-axis reached up to 46.7 MPa (honeycomb) and 34.4 MPa (rectangular), with high fracture strains (up to 13.9%).

## 1. Introduction
Lunar base construction is prioritized using In Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) to minimize transportation costs. Geopolymers are identified as a potential alternative to cement due to low density, thermal conductivity, and the suitability of lunar regolith for geopolymerization (providing Si and Al sources). To address the brittleness of geopolymers, this study combines fiber reinforcement (carbon fiber) with bio-inspired structural design (honeycomb, triangular, wave, and rectangular sandwich patterns).

## 2. Materials and Methods

### 2.1 Preparation of Lunar Regolith Simulant (HIT-LRS-1)
The simulant was designed based on the Maria geological terrain of the Moon, similar to JSC-1A. Components include basalt powder (40 wt%), plagioclase (20 wt%), forsterite (10 wt%), and others (albite, ilmenite, hematite, silica, alumina, and cristobalite).

### 2.2 3D Printing and Ink Preparation
Inks were prepared using HIT-LRS-1 powder, alkaline silicate solution (NaOH + sol-silica), and reinforcements: chopped carbon fibers (600 μm length) and quartz sand (fine aggregate). Direct Ink Writing (DIW) used a 0.82 mm nozzle, 40 mm/s speed, and 70-80 psi pressure.

## 3. Results and Discussion

### 3.1 Mechanical Performance
The composites exhibited pattern-dependent mechanical properties. The Z-axis (building direction) compressive strength was significantly higher than the Y-axis strength for all structures.
- **Honeycomb (B1):** 15.6 MPa (Y), 46.7 MPa (Z)
- **Triangular (B2):** 17.9 MPa (Y), 26.5 MPa (Z)
- **Wave (B3):** 11.3 MPa (Y), 23.8 MPa (Z)
- **Rectangular (B4):** 20.1 MPa (Y), 34.4 MPa (Z)

## 4. Conclusions
Successful 3D printing of Csf/QS@HIT-LRS-1 GP composites with biomimetic patterns was achieved. The use of carbon fiber (1.00 vol%) improved flexural strength to 41.6 MPa (a 50.7% increase). The bio-inspired structures provide excellent damage tolerance and energy absorption, making them robust candidates for lunar base construction.
