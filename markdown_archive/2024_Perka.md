## Abstract
In the construction industry, wire-based steel 3D printing (WBS-3DP) technology for structural parts has yet to be explored. This research discusses the application of wire-arc additive manufacturing (WAAM) in the fabrication of space frame structures for T-K-Y joints with structural integrity. A space frame structure was designed with multi-branch tube connectors using WBS-3DP technology. These connectors accommodate steel tubes at arbitrary angles, creating smooth and lightweight joints. The study demonstrates how the WAAM process can produce efficient structures by eliminating traditional tube joining and welding challenges. It concludes that WBS-3DP has the potential to revolutionize construction through innovative part production and improved digital techniques.

## 1 Introduction
Additive Manufacturing (AM) is a layer-based fabrication process depositing material based on 3D computer models. While aerospace and automotive industries have embraced metal 3D printing, the construction industry has been slower due to part size and load capacity requirements. Steel tubes are standard in trusses and space frames, but traditional welding at T, K, and Y connections (TKY joints) is complex, requiring skilled manual labor and facing issues like stress concentration and residual stress.

## 2 Materials and Methods

### 2.1 Prototype for Tubular Joints Design
A prototype spaceframe structure was designed to accommodate all T-K and Y-type connections (Fig. 3). Lower tubes had a 114 mm diameter (5.4 mm thick), while upper tubes were 76 mm (4.5 mm thick). Altair Inspire Print3D was used for topology optimization to reduce material usage and print time.

### 2.2 Tubular Joining Using Additive Manufacturing
Printing was conducted at Tata Steel's Centre for Advanced Welding and Joining (CAWJ) using a robotic WAAM system consisting of a Metal Inert Gas (MIG) welding torch on a 6-axis robotic arm.

### 2.3 Printing Process
Tool paths were generated via Autodesk PowerMill. Carbon steel wire (0.8 mm dia) was used with 82% Ar / 18% CO2 shielding gas. Interpass temperature was kept below 120°C.

Mechanical testing (tensile) showed isotropic properties with yield strengths between 395-405 MPa and UTS between 497-507 MPa. Microstructural analysis revealed an equiaxed ferrite matrix with 7-10% pearlite. Repeatability of the WAAM process was approximately 99% based on part weights.

## 4 Conclusions
This research successfully demonstrated a digital setup for integrated WAAM production of space frame connectors. The printed connectors achieved better tolerances and mechanical properties matching conventional steel. This method reduces welding difficulty levels (from complex 6GR to simpler 5F joints) and addresses labor shortages through automation.
