## Abstract
In the construction industry, wire-based steel 3D printing (WBS-3DP) technology for structural parts has yet to be explored. As 3D printing becomes highly productive and cost-effective, it opens a wide range of opportunities for the steel-based additive construction segment. The major advantage of large-scale 3D printing techniques like wire-arc additive manufacturing (WAAM) is the ability to develop components that are more compatible through topology optimization and with the mechanical requirements of structural standards. This research discusses the application of WAAM in the fabrication of space frame structures for T-K-Y joints with structural integrity. It concludes that WBS-3DP has the potential to revolutionize construction by producing innovative parts such as tube connectors with ease of fabrication.

## Introduction
Additive Manufacturing (AM) involves depositing thin layers of material together based on a 3D computer model. While aerospace and automotive industries have embraced metal 3D printing, the construction industry has moved slower due to part size and load capacity requirements. Steel tubes are frequently used in space frames, but traditional T-K-Y joint fabrication (profile cutting, edge preparation, manual welding) is complex and prone to defects. 3D printing helps resolve these challenges by manufacturing complex shapes with better process control.

## Materials and Methods

### 2.1 Prototype for Tubular Joints Design
A prototype spaceframe structure model was designed to accommodate all possible types of T-K and Y-type tube connections. A topology optimization tool (Altair Inspire Print3D) was used for the nodes to reduce material usage and print time.

### 2.2 Tubular Joining Using Additive Manufacturing
The printing was conducted at the Centre for Advanced Welding and Joining (CAWJ). The facility uses a robotic WAAM system consisting of Metal Inert Gas (MIG) welding connected to a 6-axis robotic arm.

### 2.3 Printing Process
- **Feedstock:** Carbon steel wire (0.8 mm diameter).
- **Shielding Gas:** 82% Ar / 18% CO2 at 15 l/min.
- **Parameters:** Bead height of 2 mm, width of 6 mm.
- **Interpass Temperature:** Kept below 120 °C.

### Mechanical Properties
Tensile test results showed isotropic nature with no significant difference based on direction:
- **Yield Strength:** 395–405 MPa
- **Tensile Strength:** 497–507 MPa
- **Elongation:** 32–40%

### Microstructure
Samples showed an equiaxed ferrite matrix with 7–10% pearlite. No directional solidification was observed due to the 90° rotation strategy between layers.

## Conclusions
This research successfully demonstrated the feasibility of using WAAM to produce six types of T-K-Y connectors for space frame structures. The printed connectors achieve mechanical properties matching conventional tubular steels and can be installed with better tolerances, reducing manual welding labor for complex 6GR joints by converting them to simpler 5F joints.
