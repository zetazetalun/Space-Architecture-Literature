# Review of research into bioregenerative life support system(s) which can support humans living in space

## Metadata
- **Journal:** Life Sciences in Space Research 31 (2021) 113–120
- **Authors:** Hong Liu, Zhikai Yao, Yuming Fu, Jiajie Feng
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.lssr.2021.09.003

## Abstract
To travel beyond the Earth and realize long-term survival in deep space, humans need to construct Bioregenerative Life Support Systems (BLSS). This review covers systematic theories, unit technologies, design/construction, and long-term operation. China's 'Lunar Palace 365' experiment realized Earth-based closed human survival for a year with material closure >98%. Future research will focus on lunar probe payloads to study space environmental impacts on these ecosystems.

## 1. Introduction
BLSS is a key technology for crewed deep space exploration, providing oxygen, water, and food through in situ circulation. It is an artificial closed ecosystem composed of producers (plants), consumers (humans/animals), and decomposers (microorganisms).

## 2. BLSS Framework and Development Path
Theories include biology, environmental engineering, ecology, and materials science. Research moves from individual units to artificial ecosystems, followed by Earth-based experimental facilities and finally extraterrestrial application.

## 3. Research Progress of Earth-based BLSS
### 3.1. Soviet Union/Russia
- **BIOS-3:** 315 m³ volume. Achieved >90% closure in gas/water and 70% food for two people.
### 3.2. United States
- **CELSS/ECLSS:** Development of Advanced Life Support (ALS) for lunar/Martian bases.
- **Biosphere 2:** Largest closed system (1.3 hectares). Produced 83% to 100% of food needs in different experiments.
- **Lunar Greenhouse (LGH):** Prototype deployable greenhouse for Moon/Mars missions.
### 3.3. Japan
- **CEEF:** Introduced animal units (goats). Conducted 'human-goat-higher plants' experiments.
### 3.4. Europe
- **MELiSSA:** ESA project focusing on five interconnected compartments (waste decomposition to crew).
- **DLR:** Studied structural design of lunar greenhouse modules (rigid-inflatable hybrid structures).
### 3.5. China
- **Lunar Palace 1:** First crewed high-closure experiment (105 days in 2014; 370 days in 2017-2018). Achieved 98.2% total material closure.

## 4. Prospect of Spatial BLSS Experimental Research
Lunar polar regions are ideal due to continuous illumination (75-90% of the year), facilitating 'seed to seed' cycles. In-situ resources (lunar soil, water ice) must be utilized to reduce construction costs.

## 5. Development Path of Extraterrestrial BLSS
1. **Stage 1:** Hydroponics and limited ISRU (lunar/Martian soil) for soil-like substrates.
2. **Stage 2:** Expansion of soil-like substrates and inclusion of animal protein (e.g., mealworms).
3. **Stage 3:** Large-scale bases with recycled inorganic salts and artificial fish-farming oceans.

## 6. Evaluation Indices
1. Regeneration rates (%) of O2, H2O, and food.
2. Stability and reliability.
3. Energy/Space per person (kWh·m³/person).
4. Equipment weight per person (kg/person).
5. Labor intensity (hour/person).
