# Review of research into bioregenerative life support system(s) which can support humans living in space

**Authors:** Hong Liu, Zhikai Yao, Yuming Fu, Jiajie Feng  
**Journal:** Life Sciences in Space Research 31 (2021) 113–120  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.lssr.2021.09.003

## Abstract
To realize long-term survival in deep space, humans must construct Bioregenerative Life Support Systems (BLSS) to recycle oxygen, water, and food. This review covers historical Earth-based experiments (BIOS-3, Biosphere 2, MELiSSA, Lunar Palace 1) and outlines future paths for extraterrestrial application, particularly focusing on the Moon's polar regions.

## 1. Introduction
BLSS is an artificial closed ecosystem composed of producers (plants), consumers (humans/animals), and decomposers (microorganisms). It is a key technology for autonomous existence in deep space exploration, minimizing Earth-reliance through in situ regeneration.

## 2. BLSS Framework
The system is multidisciplinary, involving biology, environmental engineering, ecology, and materials science. Research development proceeds from unit technologies to artificial ecosystem theories, then Earth-based experimental facilities, and finally extraterrestrial application.

## 3. Earth-based BLSS Research Progress

### 3.1 Soviet Union/Russia (BIOS-1, 2, 3)
*   **BIOS-3:** 315 m³ volume, achieved 95% closure in some experiments. Met 40-45% food needs for 3 people or 70% for 2 people.

### 3.2 United States (CELSS, LMLSTP, Biosphere 2)
*   **LMLSTP:** Integrated biological and physical/chemical technologies; achieved 91-day closure for 4 people.
*   **Biosphere 2:** 1.3 hectares, 180,000 m³. First experiment produced 83% food; second achieved 100% food production.
*   **Lunar Greenhouse (LGH):** NASA-sponsored prototype for deployable habitats.

### 3.3 Japan (CEEF/EEF)
*   Introduced goats as an animal unit. Used physical/chemical equipment for rapid material circulation.

### 3.4 Europe (MELiSSA)
*   Focuses on a five-compartment strategy including waste decomposition (thermophilic/anaerobic) and photosynthetic food production.
*   **DLR Study:** Designed a lunar greenhouse module: semi-cylindrical hybrid structure (rigid end-caps, inflatable body), 20m length, 3m radius.

### 3.5 China (Lunar Palace 1)
*   **Lunar Palace 365:** 370-day experiment (2017-2018). Achieved 100% O2/Water regeneration, 83% food regeneration, and 98.2% total material closure with 4 crew members.

## 4. Development Path for Extraterrestrial BLSS

### Three-Stage Strategy:
1.  **First Stage (Outposts):** Hydroponics-based; limited use of in situ resources (lunar/Martian soil) mixed with waste for substrates. Physical/chemical methods provide redundancy.
2.  **Second Stage (Permanent Bases):** Large-scale soil-like substrates from regolith. Introduction of animals (insects like mealworms) for protein.
3.  **Third Stage (Large-scale Bases):** Full scale; inorganic salt cycling; artificial oceans for fish cultivation; 100% biological regeneration of air/water.

## 5. Evaluation Indices for BLSS
1.  **Regeneration Rate (%):** Food, O2, water closure.
2.  **Stability and Reliability.**
3.  **Energy and Space Requirements:** (kWh•m³/person).
4.  **Equipment Weight:** (kg/person).
5.  **Labor Intensity:** (hour/person).

## 6. Key Conditions for Gas Balance (Corrigendum)
1.  If food comes from internal biosynthesis, O2 production is sufficient for breathing.
2.  External organic matter input must be balanced by internal respiration quotient outputs.
3.  Gas balance cannot be achieved by biological self-organization alone; it requires human intervention.
