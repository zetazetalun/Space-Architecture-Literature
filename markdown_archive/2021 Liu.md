# Review of research into bioregenerative life support system(s) which can support humans living in space

## Abstract
To travel beyond the Earth and realize long-term survival in deep space, humans need to construct Bioregenerative Life Support System (BLSS), which reduces the requirement for supplies from the Earth by in situ regenerating oxygen, water and food needed by astronauts, and prevents pollution to extraterrestrial bodies by recycling waste. China’s “Lunar Palace 365” experiment realized Earth-based closed human survival for a year, with a material closure of >98%.

## 1. Introduction
BLSS is an artificial closed ecosystem composed of humans, plants, animals and microorganisms based on ecological principles. It is one of the key technologies for crewed deep space exploration, supporting long-term autonomous existence of humans.

## 2. BLSS’s Theoretical and Technological System Framework
The framework includes key technologies such as:
- Animal cultivation
- Plant cultivation
- Waste treatment
- Microbial control
- Trace gas control

## 3. Research Progress of Earth-Based BLSS
### 3.1. Soviet Union/Russia
- **BIOS-3:** 315 m³ volume. Achieved >90% closure in terms of gas and water circulation and met 40–45% of food needs for three people.

### 3.2. United States
- **NASA Projects:** CELSS, ECLSS, ALS, and LMLSTP.
- **HDU-DSH:** Habitat Demonstration Unit in the Arizona desert.
- **Lunar Greenhouse (LGH):** Development of prototype deployable structures.
- **Biosphere 2:** Largest closed ecological system facility (1.3 hectares, 1.8 x 10⁵ m³).

### 3.3. Japan
- **CEEF:** Included the first animal unit (goats).

### 3.4. Europe
- **MELiSSA:** ESA-managed micro-ecological life support system alternative.
- **DLR Greenhouse:** Optimal structural design of a semi-cylindrical hybrid structure (rigid end-caps, 20 m length, 3 m radius).

### 3.5. China
- **Lunar Palace 1:** First large-scale crewed comprehensive BLSS in China. Achieved 100% regeneration of oxygen and water, and 55% regeneration of food (97% overall closure).
- **Lunar Palace 365:** 370-day experiment with 98.2% material closure.

## 5. Development Path of Extraterrestrial BLSS
The strategy is summarized in three stages:
1. **First Stage:** Hydroponics and processing of in situ resources (Lunar/Martian soil) with wastes to prepare soil-like substrates.
2. **Second Stage:** Expansion of BLSS using large amounts of soil-like substrates prepared from in situ resources; introduction of insects (mealworms) for protein.
3. **Third Stage:** Large-scale bases with cycled inorganic salts and fish cultivation; 100% biological regeneration of water and oxygen.

### Evaluation Indices
1. Regeneration rate (%) and material closure.
2. Stability and reliability.
3. Energy consumption and space (kWh·m³/person).
4. Equipment weight (kg/person).
5. Labor intensity (hour/person).
