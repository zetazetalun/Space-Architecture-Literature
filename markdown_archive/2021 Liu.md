# Review of research into bioregenerative life support system(s) which can support humans living in space

**Authors:** Hong Liu, Zhikai Yao, Yuming Fu, Jiajie Feng  
**Journal:** Life Sciences in Space Research 31 (2021) 113–120  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.lssr.2021.09.003

## Abstract
To travel beyond Earth and realize long-term survival in deep space, humans need to construct Bioregenerative Life Support Systems (BLSS). These systems reduce the requirement for Earth-based supplies by in situ regenerating oxygen, water, and food, and recycling waste. This paper reviews the progress made by the USSR/Russia, USA, Europe, Japan, and China in BLSS development, including Earth-based simulations like "Lunar Palace 365."

## 1. Introduction
BLSS is an artificial closed ecosystem composed of humans, plants, animals, and microorganisms. It mirrors Earth's ecosystem structure (producer, consumer, decomposer) to provide a comfortable environment for deep space exploration, supporting autonomous human existence.

## 2. BLSS Theoretical and Technological Framework
The framework is multidisciplinary, involving biology, environmental engineering, ecology, materials science, and systems engineering. The R&D path moves from individual unit technologies to complex integrated ecosystem research with human participation.

## 3. Research Progress of Earth-based BLSS

### 3.1 Soviet Union/Russia
*   **BIOS-1, 2, & 3:** Initiated in the 1960s. BIOS-3 achieved a 95% closure rate for gas and water and met 40–70% of food needs for a crew of three.

### 3.2 United States
*   **CELSS & ALS:** NASA projects focused on missions to Lunar and Martian bases.
*   **LMLSTP:** Integrated biological and physical/chemical technologies; achieved 91-day survival.
*   **Biosphere 2:** The largest closed ecosystem facility (1.3 hectares), testing global ecological processes and long-term habitation.

### 3.3 Japan
*   **CEEF (Closed Ecology Experiment Facilities):** Notable for introducing animal units (goats) into the loop and utilizing physical/chemical methods for rapid material circulation.

### 3.4 Europe
*   **MELiSSA (Micro-Ecological Life Support System Alternative):** Managed by ESA, focusing on a multi-compartment system (waste decomposition, nitrification, photosynthetic food production).
*   **DLR Research:** Optimized structural designs for lunar greenhouse modules, assessing rigid-inflatable hybrid structures.

### 3.5 China
*   **Lunar Palace 1 & Lunar Palace 365:** A 370-day experiment with 4 crew members. Achieved 100% regeneration of O2 and water, 83% of food, and an overall material closure of 98.2%.

## 4. Prospect of Spatial BLSS Experimental Research
Future research must focus on the impact of the space environment (gravity, radiation, magnetic fields) on these ecosystems. The Moon's polar regions are identified as ideal sites due to continuous illumination and potential water ice resources.

## 5. Development Path of Extraterrestrial BLSS
The transition is categorized into three stages:
1.  **Outposts:** Hydroponics, limited plant food, water/gas recycling.
2.  **Permanent Bases:** Solid waste cycling, using in situ resources (lunar/Martian soil) to create soil-like substrates, animal protein production.
3.  **Large-scale Bases:** Full material cycling including inorganic salts, fish cultivation, and 100% biological regeneration.

## 6. Evaluation Indices
1.  Regeneration rate (%) and material closure.
2.  Stability and reliability.
3.  Energy and space requirements (kWh·m³/person).
4.  Equipment weight per person.
5.  Labor intensity (hour/person).
