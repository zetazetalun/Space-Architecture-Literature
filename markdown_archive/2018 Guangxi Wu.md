# Fabrication of Flexible Thermoelectric Energy Harvesting System

**Authors:** Guangxi Wu, Ferin Neff, Xiong (Bill) Yu
**Published in:** Earth and Space 2018 (ASCE)

## Abstract
This paper describes procedures incurred in the fabrication of thermoelectric energy harvesters on flexible substrates. An electrically parallel structure is proposed, which features higher energy efficiency than the counterpart electrically series structure based on theoretical analyses. Flexible thermoelectric harvesters in electrically parallel and series structures are prepared with thick film printing procedures. Analytical and experimental comparisons imply a promising improvement of energy conversion efficiency by using the electrically parallel structure.

## Introduction
Thermoelectric generators (TEGs) provide continuous energy for extraterrestrial explorations where sunlight is insufficient (e.g., Mars, Jupiter). Radioisotope thermoelectric generators (RTGs) have been used for decades due to reliability. This research explores leveraging the naturally existing temperature gradients on spacecraft or extraterrestrial vehicle bodies using flexible TEGs.

## Methodology
- **Fabrication Process:** Stencil printing (thick film printing) on polyimide substrates.
- **Materials:** Bi2Te3 (p-type and n-type) powder ball-milled into nanocomposites.
- **Ink Preparation:** Ground TE powder used as filler in an epoxy matrix (EPON Resin 863) with a 4.5:1 ratio.
- **Device Architecture:** Comparison between traditional electrically series and proposed electrically parallel TEMs.

## Key Findings
1. **Structure Optimization:** Electrically parallel TEM architectures reduce efficiency losses caused by material property mismatches between p-type and n-type semiconductor legs.
2. **Printed Manufacturing:** Flexible TEMs were successfully fabricated using an MPM TF-100 Thick Film Printer, demonstrating the potential for automated printing of energy systems on flexible surfaces.
3. **Performance Characteristics:** The n-type electrically parallel structure demonstrated higher output power than the p-type parallel structure and the traditional series structure in experimental characterization.

## Conclusion
Electrically parallel TEMs are a prospective solution to improve energy conversion efficiency and simplify the fabrication process for large-area implementation on planetary surfaces.
