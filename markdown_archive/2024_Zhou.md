# Properties and Characteristics of Regolith-Based Materials for Extraterrestrial Construction

**Authors:** Cheng Zhou, Yuyue Gao, Yan Zhou, Wei She, Yusheng Shi, Lieyun Ding, Changwen Miao
**Journal:** Engineering 37 (2024) 159–181

## Abstract
The construction of extraterrestrial bases has become a new goal in deep space exploration. In situ resource-based (ISRU) construction is highly promising due to sustainability and economic factors. This paper surveys 13 types of construction materials, comparing their performance in mechanical, thermal, optical, and radiation-shielding properties within extreme environments. 

## 1. Introduction
National agencies (NASA, CNSA, Roscosmos) are planning lunar and Martian infrastructures like the Artemis base camp and International Lunar Research Station. ISRU using regolith is critical to avoid high transportation costs from Earth.

## 2. Construction Materials and Processes
Materials are categorized into two main groups:
1. **Concrete Materials:** Sulfur, biopolymer, geopolymer, polymer, silicate, and magnesium-based.
2. **Sintered/Molten Materials:** Hot pressed sintering, microwave sintering, spark plasma sintering (SPS), laser sintering, and digital light processing (DLP).

## 3. Mechanical Properties
Mechanical reliability is measured through compressive, flexural, and tensile strengths.
- **Sintered Samples:** Compressive strengths range from 2.31 to 428.10 MPa.
- **Concrete Samples:** Compressive strengths range from 0.90 to 76.80 MPa.
- **Top Performers:** DLP and Magnesium concrete show superior mechanical properties. Solar sintering and polymer concrete typically exhibit lower relative performance.

## 4. Thermal and Optical Properties
Materials must withstand extreme temperature fluctuations (96 K to 397 K on the Moon). 
- **Thermal Conductivity:** Sintered samples range from 0.525 to 1.200 W/(m·K).
- **CTE:** Microwave-sintered samples have a Coefficient of Thermal Expansion (CTE) of approximately 5 x 10^-6 /°C, similar to lunar basaltic breccias.

## 5. Radiation Shielding
- **Proton Shielding:** Geopolymer concrete with 50-100 cm thickness provides significant protection for long-term residency.
- **Neutron Shielding:** 80 cm of sintered regolith significantly improves shielding effectiveness.
- **Heavy Ion Shielding:** 46 cm of compacted powder or 150 cm of loose regolith can stop primary GCR ions.

## 6. Challenges and Future Directions
1. **Comprehensive Characterization:** Need for testing under coupled fields (radiation + thermal + vacuum).
2. **Long-term Durability:** Most tests are short-term (hours/days); real service life needs evaluation.
3. **Extraterrestrial Experiments:** Transitioning from lab simulants to real-space testing.
