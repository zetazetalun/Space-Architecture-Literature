# Properties and Characteristics of Regolith-Based Materials for Extraterrestrial Construction

**Authors:** Cheng Zhou, Yuyue Gao, Yan Zhou, Wei She, Yusheng Shi, Lieyun Ding, Changwen Miao
**Journal:** Engineering 37 (2024) 159–181
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.eng.2023.11.019

## Abstract
The construction of extraterrestrial bases has become a new goal in the active exploration of deep space. Among the construction techniques, in situ resource-based construction is one of the most promising because of its good sustainability and acceptable economic cost. A comprehensive survey and comparison of 13 types of typical construction materials are discussed in terms of their reliability and applicability in extreme extraterrestrial environments. Mechanical, thermal and optical, and radiation-shielding properties are considered. 

## 1. Introduction
Future exploration plans, including NASA's Artemis plan and the International Lunar Research Station (ILRS) by CNSA and Roscosmos, necessitate the establishment of infrastructure on extraterrestrial bodies. ISRU methods are favored due to the high cost of transportation. Regolith is the primary raw material available.

## 2. Construction Materials and Processes

### 2.1. Construction Feasibility
Extraterrestrial environments pose challenges: low gravity, hard vacuum, temperature fluctuations, space debris, and extreme radiation.

### 2.2. Typical Regolith Simulants
Various simulants have been developed to mimic lunar (e.g., JSC-1, MLS-1, FJS-1) and Martian (e.g., JSC Mars-1, MMS, MGS-1) regolith properties.

### 2.3. Processing Methods
| Preparation Process | Brief Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| SPS | Uniaxial pressure and electric field applied to regolith powders |
| Microwave sintering | Regolith absorbs electromagnetic energy for heat conversion |
| Laser sintering | High-brilliance laser beam selectively sinters layers |
| DLP | Slurry of regolith and photocurable resins hardened with UV |
| Solar sintering | Concentrated solar light utilized to sinter layers |
| Geopolymer concrete | Regolith activated by alkaline solution |
| Sulfur concrete | Regolith bonded by sulfur |
| Biopolymer concrete | Regolith mixed with biopolymer binder and water |

## 4. Thermal and Optical Properties
Thermal insulation is critical for indoor comfort. Porosity is a key factor; higher porosity improves thermal insulation but reduces mechanical strength. CTE (Coefficient of Thermal Expansion) for microwave-sintered lunar samples was found to be approximately 5 x 10⁻⁶ /°C.

## 5. Radiation Shielding
A regolith layer of 50–100 cm is recommended to provide sufficient shielding for long-term residency. Loose regolith of 150 cm can protect against GCR neutrons.

## 6. Conclusions and Future Prospects
Future directions include high-throughput characterization, accelerated durability tests, and real extraterrestrial experiments on the Moon or ISS.
