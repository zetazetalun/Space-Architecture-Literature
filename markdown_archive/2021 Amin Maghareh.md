# Role of Cyber-Physical Testing in Developing Resilient Extraterrestrial Habitats

**Authors:** Amin Maghareh, Ali Lenjani, Murali Krishnan, Shirley Dyke, and Ilias Bilionis  
**Affiliation:** School of Mechanical Engineering, Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN 47906  
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE

## Abstract
Extraterrestrial long-term habitat systems require groundbreaking technological advances to overcome extreme demands from isolation and challenging environments. This study highlights the importance of system resilience and cyber-physical testing to address the grand challenge of developing habitat systems that can adapt to disruptions such as temperature fluctuations, cosmic rays, dust, and meteoroid impacts.

## Introduction
NASA’s 2015 plan for establishing long-term settlements on Mars emphasizes the capacity for people to work and live sustainably beyond Earth. Establishing permanent settlements requires habitat systems that function under continuous disruptive conditions with limited resources.

### Environmental Hazards
- **Radiation:** High-energy Galactic Cosmic Rays (GCR) and Solar Particle Events (SPE).
- **Temperature:** Fluctuations from -171°C to 111°C lead to material fatigue.
- **Dust:** Causes malfunction of mechanical/electronic devices and power loss in solar panels.
- **Seismic:** Moonquakes can last for hours; meteorite impacts pose catastrophic risks.

## Resilience and Habitat Design
Traditional reliability-based design is insufficient for deep space because it focuses on anticipated failures and is often too costly. The paper proposes a **control-theoretic approach** to resilience, maintaining performance within a dynamic safe boundary through:
1. **Passive controls** (preventive capability).
2. **Adaptive controls** (interventive and mitigative capabilities).

Key objectives include maximizing **system plasticity** (ability to prevent loss) and **system rapidity** (timely restoration), while minimizing lifecycle costs.

## Cyber-Physical Testing (CPT)
CPT, or hybrid simulation, integrates physical testing of novel components with computational modeling of established systems. This allows for realistic examination of complex interactions at a lower cost and time than full-scale physical testing. 

### The RETHi Institute
The Resilient ExtraTerrestrial Habitats research institute (RETHi) is developing a high-fidelity CPT platform to:
- Model dynamic reconfiguration of habitats.
- Validate health management frameworks.
- Represent combined disturbances (e.g., radiation + loss of atmosphere).
- Simulate the accelerated passage of time.

## Conclusion
Resilience is critical for habitat success. This study advocates for a comprehensive approach using cyber-physical testing of interconnected habitat systems to address challenges in design and operational adaptation.
