# Role of Cyber-Physical Testing in Developing Resilient Extraterrestrial Habitats

**Authors:** Amin Maghareh, Ali Lenjani, Murali Krishnan, Shirley Dyke, and Ilias Bilionis  
**Affiliation:** School of Mechanical Engineering, Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN 47906  
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE

## Abstract
Extraterrestrial long-term habitat systems require groundbreaking technological advances to overcome extreme demands. A habitat system must operate as intended under continuous disruptive conditions such as wild temperature fluctuations, galactic cosmic rays, destructive dust, and meteoroid impacts. This study highlights the importance of system resilience and cyber-physical testing to address the grand challenge of developing habitat systems, moving beyond simple reliability to an adaptive, resilient approach.

## Introduction
NASA’s plan for long-term settlements on Mars (NASA 2015) underscores the need for people to work and sustainably live beyond Earth. Extraterrestrial habitat systems face unprecedented demands including isolation and extreme environments. Resilience is defined as the capacity to be absorptive, restorative, and adaptive. Cyber-physical testing integrates physical testing and computational modeling to enable researchers to refine their understanding of system performance under extreme conditions with reduced time and cost.

### Hazards
- **Radiation:** GCR (Galactic Cosmic Rays) and SPE (Solar Particle Events) impact survivability and component functionality.
- **Temperature:** Extreme fluctuations lead to material fatigue (e.g., Moon transition of 5 °C/hr).
- **Dust:** Causes malfunction of mechanical/electronic devices and power loss in solar panels.
- **Seismic:** Moonquakes (deep, thermal, impact, and shallow) can last for hours, unlike Earth's minutes.

## Resilience and System Failures
Habitat systems are complex and tightly-coupled. Reliability-based design (focused on probability and redundancy) is insufficient for extraterrestrial habitats because disruptions are inevitable and unpredictable. A **control-theoretic approach** is proposed, prioritizing:
1. **System Plasticity:** Ability to prevent performance loss.
2. **System Rapidity:** Timely restoration of performance.
3. **Lifecycle Cost:** Sum of development and implementation costs.

## Cyber-Physical Testing of Habitat Systems
Cyber-physical testing (also known as hybrid simulation) partitions a system into:
- **Computational Subsystems:** Reliable/accurate models of the majority of the system.
- **Physical Subsystems:** Physical specimens of novel or difficult-to-model components.

This method allows for large-scale experiments that would otherwise be cost-prohibitive. The **Resilient ExtraTerrestrial Habitats research institute (RETHi)** is currently developing such a platform to validate health management models and autonomous habitat reconfiguration.

## Conclusion
Resilience is the key contributor to success for habitat systems. The study advocates for a comprehensive approach that accounts for disruptions during design and adapts during operation, utilizing cyber-physical testing as a critical validation tool.
