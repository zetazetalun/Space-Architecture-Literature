# Role of Cyber-Physical Testing in Developing Resilient Extraterrestrial Habitats

**Authors:** Amin Maghareh, Ali Lenjani, Murali Krishnan, Shirley Dyke, and Ilias Bilionis  
**Affiliation:** School of Mechanical Engineering, Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN 47906  
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021

## Abstract
Extraterrestrial long-term habitat systems require groundbreaking technological advances to overcome the extreme demands introduced by isolation and challenging environments. A habitat system must operate as intended under continuous disruptive conditions. Designing for the demands that challenging environments will place on habitat systems (e.g., temperature fluctuations, galactic cosmic rays, destructive dust, meteoroid impacts, vibrations, and solar particle events) represents one of the greatest challenges. This engineering problem necessitates that we design and manage habitat systems to be resilient. This study highlights the importance of system resilience and cyber-physical testing to address the grand challenge of developing habitat systems.

## Introduction
The quest for long-term settlements on the Moon and Mars requires habitats that can sustainably support human life beyond Earth. Habitat systems face unprecedented demands from isolation and extreme environments, including temperature fluctuations, radiation, dust, and meteoroid impacts. These systems are complex and exhibit unpredictable interactions, requiring a shift from traditional reliability-based design to resilience-oriented design.

### Hazards
- **Radiation:** High-energy galactic cosmic rays (GCR) and solar particle events (SPE).
- **Thermal Fluctuations:** Extreme ranges causing material fatigue (e.g., -171°C to 111°C on the Moon).
- **Dust:** Impacts mechanical/electronic devices and solar power generation.
- **Seismic Activity:** Moonquakes (deep, thermal, impact-related, and shallow) can last for hours.
- **Meteoroid Impacts:** Hypervelocity impacts pose catastrophic threats due to lack of atmospheric shielding.

## Resilience and System Failure
Resilience is defined by three conceptual capacities: absorptive, restorative, and adaptive. Reliability-based design (minimizing failure probability) is insufficient for habitat systems because disruptions are inevitable and unpredictable. A control-theoretic approach is proposed to maintain performance through passive and adaptive controls within a dynamic safe boundary.

## Cyber-Physical Testing
Cyber-physical testing (hybrid simulation) integrates physical testing of novel components with computational modeling of the rest of the system. This approach allows for:
1. Examination of emergent behaviors in complex interconnected systems.
2. Realistic validation under hazardous conditions at a reduced cost.
3. Modeling dynamic reconfiguration and health management frameworks.

## Conclusion
Resilience is a key contributor to the success of extraterrestrial habitats. Countering extreme challenges requires innovative techniques like cyber-physical testing to account for disruptions throughout the design process and operational life cycle. The Resilient ExtraTerrestrial Habitats research institute (RETHi) is currently developing these platforms to validate resilient habitat designs.
