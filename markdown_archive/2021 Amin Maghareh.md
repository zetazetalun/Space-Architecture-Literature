# Role of Cyber-Physical Testing in Developing Resilient Extraterrestrial Habitats\n\n**Authors:** Amin Maghareh, Ali Lenjani, Murali Krishnan, Shirley Dyke, and Ilias Bilionis  \n**Affiliation:** School of Mechanical Engineering, Purdue University\n\n## Abstract\nExtraterrestrial long-term habitat systems require groundbreaking technological advances to overcome extreme demands like temperature fluctuations, radiation, dust, and meteoroid impacts. This paper highlights the importance of system resilience and cyber-physical testing as a comprehensive approach to design, manage, and validate habitat systems that can adapt to and recover from disruptions.\n\n## Introduction\nEstablishing permanent human settlements on the Moon and Mars necessitates habitat systems that function under continuous disruptive conditions with limited resources. Designing for these extreme environments requires a shift from traditional reliability-based design to resilience-oriented design, which accounts for anticipated and unanticipated hazards through the entire lifecycle.\n\n## Environmental Conditions\nExtraterrestrial environments present unique challenges compared to Earth. Physical comparison of Earth, the moon, and Mars.\n| Property | Earth | Moon | Mars |\n| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |\n| Mass (kg) | 5.97 × 10^24 | 7.35 × 10^22 | 6.42 × 10^23 |\n| Spherical radius (km) | 6378 | 1738 | 3393 |\n| Equatorial gravity (m/sec) | 9.80 | 1.62 | 3.71 |\n| Temperature extremes (°C) | -89 to 58 | -233 to 123 | -153 to 20 |\n| Atmospheric pressure (kPa) | 101 | 3 × 10^-12 | 0.4 to 0.87 |\n| Surface magnetic field (G) | 0.31 | ≤ 2 × 10^-3 | ≤ 5 × 10^-4 |\n| Sidereal rotation (day) | 0.997 | 27.322 | 1.03 |\n\nKey hazards include:\n- **Radiation:** High-energy galactic cosmic rays (GCR) and solar particle events (SPE).\n- **Temperature Fluctuations:** Leading to material fatigue, such as the 5 °C/hr transition on the Moon.\n- **Dust:** Mitigation is critical for mechanical/electronic devices and solar panels.\n- **Seismicity:** Lunar moonquakes can last for hours, unlike terrestrial earthquakes.\n\n## Resilience and System Design\nTraditional reliability-based design is often inefficient and fails to account for unforeseen emergent behaviors. A control-theoretic approach is proposed to maintain system performance using:\n1. **Passive Controls:** Preventive capability.\n2. **Adaptive Controls:** Interventive and mitigative capabilities.\n\nThe goals are to maximize system plasticity and rapidity while minimizing lifecycle costs.\n\n## Cyber-Physical Testing (CPT)\nCPT (or hybrid simulation) integrates physical testing of novel components with computational modeling of established systems. This approach allows for:\n- Validation of complex, interconnected subsystems.\n- Cost-effective examination of large-scale habitat responses to hazardous conditions.\n- Refinement of the 'dynamic safe boundary' of system performance.\n\n## Conclusion\nResilience is a key contributor to the success of long-term habitat systems. The Resilient ExtraTerrestrial Habitats research institute (RETHi) is developing a cyber-physical testing platform to validate resilient design frameworks and automated decision-making techniques for deep space exploration.
