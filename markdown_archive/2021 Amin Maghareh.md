# Role of Cyber-Physical Testing in Developing Resilient Extraterrestrial Habitats

**Authors:** Amin Maghareh, Ali Lenjani, Murali Krishnan, Shirley Dyke, and Ilias Bilionis  
**Affiliation:** School of Mechanical Engineering, Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN 47906  
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2021, ASCE, pp. 1059-1068

## Abstract
Extraterrestrial long-term habitat systems require groundbreaking technological advances to overcome the extreme demands introduced by isolation and challenging environments. A habitat system must operate as intended under continuous disruptive conditions. This engineering problem necessitates that we design and manage habitat systems to be resilient. This study highlights the importance of system resilience and cyber-physical testing to address the grand challenge of developing habitat systems.

## Introduction
The quest for long-term settlement on the Moon and Mars requires habitats that can function under disruptive conditions including temperature fluctuations, cosmic rays, dust, meteoroid impacts, and solar particle events. Humankind faces new challenges moving beyond Earth's benign surface. This paper objectives are to highlight (1) resilience as a comprehensive approach and (2) cyber-physical testing as an enabling tool.

### Key Hazards
- **Radiation:** Galactic Cosmic Rays (GCR) and Solar Particle Events (SPE).
- **Temperature:** Fluctuations leading to material fatigue (e.g., 5 °C/hr transitions on the Moon).
- **Dust:** Mitigation is critical due to impacts on mechanical/electronic devices and power loss.
- **Seismic Activity:** Moonquakes can last for hours, unlike Earth's minutes-long quakes.

## Resilience and System Failures
Reliability-based design (focused on minimizing anticipated failures) is insufficient for habitats because disruptions are inevitable and difficult to predict. Resilience-oriented design focuses on three capacities: absorptive, restorative, and adaptive. 

### Control-theoretic Approach
A resilient habitat system design can be simplified to a multi-objective optimization problem:
1. Maximize system **plasticity** (ability to prevent performance loss).
2. Maximize system **rapidity** (ability to restore performance timely).
3. Minimize **lifecycle cost**.

## Cyber-Physical Testing
Cyber-physical testing integrates physical testing with computational modeling to evaluate complex systems. For habitat systems, it allows for:
- Modeling dynamic reconfiguration.
- Representing combinations of disturbances (radiation + loss of atmosphere).
- Validating health management frameworks.
- Simulating the passage of time (accelerated testing).

## Conclusion
Resilience is a key contributor to the success of habitat systems, which involve complex combinations of hardware, software, and humans. Breaking through design challenges requires innovative techniques like cyber-physical testing of interconnected systems.
