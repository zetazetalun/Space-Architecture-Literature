# Multiscale Modeling and Testing of Protein-Bound Regolith and Soils

**Authors:** I. Rosa, M. D. Lepech, D. J. Loftus  
**Publication:** Earth and Space 2018, ASCE  
**Pages:** 580-590

## Abstract
Extraterrestrial construction presents challenges due to limited resource availability. This work focuses on a novel class of composites: protein-bound soils. Produced by mixing soil, water, and a biopolymer binder, the material achieves compressive strengths comparable to Portland cement concrete. A multiscale framework is proposed to model mechanical properties, from the nanoscale (nanoindentation of protein) to the macroscale (triaxial testing of bulk material).

## Introduction
NASA’s *Journey to Mars Roadmap* identifies ISRU as key to reducing exploration costs. Regolith is abundant on the Moon and Mars and can provide radiation shielding and meteorite protection. Protein-bound regolith offers a low-energy, in-situ alternative for constructing roads, landing pads, and habitat infrastructure.

## Material Properties & Comparison

| Property | Protein-Bound Regolith | Portland Cement Mortar |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Binder Weight %** | 5-15% | up to 25% |
| **Porosity** | 20-40% | 7-20% |
| **Binder Stiffness (Young's Modulus)** | ~4 MPa | 10-25 MPa |
| **Bulk Stiffness** | ~1.2 GPa | ~22 GPa |
| **Compressive Strength** | ~12 MPa | ~35 MPa |

## The Multiscale Framework
1.  **Nanoscale (< 1 µm):** Characterization of desiccated protein using Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) and Nanoindentation.
2.  **Microscale (1 – 5 µm):** MicroCT imaging to create Statistically Equivalent Periodic Unit Cells (SEPUC) to capture interactions between particles, protein bridges, and voids.
3.  **Mesoscale (0.5 – 3 mm):** Rigid Body Spring Model (RBSM) used to simulate fracture and crack propagation within the homogenized material.
4.  **Macroscale (> 0.5 cm):** Experimental validation via triaxial testing and 3D Digital Image Correlation (DIC) to determine yield surfaces and strain development.

## Conclusions
The proposed multiscale framework allows for the prediction of mechanical behavior of bio-composite regolith. This approach accelerates material development for specific extraterrestrial environments by enabling the optimization of mix designs based on available local resources (regolith and harvested water).
