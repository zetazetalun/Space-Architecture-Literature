# Determining the yield stress of a Biopolymer-bound Soil Composite for extrusion-based 3D printing applications

**Authors:** Adrian Biggerstaff, Gerald Fuller, Michael Lepech, David Loftus
**Journal:** Construction and Building Materials 305 (2021) 124730
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.conbuildmat.2021.124730

## Abstract
Biopolymer-bound Soil Composite (BSC) is a novel class of construction materials with potential use in extraterrestrial infrastructure development and more sustainable construction on Earth. Little is known about the rheological properties of this new material and its suitability for extrusion-based 3D printing (E3DP). This paper presents methods for testing BSC yield stress and proposes hydrodynamic and frictional yield stress models for predicting the static and dynamic yield stress of BSC mixtures. Using experimental results, a soil volume fraction range for BSC is identified that may be suitable for E3DP based on extrudability and shape stability requirements.

## 1. Introduction
Extrusion-based 3D printing (E3DP) is emerging as an alternative to form-based construction. Material rheological properties—yield stress, viscosity, and thixotropy—are critical for process control. BSC mixtures are explored as sustainable alternatives to cement, utilizing In Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) concepts for lunar and Martian missions. This paper evaluates BSC consisting of lunar regolith simulant (JSC-1A), bovine blood proteins, and deionized water.

## 2. BSC-E3DP Mixture Design Considerations
BSC strength originates from biopolymer cross-links binding soil particles. Mixtures must be pumpable and extrudable in the wet state. The soil volume fraction ($ϕ_{BSC}$) is limited to <80% of the maximum packing fraction ($ϕ_{mp}$) to ensure pumpability.

## 4. Results and Discussion
Static yield stress (SS) was higher than dynamic yield stress (CSR) for $ϕ_{BSC} < 0.52$. Above this value, they converged within 4%, indicating a reduction in thixotropic behavior as particle concentration increases.

## 5. Yield Stress Models
* **Hydrodynamic Regime:** The Chateau-Ovarlez-Trung (COT) model accurately predicts dynamic yield stress for $ϕ_{BSC} < 0.52$ using an intrinsic viscosity [η] of 7.9 to 9.42.
* **Frictional Regime:** The De Larrard (DL) model accurately predicts static yield stress across the range and dynamic yield stress for $ϕ_{BSC} > 0.52$.

## 7. Conclusion
BSC using low-cohesion soil is suitable for E3DP within a soil volume fraction range of $0.435 \leq \u03D5_{BSC} \leq 0.548$. The lower bound ensures shape stability (min 5mm height), and the upper bound (80% of $ϕ_{mp}$) ensures extrudability.
