# Determining the yield stress of a Biopolymer-bound Soil Composite for extrusion-based 3D printing applications

**Journal:** Construction and Building Materials 305 (2021) 124730  
**Authors:** Adrian Biggerstaff, Gerald Fuller, Michael Lepech, David Loftus  
**Published:** 8 September 2021 (Elsevier Ltd.)

## Abstract
Biopolymer-bound Soil Composite (BSC) is a novel class of construction materials with potential use in extraterrestrial infrastructure development and more sustainable construction on Earth. This paper presents methods for testing BSC yield stress and proposes hydrodynamic and frictional yield stress models for predicting static and dynamic yield stress. A soil volume fraction range for BSC suitable for E3DP is identified based on extrudability and shape stability.

## 1. Introduction
Extrusion-based 3D printing (E3DP) requires materials with low enough yield stress for pumping/extrusion but high enough yield stress for shape stability post-deposition. BSC mixtures, using polysaccharides or proteins and soils, are being explored as sustainable alternatives to cement. Specifically, this paper explores mixtures of lunar regolith simulant (JSC-1A), bovine blood proteins, and deionized water for In Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) in space.

## 2. BSC-E3DP Mixture Design Considerations
For low-cohesion soils like lunar regolith, strength comes from biopolymer cross-links. Pumpability limits the soil volume fraction (φBSC) to <80% of the soil's maximum packing fraction (φmp). High soil density (1.57 g/cm³) is optimal for strength but reduces extrudability.

## 3. Materials and Methods
Seven BSC mixtures were evaluated using JSC-1A Lunar regolith simulant and bovine blood proteins (AP920). Static yield stress was measured using Steady Stress Sweep (SS) and dynamic yield stress via Constant Shear Rate (CSR) tests using a vane geometry rheometer.

## 4. Results and Discussion
Static yield stress values were consistently higher than dynamic values for φBSC < 0.52. Above this threshold, the values converge within 4%. This suggests thixotropic behavior is linked to particle consolidation and interlock.

## 5. Yield Stress Models
- **Hydrodynamic Regime (COT Model):** Accurately predicts dynamic yield stress for φBSC < 0.52.
- **Frictional Regime (DL Model):** Accurately predicts static yield stress across the range and dynamic stress for φBSC > 0.52.
- **Transition Point:** Identified at φBSC = 0.52.

## 6. Conclusion
BSC using lunar regolith is suitable for E3DP within a volume fraction range of 0.435 ≤ φBSC ≤ 0.548. The lower bound is defined by shape stability (minimum 5 mm height) and the upper bound by extrudability (80% of max packing).
