# Lunar active seismic profiler for investigating shallow substrates of the Moon and other extraterrestrial environments

**Published:** 8 June 2023  
**Journal:** Icarus (Elsevier)  
**Authors:** Takeshi Tsuji, Taizo Kobayashi, Junji Kinoshita, Tatsunori Ikeda, et al.

## Abstract
To investigate the structures and properties of shallow material in extraterrestrial environments, we have developed a lunar active seismic profiler (LASP), which integrates active seismic sources and receivers in a short (~1 m) array that can be deployed on a rover. The results suggest that regions with even small amounts of subsurface ice (~0.5 wt%) can be identified in LASP results by their anomalously high S-wave velocity. Field experiments demonstrate that the LASP can support estimates of 3D S-wave velocity distributions and the identification of a buried geological boundary.

## 1. Introduction
Seismic studies yield information about planetary interiors. Active-source seismic surveys can obtain subsurface information in a short time and a well-controlled manner. Measurements of regolith properties (e.g., degree of compaction) in situ are crucial for developing large human-occupied rovers as well as telescopes and other installations on the Moon.

## 2. Lunar Active Seismic Profiler (LASP)
- **Hardware:** Short receiver array (~1 m) with multiple geophones and active sources.
- **Seismic Sources:** Portable Active Seismic Source (PASS) using a motor rotating an eccentric mass to generate a chirp waveform (20–250 Hz).
- **Receivers:** Array of eight geophones suspended by thin strings to decouple from the frame.

## 3. Surface Wave Analysis
Multi-channel analysis of surface wave (MASW) and continuous wavelet transform (CWT-MASW) are used to estimate dispersion curves and S-wave velocity structure. This is sensitive to depths around one-third of the corresponding wavelength.

## 4. Results
### 4.1. Depth Profile of S-wave Velocity
Field tests at a sand beach (regolith proxy) yielded an S-wave velocity profile from surface to 1.5 m depth. The accuracy of the estimated S-wave velocity was ±5 m/s.

### 4.2. Compaction and Subsurface Structures
- **Compaction Detection:** The LASP successfully detected different degrees of soil compaction as a gradient in S-wave velocities (ranging from ~65 m/s for soft to ~100 m/s for highly compacted soil).
- **3D Modeling:** Mapped a buried conical excavation and lithological boundaries through interpolation of 30 measurement points.
- **Ice Detection:** Laboratory experiments with regolith simulant showed that 0.5 wt% ice saturation increases S-wave velocity by ~40 m/s.

## 5. Summary
The LASP enables imaging of the shallow subsurface with high accuracy. This information is useful for construction of base camps, mapping resources (ice), and designing rovers by determining the degree of consolidation at shallow levels.
