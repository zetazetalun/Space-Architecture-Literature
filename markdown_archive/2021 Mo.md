# Bearing capacity of surface circular footings on granular material under low gravity fields

**Authors:** Pin-Qiang Mo, Guoqing Zhou, Feng Gao, Ruilin Li
**Journal:** Journal of Rock Mechanics and Geotechnical Engineering 13 (2021) 612–625
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.jrmge.2020.11.009

## Abstract
Low gravity fields have been simulated through magnetic acceleration to conduct experimental study on bearing capacity of circular footings on a type of crushable planetary regolith simulant, which has comparable density and particle size distribution of lunar soil. The results show that the nonlinear gravity effect is not negligible, related to stress condition, soil dilatancy and mobilised friction angle. A cone penetration test (CPT)-based method for prediction of bearing capacity is proposed, improving the design of shallow foundations on the Moon and Mars.

## 1. Introduction
Space exploration has entered a new era from the Moon to Mars. The stability of artificially created structures (lunar/Martian bases, human habitats) is significantly dependent on the interaction between foundations and planetary regolith under low gravity fields. Gravity on the Moon is ~1.625 m/s² (g/6) and on Mars is ~3.711 m/s² (0.38g).

## 2. Geotechnical low gravity model testing
The Geotechnical Magnetice-Similitudee-Gravity Model Testing (GMMT) method was adopted to simulate accurate and stable low gravity fields using magnetic acceleration in the opposite direction to Earth gravity.

## 3. Results and Analysis
- **Gravity Effect:** The bearing capacity of surface circular footings on the Moon is approximately 72% of that on Earth (for the same footing size), while the reduction on Mars is about 84%.
- **Footing Size Effect:** Bearing capacity increases with footing size (B), following power function relations where q_ult ∝ B^b. The exponent b is approximately 0.681.
- **CPT Correlation:** A clear correlation was found between ultimate bearing capacity (q_ult) and cone penetration resistance (qc). The ratio q_ult/qc remains relatively stable across different gravity fields for a given relative density.

## 4. Conclusions
- Nonlinear gravity effects on foundation stiffness and ultimate bearing capacity are significant.
- Relative density has a more pronounced effect on the mobilised friction angle and soil compressibility than gravity itself.
- The proposed CPT-based method avoids uncertainties in soil property estimation by directly using penetration resistance to predict foundation performance.
