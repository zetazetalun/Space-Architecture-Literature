# Bearing capacity of surface circular footings on granular material under low gravity fields

**Authors:** Pin-Qiang Mo, Guoqing Zhou, Feng Gao, Ruilin Li
**Journal:** Journal of Rock Mechanics and Geotechnical Engineering 13 (2021) 612-625

## Abstract
Low gravity fields have been simulated through magnetic acceleration to conduct experimental study on bearing capacity of circular footings on a type of crushable planetary regolith simulant, which has comparable density and particle size distribution of lunar soil. The results show that the nonlinear gravity effect is not negligible, related to stress condition, soil dilatancy and mobilised friction angle. A cone penetration test (CPT)-based method for prediction of bearing capacity is proposed to improve the design of shallow foundations on the Moon and Mars.

## 1. Introduction
The stability of space architecture (lunar or Martian bases, human habitats) is significantly dependent on the interaction between foundations and planetary regolith under low gravity fields. Gravity on the Moon is ~1.625 m/s² (g/6) and on Mars is ~3.711 m/s² (0.38g). Shallow foundations, currently used as isolated bearing pads for spacecraft (e.g., Lunar Module), are essential for supporting larger structures by transferring loads to subsurface materials.

## 2. Materials and Methods
### 2.1 Planetary Regolith Simulant (CUMT-1c)
- **Composition:** Mixture of cement (62.5R), iron oxide powder (BMMF-1), and water.
- **Properties:** Specific gravity (2.93), mean particle size (0.12 mm), angle of repose (~42°).
- **Crushability:** Highly crushable, reflecting the behavior of lunar agglutinates.

### 2.2 Geotechnical Low Gravity Model Testing (GMMT)
A novel approach using magnetic acceleration (magnetic force counterweighting part of Earth gravity) to simulate stable low gravity fields from 0g to 6g.

## 3. Results and Analysis
- **Gravity Effects:** Ultimate bearing capacity factor $N_\gamma$ is not constant; it decreases with absolute footing size (scale effect) and increases as gravity decreases (gravity effect).
- **Scale Effects:** For circular footings, the exponent of footing size effect is approximately $b = 0.681$.
- **Comparison Moon vs. Earth:** The ultimate bearing capacity of a surface circular footing on the Moon is approximately 72% of that on Earth (for equivalent footing size and soil density), while the reduction on Mars is about 84%.

## 4. Conclusions
- Nonlinear gravity effects on foundation stiffness and ultimate bearing capacity are significant.
- Relative density has a more dominant effect on soil compressibility and shear strength than gravity level.
- A CPT-based design method provides a robust way to estimate allowable bearing capacity for extraterrestrial habitats without requiring direct soil property estimations.
