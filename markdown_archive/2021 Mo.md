# Bearing capacity of surface circular footings on granular material under low gravity fields

**Authors:** Pin-Qiang Mo, Guoqing Zhou, Feng Gao, Ruilin Li  
**Journal:** Journal of Rock Mechanics and Geotechnical Engineering 13 (2021) 612–625  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.jrmge.2020.11.009

## Abstract
Low gravity fields have been simulated through magnetic acceleration to conduct experimental study on bearing capacity of circular footings on a type of crushable planetary regolith simulant, which has comparable density and particle size distribution of lunar soil. The load–settlement responses of surface spread footings are obtained by investigating the relative density, footing size and gravity effects. Applying the hyperbolic asymptote method, normalised foundation stiffness and ultimate bearing capacity are obtained by curve fitting and predicted by power functions using multivariate nonlinear regression. The results show that the nonlinear gravity effect is not negligible, related to stress condition, soil dilatancy and mobilised friction angle. A cone penetration test (CPT)-based method for prediction of bearing capacity is proposed with correlations between ultimate bearing capacity of footings and shallow penetration stiffness of CPTs, avoiding the uncertainties of soil property estimations.

## 1. Introduction
Near-Earth space exploration has entered a new era from the Moon to Mars. The stability of artificially created structures (lunar or Martian bases, human habitats) is significantly dependent on the interaction between foundations and planetary regolith under low gravity fields. Gravity on the surface of the Moon is about 1.625 m/s² (1/6g), and on Mars is around 3.711 m/s² (0.38g).

## 2. Geotechnical Low Gravity Model Testing
Physical modelling was conducted using the **Geotechnical Magnetic-Similitude-Gravity Model Testing (GMMT)** method. This method applies a magnetic acceleration in the opposite direction to Earth's gravity to simulate stable low-gravity fields.

### Regolith Simulant: CUMT-1c
A magnetic-type planetary regolith simulant, termed **CUMT-1c**, was prepared by mixing cement, iron oxide powder, and water.

## 3. Key Findings
1.  **Gravity Effect:** Gravity has a more pronounced influence on the bearing capacity factor than footing size. The ultimate bearing capacity on the Moon is approximately 72% (scaling by N_g factor) of that on Earth.
2.  **Foundation Stiffness:** Both foundation stiffness and ultimate load increase with footing size, relative density, and gravity level.
3.  **CPT-Based Prediction:** A new method using Cone Penetration Test (CPT) data is proposed to predict foundation performance on planetary surfaces, defining the influence zone (Z_inf) as the depth where penetration resistance and bearing capacity are equivalent.
4.  **Scale Effect:** The footing size effect is quantitatively described by a power function index (b ≈ 0.681), which aligns with terrestrial geotechnical findings but is adjusted for extraterrestrial conditions.

## 4. Conclusions
Gravity field shows significant nonlinear relations with foundation stiffness and ultimate bearing capacity. Relative density has a more important effect on the mobilised friction angle and soil compressibility. The CPT-based prediction method provides a practical tool for the design of shallow foundations for space architecture, mitigating uncertainties in estimating soil properties in extreme environments.
