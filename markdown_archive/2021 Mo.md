# Bearing capacity of surface circular footings on granular material under low gravity fields

**Authors:** Pin-Qiang Mo, Guoqing Zhou, Feng Gao, Ruilin Li
**Journal:** Journal of Rock Mechanics and Geotechnical Engineering
**Volume/Issue:** 13 (2021) 612–625
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.jrmge.2020.11.009

## Abstract
Low gravity fields have been simulated through magnetic acceleration to conduct experimental study on bearing capacity of circular footings on a type of crushable planetary regolith simulant (CUMT-1c). The load-settlement responses of surface spread footings are obtained by investigating the relative density, footing size and gravity effects. Results show that the nonlinear gravity effect is not negligible and related to stress condition, soil dilatancy, and mobilised friction angle. A CPT-based method for prediction of bearing capacity is proposed, improving the design of shallow foundations on the Moon and Mars.

## 1. Introduction
Space exploration is moving towards the creation of lunar or Martian bases and human habitats. The stability of these structures is significantly dependent on the interaction between foundations and planetary regolith under low gravity fields (Moon: 1.625 m/s², Mars: 3.711 m/s²). Isolated bearing pads are currently the typical foundation of spacecraft. However, the failure mechanisms under reduced gravity are not yet adequately investigated due to the limitations of current testing methods (drop towers, parabolic flights).

## 2. Methodology
### 2.1 Geotechnical Low Gravity Model Testing (GMMT)
A magnetic acceleration method was used to simulate stable low-gravity fields (0g to 6g). A magnetic-type planetary regolith simulant (CUMT-1c) was produced by mixing cement, iron oxide powder, and water, then smashing it into granular material.

### 2.2 Material Properties

## 3. Results
### 3.1 Bearing Capacity Factor $N_\gamma$
The bearing capacity factor $N_\gamma$ for circular footings was expressed as:
$$N_\gamma = \frac{D_1}{0.3} D_r^{D_2} \left(\frac{B}{d_{50}}\right)^{D_3} \left(\frac{\gamma B}{P_{ref}}\right)^{D_4-1} \frac{P_{ref}}{\gamma B}$$

Where coefficients obtained from nonlinear regression are:
- $D_1 = 3.655$
- $D_2 = 7.276$
- $D_3 = 0.497$
- $D_4 = 0.184$

### 3.2 Key Findings
- **Gravity Effect:** The bearing capacity of a surface circular footing on the Moon is approximately 72% of the same footing on Earth. On Mars, it is approximately 84%.
- **Density Influence:** Relative density has a more significant impact on footing pressure and soil compressibility than gravity itself for dense soils ($D_r = 60\%–70\%$).
- **Scale Effects:** Larger footings carry higher pressures, and the influence zone below the footing increases with relative density but decreases with footing size.

## 4. Conclusions
The study successfully simulated low-gravity effects on regolith using GMMT. It established that gravity effects on foundation stiffness and ultimate bearing capacity are nonlinear. A CPT-based prediction method was proposed to avoid uncertainties in soil property estimations, providing a quantitative tool for space architecture foundation design.
