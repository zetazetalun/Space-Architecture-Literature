# Silicate-lunar regolith composite: A vacuum self-hardened and comprehensively durable extraterrestrial construction material

## Abstract
Extraterrestrial exploration, represented by Lunar base construction, has been promoted worldwide. Herein we proposed a novel extraterrestrial construction material composed of Lunar/Martian regolith simulants and only 4 wt% of silicate adhesive. This silicate-regolith composite can be directly self-hardened in vacuum within 4.5 h without any curing to achieve 29 MPa of hardening strength. Meanwhile, it shows comprehensive durability with stable microstructures and >70 % of residual strength against long-term vacuum exposure, huge temperature difference and intense γ-ray and proton radiations.

## 1. Introduction
Infrastructure on the Moon, especially the outer shell for loading and/or shielding, will face rigorous environments including huge temperature differences (-200 °C to 120 °C), high vacuum ($10^{-10}$ to $10^{-9}$ Pa), and intense radiation. Novel construction materials and feasible processing methods are urgently desired to in-situ utilize lunar resources and automatically set up structures.

## 2. Materials and Methods
### 2.1. Materials
*   **Lunar Regolith Simulants (LRS):** LRS-1 (Apollo-17 target), LRS-2, LRS-3, and MRS (Martian Regolith Simulant).
*   **Silicate Solution:** Sodium silicate (SS) with modulus (SiO2/Na2O ratio) adjusted between 2.0, 2.5, and 3.0. 
*   **Additives:** Sodium pyrophosphate (P), sodium dodecyl sulfate (SDS), and hydrothermal treatment (HT).

### 2.2. Sample Preparation
Regolith simulants were mixed with silicate solution (mass ratio 0.14:1), cold-pressed into 40x10x10 mm³ green bodies under 9.5 MPa, and then placed in a vacuum tank (<10 Pa) for self-hardening.

## 3. Results and Discussion
### 3.1. Self-hardening and Durability
*   **Strength:** SS2.5 generated 5.48 MPa flexural and 18.19 MPa compressive strength. Modified versions (SS2.5HT) reached higher flexural strength (6.54 MPa) and compressive strength (20.34 MPa).
*   **Cryogenic Preparation:** The material can be prepared and hardened even below -40 °C (freeze-drying), yielding 19.54 MPa compressive strength after 15 hours.
*   **Environmental Stability:** Retains 71%-81% flexural and 88%-98% compressive strength after 108 days (4 lunar days) of exposure to thermal cycling (-196 °C to 78 °C) and radiation.

## 4. Conclusion
The study proposes a facile, durable, and versatile solution for in-situ extraterrestrial construction. SLRC requires minimal carried material (4 wt%), no external heat for curing, and demonstrates exceptional stability in harsh lunar/Martian conditions.
