# Extraterrestrial Construction Materials

**Author:** M.Z. Naser  
**Publication:** Progress in Materials Science (2019)  

## Abstract
This review explores the suitability of construction materials derived from lunar and Martian regolith along with concrete derivatives, space-native metals and composites, as well as advanced and non-traditional materials for interplanetary construction. It identifies processing techniques suitable to produce non-terrestrial construction materials in space environments (vacuum, low gravity, etc.) and highlights emerging trends.

## 2. Properties of Extraterrestrial Construction Materials

### 2.1 Regolith and Basalt
Regolith is considered the most abundant resource. Cast basalt (melted and slowly cooled regolith) shows superior mechanical properties compared to ordinary concrete.

### 2.2 Concrete and Concrete-like products
*   **Ordinary Concrete:** Requires water, which is scarce. Vulnerable to outgassing in vacuum.
*   **Polymer Concrete:** Uses thermoplastic/thermosetting polymers as binders. Achieves 70-75% strength within 1 day.
*   **Sulfur Concrete:** Molten sulfur (120-150°C) mixed with aggregate. Gains strength quickly without water but sensitive to high temperatures (sublimation above 120°C).
*   **Geopolymer Concrete:** Non-hydraulic, utilizes alkali-activated aluminosilicate-rich materials. High resistance to thermal cycling and vacuum.

### 2.3 Metals and Alloys
*   **Magnesium:** Lightest, ease of casting/recycling, high strength-to-weight ratio.
*   **Aluminum:** High strength, does not sublime, commonly used in modules.
*   **Iron/Steel:** Plentiful in NEOs and regolith, essential for critical components and radiation shielding.
*   **Titanium:** High strength-to-weight ratio, 40% lighter than steel, stable at high temperatures.

## 3. Processing Methods
*   **Melting and Sintering:** Involves heating regolith to fuse particles. Solar sintering (concentrators), microwave, and laser sintering are primary methods.
*   **Combustion Synthesis (SHS):** Exploits exothermic reactions to produce ceramic-composite materials. Energy efficient.
*   **Dry-Mix/Steam-Injection (DMSI):** Minimizes water requirement by injecting steam into dry-mixed cement and aggregate.
