# Extraterrestrial construction materials

**Author:** M.Z. Naser
**Journal:** Progress in Materials Science 105 (2019) 100577

## Abstract
In recognition of the 50th anniversary of the first manned lunar landing, NASA and ESA revealed plans to resume manned exploration missions and to establish permanent human presence in outposts (habitats) on the Moon and Mars by 2040. In order to promote feasible and sustainable space exploration, these habitats are envisioned to be built from lunar and Martian in-situ resources. This review explores the suitability of construction materials derived from lunar and Martian regolith along with concrete derivatives, space-native metals and composites, as well as advanced and non-traditional materials for interplanetary construction. It identifies processing techniques suitable to produce non-terrestrial construction materials in space environments (i.e., vacuum, low gravity, etc.) and highlights emerging trends.

## 1. Introduction
The paper provides a brief history of space exploration and emphasizes the necessity of In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) to minimize Earth-dependency and reduce exploration costs.

## 2. Space environment and resources
Explores the unique conditions of space including hard vacuum, extreme radiation, temperature fluctuations, and low gravity.

## 3. Properties of extraterrestrial construction materials

### 3.1 Regolith derivatives
- **Regolith:** Most abundant resource. Can be pressed into blocks (duricrete) or processed through melting/sintering.
- **Cast and Sintered Basalt:** Provides superior mechanical properties compared to terrestrial concrete.
- **Lunar and Martian Simulants:** Discusses various simulants used in research (JSC-1, FJS-1, DNA, etc.).

### 3.3 Concrete and concrete-like products
- **Ordinary Concrete:** Feasible but limited by the need for water.
- **Polymer Concrete:** Replaces water/cement with thermoplastic/thermosetting polymers.
- **Sulfur Concrete:** Water-free concrete using molten sulfur as a binder.
- **Geopolymer Concrete:** Inorganic polymers offering high radiation resistance and low water consumption.

### 3.4 Metals, alloys and metal foams
Discusses the extraction and use of Magnesium, Aluminum, Iron, and Titanium from regolith. Highlights metal foams for shielding and structural applications.

### 3.6 Advanced materials
- **Shape Memory Materials (SMMs):** Nitinol and shape memory polymers for deployable structures.
- **Biological Origin:** Spider silk and protein-regolith composites (Regolith Bio-Composite).

## 4. Processing of extraterrestrial construction materials
- **Melting and Sintering:** Including solar, microwave, and laser sintering.
- **Combustion Synthesis (SHS):** Self-sustaining exothermic reactions.
- **Dry-Mix/Steam-Injection (DMSI):** Efficient hydration method for space environments.
- **Additive Manufacturing:** 3D/4D printing using regolith-based inks or Contour Crafting.

## 5. Research needs and future directions
Identifies the need for better property characterization of indigenous resources, integration of AI for structural design, and the development of high-fidelity simulants.
