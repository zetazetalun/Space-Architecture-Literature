**Date:** Published June 1, 2023

## Abstract
Building a lunar base by in situ resource utilization (ISRU) will drastically reduce reliance on Earth. This article provides a comprehensive review of ISRU technologies, including water access, oxygen production, in situ construction, manufacturing, and energy utilization. It proposes a 3-step development plan for lunar ISRU technologies in the next decade.

## Introduction
The Moon's unique environment (high vacuum, low gravity, extreme temperatures) and resources (minerals, water/ice, solar energy) make it a target for permanent stations. ISRU is critical for sustainability, reducing mass and cost. Major ISRU goals include life support, construction of habitats/landing pads, and propellant production.

## Natural Resources on the Moon

## In Situ Lunar Construction Techniques

### Additive Manufacturing (AM) Techniques
- **Contour Crafting:** A gantry-based 3D printing technique using liquid-state binders. NASA selected this for quick infrastructure development. Pumping binders under vacuum remains a challenge.
- **D-Shape Method:** Developed by ESA to produce outer protective shells using regolith. Requires screening soil and transporting significant binder material from Earth.

### Sintering Techniques
- **Microwave Sintering:** Higher efficiency and more even heating than conventional methods. Proven performance with simulants like JSC-1A.
- **Solar Sintering:** Uses concentrated sunlight. Limitations include limited sintering depth compared to microwave treatments.

### Extrusion Printing of Regolith Inks
- Uses premixed pastes of regolith simulant and liquid binders (e.g., PLGA or photocurable resins).
- Samples prepared with photocurable resins achieved an average compressive strength of 428.1 MPa and flexure strength of 129.5 MPa.

### Fiber Refining
- Continuous basalt fibers can replace metal brackets, improving mechanical properties and resistance.
- Fabricated continuous fibers from CSA-1 simulant achieved tensile strengths as high as ~1400 MPa.

## Roadmaps for Future ISRU
1. **Before 2027:** Mineral resource exploration and assessment.
2. **Around 2030:** In situ experiments on the lunar surface via payload experiments.
3. **Around 2035:** Pilot-scale replenishment of survival matters for medium-term residence.
4. **Around 2040:** Large-scale replenishment for long-term residence and Mars missions.
