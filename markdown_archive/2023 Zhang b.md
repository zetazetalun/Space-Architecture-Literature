# Overview of the Lunar In Situ Resource Utilization Techniques for Future Lunar Missions

**Authors:** Peng Zhang, Wei Dai, Ran Niu, Guang Zhang, Guanghui Liu, Xin Liu, Zheng Bo, Zhi Wang, Haibo Zheng, Chengbao Liu, Hanzhe Yang, Yifan Bai, Yang Zhang, Dong Yan, Kefa Zhou, and Ming Gao

**Publication:** *Space: Science & Technology*, 2023;3:Article 0037.
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.34133/space.0037

## Abstract
Building a lunar base by in situ resource utilization (ISRU) will drastically reduce the reliance on supplies from Earth. This article provides a comprehensive review of ISRU technologies, including in situ water access, oxygen production, in situ construction and manufacture, energy utilization, and in situ life support and plant cultivation on the Moon. A 3-step development plan for lunar ISRU technologies in the next decade is proposed.

## Natural Resources on the Moon

### Mineral Resources
Lunar crust minerals are categorized as silicate minerals, oxide minerals, native metals, sulfide minerals, and phosphate minerals. Silicate minerals (pyroxene, plagioclase feldspar, and olivine) make up more than 90% of the volume of most lunar rocks.

### Water/Ice Resources
Estimated total mass of water in permanently shadowed regions (PSRs) is ~2.9 × 10¹² kg. Infrared sensing indicates pyroclastic deposits may contain up to 1,500 ppm of water.

## In Situ Construction Techniques

### Additive Manufacturing (AM)
1. **Contour Crafting:** Uses liquid-state binders (e.g., sulfur concrete) to build objects layer by layer. NASA explored this for quick infrastructure, though pumping under vacuum remains a challenge.
2. **D-Shape:** Suitable for large-scale construction of protective shells using lunar regolith and liquid ink.

### Sintering Techniques
1. **Microwave Sintering:** Higher efficiency and more even heating than radiant furnaces. Studies on Apollo 17 soil and simulants show improved formability.
2. **Solar Sintering:** Uses concentrated sunlight. Limited depth compared to microwave sintering and takes longer to fabricate.

### Extrusion Printing of Inks
Involves mixing regolith with photocurable resins or polymers. Samples using CLRS-2 simulant and photocurable resins reached average compressive strengths of 428.1 MPa.

## Recommendations and Roadmaps
- **2027:** Mineral resource exploration and usability assessment.
- **2030:** In situ experiments on lunar surfaces using experimental payloads.
- **2035:** Pilot-scale replenishment of vital survival matters for medium-term residence.
- **2040:** Large-scale replenishment for long-term residence and Mars missions.
