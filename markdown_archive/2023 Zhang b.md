# Overview of the Lunar In Situ Resource Utilization Techniques for Future Lunar Missions

## Metadata
- **Journal:** Space: Science & Technology
- **Year:** 2023
- **DOI:** 10.34133/space.0037
- **Authors:** Peng Zhang et al.

## Abstract
This article provides a comprehensive review of the past developing processes of ISRU and the latest progress of several ISRU technologies, including in situ water access, in situ oxygen production, in situ construction and manufacture, in situ energy utilization, and in situ life support and plant cultivation on the Moon. A 3-step development plan for lunar ISRU technologies in the next decade is proposed.

## ISRU Techniques on the Moon

### Natural Resources
Lunar resources include mineral resources (silicates, oxides, native metals), water/ice (mostly in PSRs), volatiles (solar wind implanted), and solar energy.

### In Situ Water and Oxygen Production
1. **Water Extraction:** Methods include thermal mining in PSRs (solar-thermal) and drill-based systems (Planetary Volatiles Extractor). Energy consumption is estimated at 8.6 to 37.9 Wh/g.
2. **Hydrogen Reduction:** FeTiO3 + H2 -> Fe + TiO2 + H2O. Efficiency for high-Ti samples is ~0.94% mass percentage oxygen reduction.
3. **Electrolytic Reduction:** Molten Regolith Electrolysis (MRE) can achieve oxygen yields of 15% to 37.5%.

### In Situ Lunar Construction Techniques
1. **Additive Manufacturing (AM):** 
   - **Contour Crafting:** Gantry-based, uses liquid binders. 
   - **D-Shape:** 3D printing suitable for large-scale protective shells.
2. **Sintering Techniques:** 
   - **Microwave Sintering:** Higher efficiency and more even heating than radiant furnaces.
   - **Solar Sintering:** Uses concentrated sunlight; limited by penetration depth.
3. **Extrusion Printing:** Uses lunar regolith inks. Samples using photocurable resins achieved compressive strengths of 428.1 MPa.
4. **Basalt Fibers:** Continuous fibers fabricated from simulants reached tensile strengths of ~1,400 MPa.

### Energy and Life Support
- **Thermal Energy Storage (TES):** Regolith as thermal mass; heat engines run during the lunar night.
- **Artificial Ecosystems:** China's 'Lunar Palace 1' achieved 100% regeneration of oxygen/water and 83% of food for 4 crew members.

## Conclusion and Roadmap
Proposed 3-step plan: 
- 2027: Mineral resources assessment.
- 2030: In situ experimental payloads.
- 2035: Pilot-scale replenishment of survival matters.
- 2040: Large-scale replenishment for residence.
