# Geotechnical Engineering Considerations for Extraterrestrial Infrastructure Construction

**Authors:** James K. Mitchell and W. David Carrier III  
**Publication:** The Art of Foundation Engineering Practice Congress 2010 (ASCE)

## Abstract
It is only a matter of time until space exploration enters a new phase including manned and unmanned landings on the Moon, Mars, and asteroids. Detailed knowledge of soils and rocks is essential for the design of foundations, excavation, underground space utilization, surface vehicles, and transportation systems. In-situ resource utilization (ISRU) will play a major role due to the high cost of transporting materials from Earth.

## Lunar Geotechnical Properties
- **Soil Type:** Referred to as regolith, composed of basaltic volcanic rocks, pristine highland rocks, and complex polymict breccias.
- **Density:** Relative density increases rapidly with depth: 0-30% at the surface to >90% below 30 cm.
- **Shear Strength:** Characterized by high friction angles (41° to 55°) and cohesion (0.4 to 3.8 kPa) depending on depth.
- **Compressibility:** Evaluated for remolded samples with a compression index (Cc) of 0.3 for loose and 0.05 for dense soil.

## Martian Geotechnical Properties
- **Geologic Processes:** Tectonic, volcanic, cratering, hydro, mass movements, and aeolian processes.
- **Soil Composition:** Contains silicon, iron, aluminum, magnesium, calcium, titanium, sulfur, and chlorine.
- **Mechanical Properties:** Similar to moderately dense soils on Earth, such as clayey silt with embedded sands and granules.

## Geotechnical Engineering Considerations
- **Trafficability:** Vehicle mobility is generally manageable on the Moon but may face difficulties on Mars due to rocky areas and soft zones (dust/ice).
- **Foundations:** Lunar silty fine sand is capable of supporting high foundation loads on shallow footings due to high density at shallow depths.
- **Excavation:** Core tube and drill holes on the Moon remain open, allowing stable vertical walls for excavation to depths of 2m or more.
- **ISRU:** Essential for extracting oxygen and metals; requires geotechnical input for drilling, sampling, and dust control.
