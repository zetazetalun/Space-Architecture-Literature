## ABSTRACT
It is only a matter of time until space exploration enters a new phase that will include additional manned and unmanned landings and explorations on the Moon, Mars, and perhaps on asteroids. Detailed knowledge of the soils and rocks will be needed for the design of foundations for structures, for excavation and underground space utilization, for design of surface vehicles and construction of transportation systems, for evaluation of their suitability as construction materials and development of construction methods. In-situ resource utilization can be expected to play a major role in these activities owing to the high cost and difficulty in transporting needed materials from Earth.

## LUNAR GEOTECHNICAL PROPERTIES

### Lunar Rock and Soil Types
Consolidated rocks on the Moon fall into three categories: 
1. Basaltic volcanic rocks
2. Pristine rocks from the lunar highlands
3. Complex polymict breccias formed by impacts

The regolith covers virtually the entire surface to a depth of 2m to over 20m. The soil is characterized as broadly-graded silty fine sand to sandy silt (SM or ML).

### Mechanical Properties
Lunar soil properties depend primarily on relative density:
- **Relative Density:** 0-30% (surface) to >90% (below 30 cm).
- **Bulk Density:** 1.3 to 1.8 g/cm³.
- **Compressibility Index (Cc):** 0.3 (loose) to 0.05 (dense).
- **Friction Angle (φ):** Increases with depth from 41° to 55°.
- **Cohesion (c):** Increases with depth from 0.4 kPa to 3.8 kPa.

## MARTIAN GEOTECHNICAL PROPERTIES

Martian soil is more complex due to tectonic, volcanic, impact, hydro, mass movement, and aeolian processes. Martian soil is described as similar to moderately dense soils on Earth, such as clayey silt with embedded sands, granules, and pebbles.

## ENGINEERING CONSIDERATIONS
- **Trafficability:** Generally good on the Moon, but soft zones on Mars may pose risks (e.g., Spirit rover).
- **Foundations:** Lunar soil can support high foundation loads due to high density at shallow depths. Martian bearing capacity is site-specific.
- **Excavation:** Open drill holes and vertical walls are stable up to 2m on the Moon. Soil bulking will occur upon excavation and recompaction.
- **ISRU:** Essential for sustainability; goals include oxygen production (1-10 tons/year) and extraction of metals for fabrication.

## CONCLUSIONS
Reliable knowledge of soil properties is essential for extraterrestrial development. Lunar conditions are relatively consistent and well-understood, while Martian conditions are highly variable and influenced by a thin atmosphere and historical water activity.
