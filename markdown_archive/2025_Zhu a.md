# Habitat site selection on Mars: Suitability analysis and mapping

**Authors:** Sijie Zhu, Bing Zhao, Yu Yan, Xing Shi  
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 226 (2025) 1–22  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.actaastro.2024.11.019

## Abstract
Growing interest in extraterrestrial colonization has centered on Mars. This study evaluated potential habitats by considering factors such as temperature, wind, radiation, terrain, dust, and water ice. Through comprehensive suitability maps, potential areas for future construction were identified, primarily in south Arcadia Planitia, south Utopia Planitia, and the junction of Acidalia Planitia and Arabia Terra. The study revealed that most of Mars’ southern hemisphere is largely unsuitable for long-term habitation, except for Hellas Planitia.

## 1. Introduction
Humanity's quest for long-term survival drives the exploration of potential habitats beyond Earth. Progress is being made in life-support systems, construction materials, and building technologies. However, few studies have evaluated Mars from a habitat planning perspective. This study examines environmental factors including terrain flatness, temperature stability, dust storm activity, water ice resources, and radiation levels to provide a preliminary assessment of potential long-term habitation sites.

## 2. Material and Methods
The study used global data from MGS TES, SWIM, MOLA, and MDAD. Suitability indices were scaled from 0 to 1 for five factors:
1. **Terrain Suitability Index (TrSI):** Based on the proportion of flat land (slope < 3°).
2. **Temperature Suitability Index (TSI):** Combines average annual temperature with seasonal and diurnal variations.
3. **Dust Activity Suitability Index (DASI):** Combines Local (LDAI) and Global (GDAI) dust activity.
4. **Water Ice Suitability Index (WISI):** Integrates water ice consistency at different depths (0–1m, 1–5m, >5m).
5. **Radiation Suitability Index (RSI):** Based on atmospheric shielding density derived from surface pressure.

## 3. Results
- **Terrain:** Flat land (slope < 3°) constitutes 79.91% of Mars' surface. Northern hemisphere plains (Arcadia, Acidalia, Utopia) show high suitability.
- **Temperature:** Most stable climatic conditions found in Chryse Planitia, Isidis Planitia, and southwestern Utopia Planitia.
- **Water Ice:** Shallow ice deposits are predominantly above 50° latitude.
- **Multifactor Suitability (Aboveground):** High suitability zones near 30°N (Arcadia Planitia, Utopia Planitia, Acidalia-Arabia Terra junction).
- **Multifactor Suitability (Underground):** Optimal zones shift toward higher latitudes due to the increased importance of water ice accessibility and the insulating properties of regolith.

## 4. Discussion
- **Impact of Building Forms:** Aboveground structures require flat, stable terrain. Underground structures (caves, lava tubes) offer radiation protection and thermal insulation but depend on specific geological features.
- **3D Printing:** Martian materials (basalt, regolith) and water ice are identified as promising for extrusion-based construction to minimize Earth-imported resources.
- **Landing Zones:** Most high-suitability regions overlap with accessible landing zones (elevations below -1.4 km), except for areas like the northern side of Alba Mons.

## 5. Conclusion
The study provides a methodological reference for future extraterrestrial habitat siting. Optimal regions are primarily in south Arcadia Planitia, south Utopia Planitia, and the junction of Acidalia Planitia and Arabia Terra.
