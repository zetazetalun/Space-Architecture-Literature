# Habitat site selection on Mars: Suitability analysis and mapping

**Authors:** Sijie Zhu, Bing Zhao, Yu Yan, Xing Shi

**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 226 (2025) 1–22

## Abstract
Growing interest in extraterrestrial colonization has increasingly centered on Mars, bolstered by extensive environmental data from Mars exploration missions. However, the planet’s harsh conditions—including extreme temperatures, dust storms, varied terrain, high radiation, and scarce water resources—pose significant challenges to selecting suitable sites for human habitation. This study evaluated potential habitats on Mars by considering factors such as temperature, wind, radiation, terrain, dust, and water ice. Through the development of comprehensive suitability maps, we identified potential areas for future human construction, primarily concentrated in south Arcadia Planitia, south Utopia Planitia, and the junction of Acidalia Planitia and Arabia Terra.

## 1. Introduction
Humanity’s quest for long-term survival drives the exploration of potential habitats beyond Earth. Despite the stark differences from Earth’s environment, progress is being made in the development of life-support systems, construction materials, and building technologies. The advancement is facilitated by theoretical numerical simulations and experimental studies within closed systems that replicate Mars’ gravity, climate, atmospheric pressure, radiation, soil, and gas composition.

## 2. Material and Methods
For the selection of habitat sites on Mars, five crucial factors were identified:
1. **Terrain Suitability Index (TrSI):** Evaluates flatness (slopes < 3°) for stability and construction.
2. **Temperature Suitability Index (TSI):** Combines average annual temperature with seasonal and diurnal variations.
3. **Dust Activity Suitability Index (DASI):** Assesses the frequency of localized and global dust storms.
4. **Water Ice Suitability Index (WISI):** Evaluates the presence and depth of subsurface water ice.
5. **Radiation Suitability Index (RSI):** Based on atmospheric shielding density derived from surface pressure.

## 3. Results
### 3.1. Single-factor suitability maps
- **TrSI:** Flat land constitutes approximately 79.91% of Mars' surface, with high suitability in northern plains like Arcadia and Utopia Planitia.
- **TSI:** Favorable conditions (stable and warm) are found between 0° and 30°N latitude.
- **WISI:** Accessible shallow ice is primarily above 50° latitude, but exists in mid-latitudes.
- **RSI:** Hellas Planitia provides the highest atmospheric shielding due to low altitude.

### 3.2. Multifactor suitability scenarios
The study analyzed two built form scenarios using the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP):
1. **Aboveground Buildings:** Emphasizes terrain flatness and temperature stability.
2. **Underground Buildings:** Emphasizes water ice proximity and reduces the importance of surface temperature due to soil insulation.

## 4. Discussion
- **Dual Impact:** Dust is a hazard for solar panels but a potential resource for silica/iron-rich building materials.
- **Building Forms:** Aboveground structures (domes/membranes) require flat terrain. Underground structures (lava tubes/caves) offer superior radiation and thermal protection.
- **Landing Zones:** Most high-suitability regions overlap with accessible landing zones (elevations < -1.4 km), except for some high-altitude areas like Alba Mons.

## 5. Conclusion
The study identifies south Arcadia Planitia, south Utopia Planitia, and the junction of Acidalia Planitia and Arabia Terra as optimal regions. The framework is flexible and can be adjusted as technological capabilities (e.g., improved landing technology or water extraction methods) evolve.
