# Karst Cave as Terrestrial Simulation Platform to Test and Design Human Base in Lunar Lava Tube

**Journal:** Space: Science & Technology (AAAS Science Partner Journal)  
**Volume:** 2022, Article ID 9875780  
**Authors:** Jinghang Ding, Gengxin Xie, Linli Guo, Xin Xiong, Ya Han, and Xi Wang  
**Published:** 4 November 2022  
**DOI:** 10.34133/2022/9875780

## Abstract
Developing efficient approaches to building a suitable environment for humans on the moon is key to future long-term sustainable lunar exploration. Lunar lava tubes discovered by missions like Kaguya offer potential habitat sites. This paper summarizes research on the stability, temperature, and radiation protection of these tubes. It proposes using Earth’s Karst caves (specifically in Chongqing) as simulation platforms to address technical bottlenecks in cave sealing, daylight systems, and artificial ecosystems using natural soil and rock.

## 1. Introduction
Extraterrestrial environments are hostile due to extreme temperatures, vacuum, low gravity, radiation, and meteorite impacts. Safe, stable, long-term shelters are required. Lunar lava tubes are geological structures formed by volcanic activity, providing a hard basalt roof and a relatively stable internal environment.

## 2. Rationality of Using Lunar Lava Tubes

### 2.1 Energy Estimation
The energy required for a lunar base consists of human respiration, plant photosynthesis, and internal temperature control. 
- **Assumed Habitat:** Semicircular tunnel cave, ~8,000 m², height 50m.
- **Occupancy:** 8 astronauts.
- **Oxygen Demand:** ~101.155 kg for 27 days.
- **Calorie Demand:** ~557,361 kcal/day for 8 people.
- **Power Calculation:** Total energy required to support 8 astronauts is approximately 1.76 × 10¹¹ J (48,889 kW·h).

### 2.2 Safety Assessment
- **Radiation:** At 6m depth, radiation intensity is near zero. Even at <1m, solar flares do not affect the interior.
- **Meteorites:** The basalt roof provides protection. Safety depends on the ratio of target thickness to crater diameter (ideally <0.87).
- **Thermal Stability:** Energy required to maintain 20°C in a lava tube is significantly lower (5.74 × 10⁶ kW·h) than on the surface (2.89 × 10⁷ kW·h).

## 3. Karst Cave Simulation Platform
The authors propose using Karst caves in Chongqing as analogs. These caves offer comparable scale and environmental isolation.
- **Research Directions:**
  1. **Cave Sealing Technology:** Developing ISRU-based methods to seal cave entrances to maintain atmosphere.
  2. **Efficient Daylight System:** Using light pipes to bring sunlight into the habitat for agriculture.
  3. **Internal Circulation:** Studying artificial ecosystems that utilize natural rock and soil.

## 4. Challenges and Roadmap
The Roadmap for construction includes:
1. Site selection (2030)
2. Robot exploration and ISRU utilization (2035)
3. Automatic construction systems (2045)
4. Long-term artificial ecosystems (2060)

## 5. Conclusion
Lunar lava tubes are excellent candidates for human bases due to their stable environments. Terrestrial Karst caves provide a viable platform to simulate and evaluate these bases while promoting construction technologies like 3D printing, sintering, and autonomous robotics.
