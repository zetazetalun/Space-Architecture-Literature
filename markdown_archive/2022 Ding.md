# Karst Cave as Terrestrial Simulation Platform to Test and Design Human Base in Lunar Lava Tube

**Authors:** Jinghang Ding, Gengxin Xie, Linli Guo, Xin Xiong, Ya Han, and Xi Wang  
**Journal:** Space: Science & Technology  
**Volume:** 2022, Article ID 9875780  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.34133/2022/9875780

## Abstract
Developing efficient approaches to building a suitable environment for humans on the moon plays a key role in future long-term sustainable lunar exploration activities. The lava tubes discovered by the Kaguya mission offer huge potential sites to host such bases. Through computation and analysis, we show that lunar lava tubes offer stable structures, suitable temperatures, low radiation doses, and low meteorite impact rates. We summarize previous research results and put forward the conditions to find and use a suitable lunar lava tube for human habitation on the moon. We proposed the idea of using the Earth's karst caves to simulate extraterrestrial lava tubes, selected caves in Chongqing as the simulation site, and demonstrated the feasibility from both structural and environmental aspects. Finally, we proposed a karst cave simulation platform with three main research directions: cave sealing technology, efficient daylight system, and internal circulation research of artificial ecosystems containing natural soil and rock.

## 1. Introduction
Extraterrestrial exploration requires a safe, stable, and long-term shelter. Lunar lava tubes are geological structures formed by volcanic activity. Because a lunar lava tube has a hard basalt roof, its internal environmental factors such as temperature changes, radiation doses, and the probability of being hit by meteorites are relatively limited, offering an ideal human lunar habitat.

## 3. Using a Karst Cave to Simulate Lunar Lava Tubes
Karst caves in Chongqing, China, are proposed as analogs. While formation mechanisms differ (dissolution vs. lava cooling), the structural dimensions (tens of meters high, hundreds of kilometers long) and environmental stability make them suitable candidates.

## 4. Significance of Simulation Platforms
The simulation platform proposed includes:
1. **Cave Sealing Technology:** Testing extrusion and sintering technologies for sealing cave entrances.
2. **Daylight Systems:** Using light pipes to introduce sunlight for biological systems.
3. **Automated Construction:** Utilizing robots for interior construction and ISRU-based material application.

## 5. Conclusion
Lunar lava tubes are excellent candidates for lunar bases. Terrestrial karst caves provide a natural ecosystem (soil and rock) and structural volume to simulate these habitats at a lower cost than fully artificial structures. Research should focus on sealing performance and material circulation in artificial ecosystems.
