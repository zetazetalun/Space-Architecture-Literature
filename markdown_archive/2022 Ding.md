# Karst Cave as Terrestrial Simulation Platform to Test and Design Human Base in Lunar Lava Tube

**Journal:** Space: Science & Technology  
**Volume:** 2022, Article ID 9875780  
**DOI:** 10.34133/2022/9875780

## Abstract
Developing efficient approaches to building a suitable environment for humans on the moon is key for long-term exploration. Lunar lava tubes, such as those discovered by the Kaguya mission, offer stable structures, suitable temperatures, and low radiation. This paper proposes using Earth's karst caves in Chongqing as a simulation platform to research cave sealing, daylighting, and artificial ecosystems.

## 1. Introduction
Caves provide natural protection against harsh environments. Lunar lava tubes are hollow pipe-shaped caves formed by volcanic activity. With hard basalt roofs, they offer an ideal human habitat by limiting temperature changes, radiation doses, and meteorite impact probability.

## 2. Rationality of Using Lunar Lava Tubes
### 2.1 Energy Estimation
- **Respiration and Nutrition:** For 8 astronauts, metabolic needs require approximately 101 kg of oxygen and 2.33 million kJ of energy for food over a 27-day lunar cycle. This can be met by 900 m² of soybean planting.
- **Thermal Environment:** Internal cave temperatures are more stable. Maintaining 20°C in a cave requires significantly less energy (5.7 x 10^6 kWh) than on the lunar surface (2.89 x 10^7 kWh).

### 2.2 Safety Assessment
- **Radiation:** At a depth of 6m, radiation intensity is nearly zero.
- **Meteorites:** Burial depth protects habitats; stability is assessed by the ratio of target thickness to crater diameter.
- **Moon Dust:** Lava tubes are permanent shadow zones, preventing the formation of harmful charged dust.

## 3. Karst Cave Simulation Platform
Karst caves are proposed as analogs due to comparable sizes (up to tens of meters in height and kilometers in length) and stable internal temperatures. The proposed simulation site in Chongqing will focus on:
1. **Cave Sealing:** Developing technologies to create airtight environments using in-situ materials.
2. **Daylighting:** Using light pipes to introduce sunlight for biological systems.
3. **Automated Construction:** Utilizing robots for site preparation and structural reinforcement.

## 5. Conclusion
Lunar lava tubes are excellent candidates for lunar bases. Terrestrial karst caves provide a low-cost, natural ecosystem for simulating these environments and testing automated construction and sealing technologies.
