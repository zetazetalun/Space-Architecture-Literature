# Karst Cave as Terrestrial Simulation Platform to Test and Design Human Base in Lunar Lava Tube

**Authors:** Jinghang Ding, Gengxin Xie, Linli Guo, Xin Xiong, Ya Han, and Xi Wang
**Journal:** Space: Science & Technology (AAAS Science Partner Journal)
**Volume:** 2022, Article ID 9875780
**Published:** November 4, 2022

## Abstract
Developing efficient approaches to building a suitable environment for humans on the moon plays a key role in future long-term sustainable lunar exploration activities. This paper proposes using Earth's karst caves to simulate extraterrestrial lava tubes, specifically selecting caves in Chongqing as simulation sites. The research focuses on cave sealing technology, efficient daylight systems, and internal circulation of artificial ecosystems.

## 1. Introduction
Lunar lava tubes are pipe-shaped caves formed by volcanic activity. Because they have hard basalt roofs, internal factors such as temperature changes and radiation doses are limited, offering an ideal lunar habitat. Tubes of 1 km size or larger are structurally sound and could protect against lunar environmental challenges.

### 2.2 Safety Assessment
*   **Radiation:** Lava tubes deeper than 6 m are effectively shielded from cosmic rays and solar wind.
*   **Meteorites:** Burial depth prevents structural collapse unless the thickness-to-crater diameter ratio is less than 0.87.
*   **Moon Quakes:** Lava tubes have survived millions of years, indicating structural stability against seismic events.

## 3. Karst Cave Simulation Platform
Karst caves are proposed as analogs due to similar structural (semicircular top, flat bottom) and environmental characteristics. 

### Research Directions:
1.  **Cave Sealing:** Developing technologies to prevent air leakage from the natural rock structure.
2.  **Daylight Systems:** Using light pipes to introduce natural sunlight for plant growth.
3.  **Automated Construction:** Utilizing robots for in situ resource utilization and habitat setup within the cave.

## 4. Conclusion
Lunar lava tubes provide stable environments with low energy requirements for thermal control compared to surface bases. Terrestrial Karst caves offer a cost-effective platform to evaluate sealing, structural integrity, and ecological stability for future extraterrestrial settlements.
