# Karst Cave as Terrestrial Simulation Platform to Test and Design Human Base in Lunar Lava Tube

**Authors:** Jinghang Ding, Gengxin Xie, Linli Guo, Xin Xiong, Ya Han, and Xi Wang
**Publication:** Space: Science & Technology, Volume 2022, Article ID 9875780
**DOI:** 10.34133/2022/9875780

## Abstract
Developing efficient approaches to building a suitable environment for humans on the moon play a key role in future long-term sustainable lunar exploration activities. The lava tubes discovered by the Kaguya mission offer huge potential sites to host such bases. This paper shows through computation and analysis that lunar lava tubes offer stable structures, suitable temperatures, low radiation doses, and low meteorite impact rates. The authors propose using Earth’s karst caves (specifically in Chongqing) to simulate extraterrestrial lava tubes and outline a simulation platform with three research directions: cave sealing technology, efficient daylight systems, and artificial ecosystems.

## 1. Introduction
Extraterrestrial exploration requires safe, stable, and long-term shelters. Lunar lava tubes, formed by volcanic activity, provide natural protection with hard basalt roofs. They offer limited temperature changes, low radiation, and protection from meteorites compared to the lunar surface.

### 2.2 Safety Assessment
- **Radiation:** At 6m depth, radiation intensity is almost zero. Solar flares do not affect the interior.
- **Meteorites:** Tubes are protected if the burial depth is sufficient (ratio of target thickness to crater diameter < 0.87).
- **Moon Dust:** The interior is a permanent shadow zone where charged moon dust is hardly produced.
- **Moon Quakes:** Lava tubes have survived for millions of years, suggesting structural stability against seismic events.

## 3. Using a Karst Cave to Simulate Lunar Lava Tubes
Karst caves (e.g., in Wulong and Youyang, Chongqing) are proposed as analogs. While formation mechanisms differ (chemical dissolution vs. volcanic activity), they are comparable in size and offer mild, stable internal environments (14–21 °C in Wulong).

### 3.3 Simulation Platform Components
- **Cave Sealing Technology:** Reforming caves into airtight environments using in-situ resources.
- **Daylight System:** Light pipes to introduce sunlight into the ecological laboratory.
- **Automated Construction Laboratory:** Testing robotics for internal base assembly.

## 4. Significance and Conclusion
Lunar lava tubes are ideal candidates for habitats due to their stability and protective qualities. Earth-based karst caves provide a natural ecosystem (soil and rock) to test simulation platforms for cave sealing, internal circulation, and automatic construction at a lower cost than fully artificial structures.
