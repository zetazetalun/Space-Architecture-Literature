# Karst Cave as Terrestrial Simulation Platform to Test and Design Human Base in Lunar Lava Tube

## Abstract
Developing efficient approaches to building a suitable environment for humans on the moon is key for long-term lunar exploration. Lunar lava tubes, discovered by the Kaguya mission, offer high potential as base sites. This paper shows through analysis that these tubes provide stable structures, suitable temperatures, low radiation, and low meteorite impact rates. The authors propose using Earth's karst caves in Chongqing as simulation platforms, focusing on cave sealing technology, daylighting systems, and artificial ecosystems.

## 1. Introduction
Lunar lava tubes are pipe-shaped caves formed by volcanic activity. Their hard basalt roofs offer natural protection against extreme lunar temperatures, radiation, and meteorites. The GRAIL probe suggests these tubes can reach widths of 1-2 km and lengths of hundreds of kilometers.

### 2.2. Safety Assessment
- **Radiation:** Lava tubes deeper than 6m provide near-zero radiation exposure from cosmic rays and solar particles.
- **Meteorites:** The ratio of target thickness-to-crater diameter must be < 0.87 for safety. Most tubes meet this threshold.
- **Moon Dust:** The interior of lava tubes is a permanent shadow zone where charged moon dust is hardly produced.

## 3. Using a Karst Cave to Simulate Lunar Lava Tubes
Karst caves are formed by the dissolution of limestone. Chongqing’s caves (e.g., Wulong, Youyang) are proposed as analogs due to:
1. **Structure:** Large volumes reaching tens of thousands of cubic meters.
2. **Environment:** Stable internal temperatures (14-21°C in Wulong) compared to the lunar tube range (-20 to 30°C).
3. **Sealing:** Necessary for simulating closed ecosystems; the paper suggests extruded lunar soil has good potential for sealing.

## 5. Conclusion
Lunar lava tubes are superior to surface habitats due to natural shielding and thermal stability. Terrestrial karst caves provide an excellent platform to test automatic construction, ISRU, and life support systems at a low cost compared to purely artificial structures.
