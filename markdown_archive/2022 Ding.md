# Karst Cave as Terrestrial Simulation Platform to Test and Design Human Base in Lunar Lava Tube

**Authors:** Jinghang Ding, Gengxin Xie, Linli Guo, Xin Xiong, Ya Han, and Xi Wang
**Publication:** Space: Science & Technology (AAAS Science Partner Journal), Volume 2022
**DOI:** 10.34133/2022/9875780

## Abstract
Developing efficient approaches to building a suitable environment for humans on the moon is key for long-term exploration. This paper proposes using Earth’s karst caves as terrestrial simulation platforms to test and design human bases in lunar lava tubes. It analyzes the rationality of lava tubes for habitation, focusing on energy budgets for life support and safety against radiation and meteorites. The study selects karst caves in Chongqing, China, as simulation sites and outlines research directions including cave sealing technology, daylight systems, and artificial ecosystems.

## 1. Introduction
Lunar lava tubes offer natural protection against extreme temperatures, high vacuum, low gravity, cosmic radiation, and meteorite impacts. These hollow pipe-shaped caves, formed by volcanic activity, provide a stable thermal environment and a hard basalt roof suitable for long-term shelters.

## 2. Rationality of Using Lunar Lava Tubes
### 2.1 Energy Estimation
The study estimates the survival needs for 8 astronauts in a semicircular tunnel cave (8,000 m², 50m height).
- **Oxygen/Food:** 900 m² of soybeans are required to meet needs for 27 days.
- **Thermal Control:** Maintaining 20°C in a lava tube requires significantly less energy ($2.05 \times 10^{13}$ J) compared to the lunar surface ($1.04 \times 10^{14}$ J) due to the insulating properties of the basalt roof.

### 2.2 Safety Assessment
- **Radiation:** At 6m depth, radiation intensity is nearly zero. Even at <1m, solar flares do not affect the interior.
- **Meteorite Impact:** Tubes buried deep are protected. A ratio of target thickness to crater diameter < 0.87 prevents 'skylight' formation.
- **Moon Dust:** Lava tubes are permanent shadow zones where charged moon dust is hardly produced.
- **Seismic Stability:** Lunar lava tubes have survived for billions of years, suggesting structural stability against moonquakes.

## 3. Karst Caves as Simulation Platforms
Karst caves share structural similarities with lava tubes (curved semicircular tops) and offer relatively stable internal environments (14–21°C). 

### Proposed Research Directions:
1.  **Cave Sealing Technology:** Reforming the cave to maintain a closed artificial ecosystem. Preliminary work suggests extruded lunar soil analogs have good sealing properties.
2.  **Efficient Daylight System:** Using light pipes and automatic control systems to introduce sunlight for biological needs.
3.  **Internal Circulation:** Researching artificial ecosystems containing natural soil and rock to achieve a closed loop.

## 4. Conclusion
Lunar lava tubes are excellent candidates for lunar bases. Karst caves provide a natural ecosystem to simulate and evaluate extraterrestrial bases, particularly for testing automated construction, ISRU for sealing, and life support integration.
