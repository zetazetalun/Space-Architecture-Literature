# Envisioning Extraterrestrial Construction and the Future Construction Workforce: A Collective Perspective

**Amirhosein Jafari, Yimin Zhu, Suniti Karunatillake, Jennifer Qian, Shinhee Jeong, Ali Kazemian, and Andrew Webb**

## Abstract
Humanity’s evolution toward an interplanetary species poses a new frontier for the construction industry: extraterrestrial construction. With fast technological advancements in manufacturing and robotics, it is a matter of when, not if, before humans make perpetual habitats on nearby planetary bodies. We envision the emerging frontier of the construction industry as extraterrestrial construction, where the role of workers and their required skills will change dramatically. Due to the extreme and hazardous outer-space conditions, the future of extraterrestrial construction will be technology-intensive, from using onsite machine and robot operations to using cyber-physical systems for managing logistical operations. Accordingly, the role and skillsets of construction workers in future extraterrestrial construction projects will contrast with the current practices on Earth. We aim to present a collective perspective on the nature of future extraterrestrial construction and the hierarchy of the skills required by future workers, as well as emerging technologies that can be used for developing a future-ready workforce.

## 1 Introduction
Extraterrestrial construction, constructing structures on a planetary body outside of the Earth and its atmosphere, will be a significant part of the future market for the construction industry. Building a human habitat in uninhabitable environments on other planetary bodies will require automated construction processes optimized for that environment. To promote feasible and sustainable space exploration, these habitats and other structures are envisioned to require in-situ construction.

## 4 Summary of Outcomes

### 4.1 Theme 1: Extraterrestrial Construction
1.  **Site Conditions:** Differences in atmospheric disturbances, quakes, radiation, gravity, vacuum, and abrasive Lunar regolith must be determined.
2.  **Protocols:** Construction tasks change under planetary conditions (e.g., lower gravity affects excavation forces).
3.  **ISRU:** In-situ Resource Utilization is a necessity due to prohibitive delivery costs from Earth.
4.  **Taxonomy:** A technical taxonomy is needed to ensure consistent use of terms and units.
5.  **Human-in-the-loop:** Robot-intensive construction still requires humans for repairs, intervention, and high-level instinct/creativity.
6.  **HRC:** Human-robot collaboration will evolve from automated landing platforms to onsite corporeal collaborations.
7.  **Teleoperation:** Essential for integration but limited by latency (e.g., 20 min for Mars).

### 4.2 Theme 2: Extraterrestrial Construction Workforce
The future workforce requires:
1.  Situational Awareness.
2.  Geological Knowledge (thermal gradients, atmospheric pressure).
3.  Microenvironment Understanding (suit operations).
4.  Teleoperation Skills.
5.  Safety Training for isolated settings.
6.  Trust in Technology.
7.  Familiarity with Hybrid Collaboration.
8.  Task Compartmentalization.
9.  IT Awareness.
10. Cross-disciplinary Collaboration.
11. Human Factors (fatigue, isolation).
12. Cross-training (mechatronics, programming).

### 4.3 Theme 3: Emerging Technologies for Workforce Training
AI-XR (Artificial Intelligence and Extended Reality) and digital twins are identified as key tools for simulating low gravity, different lighting, and sensory deprivation.

## 5 Conclusions and Takeaways
Extraterrestrial construction will be robot-intensive but requires a human-in-the-loop approach. Training must transition from traditional methods to immersive, learner-centered constructivist learning using AI-XR technologies to replicate harsh environment dynamics.
