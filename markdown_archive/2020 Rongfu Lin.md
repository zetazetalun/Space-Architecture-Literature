# Topological design of a new family of legged mobile landers based on Truss-Mechanism Transformation method\n\n**Authors:** Rongfu Lin, Weizhong Guo, Changjie Zhao, Youyuan Tang, Chenyao Zhao, Ziyue Li\n**Journal:** Mechanism and Machine Theory, 149 (2020) 103787\n**DOI:** 10.1016/j.mechmachtheory.2020.103787\n\n## Abstract\nTo meet the needs of the \"Returning\" and \"Base construction\" missions of the extraterrestrial body’s exploration task, it is necessary to enable the legged stationary lander (LSL) to walk, i.e., it is important and imperative to design novel legged mobile landers (LMLs). This paper aims to address a novel synthesis method based on Truss-Mechanism Transformation (called as TMT method) for LMLs with the capabilities of trusses and mechanisms during different phases. The overall topological design concept and procedure of TMT method are proposed.\n\n## 1. Introduction\nDeep space exploration mainly includes three missions: \"Circling,\" \"Landing,\" and \"Returning\", while \"Base construction\" is a mission for the future. Stationary landers (LSLs) have advantages in stability and payload capacity but are limited in exploration range. Legged Mobile Landers (LMLs) can meet requirements for large area exploration and base construction. The biggest design challenge is the degree of freedom (DoF) contradiction: landing requires 0 DoF (truss), while walking requires multiple DoF (mechanism).\n\n## 2. Truss-Mechanism Transformation (TMT) Method\nThe TMT method involves four stages:\n1. **Motion Requirements:** Extracting motions from functions (landing buffer, walking, orientation adjustment, etc.).\n2. **Truss Design:** Designing polyhedral truss units using Euler's formula ($V - E + F = 2$).\n3. **Motion Design:** Allocating motions (joints) into the truss structure while maintaining \"Truss Position of Mechanism\" (TPM) using dead points or locking devices.\n4. **Type Evaluation:** Analyzing overconstraints and actuator positions.\n\n## 3. Results and Design Analysis\nThe paper applies the TMT method to the Chang'e lander structure. By replacing static backbones with active limbs and utilizing electromagnetic bolts for phase transitions, the researchers synthesized several LML leg configurations:\n- **Case I:** UP&2-RUS with transmission limbs.\n- **Case II:** UP&2-RUS with electromagnetic bolts.\n- **Case III:** RU&2-RUS with electromagnetic bolts.\n\nEvaluations show these designs achieve zero overconstraints ($\Delta=0$) and allow the body to have 6-DoF motion relative to the ground during walking and orientation phases.\n\n## 4. Conclusion\nThe TMT method is universal and effective for the topological design of robots that transform between truss and mechanism phases. These LMLs provide technical support for future deep space exploration and automated base construction missions on the Moon, Mars, and asteroids.
