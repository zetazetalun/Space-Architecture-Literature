# Microbially-Enhanced Vanadium Mining and Bioremediation Under Micro- and Mars Gravity on the International Space Station

**Journal:** Frontiers in Microbiology  
**Published:** 01 April 2021  
**DOI:** 10.3389/fmicb.2021.641387

## Abstract
As humans explore and settle in space, they will need to mine elements to support industries such as manufacturing and construction. In preparation for the establishment of permanent human settlements across the Solar System, we conducted the ESA BioRock experiment on board the International Space Station to investigate whether biological mining could be accomplished under extraterrestrial gravity conditions. We tested the hypothesis that the gravity (g) level influenced the efficacy with which biomining could be achieved from basalt, an abundant material on the Moon and Mars, by quantifying bioleaching by three different microorganisms under microgravity, simulated Mars and Earth gravitational conditions.

## Key Findings
- **Microbial Efficacy:** *Sphingomonas desiccabilis* and *Bacillus subtilis* enhanced the leaching of vanadium by 184.92% to 283.22% compared to sterile controls.
- **Gravity Independence:** Gravity level (microgravity, simulated Mars gravity, or Earth gravity) did not have a significant effect on mean leaching efficacy.
- **Structural Application:** Vanadium is highlighted as a critical additive for steel alloys used in structural materials for buildings and transportation beyond Earth.

## Methodology
- **Substrate:** Olivine basalt rock collected in Iceland (analog for lunar/Martian regolith).
- **Microorganisms:** *Sphingomonas desiccabilis* CP1D, *Bacillus subtilis* NCIB 3610, and *Cupriavidus metallidurans* CH34.
- **Apparatus:** KUBIK incubators on the ISS using Experiment Units (EU) with gas-permeable silicone membranes.
- **Analysis:** Inductively Coupled Plasma Mass Spectrometry (ICP-MS) was used to determine the concentration of vanadium in the bulk fluid and cell pellets.

## Conclusions
The results demonstrate the potential to use microorganisms to conduct elemental mining and bioindustrial processes in space locations with non-Earth gravity. This provides a baseline for a miniature space biomining reactor that could be upscaled to an industrial size to provide elements for the construction of high-quality metal alloys to support extraterrestrial construction.
