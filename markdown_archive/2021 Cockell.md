# Microbially-Enhanced Vanadium Mining and Bioremediation Under Micro- and Mars Gravity on the International Space Station

**Authors:** Charles S. Cockell, Rosa Santomartino, Kai Finster, et al.
**Published:** 01 April 2021
**Journal:** Frontiers in Microbiology
**DOI:** 10.3389/fmicb.2021.641387

## Abstract
As humans explore and settle in space, they will need to mine elements to support industries such as manufacturing and construction. The ESA BioRock experiment on board the International Space Station investigated whether biological mining could be accomplished under extraterrestrial gravity conditions. The experiment tested the hypothesis that gravity (g) level influenced the efficacy of biomining from basalt. Results showed that *Sphingomonas desiccabilis* and *Bacillus subtilis* enhanced the leaching of vanadium under microgravity, simulated Mars, and Earth gravitational conditions by 184.92 to 283.22% compared to sterile controls. Gravity did not have a significant effect on mean leaching.

## Introduction
To move permanently into space, industrial processes such as mining natural resources must be implemented. Biomining uses microorganisms to enhance the release of elements from rocks. Vanadium (V) is of strong industrial interest as it is used in alloys to enhance hardness and tensile strength, suitable for structural materials, rovers, and pressurized buildings. The BioRock experiment investigated the leaching of elements from basalt, a regolith analog for the Moon and Mars.

## Methods
- **Microorganisms:** *Sphingomonas desiccabilis* CP1D, *Bacillus subtilis* NCIB 3610, and *Cupriavidus metallidurans* CH34.
- **Substrate:** Olivine basalt rock collected near Reykjavik, Iceland.
- **Gravity Conditions:** Microgravity, simulated Mars gravity (0.38 x g), and Earth gravity (1 x g) using KUBIK incubators on the ISS.
- **Duration:** 21 days of active growth and leaching.
- **Analysis:** ICP-MS (inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry) to determine vanadium concentration in the bulk fluid.

## Results

### Vanadium in Basalt Substrate
The vanadium concentration in the basalt was 175.03 ± 21.01 ppm.

| Organism | Gravity Condition | Quantity (ng) | Comparison with Control (%) |

## Discussion and Conclusion
*Sphingomonas desiccabilis* and *Bacillus subtilis* significantly enhanced vanadium leaching across all gravity conditions. The lack of significant difference between gravity levels suggests that biomining can be effectively performed on diverse planetary bodies (asteroids, Moon, Mars). This demonstrates the potential for using miniature bioreactors for extraterrestrial construction industry support and resource recycling.
