# Microbially-Enhanced Vanadium Mining and Bioremediation Under Micro- and Mars Gravity on the International Space Station

## Abstract
As humans explore and settle in space, they will need to mine elements to support industries such as manufacturing and construction. This study conducted the ESA BioRock experiment on the International Space Station (ISS) to investigate whether biological mining could be accomplished under extraterrestrial gravity conditions. The experiment used basalt, an abundant material on the Moon and Mars, and three microorganisms (*Sphingomonas desiccabilis*, *Bacillus subtilis*, and *Cupriavidus metallidurans*) under microgravity, simulated Mars, and Earth gravitational conditions.

## Key Research Areas
- **Biomining (ISRU):** Using microbes to leach structural metals from regolith.
- **Structural Materials:** Vanadium (V) extraction for high-strength, corrosion-resistant steel alloys.
- **Gravitational Effects:** Impacts of micro- and fractional gravity on microbial-mineral interactions.

## Methodology
- **Substrate:** Olivine basalt rocks (1.5 cm x 1.6 cm x 3 mm slides).
- **Hardware:** BioRock Experimental Unit (EU) within KUBIK incubators on the ISS.
- **Gravity Levels:** Microgravity (μg), simulated Mars gravity (0.38 g), and Earth gravity (1 g).
- **Analysis:** ICP-MS (inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry) to measure vanadium quantities.

## Results Summary
| Treatment | Gravity Condition | Quantity (ng) | % vs Control |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|--------------|
| *S. desiccabilis* | Microgravity | 19.84 ± 2.29 | 184.92 ± 75.33 |
| *S. desiccabilis* | Mars gravity | 23.45 ± 2.70 | 216.32 ± 68.43 |
| *B. subtilis* | Microgravity | 30.38 ± 3.90 | 283.22 ± 116.44 |
| *B. subtilis* | Mars gravity | 23.83 ± 4.78 | 219.78 ± 78.37 |
| Sterile Control | Microgravity | 10.73 ± 4.19 | - |

## Discussion and Conclusion
*S. desiccabilis* and *B. subtilis* significantly enhanced vanadium leaching compared to sterile controls across all gravity conditions. Crucially, the level of gravity did not have a significant effect on the mean leaching efficacy. These findings demonstrate the potential for ISRU biomining to support extraterrestrial settlement construction and industrial processes.
