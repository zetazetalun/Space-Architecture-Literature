# Two-stage DRL with hybrid perception of vision and force feedback for lunar construction robotic assembly control

**Authors:** Li Boxin, Wang Zhaokui  
**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 229 (2025) 357–373  
**Available online:** 18 January 2025  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.actaastro.2025.01.017

## Abstract
The moon, as the closest celestial body to Earth, serves as an outpost for humans to move further towards other deep space celestial bodies. Lunar construction is necessary for long-term deep space exploration and resource utilization. This paper proposes a two-stage Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) control method with hybrid perception of visual and force feedback for a lunar construction robot to perform assembly operations. A 1:1 simulation scenario and a ground real-robot prototype were developed to verify the control method, demonstrating effectiveness in autonomous assembly in open environments.

## 1. Introduction
Future lunar surface exploration requires tasks such as autonomous robotic assembly, robotic mining, In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU), and infrastructure construction. Robots are the main force in lunar construction due to extreme environments and scarcity of manpower. Unlike industrial scenarios, lunar construction occurs in an open, unstructured environment with large communication delays, making autonomous assembly a critical technology.

## 2. Mission Overview and Problem Statement
The mission consists of lunar construction robots, assembly objects, and a background lunar open-environment. The authors designed a **Lunar Construction Smart Robot (LCSR)** consisting of:
- Omnidirectional mobile chassis
- Lifting mechanism
- 6-DOF high-precision end effector
- 6-dimensional force sensor
- RGB-D cameras (eye-in-hand)

### 2.2 Problem Statement
The goal is to learn a manipulation policy based on DRL for assembly tasks. The state $s_t$ includes the six-dimensional pose, forces, and moments at the robot end effector. The observation $o_t$ consists of RGB-D images.

## 3. Control Method
The paper proposes a two-stage strategy based on the **Twin Delayed Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient (TD3)** algorithm:

1.  **Stage 1: Free spatial posture exploration:** Adjusts the end effector to align the assembly part with the target part using visual feedback (Vision-TD3).
2.  **Stage 2: Constrained contact interaction and locking:** Performs precise interactive control based on force feedback (Force-TD3) once contact is initiated.

### Staged Reward Functions
- **Vision Reward:** Based on the norm of position and attitude differences between the part and a virtual target point.
- **Force Reward:** Based on the first-order derivative of the piecewise force to manage the transition from contact to locking.

## 4. Simulation and Experiments
### 4.1 Simulation Setup
- **Platform:** CoppeliaSim with Bullet Physics 2.83.
- **Training:** Parallel training of Vision-TD3 and Force-TD3 networks.

### 4.2 Simulation Results
The Vision-TD3 network successfully reached the virtual target point within an error of 5 mm and 2 degrees. The Force-TD3 network successfully managed the locking mechanism, with the reward stabilizing after approximately 2,000 episodes.

## 5. Ground Real-Robot Experiment
A sim-to-real pipeline was established using **multi-Aruco visual calibration** to eliminate reality gaps. In 30 ground experiments, the robot successfully completed the assembly task 24 times, achieving an **80% success rate**.

## 6. Conclusion
The study confirms that the application of DRL and cross-disciplinary integration allows lunar robotic systems to perform complex assembly operations autonomously in open environments. Future work will focus on synchronous verification for connecting ground and lunar surfaces.
