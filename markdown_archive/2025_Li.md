# Two-stage DRL with hybrid perception of vision and force feedback for lunar construction robotic assembly control

**Journal:** Acta Astronautica  
**Volume:** 229 (2025) 357–373  
**Authors:** Li Boxin, Wang Zhaokui  
**Published by:** Elsevier Ltd. (January 2025)

## Abstract
Lunar construction is essential for long-term deep space exploration and resource utilization. This paper proposes a two-stage Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) control method for lunar construction robots performing assembly operations autonomously. The method utilizes hybrid perception from visual and force feedback. A staged reward function and a 1:1 simulation scenario were developed for policy training, followed by verification on a ground real-robot prototype using a sim-to-real pipeline to bridge the reality gap.

## 1. Introduction
Future lunar surface exploration requires autonomous robotic tasks such as exploration, assembly, mining, In Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU), and infrastructure construction. Robots are the primary workforce for lunar construction due to extreme conditions and communication delays. The assembly control of these robots is a critical technology for lunar infrastructure development.

## 2. Mission Overview and Problem Statement
### 2.1 Mission Overview
The scenario includes lunar construction robots, assembly objects, and an open lunar environment. The designed Lunar Construction Smart Robot (LCSR) features an omnidirectional mobile chassis, a 6-DOF high-precision end effector, and multi-modal sensors (RGB-D cameras and a 6-axis force sensor). The assembly uses a universal two-point docking interface.

## 3. Control Method
The control strategy is based on two stages using the Twin Delayed Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient (TD3) algorithm:
- **Stage 1: Free Spatial Posture Exploration:** Uses the Vision-TD3 network to align the end effector with a virtual target point using RGB-D images.
- **Stage 2: Constrained Contact Interaction and Locking:** Uses the Force-TD3 network for precise fine-tuning based on six-dimensional force sensor data to complete the mechanical locking.

## 4. Simulation and Experimental Setup
A 1:1 digital prototype environment was built in CoppeliaSim using Bullet Physics. Ground experiments used a real-robot prototype and a sim-to-real pipeline based on multi-ArUco visual calibration.

## 5. Results and Discussion
- **Simulation:** Vision-TD3 and Force-TD3 networks converged successfully. Vision-TD3 reached stable rewards after ~4.25k episodes; Force-TD3 after ~2k episodes.
- **Ground Experiments:** In 30 trials, the robot completed the assembly task 24 times, achieving an 80% success rate. Asynchronous locking at the two points was observed and handled by the force control policy.

## 6. Conclusion
The two-stage DRL control method with hybrid perception effectively enables autonomous lunar robotic assembly. Future work will focus on embodied intelligence and parallel intelligence to improve performance and accuracy further.
