# Silicate-lunar regolith composite: A vacuum self-hardened and comprehensively durable extraterrestrial construction material

## Abstract
Extraterrestrial exploration, represented by Lunar base construction, has been promoted worldwide. This paper proposes a novel extraterrestrial construction material composed of Lunar/Martian regolith simulants and 4 wt% of silicate adhesive. This silicate-regolith composite (SLRC) self-hardens in vacuum within 4.5 h to achieve 29 MPa of strength. It shows comprehensive durability (>70% residual strength) against vacuum exposure, temperature fluctuations, and γ-ray/proton radiation. The solidification originates from vacuum dehydration, where silica clusters cohere into a solid network.

## 1. Introduction
Future lunar habitats face extreme environments (-200 °C to 120 °C), high vacuum (10^-9 Pa), and radiation. Transporting materials is costly, necessitating in-situ resource utilization (ISRU). Existing methods like high-temperature sintering (>1000 °C) or polymers/sulfur binders have drawbacks (low efficiency, durability risks, or high energy needs). This study explores vacuum self-hardening of silicate-based composites as a low-energy solution.

## 2. Materials and Methods
### 2.1 Materials
- **Lunar Regolith Simulants (LRS):** LRS-1 (Apollo-17 simulant), LRS-2, LRS-3.
- **Martian Regolith Simulant (MRS).**
- **Silicate Solution:** Sodium silicate (SS) with varying moduli (2.0, 2.5, 3.0) and modifying agents (sodium pyrophosphate, sodium dodecyl sulfate).

### 2.2 Sample Preparation
Regolith and silicate solution (0.14:1 mass ratio) were mixed, cold-pressed at 9.5 MPa into 40x10x10 mm³ green bodies, and exposed to a vacuum (<10 Pa).

## 3. Results and Discussion
- **Self-hardening:** SS2.5 achieved 5.48 MPa flexural and 18.19 MPa compressive strength. SS2.5HT (hydrothermal treatment) reached 6.54 MPa flexural and 20.34 MPa compressive strength within 4.5 h.
- **Cryogenic Performance:** Freeze-dried SLRC prepared at -40 °C generated 19.54 MPa compressive strength after 15 h.
- **Durability:** After 108 days of simulated lunar diurnal cycles, the material reserved 71-81% of flexural and 88-98% of compressive strength.

## 4. Conclusion
The SLRC offers a versatile solution for in-situ extraterrestrial construction, particularly for cryogenic polar regions. It requires minimal terrestrial binder and energy, relying on vacuum-induced dehydration for rapid solidification.
