# Silicate-lunar regolith composite: A vacuum self-hardened and comprehensively durable extraterrestrial construction material ## Abstract Extraterrestrial exploration, represented by Lunar base construction, has been promoted worldwide. Herein we proposed a novel extraterrestrial construction material composed of Lunar/Martian regolith simulants and only 4 wt% of silicate adhesive. This silicate-regolith composite can be directly self-hardened in vacuum within 4.5 h without any curing to achieve 29 MPa of hardening strength. It shows durability against long-term vacuum exposure, temperature differences (-200 to 120 C), and radiation. ## 1. Introduction Establishing a lunar habitat is supported by NASA (Artemis) and ESA projects. Infrastructure faces rigorous environments: temperature differences, vacuum (10^-10 Pa), and radiation. Novel construction materials are needed to utilize lunar resources (ISRU) and minimize transport costs. ## 2. Materials and methods ### 2.1. Materials Lunar regolith simulants (LRS-1, LRS-2, LRS-3) and Martian regolith simulant (MRS) were used. Silicate solution (sodium silicate) with modulus 2.0, 2.5, and 3.0 was employed. ### 2.2. Sample preparation Mixing regolith and 4 wt% silicate binder, cold-pressing at 9.5 MPa, and vacuum dehydration (<10 Pa). Testing environments mimicked lunar diurnal cycles (-196 to 78 C). ## 3. Results and discussion ### 3.1. Self-hardening and durability SLRC rapidly loses water and self-hardens in vacuum. SS2.5 generates 5.48 MPa flexural and 18.19 MPa compressive strength. Modified binders (SS2.5HT) achieve 20.34 MPa compressive strength within 4.5 h. Over long-term (108 days), SLRC retains 71-81% of flexural and 88-98% of compressive strength. ### 3.4. Comprehensive evaluation SLRC consumes minimal carried materials (<=4%) compared to geopolymers (6-32%) or sulfur concrete (25-40%). It allows cryogenic preparation below -40 C, unlike geopolymers requiring >60 C curing. ## 4. Conclusion The material satisfies requirements for in-situ extraterrestrial construction, especially for polar regions and lava tubes. It simplifies logistics by requiring only a single-component binder and no thermal curing.
