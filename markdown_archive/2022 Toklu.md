# Lunar soils, simulants and lunar construction materials: An overview

**Authors:** Yusuf Cengiz Toklu, Pinar Akpinar
**Published:** Advances in Space Research 70 (2022) 762–779

## Abstract
This paper presents an overview of three aspects of using local materials in lunar construction: the nature of in-situ soils obtained from lunar missions, the variety of lunar soil simulants produced on Earth, and their use in producing lunar construction materials like lunar concrete and bricks.

## 1. Introduction
Lunar construction materials will primarily be raw materials found on the Moon due to the high cost of transportation from Earth. The primary available material is regolith, which can be used to yield materials like lunar cement, concrete, and bricks.

## 2. Lunar missions and lunar soil samples
- **Apollo Missions:** 382 kg of samples brought to Earth from six landing sites.
- **Luna Program (Soviet):** ~300 g of samples from three missions.
- **Chang’e 5 (China):** ~2 kg of samples returned in 2020.

## 3. Lunar soil simulants
The paper identifies 54 different lunar soil simulants developed globally between 1971 and 2021. Simulants are necessary for geotechnical testing, mobility studies, and construction material research because original lunar samples are extremely limited.

## 4. Lunar construction materials
Various ISRU-based construction materials are summarized, focusing on strength and manufacturing processes.

## 5. Conclusive remarks
The study concludes that while numerous techniques for ISRU-based construction show promise (sintering, 3D printing, waterless concrete), the research is still in its infancy. There is a critical need for standardized, high-fidelity simulants to ensure accurate results before mission deployment.
