# Lunar soils, simulants and lunar construction materials: An overview

**Authors:** Yusuf Cengiz Toklu, Pinar Akpinar  
**Journal:** Advances in Space Research 70 (2022) 762–779  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.asr.2022.05.017

## Abstract
One of the hot subjects in space civil engineering is the research for establishing outposts on space bodies, starting with the Moon. This paper presents an overview of three aspects: the nature of in-situ soils from lunar missions, the variety of lunar soil simulants produced on Earth, and their use in producing lunar construction materials.

## 1. Introduction
Construction materials for lunar structures will be mostly raw materials found on the Moon or manufactured using lunar soil as the main input due to the high cost of transporting materials from Earth. The primary available material is regolith.

## 2. Lunar Missions and Soil Samples
- **Luna Program (Soviet):** Missions brought back samples (Luna 16, 20, 24).
- **Apollo Program (US):** Missions 11, 12, 14, 15, 16, and 17 brought ~382 kg of samples.
- **Chang’e 5 (China):** Brought ~2 kg of samples in 2020.

## 3. Lunar Soil Simulants
Over 50 simulants have been developed globally to substitute for the limited supply of real lunar regolith. Key examples include:
- **MLS-1 (USA, 1971):** High-Ti basalt based mare simulant.
- **JSC-1 (USA, 1993):** Widely used mare simulant from volcanic ash.
- **FJS-1 (Japan, 1998):** Fuji Japanese Simulant.
- **EAC-1 (Germany, 2017):** Primary simulant for ESA's LUNA testbed.
- **TBG-1 (Turkey, 2021):** Recent simulant resembling Apollo-14 samples.

## 4. Lunar Construction Materials

### 4.1. Cements and Concretes
- **Sulfur-based concrete:** Thermoplastic material, water-less, quick hardening (70-80% strength in 24h). Requires sulfur (found in troilite) and aggregates.
- **Polymer-based concrete:** Uses binders like epoxy or polyethylene.
- **Geopolymer concrete:** Produced through dehydration-polycondensation of regolith with alkaline activators.

### 4.2. 3D Printing (Additive Manufacturing)
- **Binder Jetting:** Cesaretti et al. (2014) produced full-scale components using liquid binder and metal oxide.
- **Sintering:** Solar and laser sintering methods are being explored to bond regolith without terrestrial binders.

## 5. Conclusion
There is still a long way to go for producing lunar soil simulants with all properties matching original samples. Construction methods using in-situ resources (Class III habitats) are essential for long-term lunar colonization.
