# Regolith sintering and 3D printing for lunar construction: An extensive review on recent progress

## Abstract
In-Situ Resource Utilisation (ISRU) is increasingly being seen as a viable and essential approach to constructing infrastructure for human habitation on the moon. This review presents a comprehensive study on the development of several sintering techniques of lunar regolith simulant to demonstrate its feasibility for ISRU on the moon. Various critical processing parameters are evaluated in pursuit of creating a structural material that can withstand the extreme lunar environment.

## 1. Background of lunar construction
Transporting materials from Earth is prohibitively expensive ($3k-$62k per kg). A sustainable long-term lunar base can only be realized through ISRU, utilizing locally resourced materials (regolith) to construct habitats and landing pads.

## 2. Regolith Simulants and Soils
### 2.1 Characteristics of regolith
- **Bulk density:** 1.5–1.66 g/cm³.
- **Thermal conductivity:** 0.001 W/mK under vacuum.
- **Composition:** >40% silicon, with varying portions of Ti, Al, and Fe depending on location (Mare vs. Highlands).
- **Melting point:** Between 1100 and 1380 °C.

## 3. Bulk Sintering
### 3.1 Radiant Heat Sintering
- **Method:** Uses heating elements to irradiate sample volumes.
- **Performance:** Achieved compressive strengths of 30–200 MPa with porosity below 1%.

### 3.2 Microwave Sintering
- **Method:** 2.45 GHz frequency couples well with regolith.
- **Challenges:** Heating 'runaway' can cause non-uniformity; thermal cracking is a risk.
- **Performance:** Typical strengths of 10–50 MPa.

### 3.3 Spark Plasma Sintering (SPS)
- **Method:** Pulsed electric current and pressure in a vacuum.
- **Performance:** High density (up to 2.9 g/cm³) and rapid processing at lower temperatures.

## 4. Focused Sintering
### 4.1 Laser Sintering
- **Method:** High-power laser fuses powder. Excellent absorption in the 1000 nm range.
- **Applications:** Small precision-engineered parts or paving for dust mitigation.

### 4.2 Solar Sintering
- **Method:** Concentrated sunlight via heliostats/mirrors.
- **Project Example:** RegoLight (ESA/DLR). Achieved 2.31 MPa compressive strength for bulk shielding.

## 5. Additive Manufacturing (AM)
- **Direct Ink Writing (DIW):** Uses regolith-based pastes with organic binders. Suitable for complex structures but limited by binder stability and low mechanical strength (1-19 MPa).

## 6. Conclusion
ISRU is the pragmatic approach for lunar settlement. While radiant and SPS methods provide superior structural strength, 3D laser and solar sintering offer geometric complexity for specialized components.
