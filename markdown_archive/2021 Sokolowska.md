# Rheological Properties of Lunar Mortars

**Authors:** Joanna J. Sokołowska, Piotr Woyciechowski, Maciej Kalinowski  
**Journal:** Applied Sciences (MDPI)  
**Published:** 28 July 2021  
**DOI:** [10.3390/app11156961](https://doi.org/10.3390/app11156961)

## Abstract
NASA plans to resume manned missions to the Moon and establish permanent habitats. These habitats are expected to be built using local resources, specifically lunar regolith as aggregate in lunar concrete. This study investigates the rheological properties (consistency) of cement-based "lunar micro-mortars" and "lunar mortars" using a Polish Lunar Regolith Simulant (LRS) that maps the grain size distribution and morphology of real regolith. Results suggest that mortars containing LRS are suitable for applications like 3D concrete printing on the Moon.

## 1. Introduction
The development of extraterrestrial habitats requires innovative material and execution concepts. Due to the high cost of transport (ca. USD 75,000/kg), In Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) is essential. While polymer and sulfur concretes are explored, this study focuses on ordinary Portland cement (OPC) based lunar mortars, assuming water ice could be sourced locally.

## 2. Materials and Methods

### 2.1 Lunar Regolith Simulant (LRS)
- **Simulant Name:** AGK-2010 (Polish lunar regolith simulant).
- **Composition:** Mechanically crushed quartz, granite sand, and grit.
- **Morphology:** Sharp-edged particles.
- **Density:** 2.641 g/cm³.
- **Median Particle Size (d50):** 37.0 µm (compared to Apollo mission ranges of 42–130 µm).

### 2.2 Cement and Mortar Mixes
- **Binder:** Ordinary Portland cement (CEM I 42.5 R).
- **Admixture:** Polycarboxylate ether-based superplasticizer.
- **Test Groups:** Micro-mortars (cement + LRS/Quartz powder) and Lunar mortars (cement + sand + LRS/Quartz powder).

## 3. Results and Discussion

### 3.1 Consistency and Workability
- The sharp-edged morphology of LRS increases water demand compared to rounded sand particles.
- The use of superplasticizers allowed for the inclusion of LRS while maintaining a consistency suitable for 3D printing.
- A correlation was established between LRS content and mix consistency (flow diameter and Vicat penetration depth).

### 3.2 Mechanical Properties
- Mortars with 20% LRS in the aggregate (13.3% of total mass) were tested against standard mortars.

| Property | Standard Mortar (Ref) | 20% QP (Quartz Powder) | 20% LRS (Lunar Simulant) |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Compressive Strength (MPa) | 49.9 | 59.7 | 50.6 |
| Flexural Strength (MPa) | 6.9 | 9.5 | 6.8 |

## 4. Conclusions
- The Polish LRS (AGK-2010) effectively simulates the behavior of lunar regolith in cement pastes.
- Incorporating 20% LRS into the aggregate does not significantly decrease the mechanical performance of the mortar.
- The developed mortars meet the consistency requirements for 3D printing technology, which is a potential solution for lunar construction.
