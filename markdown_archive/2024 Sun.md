# Designing sustainable built environments for Mars habitation: Integrating innovations in architecture, systems, and human well-being

**Authors:** Hongli Sun, Mengfan Duan, Yifan Wu, Yunyi Zeng, Hengxin Zhao, Shuangdui Wu, Borong Lin, Ronggui Yang, Gang Tan  
**Journal:** Nexus (CellPress)  
**Date:** September 17, 2024  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ynexs.2024.100030

## Summary
This review offers a perspective on developing Mars habitation modules from design to operation. It highlights the multidisciplinary challenges of combining resource sustainability with human health and well-being in the harsh Martian environment.

## Key Habitation Technologies

### Habitat Construction
- **Materials:** Abundant Martian basalt is a primary candidate for construction due to radiation resistance and high specific heat. Sulfur-based concrete is highlighted for its ease of operation and fast solidification in dry/cold environments. 
- **Techniques:** 
  - **3D Printing:** Utilizing in situ regolith for module construction to protect against radiation.
  - **Robotic Construction:** Automated assembly systems (e.g., 'Chinese Super Mason') to assemble bricks produced by 3D printing.
  - **Structural Forms:** Above-ground (inflatable cores with shells), semi-underground, and underground (utilizing lava tubes to mitigate radiation and thermal risks).

### Habitat Operation
- **Thermal Control:** Use of high-insulation materials (aerogels, vacuum panels), heat pipes, and phase change materials.
- **Air Support:** Electrolyzed water and CO2-to-Oxygen conversion via solid oxide electrolysis or microwave-plasma technology.
- **Energy:** Nuclear energy for consistent output; PV panels (8-13.5% efficiency on Mars); and vortex-induced vibration wind generators.

### Human Well-being
- **Indoor Environment:** Dynamic bionic control of temperature and lighting (biodynamic lighting) to maintain circadian rhythms and psychological health.
