# Designing sustainable built environments for Mars habitation: Integrating innovations in architecture, systems, and human well-being

**Authors:** Hongli Sun, Mengfan Duan, Yifan Wu, Yunyi Zeng, Hengxin Zhao, Shuangdui Wu, Borong Lin, Ronggui Yang, and Gang Tan

**Publication:** Nexus 1, 100030, September 17, 2024
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ynexs.2024.100030

## Summary
Migration to Mars and building livable habitation gradually become prosperous and arduous roads for human progress. Although many technical breakthroughs have been achieved for supporting migration, sustainable survival on Mars needs further attention. The Mars habitation module serves as humans' first barrier from the harsh extraterrestrial conditions that people will spend almost 100% of time in the module. Thus, the indoor physical environment plays an important role in long-term and even permanent living on Mars. However, unlike developing a single technology, achieving a sustainable extraterrestrial residential environment is not simply a technical stacking but a comprehensive multidisciplinary issue that combines resources, energy, and human health. Therefore, this review gives a new angle of providing a developmental path from "habitation design to operation," illustrating a systemic outlook of promising Mars habitation environment control technologies for the sustainability of both habitation resources and the occupant’s health.

## Introduction
Mars’s environment is much harsher for human life than the Earth’s (colder, less solar energy, no oxygen, higher electron radiation), yet it shows better comprehensive conditions than the ISS or Moon (appropriate rhythm of 24 h, safer atmosphere, larger space, and in situ resources). Mars dwelling involves two key aspects: resource sustainability and human sustainability, encompassing both efficient construction and operation of Mars habitation and the healthy survival of humans.

## Mars Habitation Technologies: From Design to Operation

### Design of Mars habitation module
Concepts include:
- **Above-ground:** Prefab pressurized cores with outer shells to resist radiation (e.g., Mars Ice House, Marsha).
- **Underground/Semi-underground:** Utilizing Martian lava tubes to withstand space risks.

### Construction and Materials
- **ISRU:** Utilizing Martian basalt, soil, clay, and steel. Sulfur-based concrete is identified as suitable due to ease of operation and fast solidification in cold environments.
- **Automated Construction:** 3D printing offers possibilities for using in situ materials. Robotic systems like the "Chinese Super Mason" are mentioned for assembling planetary bricks.

### Operation of Mars Habitation Module
- **Thermal Control:** High-insulation materials (aerogels, PU foam), heat pipes, and phase change materials.
- **Air Generation:** Electrolyzed water and CO₂ recovery systems.
- **Lighting:** Rhythmic artificial lighting to maintain human biorhythms due to the absence of natural daylight cycles.

## New Paths of Mars Habitation Design
- **Performance-Centered Design:** Shifting from structure-centered to performance-centered parametric design using AI and genetic algorithms to optimize energy efficiency and thermal stability.
- **Building Envelope:** Innovations in translucent aerogels for windows and Building Integrated Photovoltaics (BIPV).

## Key Findings Summary
1. Mars habitation requires a multidisciplinary integration of architecture, systems engineering, and human psychology, moving from purely technical solutions to human-centered designs. 
2. In situ resource utilization (ISRU), particularly using basalt fiber and sulfur-based concrete combined with autonomous 3D printing, is essential for sustainable habitat construction.
3. Future development should leverage AI and parametric design to optimize building envelopes for extreme thermal regulation and air-tightness while maintaining occupant well-being through bionic environmental controls.
