**Journal:** Nexus (Cell Press Partner Journal)
**Date:** September 17, 2024
**DOI:** [10.1016/j.ynexs.2024.100030](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ynexs.2024.100030)

## SUMMARY
Migration to Mars and building livable habitation gradually become prosperous and arduous roads for human progress. The Mars habitation module serves as humans' first barrier from the harsh extraterrestrial conditions. This review provides a systemic outlook of promising Mars habitation environment control technologies for the sustainability of both habitation resources and the occupant's health, shifting from a "technology-centered" to a "human-centered" design to operation approach.

## INTRODUCTION
Mars exploration faces primary obstacles in transportation and dwelling. Mars dwelling involves two key aspects: resource sustainability (efficient construction and operation) and human sustainability (healthy survival). Previous experiences from Antarctic stations and the ISS provide references, but Mars requires higher self-sufficiency due to greater isolation.

## MARS HABITATION TECHNOLOGIES

### Design of Mars habitation module
Habitation designs are categorized by location and structural type:
1. **Above-ground:** Pre-fabricated pressurized/inflatable cores with outer shells (e.g., Mars Ice House, Marsha).
2. **Underground/Semi-underground:** Utilizing Martian lava tubes to protect against radiation and thermal extremes.

### Construction Materials & ISRU
- **Martian Basalt:** Radiation resistance, high specific heat, and low permeability.
- **Sulfur-based Concrete:** Developed using Martian soil (avg. 6 wt% sulfur). It offers ease of operation, fast solidification, and recyclability.
- **3D Printing:** Technologies for utilizing in situ materials are evolving, including high-temperature sintering to produce "Planetary LEGO Bricks."
- **Robotic Construction:** Automated systems (e.g., Team Zopherus, Chinese Super Mason) for assembly and printing.

### Operation & Environmental Control
- **Thermal Control:** High-insulation materials (aerogels, multilayer insulation) and active systems (heat pipes, phase change materials).
- **Air & Water:** Regenerative life support systems (electrolyzed water, CO₂ recovery, waste air treatment).
- **Lighting:** Rhythmic artificial lighting to support human circadian rhythms in the absence of natural cycles.

## PERFORMANCE-CENTERED DESIGN
Modern design methodologies include data-driven parametric design, generative design via neural networks, and building optimization using genetic algorithms. These tools aim to balance conflicting objectives like energy efficiency, structural reliability, and occupant comfort.

## CONCLUSION
Sustainable Mars migration requires a multidisciplinary integration of architecture, materials science, and AI. Future work must bridge the gap between initial design and long-term operational performance, ensuring that habitats are not only structurally sound but also conducive to human health.
