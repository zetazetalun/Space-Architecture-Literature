# Designing sustainable built environments for Mars habitation: Integrating innovations in architecture, systems, and human well-being

**Authors:** Hongli Sun, Mengfan Duan, Yifan Wu, Yunyi Zeng, Hengxin Zhao, Shuangdui Wu, Borong Lin, Ronggui Yang, and Gang Tan
**Publication:** Nexus (Cell Press Partner Journal), Volume 1, 100030, September 17, 2024
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ynexs.2024.100030

## Summary
Migration to Mars and building livable habitation are arduous roads for human progress. The Mars habitation module serves as humans' first barrier from harsh extraterrestrial conditions. This review provides a developmental path from "habitation design to operation," illustrating a systemic outlook of promising Mars habitation environment control technologies for the sustainability of both resources and occupant health.

## Introduction
Mars offers better comprehensive conditions for habitation compared to the ISS or the Moon due to its 24h rhythm, safer atmosphere, and available in situ resources. Mars dwelling involves two key aspects: resource sustainability (efficient construction/operation) and human sustainability (healthy survival).

## Mars Habitation Technologies

### Design of Mars Habitation Module
*   **Module Forms:** Above-ground (pressurized inflatable cores with outer shells like Mars Ice House), semi-underground, and underground (utilizing lava tubes).
*   **Materials & ISRU:** Martian basalt is a primary resource for radiation resistance and low permeability. Martian soil with high sulfur content (avg 6 wt%) enables sulfur-based concrete, which offers ease of operation, fast solidification, and recyclability in cold environments.
*   **Automated Construction:** 3D printing offers possibilities for utilizing in situ materials. Robotic construction systems (e.g., Team Zopherus, Chinese Super Mason) are being developed for autonomous assembly of planetary bricks.

### Operation and Well-being
*   **Thermal Control:** Use of multi-layer insulation (MLI), PU polyfoam, heat pipes, and flexible radiation materials.
*   **Life Support:** Regenerative systems for air (electrolyzed water, CO2 scrubbing) and water recovery.
*   **Human Factors:** Implementation of biodynamic lighting to mimic Earth's circadian rhythms and personalized ventilation/thermal controls using AI and reinforcement learning.

## New Paths of Design
Performance-centered building structures utilize parametric architectural design and AI (genetic algorithms) to balance conflicting objectives like volume factor, window-to-wall ratio, and thermal insulation. Building-integrated photovoltaics (BIPV) are suggested to augment ground-based solar plants.
