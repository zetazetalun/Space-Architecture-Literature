## ABSTRACT
The greenhouse is a vital module for a long-duration mission to support fresh nutrition for the crew. This paper discusses two concepts of extraterrestrial greenhouses. The first one considers a habitable greenhouse, which has physical features of the habitat module. Human factors are the main focus of such design, aiming to design an interior garden for the crew. The second approach is an industrial greenhouse to produce maximum nutrition with the lowest cargo mass and resource consumptions. Such a low-pressure greenhouse has the optimum environment for plant growth. This paper presents a new concept of a greenhouse as a prebreathing chamber because the pressure value does not affect the plant growth cycle. A greenhouse module would accommodate a spectrum of pressure, oxygen, and carbon dioxide levels from the plant’s to human’s living zones. To prevent decompression sickness, the crew has to dedicate 2–4 hours for prebreathing in an airlock before and after each EVA. From a psychological standpoint, spending time in a greenhouse environment would be a better alternative to airlock conditions.

## INTRODUCTION
For humans to survive a long-duration mission on Mars's surface, the presence of a greenhouse module is essential. Two categories of greenhouse designs have been proposed: an industrial-scale greenhouse, which prioritizes efficiency, and a habitable greenhouse, which supports psychological wellness.

## HABITABLE GREENHOUSES
This approach assumes that the habitat and greenhouse utilize a similar structure and function under equal pressure (101.3 kPa). This simplifies the overall system and enables modules' individual ECLSS systems to unify as one expanded system. All atmospheric parameters would be similar to Earth conditions.

## INDUSTRIAL GREENHOUSE APPROACH
Designed to maintain a low-pressure environment to minimize the use of resources. A pressure under 60 kPa allows for using new types of thermal insulation, such as materials based on SiO2 aerogel or their combination with vacuum insulation panels. The application of aerogel-type materials can provide translucent qualities of the external envelop structure.

## THE MIDDLE PATH
A third greenhouse design approach is to allow its internal pressure to fluctuate, accommodating the needs of crewmembers and plants alike.

### The Pre-Breathe Greenhouse
The proposed low-pressure greenhouse can enhance the crew's pre-breathing routine and reduce stress levels by offering interactions with plants. The greenhouse architecture utilizes 34.48 to 56.5 kPa pressure to accommodate operational modes.

## CONCLUSIONS
A pre-breathe greenhouse approach may offer the maximum benefits while utilizing fewer resources. It offers a compromise between habitable and industrial designs, providing multifunctional capabilities, low impact on habitat systems, and lower launch/landing mass requirements.
