# Development of lunar regolith-based composite for in-situ 3D printing via high-pressure extrusion system

**Authors:** Hua ZHAO, Jihong ZHU, Shangqin YUAN, Shaoying LI, Weihong ZHANG
**Published in:** Frontiers of Mechanical Engineering (2023) 18(2): 29
**DOI:** 10.1007/s11465-022-0745-8

## Abstract
To fully utilize the in-situ resources on the moon to facilitate the establishment of a lunar habitat is significant for long-term residence. This work proposes a cost-effective melting extrusion system for lunar regolith-based composite printing, using engineering thermoplastic powders (PA12) as a bonding agent. The high-pressure extrusion system enhances interface affinity and maximizes the loading ratio of regolith up to 80 wt.%. Mechanical properties including tensile strength (max 36.2 MPa) and impact toughness (max 5.15 kJ/m²) were evaluated, demonstrating suitability for lunar surface construction.

## 1. Introduction
Lunar construction requires utilizing local resources (ISRU) to avoid the high cost of Earth-to-Moon transportation. Lunar regolith provides excellent thermal and radiation protection. Additive manufacturing (AM) offers geometric freedom for habitat design. While laser sintering is common, it is energy-intensive. Melt extrusion molding is proposed as a simpler, more stable alternative.

### 2.4 Specimen Fabrication
Two types of printers were used: a piston-based pellet extruder for small batches and a robot-assisted screw-based extruder for large components. The screw-based system achieved loading up to 80 wt.% regolith.

**Table 2: Process parameters for tensile specimen fabrication**

- Infill: 100% (Rectilinear 45°/-45°)

## 3. Results and Discussion
- **Thermal Analysis:** DSC tests showed melting peaks around 187.7–187.9 °C. Addition of basalt promotes recrystallization of PA12.
- **Mechanical Properties:** Tensile strength decreases as basalt weight fraction increases, while elastic modulus increases (basalt acts as a hard phase). Maximum tensile strength was 36.162 MPa (40 wt.% basalt, 225 °C, 7 mm/s).
- **Impact Properties:** Impact toughness increases with basalt content. At 60 wt.% basalt, impact toughness reached 5.15 kJ/m² (45°/-45° infill).

## 4. Conclusions
The study verified the feasibility of high-pressure melt extrusion for lunar ISRU. The system allows high regolith loading (80 wt.%) and produces components with improved impact resistance compared to pure polymers, suitable for resisting meteorite impacts. Large-scale habitat models were successfully printed using a robotic screw-extrusion system.
