# Development of lunar regolith-based composite for in-situ 3D printing via high-pressure extrusion system

**Authors:** Hua ZHAO, Jihong ZHU, Shangqin YUAN, Shaoying LI, Weihong ZHANG
**Affiliation:** Northwestern Polytechnical University, Xi’an, China
**Publication Year:** 2023
**Journal:** Front. Mech. Eng. 2023, 18(2): 29
**DOI:** 10.1007/s11465-022-0745-8

## Abstract
To fully utilize the in-situ resources on the moon to facilitate the establishment of a lunar habitat is significant to realize the long-term residence of mankind. This work proposes a cost-effective melting extrusion system for lunar regolith-based composite printing, employing engineering thermoplastic powders (PA12 nylon) as a bonding agent. Mechanical properties including tensile strength, elastic modulus, and impact performance were evaluated. Results show a maximum tensile strength of 36.2 MPa and impact toughness of 5.15 kJ/m². The system can increase the effective loading fraction of regolith up to 80 wt.% and adapt to extreme conditions for in-situ manufacturing.

## 1 Introduction
Construction of lunar habitats must protect humans from temperature differences, vacuum, meteorite impacts, and radiation while using local resources (ISRU). Current 3D printing methods using raw regolith often consume high energy. This study explores melt extrusion molding (MEM) as a more convenient and cost-effective alternative to laser sintering.

## 2 Experimental

- **Regolith Simulant:** Basalt powder (particle size 50–200 μm, mean size ~101 μm).
- **Binder:** PA12 thermoplastic powder.
- **Composites:** PA12-Basalt mixtures at 40, 50, and 60 wt.% basalt.

### 2.4 Specimen Fabrication
Two types of printers were used:
1. **Piston-based pellet extruder** (for small-batch testing).
2. **Robot-assisted screw-based pellet extruder** (for large-scale components).

## 3 Results and Discussion

### 3.1 Thermal and Microstructure Analysis
- Adding basalt particles promotes the recrystallization of PA12, benefiting the melt extrusion process.
- Regolith simulant acts as a hard phase, significantly improving deformation resistance but creating crack points at the interface which can reduce overall tensile strength at very high loadings.

### 3.3 Mechanical Properties
- **Tensile Strength:** Decreases as basalt loading increases from 40% to 60%.
- **Elastic Modulus:** Increases with basalt loading.
- **Maximum Tensile Strength:** 36.2 MPa (at 40 wt.% basalt, 7 mm/s printing speed).
- **Impact Toughness:** Reached 5.15 kJ/m² at 60 wt.% basalt, indicating improved resistance to meteorite impacts.

## 4 Conclusions
Lunar regolith-based composites can be effectively printed via high-pressure melt extrusion. The screw-based extruder allows for a high regolith loading of up to 80 wt.%, making it a highly efficient technology for lunar in-situ manufacturing of habitats and large structural components.
