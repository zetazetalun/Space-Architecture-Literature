# Development of lunar regolith-based composite for in-situ 3D printing via high-pressure extrusion system

**Authors:** Hua ZHAO, Jihong ZHU, Shangqin YUAN, Shaoying LI, Weihong ZHANG
**Published in:** Front. Mech. Eng. 2023, 18(2): 29
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s11465-022-0745-8

## Abstract
To fully utilize the in-situ resources on the moon to facilitate the establishment of a lunar habitat is significant for long-term residence. This work proposes a cost-effective melting extrusion system for lunar regolith-based composite printing, using engineering thermoplastic powders (PA12) as a bonding agent. High-pressure melt extrusion can increase the effective loading fraction of regolith up to 80 wt.%. Printed specimens reached a maximum tensile strength of 36.2 MPa and impact toughness of 5.15 kJ/m².

## 1. Introduction
The construction of a lunar habitat requires protection from large temperature differences, high vacuum, meteorite impact, and radiation. Transporting construction materials from Earth is economically unaffordable, making lunar in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) essential. While methods like laser sintering and D-shape printing have been explored, melt extrusion molding offers a simpler, stable, and cost-effective alternative for lunar surface construction.

## 2. Experimental
### 2.1 Materials
- **Lunar Regolith Simulant:** Basalt powder with particle sizes mainly between 50 and 200 μm (mean 101.084 μm).
- **Binder:** PA12 powder (thermoplastic polymer).
- **Composites:** Mixed at 40, 50, and 60 wt.% basalt fractions initially, later increased to 80 wt.% for the screw-based system.

### 2.4 Specimens fabrication
Two types of printers were used:
1. **Piston-based pellet extruder:** For small-batch material performance experiments.
2. **Screw-based pellet extruder:** Mounted on a multi-axis robot for printing large-scale or complex curved components and verifying high-loading (80 wt.%) regolith utilization.

### 3.4 Manufacturing verification via screw-based pellet extruder
The screw-based extruder provided more uniform mixing and allowed for a basalt loading ratio of 80 wt.%. Successful prints included a lunar habitat architectural model, honeycomb structures, and board structures.

## 4. Conclusions
- Lunar regolith-based composites blended with thermoplastic polymers can be printed via high-pressure melt extrusion at relatively low temperatures.
- The maximum tensile strength reached 36.2 MPa (at 40 wt.% loading).
- Effective loading of lunar regolith can be increased up to 80 wt.% using screw-based extrusion.
- The method is efficient and adaptable for future lunar habitat construction.
