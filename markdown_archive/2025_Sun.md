## Abstract
Space resource development has become a frontier hot topic in international space exploration. The utilization of asteroid resources necessitates extensive regolith mining operations. Catering to the demands of extensive space activities, extraterrestrial construction, and resource development, this paper presents the design and simulation analysis of an extraterrestrial surface regolith composite sampling system. The findings demonstrate that the composite sampling approach, which combines spiral drilling and transport with kinetic energy directional excitation, is highly effective. It is capable of collecting no less than 500 g within 5 seconds at a rotation speed of 3000 rpm, while the reaction force remains below 5 N.

## Introduction
Space resource development is critical for planetary exploration and lunar space development. Asteroids are rich in precious metals and rare elements with significant economic potential. Unlike previous scientific missions, future exploration aims for large-scale mining and transportation technology of regolith to provide raw materials for extraterrestrial construction and resource development.

## Scheme Design of Sampling System
The proposed system addresses challenges like weak surface gravity and irregular shapes of asteroids. 

### Design of Kinetic Energy Directional Sampling
Uses a motor-driven brush on a rotating shaft to interact with particles, imparting velocity and directing them into a sample channel. This is crucial for microgravity conditions.

### Design of Auger Drilling Transport Sampling
Employs a cutting process where debris is expelled through an outer spiral flange. It allows collection from deeper levels and uses a deep groove spiral structure to retain regolith samples.

### Design of Compound Sampling Principle
The composite system combines these two methods: the drilling tool crushes rocks and excavates regolith, while the high-speed brush directs the loosened particles into the collection container.

### Kinetic Energy Sampling Reaction Force
Analysis shows a maximum reaction force of 6.6 N in the Z-axis, 0.78 N in the X-axis, and less than 1.3 N in the Y-axis. The low reaction force (under 7 N) makes it suitable for spacecraft operating in microgravity without heavy anchoring.

## Conclusion
The system demonstrates superior adaptability to varied asteroid surface conditions. Peak efficiency is achieved at 3000 rpm, collecting over 500 g in 5 seconds. The low reaction force profile ensures the stability of the mining craft during large-scale resource extraction.
