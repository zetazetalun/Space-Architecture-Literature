# The principle of topological interlocking in extraterrestrial construction

**Journal:** Acta Astronautica 57 (2005) 10 – 21
**Authors:** A.V. Dyskin, Y. Estrin, E. Pasternak, H.C. Khor, A.J. Kanel-Belov

## Abstract
Applications of a newly established principle of topological interlocking to different types of extraterrestrial construction are considered. Topological interlocking arises when elements of special shapes (usually convex or nearly convex) are arranged such that neither of them can be removed from the assembly without disturbing the neighbouring elements. Two types of structures are considered: mortar-free structures built from osteomorphic bricks and modular extraterrestrial bases or space ships organized in interlocking assemblies.

## 1. Introduction
Extraterrestrial construction requires methods that minimize the need for Earth-delivered materials. Challenges include vacuum conditions (detrimental to mortar curing), lunar dust (interfering with precision fits), and the need for radiation protection. Interlocking removes the need for binders like cement and water.

## 2. Principle of Topological Interlocking
Topological interlocking is achieved by geometry and arrangement where peripheral constraints lock all internal elements. 
*   **Osteomorphic Bricks:** Bricks with curved surfaces that prevent displacement along the normal to the assembly in either direction. They are self-adjusting, allowing for low-precision manufacturing and robotized assembly.
*   **Platonic Solids:** Tetrahedra, cubes, and octahedra can also be arranged into interlocking layers.

## 3. Extraterrestrial Construction based on Osteomorphic Bricks
### 3.1. Mortar-free structures
*   **Self-adjustment:** The blocks tolerate variations in dimensions and positioning.
*   **Constraints:** Structures require compression, which can be provided by planetary topography (pits, craters, caverns) or artificial means (external frames, post-tensioning cables).
*   **In situ production:** Sintering in a mould or selective laser sintering (SLS) of regolith dust are proposed for mass production.

### 3.2. Radiation Protection
A double-skin construction with shifted brick patterns can eliminate radiation passage channels inherent in mortar-free joints.

## 4. Protective Cladding
Osteomorphic tiles can be used to shield pre-manufactured modules or spacecraft, providing high fracture resistance. Unlike solid plates, fractures in interlocking assemblies are contained within individual blocks.

## 5. Interlocking Modular Structures
Modular bases or spaceships can be assembled from independent units (tetrahedra or cubes). 
*   **Self-assembly:** In low-gravity environments, applying lateral force to a group of uploaded modules can force them into a regular planar arrangement even on uneven ground.
*   **Repairability:** Faulty modules can be accessed by removing neighboring blocks along specific paths.

## 6. Conclusion
1. Osteomorphic bricks relax accuracy requirements for production/assembly and offer high fracture resistance.
2. In situ production via sintering is viable.
3. Modular interlocking allows for flexible spacecraft and station configurations.
4. Peripheral constraints are essential for structural integrity.
